<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Townley, Sir  Charles</span>  (1713&#150;1774), <span class="occ">herald</span>, was born on Tower Hill, London, on 7 May 1713, the eldest son of Charles Townley of Clapham, Surrey, and descended from a younger branch of the ancient family of Towneley Hall, near Burnley, Lancashire. <script>writeSeealsoLink('../view/article/27606/', "James Townley (1714&#150;1778)")</script> was his younger brother. He was sent to Merchant Taylors' School, London, in 1727. He entered the College of Arms and was appointed York herald in July 1735, Norroy king-of-arms on 2 November 1751, Clarenceux king-of-arms on 11 January 1755, and Garter principal king-of-arms on 27 April 1773. He was knighted at George III's coronation in 1761. On 24 June 1748 he married Mary, daughter of George Eastwood of Thornhill, Yorkshire. They had three sons and a daughter.<br>
<br>Townley died at his home in Camden Street, Islington, London, on 7 June 1774, and was buried, according to the terms of his will, in the family vault in the church of St Dunstan-in-the-East; his wife survived him. His eldest son, Charles Townley (1749&#150;1800), became Bluemantle pursuivant on 31 December 1774 and Lancaster herald on 24 December 1781.<br>
<br>Thompson Cooper<span class="italic">, rev. </span> J. A. Marchand </div>
