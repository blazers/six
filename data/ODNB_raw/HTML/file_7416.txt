<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Dee,  Duncan</span>  (1657&#150;1720), <span class="occ">lawyer</span>, was born on 3 November 1657, a younger son of Rowland Dee (1613&#150;1687), London merchant, and his wife, Jane (<i>d</i>. <i>c</i>.1698), and a grandson of <script>writeSeealsoLink('../view/article/7415/', "Arthur Dee")</script>, physician to Charles I. He was educated at Merchant Taylors' School (1672&#150;3), and went thence in 1673 to St John's College, Oxford. It does not appear that he graduated. He married Mary Everard at St Dionis Backchurch, London, on 4 July 1682 and in the same year was called to the bar at the Inner Temple. He was a common pleader of the City of London (1682&#150;90), a judge of the sheriff's court (1690&#150;1700), and common serjeant of the City of London from 1700 until he died. He was also commissioner of appeals in the excise in 1713&#150;14. Dee defended Dr Sacheverell in his trial before the House of Lords in 1710, speaking on four successive days. He lived in the parish of St Mary Aldermanbury from at least 1684&#151;he and his wife had eight children baptized in the parish church between 1684 and 1696. Dee died in 1720, and was buried on 8 June next to his son Duncan in the great vault in St Mary Aldermanbury.<br>
<br>C. J. Robinson<span class="italic">, rev. </span> S. M. Wynne </div>
