<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Price,  John</span>  (1773&#150;1801), <span class="occ">topographer</span>, was born at Leominster, Herefordshire. He gave lessons there in French, Latin, Italian, and Spanish. Subsequently he became a bookseller at Hereford, but finally settled at Worcester. He occasionally made walking tours on the continent. In 1795 he published <span class="italic">An Historical and Topographical Account of Leominster and its Vicinity</span>. This was followed in 1796 by <span class="italic">An Historical Account of the City of Hereford, with some Remarks on the River Wye</span>. This &#145;very respectable performance&#146; was founded on collections given to the writer by John Lodge, author of <span class="italic">Introductory Sketches towards a Topographical History of Herefordshire</span> (1793). In 1797 Price published <span class="italic">The Ludlow guide, comprising an historical account of the castle and town, with a survey of the various seats, views, &amp;c., in that neighbourhood</span>. In 1799 appeared a similar <span class="italic">Worcester Guide</span>. Price's other publications included <span class="italic">The Seaman's Return, or, The Unexpected Marriage</span> (1795), an operatic farce, partly translated from German. He died at Worcester on 5 April 1801.<br>
<br>G. Le G. Norgate<span class="italic">, rev. </span> Robin Whittaker </div>
