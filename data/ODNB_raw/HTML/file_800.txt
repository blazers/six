<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Askew,  Egeon</span>  (1575/6&#150;1637), <span class="occ">Church of England clergyman</span>, was a native of Lancashire. His family originated from Mulcaster, Cumberland, and lived subsequently at Kirby Ireleth, Lancashire, where Thomas Askew, probably Egeon's younger brother, was instituted vicar in 1606. Having matriculated from Queen's College, Oxford, in July 1593, Egeon Askew graduated BA in April 1597, was chaplain of Queen's in 1598, and proceeded MA in June 1600. By about 1603 this &#145;great admirer of Dr John Reynolds and Richard Crackanthorpe&#146;  (Wood, <span class="italic">Ath. Oxon.</span>, 1.756) was minister of Greenwich, Kent, and had a reputation as a preacher. His only book, <span class="italic">Brotherly reconcilement; preached in Oxford for the union of some, and now published &hellip; for the unitie of all in this church and commonwealth</span>, published in 1605 and dedicated to James I, is dated from Greenwich. Supported by wide biblical, patristic, and classical references, the book beseeches fellow clergy to preach the necessity of good works as well as faith, castigates &#145;deceivers of minds, Puritans or Pruritans&#146; as guilty of &#145;sinful scisme&#146; and, while acknowledging Calvin's learning and approving his use of extra-scriptural learning in sermons, criticizes both his attitude to vestments and his adherents' worship of him.<br>
<br>In May 1609 Askew was instituted to the rectory of Great Hampden, Buckinghamshire, on the presentation of Robert Heynes and George Herst. Here he married Elizabeth Smith on 14 May 1610 and died after a long ministry; he was buried on 10 May 1637.<br>
<br>J. E. Bailey<span class="italic">, rev. </span> Vivienne Larminie </div>
