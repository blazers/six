<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Balnea,  Henry</span>  (<em>supp. </em><em>fl. </em>early 15th cent.), <span class="occ">supposed religious writer</span>, was stated by Thomas Tanner (1674&#150;1735) to be the author of the <span class="italic">Speculum spiritualium</span>, an anonymous early fifteenth-century devotional and mystical miscellany associated with the Bridgettines and Carthusians which survives in at least eleven manuscripts as well as in an early edition printed in Paris and published in London in 1510. Balnea's name appears to stem from a misreading of the contracted form of &#145;Henricus domus Cartusiensis de Bethleem&#146; (&#145;Henry of the Carthusian house of Bethlehem of Sheen&#146;), with whom the <span class="italic">Speculum spiritualium</span> is associated in an ambiguous entry in the early sixteenth-century catalogue of the brothers' library of the Bridgettine abbey of Syon. There were two monks named Henry at Sheen in the early fifteenth century. Both were Germans, but although Tanner supposed its compiler to have been English, there is in fact nothing in the <span class="italic">Speculum spiritualium</span> to make this essential; indeed, many of its sources are foreign, and such as might have appealed to, or been brought to England by, monks from Germany. In any event, it is possible that the author of the catalogue was identifying &#145;Henricus&#146; as the compiler not of the <span class="italic">Speculum spiritualium</span> but of the indexes to it.<br>
<br>W. N. M. Beckett </div>
