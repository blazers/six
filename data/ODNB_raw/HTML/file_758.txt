<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Ashley,  Charles Jane</span>  (1772&#150;1843), <span class="occ">cellist</span>, was born on 30 December 1772 in London, probably at 4 Pimlico Terrace, Chelsea, the third son of <script>writeSeealsoLink('../view/article/760/', "John Ashley (c.1734&#150;1805)")</script> and his wife Mary, <i>n&eacute;e</i> Jane (<i>d</i>. 1809). He played at Ranelagh and at Covent Garden, carrying on the Lenten oratorios with his brothers <script>writeSeealsoLink('../view/article/759/', "General Christopher Ashley")</script> [<i>see under </i><script>writeSeealsoLink('../view/article/760/', "Ashley, John")</script>] and <script>writeSeealsoLink('../view/article/761/', "John James Ashley")</script>, and at his father's festivals, where his playing was greatly admired. He was one of the founders of the Glee Club in 1793. On 2 May 1811 he was elected secretary of the Royal Society of Musicians, of which he had been a member since 4 May 1794, and he was one of the original members of the Philharmonic Society on its foundation in 1813. He was sole manager of the Covent Garden oratorios from 1816 to 1819, and also organized musical meetings in Edinburgh and the provinces. However, in 1820 he was imprisoned for debt and not discharged from the king's bench until August 1834. In the latter part of his life he was for some seasons manager of the Tivoli Gardens at Margate. He never married. He died in Margate on 28 August 1843, of paralysis.<br>
<br>W. B. Squire<span class="italic">, rev. </span> John Warrack </div>
