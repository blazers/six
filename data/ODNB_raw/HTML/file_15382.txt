<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Kennedy,  John</span>  (1698&#150;1782), <span class="occ">Church of England clergyman and chronologist</span>, on 10 November 1732 succeeded William Hawford as rector of All Saints', Bradley, near Ashbourne, Derbyshire, a position he retained until his death. He was the author of several controversial works on the chronology of the <span class="roman">Bible</span>, the first of which was <span class="italic">A New Method of Stating and Explaining the Scriptural Chronology</span>, published in 1751. In 1753 he drew attention to what he considered to be the errors and defects of the Revd John Jackson's chronological calculations in a work entitled <span class="italic">Examination of the Reverend Mr. Jackson's &#145;Chronological Antiquities&#146;</span>. Kennedy's best-known publication was <span class="italic">A Complete System of Astronomical Chronology, Unfolding the Scriptures</span> (1762), which contains a dedication to George III written by Samuel Johnson (Boswell refers to Kennedy as the Revd Dr Kennedy in his <span class="italic">Life of Johnson</span>). In this Kennedy attempted to date some of the most important events of the <span class="roman">Bible</span>. However, his calculations and conclusions were challenged by the astronomer James Ferguson and, perhaps more eloquently, by the Revd Thomas Bowen of Bristol in a series of letters in the <span class="italic">Christian Magazine</span>. Kennedy defended his findings in a publication of 1774 entitled <span class="italic">An Explanation and Proof of &#145;A Complete System of Astronomical Chronology&#146;</span>, which took the form of a series of letters to James Ferguson, but he was never able to convince a sceptical public of the reliability and accuracy of his dating.<br>
<br>Kennedy died at Bradley on 4 February 1782 and was buried in the churchyard there. The inscription on his gravestone credits him with the degree of MA but his name does not appear on the lists of graduates from the English or Scottish universities.<br>
<br>W. C. Sydney<span class="italic">, rev. </span> M. J. Mercer </div>
