<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Bowness,  William</span>  (1809&#150;1867), <span class="occ">portrait and genre painter</span>, was born at Kendal, Westmorland. He was self-taught, and after some practice in his native town, he went to London about 1829 and met with moderate success as a portrait and figure painter. He exhibited at the Royal Academy from 1836 until 1863, but more often at the British Institution and at the Society (later Royal Society) of British Artists in Suffolk Street. He also wrote a number of poems, in the Westmorland dialect, which were collected under the title <span class="italic">Rustic studies in the Westmoreland dialect, with other scraps from the sketch-book of an artist</span> (1868). He married the eldest daughter of the marine painter John H. Wilson; they had at least one child. He died a widower on 27 December 1867 at his home at 30 Fitzroy Street, Fitzroy Square, London.<br>
<br>Walter Hepworth<span class="italic">, rev. </span> Emily M. Weeks </div>
