<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Lodvill,  Philip</span>  (<em>d.</em> 1767), <span class="occ">religious writer</span>, was born in Oxfordshire into an established family. He was the author of the first authoritative account in English of the doctrines and practices of the Orthodox church, which was published as <span class="italic">The Orthodox Confession of the Catholic and Apostolic Eastern Church</span> in 1762. It was a translation of a confession written in the seventeenth century by Pyotr Mogila, metropolitan of Kiev, and approved by a synod of Orthodox bishops. Lodvill was a regular attendant at the Russian Orthodox church at 32 Welbeck Street in London and received the prayer oil from the hands of Father Andrey Samborsky, who became confessor to Catherine the Great of Russia. Lodvill states in his brief preface that he intended his translation to spread understanding of his faith and to encourage others to join the Orthodox church.<br>
<br>Lodvill was married, probably to a Greek woman, and one of his daughters married the British consul at Salonika, Peter Paradise; their son was <script>writeSeealsoLink('../view/article/21258/', "John Paradise (1743&#150;1795)")</script>. Lodvill died on 14 March 1767 and was buried on 22 March in Bow church, London.<br>
<br>Thomas Seccombe<span class="italic">, rev. </span> Emma Major </div>
