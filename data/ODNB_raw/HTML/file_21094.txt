<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Page,  John</span>  (<em>c.</em>1760&#150;1812), <span class="occ">singer and music editor</span>, was from 1790 until 1795 lay clerk of St George's Chapel, Windsor, and he also deputized in the Chapel Royal and St Paul's Cathedral, as a tenor. In 1801 he was appointed vicar-choral at the cathedral. He was a professional member of the Catch Club between 1792 and 1797. In 1800 Page completed the three volumes of <span class="italic">Harmonia sacra</span>, a collection of anthems by English composers of the sixteenth to eighteenth centuries, including previously unpublished works by contemporary composers such as his friend Jonathan Battishill, Samuel Wesley, and Thomas Attwood. This was intended as a supplement to the collections of <span class="italic">Cathedral Music</span> of William Boyce (1760&#150;73) and Samuel Arnold (1843). Among other collections of religious music made by Page were <span class="italic">The Anthems and Psalms as Performed in St Paul's Cathedral</span> (1795) and <span class="italic">Divine Harmony</span> (1798). In 1804 a collection of four volumes of madrigals, glees, and elegies, <span class="italic">Festive Harmony</span>, appeared. Page edited the music from Lord Nelson's burial service in 1806, and he composed a little himself, notably the Christmas hymn &#145;See the morning star appear&#146;. He died on 16 August 1812, at 19 Warwick Square, Newgate Street, London.<br>
<br>L. M. Middleton<span class="italic">, rev. </span> Anne Pimlott Baker </div>
