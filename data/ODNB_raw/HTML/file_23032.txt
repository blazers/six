<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Rainsford,  Marcus</span>  (<em>b. </em><em>c.</em>1750, <em>d.</em> in or after 1805), <span class="occ">author</span>, was the younger son of Edward Rainsford of Sallins, co. Kildare. He obtained a commission and served in the 105th regiment during the American War of Independence. In 1794 he served under the duke of York in the Netherlands, and afterwards helped to raise black troops in the West Indies. In 1799 he visited Santo Domingo, and met Toussaint l'Ouverture, the black rebel leader and later governor of Santo Domingo. He was subsequently arrested and condemned to death as a spy, but was reprieved and eventually set free. He published an account of this adventure, entitled <span class="italic">A Memoir of Transactions that Took Place in St Domingo in the Spring of 1799</span> (1802). The book received severe reviews but a second edition, entitled <span class="italic">St Domingo, or, An Historical, Political, and Military Sketch of the Black Republic</span>, was published in the same year. He retired from the army with the rank of captain about 1803. He also published <span class="italic">An Historical Account of the Black Empire of Hayti</span> (1805), and a poem, <span class="italic">The Revolution, or, Britain Delivered</span> (2nd edn, 1801). The date of Rainsford's death is uncertain. His sister Frances (<i>d</i>. 1809) married, first, in 1774, Major-General Wellbore Ellis Doyle (<i>d</i>, 1797), and, second, Count Joseph Grimaldi, brother of the prince of Monaco.<br>
<br>J. M. Rigg<span class="italic">, rev. </span> Elizabeth Baigent </div>
