<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Arnold,  Thomas</span>  (1679&#150;1737), <span class="occ">naval officer</span>, was born in Lowestoft, the second of five sons of Matthew Arnold and his wife (<i>n&eacute;e</i> Coe). After gaining his commission on 23 October 1708 he served on the <i>Captain</i>, <i>Ormond</i>, and <i>Dragon</i> under the patronage of Sir George Byng and was, in 1718, first lieutenant of the French-built <i>Superbe</i> (64 guns), in which he distinguished himself during the battle off Cape Passaro, Sicily, on 11 August 1718 by heading the boarders and carrying the Spanish flagship, the <i>Real Felipe</i>. He was severely wounded in the battle, and lost the use of one arm.<br>
<br>Arnold's gallantry was rewarded by his appointment as master and commander of the <i>Spy</i> sloop in the West Indies on 18 October 1726. On 17 September 1727 he was made a captain, and on 14 November appointed to the <i>Fox</i> frigate, and sent to the coast of Carolina, where he was for some time under the orders of Captain Anson (in whose squadron Arnold's son, also Thomas, was to circumnavigate the globe in 1740&#150;44).<br>
<br>On his return to England Arnold retired from active service and settled at Lowestoft; he died there on 31 August 1737, and was buried in Lowestoft church. According to his monument in the church, he was married and had children apart from his son Thomas.<br>
<br>J. K. Laughton<span class="italic">, rev. </span> Randolph Cock </div>
