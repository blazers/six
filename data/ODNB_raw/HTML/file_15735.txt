<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Knight,  Samuel</span>  (1759&#150;1827), <span class="occ">Church of England clergyman</span>, was born in Halifax on 9 March 1759, the son of Titus Knight (<i>d</i>. 1793), Independent minister at Halifax, and his second wife. Influenced by the countess of Huntingdon, Titus Knight became minister of a Methodist chapel in 1763, and regularly assisted George Whitefield at Tottenham Court Chapel and elsewhere.<br>
<br>Samuel Knight attended Hipperholme grammar school and entered Magdalene College, Cambridge, as a sizar in 1779. He graduated BA as seventh wrangler in 1783 and was elected fellow, proceeding MA in 1786. In April 1783 he was appointed curate of Wintringham, Lincolnshire, and took pupils. On 13 July 1789 he married Frances Lawrence at Roxby-cum-Risby, Lincolnshire. They had at least two sons and three daughters. In 1794 he was presented to the vicarage of Humberstone, Lincolnshire, but continued to reside at Wintringham, where he received pupils into his house; he was also curate of Roxby. In 1798 he was the first to obtain the perpetual curacy of Holy Trinity, Halifax, and he moved there with his pupils. In December 1817 he was appointed vicar of Halifax.<br>
<br>Knight was the author of the popular devotional work, <span class="italic">Forms of Prayer</span> (1791), which passed through sixteen editions in his lifetime. He died in Halifax on 9 January 1827.<br>
<br>The younger of Knight's two sons, <a name="cosubject_15723"></a><span class="headword">James Knight</span>  (<em>bap. </em>1793, <em>d.</em> 1863), <span class="occ">religious writer</span>, was baptized on 25 April 1793 at Wintringham. He matriculated from Lincoln College, Oxford, in 1811, and was a scholar there from 1812 to 1815, graduating BA in 1814 and proceeding MA in 1817. He was appointed perpetual curate of St Paul's Church, Sheffield, in 1824, and resigned the living in 1860. He published various commentaries on Christianity, and edited his father's <span class="italic">Sermons and Miscellaneous Works</span> (2 vols., 1828), which were published together with a memoir by his brother, the Revd William Knight. He died at Barton upon Humber, Lincolnshire, on 30 August 1863.<br>
<br>Gordon Goodwin<span class="italic">, rev. </span> Emma Major </div>
