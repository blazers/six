<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Donaldson,  James</span>  (<em>fl. </em>1794&#150;1796), <span class="occ">writer on agriculture</span>, practised as a land surveyor in Dundee. He was also agent for the earl of Panmure. His chief work was <span class="italic">Modern Agriculture, or, The Present State of Husbandry in Great Britain</span> (4 vols., 1795&#150;6). Donaldson was one of those employed by the board of agriculture to make surveys of the agriculture of each county, and he was responsible for <span class="italic">A General View of the Agriculture of the County of Banff</span> (1794) and similar reports on the agriculture of the Carse of Gowrie, and the counties of Elgin and Nairn, all published in 1794, and Kincardine (1795). His <span class="italic">General View of the Agriculture of the County of Northampton</span> (1794) includes an appendix comparing the systems of agriculture in Northampton and Perth.<br>
<br>Gordon Goodwin<span class="italic">, rev. </span> Anne Pimlott Baker </div>
