<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Lound,  Thomas</span>  (1802&#150;1861), <span class="occ">landscape painter</span>, worked in the family brewery in Norwich and spent his spare time painting landscapes in oils and watercolours. He is said to have had lessons from John Sell Cotman, whose influence is greater in his oil paintings, and his works show the influence of watercolours by John Crome, David Cox, and Joseph Stannard. He collected paintings by other Norfolk artists, including Henry Bright, and at his death he owned seventy-five paintings by John Thirtle. Many of his best pictures are of scenery near Cromer, on Norfolk's north coast; during summer holidays he also painted in Wales and Yorkshire.<br>
<br>Although not a member of the Norwich Society of Artists, Lound exhibited there regularly from 1820 to 1833 and later at the Norfolk and Norwich Fine Arts Association. Between 1845 and 1857 he showed eighteen watercolours at the Royal Academy, all of which were Norfolk scenes. He was one of the most prolific watercolourists of the Norwich school. <span class="italic">Framlingham Castle, Suffolk</span>, <span class="italic">View Looking up the Wensum at Norwich</span> (1832), and <span class="italic">View from Rusthall Common, Near Tunbridge Wells, Kent</span> (1845) are in the Victoria and Albert Museum, London, and <span class="italic">Yarmouth Beach</span>, <span class="italic">Richmond Castle</span>, and <span class="italic">Ely Cathedral</span> are in the British Museum in London. Two of his etchings are in the National Maritime Museum, Greenwich.<br>
<br>He was married, though nothing is known of his wife. They had two daughters. Thomas Lound died following a stroke at his home in King Street, Norwich, on 18 January 1861.<br>
<br>Anne Pimlott Baker </div>
