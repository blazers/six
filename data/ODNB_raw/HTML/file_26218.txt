<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Stafford,  William</span>  (1593&#150;1684), <span class="occ">pamphleteer</span>, was born in Antingham, Norfolk, the son of <script>writeSeealsoLink('../view/article/26217/', "William Stafford (1554&#150;1612)")</script> and his wife, Anne (<i>d</i>. 1612), daughter of Thomas Gryme of Antingham. He matriculated from Christ Church, Oxford, on 8 November 1611, graduated BA on 4 July 1614, and was created MA on 5 March 1618. On the death of his uncle Sir John Stafford in 1624 he succeeded to the estate of Marlwood Park in Thornbury, Gloucestershire. He married on 20 February 1672, at Thornbury, Ursula Moore, and through their son John was the grandfather of Richard Stafford (1663&#150;1703), also a pamphleteer.<br>
<br>Stafford was the author of <span class="italic">The Reason of the War, with the Progress and Accidents Thereof, Written by an English Subject</span> (1646). He writes as a moderate parliamentarian, eager for peace on the basis of a constitutional monarchy. In the preface he mentions that parts of his work had been published in the previous year &#145;in much imperfection and some haste&#146;. Wood was wrong in supposing that this treatise might be identical with a pamphlet entitled <span class="italic">An orderly and plaine narration of the beginnings and causes of this warre: also a conscientious resolution against the warre on the parliament side</span> (1644); the latter publication, written by a staunch royalist, bitterly attacks the action of parliament. Stafford lived to a great age, and died at Thornbury, where he was buried on 4 July 1684.<br>
<br>E. I. Carlyle<span class="italic">, rev. </span> Anita McConnell </div>
