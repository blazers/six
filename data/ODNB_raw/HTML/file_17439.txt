<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Macdonald,  John</span>  (1727&#150;1779), <span class="occ">vicar apostolic of the highland district</span>, was born at Ardnamurchan, Argyll, the son of Donald Macdonald of Ardnamurchan and Catherine Macdonald of Morar, and nephew on his mother's side to <script>writeSeealsoLink('../view/article/17433/', "Bishop Hugh Macdonald")</script>. After attending the highland seminary at Guidal he entered the Scots College, Rome, in 1743, was ordained there in 1752 and returned to Scotland in April 1753. He officiated as missionary on the island of South Uist until 1761. A man of &#145;learning, and exceeding good nature and temper&#146;  (Blairs letters, 3/146/2), he was nominated titular bishop of Tiberiopolis and coadjutor to his uncle in February 1761, and was consecrated at Preshome on 27 September of the same year. On Bishop Hugh Macdonald's death in 1773 he succeeded him as vicar apostolic of the highland district of Scotland. He died on 9 May 1779 at Knoydart, Inverness-shire, and was buried there.<br>
<br>Thompson Cooper<span class="italic">, rev. </span> Clotilde Prunier </div>
