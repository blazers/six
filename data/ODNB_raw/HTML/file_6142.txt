<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Cook,  John</span>  (1771&#150;1824), <span class="occ">Church of Scotland minister and Hebrew scholar</span>, eldest son of the Revd John Cook, professor of moral philosophy at the University of St Andrews, and his wife, Janet, daughter of the Revd John Hill, was born on 24 November 1771. He graduated at St Andrews in 1788. He was licensed for the ministry of the Church of Scotland on 19 September 1792, and was ordained minister of Kilmany, Fife, on 9 May 1793. He held this charge until 12 October 1802; his immediate successor was Thomas Chalmers. Cook left Kilmany to fill the chair of Hebrew and divinity at St Mary's College, St Andrews, a position which he held until his death. On 2 July 1803 he married Elizabeth (<i>d</i>. 1848), daughter of <script>writeSeealsoLink('../view/article/13274/', "George Hill (1750&#150;1819)")</script>, principal of St Mary's College. They had three daughters and five sons, among whom was the ecclesiastical historian <script>writeSeealsoLink('../view/article/6143/', "John Cook (1807&#150;1869)")</script>. On 16 May 1816 Cook became moderator of the general assembly. He published <span class="italic">Inquiry into the Authenticity of the Books of the New Testament</span> (1821). He died on 28 November 1824.<br>
<br>Alexander Gordon<span class="italic">, rev. </span> Rosemary Mitchell </div>
