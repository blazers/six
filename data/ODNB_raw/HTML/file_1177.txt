<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Bale,  Robert</span>  (<em>d.</em> 1503), <span class="occ">prior of Burnham Norton and historian</span>, was born 3 miles from Walsingham, Norfolk, and joined the Carmelites at their house at Burnham Norton in the same county. He was a great lover of the liturgy and history of his order, which is reflected in his three known works. He composed a short chronicle of the Carmelites  (Bodl. Oxf., MS Selden supra 72, fols. 5&#150;11<i>v</i>) and a liturgical office to the prophet Elijah  (CUL, MS Ff.6.28, fols. 34&#150;42<i>v</i>) which has been published by B. Zimmerman, <span class="italic">Ordinaire de l'ordre de Notre-Dame du Mont-Carmel</span> (1910), though the lessons of matins are omitted. An office of St Simon Stock, now lost, was probably a text used in the English province. John Bale (no relation) noted a few years later that, although not an eloquent preacher, he impressed with his learning and his ability to resolve legal problems. Bale adds that his works were &#145;unpolished and dull&#146; and certainly there is little in them to support the assertion that Robert Bale spent part of each year studying at Oxford and Cambridge. His title of lector was an award from the order itself. He was prior for some years in Burnham Norton and died there on 11 November 1503. He bequeathed his collection of books to his convent.<br>
<br>Richard Copsey </div>
