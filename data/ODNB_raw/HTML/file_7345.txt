<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Dawson,  Edward</span>  (<em>c.</em>1579&#150;1622), <span class="occ">Jesuit</span>, was the only son of &#145;respectable&#146; parents, &#145;connected with Sir Anthony Staunden&#146;, and was born in London. He completed his studies in Spain, and Louvain, in the Spanish Netherlands, and after being ordained priest was sent to the English mission. He was soon apprehended and imprisoned until 1606, when he was sentenced to perpetual exile with forty-seven other priests. He entered the Society of Jesus in Rome the same year. Having been sent back to England on the mission about 1610 he laboured for some time in London and in Lincolnshire. Recalled by his superiors to Ghent he obtained permission to devote himself to the spiritual care of the English and Irish soldiers who were suffering from the plague in the Low Countries. He soon caught the disease, and died at Brussels on 22 December 1622. He was the author of <span class="italic">The Practical Methode of Meditation</span>, printed in Vicenzo Bruno's <span class="italic">Meditations</span>, published at St Omer in 1614.<br>
<br>Thompson Cooper<span class="italic">, rev. </span> G. Bradley </div>
