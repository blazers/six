<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Nuttall,  William</span>  (<em>d.</em> 1840), <span class="occ">topographer</span>, son of John Nuttall, master fuller, was born at Rochdale, Lancashire. He kept a school in that town for many years and lived opposite St James's Church, Whitworth Road. He married three times, the last time unhappily. His first wife was Mary, daughter of William Dutton of Morton Wood, Shropshire. They had two daughters.<br>
<br>Nuttall wrote <span class="italic">Le voyageur, or, The Genuine History of Charles Manley</span> (1806) and <span class="italic">Rochdale, a Fragment, with Notes, Intended as an Introduction to the History of Rochdale</span>, which was published in 1810. It is in doggerel verse, and is curious as the first attempt at a history of the town. In his opening address to George Gordon, Lord Byron, baron of Rochdale, he claimed to have &#145;nothing in view but the Welfare of my Fellow Townsmen, and a desire to render myself useful to Mankind&#146;. The manuscript of his intended history of Rochdale was utilized by Edward Baines in his <span class="italic">History of Lancashire</span>, but Baines made no acknowledgement of this in his preface.<br>
<br>About 1828 Nuttall moved to Oldham but, overcome by poverty, he committed suicide in 1840. He was buried in Oldham churchyard.<br>
<br>C. W. Sutton<span class="italic">, rev. </span> Joanne Potier </div>
