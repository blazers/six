<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Mason,  William Shaw</span>  (1774&#150;1853), <span class="occ">statistician and bibliographer</span>, was born in Dublin, the son of Henry Mason, described as <i>publicanus</i>  (Burtchaell &amp; Sadleir, <span class="italic">Alum. Dubl.</span>). He was educated, first, under a Mr Carpendale, and then at Trinity College, Dublin, to which he was admitted in 1791, graduating BA in 1796. In conjunction with two others he was appointed by patent in 1805 to the office of remembrancer or receiver of the first-fruits and twentieth parts in Ireland; to this was added in September 1810 the post of secretary to the commissioners for public records in Ireland. Sir Robert Peel, while chief secretary to the lord lieutenant of Ireland, conceived a high opinion of Mason, and encouraged him to undertake an Irish statistical work similar to that executed by Sir John Sinclair for Scotland. The first volume of Mason's publication was issued at Dublin in octavo, with maps and plates, in 1814, as <span class="italic">A Statistical Account or Parochial Survey of Ireland, drawn up from the Communications of the Clergy</span>. The second volume appeared in 1816, and a third followed in 1819. Mason devoted much attention to the subject of the census of Ireland, and compiled a <span class="italic">Survey, Valuation, and Census of the Barony of Portnahinch</span> in Queen's county. This was printed in 1821 in a folio volume, and submitted to George IV during his visit to Ireland as a model for a statistical survey of the whole country. Mason's annotated catalogue of the books relating to Ireland that he had collected for Peel was published as <span class="italic">Bibliotheca Hibernicana</span> (1823). During an investigation into the integrity of the Irish records, their keeper alleged that Mason had refused him the keys on the grounds of being &#145;servant to the Record Commissioners, and not of the Lord Lieutenant&#146;  (&#145;Observations&#146;, 19), and said that Mason had been threatened with dismissal by the lord chancellor. Mason died in Camden Street, Dublin, on 11 March 1853.<br>
<br>J. T. Gilbert<span class="italic">, rev. </span> Alan Yoshioka </div>
