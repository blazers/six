<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Palin,  William</span>  (1803&#150;1882), <span class="occ">Church of England clergyman and author</span>, youngest son of Richard Palin and his wife, Sarah Durden, was born at Mortlake, Surrey, on 10 November 1803. He was educated at Mortlake School, Lewes, in Sussex. While a private tutor he published in June 1829, when living at Southampton, <span class="italic">The Persians of Aeschylus</span>. On 17 December 1829 he matriculated from St Alban's Hall, Oxford, but soon migrated to Trinity College, Cambridge, where he graduated BA in 1833 and MA in 1851. He was admitted <i>ad eundem</i> at Oxford on 21 June 1861. He married Emily Isabella Slaughter (1813&#150;1878), daughter of Stephen Long, a solicitor; they had three daughters and one son.<br>
<br>Palin was ordained deacon by the bishop of London on Trinity Sunday, 1833, and was curate-in-charge of Stifford in Essex for twelve months. He was ordained priest in May 1834. From July 1834 until his death he was rector of Stifford. Between 1861 and 1863 the parish church was restored through his efforts. With one of his daughters he compiled <span class="italic">Stifford and its Neighbourhood, Past and Present</span>, a description of twenty parishes in south Essex (privately printed, 1871), and <span class="italic">More about Stifford and its Neighbourhood</span> (1872); both volumes contained extracts from parish registers, and were full of information on local social life.<br>
<br>Palin also published many religious works, including <span class="italic">Village Lectures on the Litany</span> (1837), <span class="italic">Bellingham: a Narrative of a Christian in Search of the Church</span> (1839), several papers on the weekly offertory, and <span class="italic">The Christian Month: Original Hymns for each Day of the Month</span>. He wrote two hymns for Orby Shipley's <span class="italic">Lyra messianica</span> (1864), to which his daughter Emily Isabella Jane also contributed. From 1853 to 1857 he edited the <span class="italic">Churchman's Magazine</span>, and he contributed frequently to various church periodicals. In 1851 he published a <span class="italic">History of the Church of England, 1688&#150;1717</span>. He planned to write a continuation, but the work involved more research than his clerical duties allowed. Palin died at the rectory, Stifford, Essex, on 16 October 1882, and was buried in the churchyard there.<br>
<br>W. P. Courtney<span class="italic">, rev. </span> Mari G. Ellis </div>
