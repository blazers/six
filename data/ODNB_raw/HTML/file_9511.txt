<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Fisher,  William</span>  (1780&#150;1852), <span class="occ">naval officer</span>, second son of John Fisher of Yarmouth, Norfolk, was born on 18 November 1780, and entered the navy in August 1795. After serving in the North Sea, at the Cape of Good Hope, and in the Mediterranean, and as acting lieutenant of the <i>Foudroyant</i> on the coast of Egypt, he was confirmed in the rank on 3 September 1801. In 1805 he was lieutenant of the <i>Superb</i> during the chase of Villeneuve to the West Indies; and in 1806 he was promoted commander. In 1808 he commanded the <i>Racehorse</i> (18 guns) in the channel, and then, in 1809 and 1810, in the survey of the Mozambique Channel. At the end of the survey, in May 1810, he married Elizabeth, sister of Sir James Rivett Carnac, bt, governor of Bombay; they had a daughter and a son.<br>
<br>In March 1811 Fisher was promoted captain; during 1816 and 1817 he commanded in succession the <i>Bann</i> and the <i>Cherub</i> (each 20 guns) on the coast of Guinea, in both of which he captured several slavers and pirates, some after desperate resistance. While on the <i>Cherub</i> he proposed to the Admiralty an improved method of watering ships, which was officially adopted.<br>
<br>From March 1836 to May 1841 Fisher commanded the <i>Asia</i> (84 guns) in the Mediterranean, and in 1840, during the operations on the coast of Syria, was senior officer of the detached squadron off Alexandria, with the task of keeping open the mail communication through Egypt. For this he received the Turkish gold medal, sword, and diamond decoration. He had no further service afloat, but became, in due course, rear-admiral in 1847. During retirement he wrote two novels: <span class="italic">The Petrel, or, Love on the Ocean</span> (1850), and <span class="italic">Ralph Rutherford, a Nautical Romance</span> (1851), which combined improbable love stories with exaggerated, stilted language. He died at his residence, 38 Blandford Square, London, on 30 September 1852.<br>
<br>J. K. Laughton<span class="italic">, rev. </span> Roger Morriss </div>
