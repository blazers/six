<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Brackenbury,  Joseph</span>  (1787&#150;1864), <span class="occ">poet and Church of England clergyman</span>, was born on 27 September 1787 at Langton by Partney, Lincolnshire, the third son of the Revd William Brackenbury (1753/4&#150;1824) and his wife, Juliet (<i>d</i>. 1790), the daughter of Benet Langton, of Langton Hall, Lincolnshire. He matriculated at Bene't College (later Corpus Christi), Cambridge, in Michaelmas term 1806, and became a scholar there. In 1810 he published by subscription his <span class="italic">Natale solum, and other Poetical Pieces</span>, and on 11 December 1810 he married Mary (1788&#150;1862), daughter of Colonel Richard Brackenbury of Aswardby, Lincolnshire, and his wife, Janetta, <i>n&eacute;e</i> Gunn. They were to have four sons and three daughters. Brackenbury took his BA in 1811 and was ordained priest in the same year. He served as chaplain to the Madras establishment between 1818 and 1820. From 1828 to 1860 he was chaplain and secretary to the Magdalen Hospital, Blackfriars Road, Southwark, London. In 1862 he became rector of Quendon, Essex, and he died there, in the rectory, of apoplexy, on 31 March 1864, aged seventy-six.<br>
<br>Jennett Humphreys<span class="italic">, rev. </span> Megan A. Stephan </div>
