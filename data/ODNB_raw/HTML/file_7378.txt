<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Deacon,  James</span>  (<em>d.</em> 1750), <span class="occ">miniature painter</span>, of whose parents nothing is known, was talented as a painter and a musician. In 1746 the miniature and enamel painter C. F. Zincke was obliged, his sight failing, to give up his house in Tavistock Row, Covent Garden, and retire from his profession. Deacon then took this house and quite probably a proportion of the older painter's business. Horace Walpole noted that he &#145;painted portraits in miniature in a very masterly manner&#146;  (Walpole, 4.183). In the print room of the British Museum there are portraits by Deacon, in Indian ink, of the marine painter Samuel Scott and his wife. Deacon had not long been established in his profession when, attending as a witness at the Old Bailey, apparently at the &#145;black sessions&#146;, he caught gaol fever and died, young, on 21 May 1750.<br>
<br>Ernest Radford<span class="italic">, rev. </span> Emma Rutherford </div>
