<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Ray,  James</span>  (<em>fl. </em>1745&#150;1746), <span class="occ">chronicler</span>, was a native of Whitehaven in Cumberland. On the advance from Edinburgh of the Jacobite army under Charles Edward Stuart in the autumn of 1745, Ray marched with others from his town to join the royal garrison at Carlisle. The surrender of Carlisle to the Jacobites prior to his arrival led Ray then to follow the Jacobite advance to Derby as closely as he was able. All the information he obtained concerning them he reported to the duke of Cumberland, whose forces he met at Stafford on 5 January 1746. He continued with the duke's army until the final victory at Culloden in April. In the same year he published a 32-page pamphlet, <span class="italic">The Acts of the Rebels, Written by an Egyptian</span> (2nd edn, 1746). About the same date he published <span class="italic">A Complete History of the Rebellion in 1745</span>, which passed through nine editions by 1760. It remains a useful account of the campaign and of the state of feeling in England at this time.<br>
<br>Albert Nicholson<span class="italic">, rev. </span> Eirwen E. C. Nicholson </div>
