<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Ramadge,  Francis Hopkins</span>  (1793&#150;1867), <span class="occ">physician</span>, was born in Dublin, the eldest son of Thomas Ramadge of that city. He was educated at Trinity College, Dublin, where he graduated BA in 1816 and MB and MA in 1819. He was incorporated on his MB degree at Oxford as a member of Magdalen Hall on 4 May 1821, and proceeded MD on 27 June in the same year. Ramadge was admitted an inceptor candidate of the Royal College of Physicians on 26 June 1820, a candidate on 1 October 1821, and a fellow on 30 September 1822. He was censor in 1825. Ramadge established himself in London, where he became physician to the Central Infirmary and Dispensary, lecturer there on the principles and practice of medicine and chemistry, and senior physician to the infirmary for asthma, consumption, and other diseases of the lungs. On 16 September 1826 he married Elizabeth Batts Bell at St Clement Danes, Westminster.<br>
<br>Besides contributions to <span class="italic">The Lancet</span>, <span class="italic">Medical Times</span>, and <span class="italic">Notes and Queries</span>, Ramadge wrote <span class="italic">Consumption Curable</span> (1834), which later appeared in two further editions and was also published in New York and translated into German and French. He also wrote <span class="italic">On Asthma and Diseases of the Heart</span> (1847). He translated R. T. H. Laennec's <span class="italic">De l'auscultation m&eacute;diate</span>; <span class="italic">Treatise on Mediate Auscultation</span> (1846) appeared in an edition with Ramadge's own annotations and was seen through the press by Theophilus Herbert. Ramadge died at 12 Clarges Street, Piccadilly, London, on 8 June 1867, leaving a widow, Harriette, presumably his second wife.<br>
<br>Gordon Goodwin<span class="italic">, rev. </span> Kaye Bagshaw </div>
