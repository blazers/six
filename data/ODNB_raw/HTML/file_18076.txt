<span class="headword">Markwick <span class="st"> [Markwith]</span>,  Nathaniel</span>  (1664&#150;1735), <span class="occ">Church of England clergyman</span>, son of James Markwick of Croydon, was born in April 1664. He was admitted to Merchant Taylors' School in 1677, and matriculated as a commoner at St John's College, Oxford, on 14 July 1682, aged eighteen. He graduated BA in 1686, and proceeded MA in 1690, and BD (under the name of Markwith) on 1 February 1696. After being ordained a priest he was vicar of Westbury, Buckinghamshire, from 1692 to 1694, and of St Mary Magdalen's, Taunton, from 1696 until 1703. He had, in addition, become a prebendary of Bath and Wells on 4 October 1699. From 1703 until his death, on 20 March 1735, he was vicar of East Brent, Somerset.<br>
<br>Between 1728 and 1734 Markwick published a number of works calculating the date of the apocalyptic destruction of Jerusalem foretold in the <span class="roman">Old Testament</span>. The first of these, subsequently extended and supplemented, was <span class="italic">A Calculation of the LXX Weeks of Daniel, Chapter IX Verse 24</span> (1728) or <span class="italic">Stricturae Lucis</span>.<br>
<br>G. Le G. Norgate<span class="italic">, rev. </span> Robert Brown </div>
