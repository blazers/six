<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Lovell,  Robert</span>  (1630?&#150;1690), <span class="occ">naturalist</span>, was born at Lapworth, Warwickshire, the son of Benjamin Lovell (<i>b</i>. 1607/8), rector of Lapworth, and his first wife, Mary Godwin (<i>d</i>. 1658). <script>writeSeealsoLink('../view/article/17064/', "Sir Salathiel Lovell")</script> was his brother. In 1648 he became a student of Christ Church, Oxford &#145;by favour of the visitors appointed by Parliament&#146;  (Wood, 296), studying botany with the benefit of the recently established Physic Garden, zoology, and mineralogy. He graduated BA in 1650 and MA in 1653.<br>
<br>Lovell was apparently still in Oxford when his two books were published. The first, <span class="italic"><i>Pambotanologia</i></span> (1659), was a pharmacop&#339;ia rather than the &#145;compleat herball&#146; that its sub-title proclaimed. Dedicated to Charles II, it demonstrated Lovell's industry in ransacking libraries to list nearly two hundred and fifty authors, rather than any understanding of botany. There was nothing original in its content or arrangement, which followed the antique Galenic principles of the four elements, temperaments, and qualities. Remarkably, the work reached a second edition in 1665, Lovell having meanwhile issued a companion volume, <span class="italic"><i>Panz&#333;oryktologia</i></span>, claiming to be a complete history of animals and minerals.<br>
<br>&#145;Afterwards he retired to Coventry, professed physic, and had some practice therein, lived a conformist, and died [there] in the communion of the church&#146;  (Wood, 296). Lovell died in November 1690 and was buried in Holy Trinity Church, Coventry.<br>
<br>G. S. Boulger<span class="italic">, rev. </span> Anita McConnell </div>
