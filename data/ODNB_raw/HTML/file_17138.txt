<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Lucas,  Robert</span>  (1747/8&#150;1812), <span class="occ">Church of England clergyman and poet</span>, was born in Northampton and was educated at the free grammar school there. He was admitted to Trinity College, Cambridge, in December 1776 and graduated BD as a ten-year man in 1787. In 1793 he graduated DD. In 1772 he may have been acting as curate of Brixworth, and in 1778 he was curate of Hardingstone, Northamptonshire, but on 8 March 1782 he was instituted to the vicarage of Pattishall in the same county. Lucas married a daughter of Thomas Hurd, the younger brother of Bishop Richard Hurd. They had a son, Richard Hurd Lucas (<i>b</i>. 1789), and a daughter, Harriet Charlotte. The marriage probably took place before 1787, in which year, on 20 March, Lucas was collated by Bishop Hurd to the rectory of Ripple, Worcestershire, which he held with his vicarage.<br>
<br>Lucas produced two volumes of poetry: <span class="italic">Hymn to Ceres</span> (1781), a translation into English heroic verse of the Homeric hymn, and <span class="italic">Poems</span> (1810), which included a reprint of his translation. His relationship to Hurd prompted a further publication, <span class="italic">A Letter to the Reverend Doctor Parr</span> (1789), defending the reputation of his patron, and he may also have written the memoir of Hurd in the <span class="italic">Ecclesiastical and University Annual Register</span> for 1809. As a clergyman Lucas was an enthusiastic proponent of the Sunday school movement and published a number of sermons in its support. These were reprinted with other predominantly charity sermons in <span class="italic">Occasional Sermons</span> (2 vols., 1809). He died at Ripple on 1 March 1812.<br>
<br>Gordon Goodwin<span class="italic">, rev. </span> Sarah Brewer </div>
