<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Baker,  William</span> <span class="st"> [<em>name in religion </em>Anselm]</span>  (1833&#150;1885), <span class="occ">heraldic artist and mural painter</span>, was born in Birmingham on 23 January 1833, the son of Thomas Baker, a coach painter. He appears to have worked as a gun-stocker, and he learned to paint and draw at the Hardmans' studios in Birmingham. He became a Cistercian monk at Mount St Bernard's Abbey, near Coalville, Leicestershire, in 1857. The leading heraldic artist in England, nicknamed the Herald Monk, he drew two-thirds of the coats of arms in Joseph Foster's <span class="italic">Peerage of the British Empire</span> (1880), signing his work F. A. (Frater Anselmus).<br>
<br>Anselm also painted the murals in the chapel of St Scholastica's Priory, Atherstone, Warwickshire, in St Winifred's, Sheepshed, in the Temple in Garendon Park, and in the lady and infirmary chapels at Mount St Bernard's Abbey: all show the influence of Fra Angelico. He illustrated editions of the liturgical works <span class="italic">Hortus anim&aelig;</span> and <span class="italic">Hor&aelig; diurn&aelig;</span>, published in London, and several works published in Mechelen and Tournai. His <span class="italic">Liber vit&aelig;</span>, a record of the benefactors of St Bernard's Abbey, is magnificently illustrated with pictures of the arms and patron saints of the benefactors. Illustrations from &#145;The armorial bearings of English cardinals&#146; were published in 1988 (M. T. Elvins, <span class="italic">Cardinals and Heraldry</span>, illustrated by Dom Anselm Baker). His &#145;The arms of the Cistercian houses of England&#146; was never published. Anselm died at Mount St Bernard's Abbey on 11 February 1885 and was buried there two days later.<br>
<br>Thompson Cooper<span class="italic">, rev. </span> Anne Pimlott Baker </div>
