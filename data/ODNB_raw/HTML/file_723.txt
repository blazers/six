<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Arundell,  Mary</span> <span class="st"> [<em>married names </em>Mary Radcliffe, countess of Sussex; Mary Fitzalan, countess of Arundel]</span>  (<em>d.</em> 1557), <span class="occ">courtier</span>, was the only child of Sir John Arundell (<i>c</i>.1474&#150;1545), of Lanherne in Cornwall, and his second wife, Katherine Grenvile (<i>b</i>. 1489&times;93), the youngest of eight children of Sir Thomas Grenvile (<i>d</i>. 1513) of Stowe, Cornwall, and his first wife, Isabella Gilbert. Mary arrived at the court of Henry VIII in 1536 and subsequently served at least two of his queens, Jane Seymour and Anne of Cleves, and his daughter Princess, later Queen, Mary. She married twice, first on 14 January 1537, when she became the third wife of <script>writeSeealsoLink('../view/article/22991/', "Robert Radcliffe, first earl of Sussex (1482/3&#150;1542)")</script>. The couple had two sons, of whom the elder was baptized on 22 March 1538 and died in infancy; their second son, John (<i>bap</i>. 1539), was knighted in February 1547 and died on 9 November 1568. In 1545 Mary became the second wife of <script>writeSeealsoLink('../view/article/9530/', "Henry Fitzalan, earl of Arundel (1512&#150;1580)")</script>. For nearly three centuries some writers have confused her with two other women: as the author of manuscript translations actually done by her stepdaughter, <script>writeSeealsoLink('../view/article/47847/', "Mary Howard, duchess of Norfolk (1539/40&#150;1557)")</script>, daughter of the earl of Arundel and his first wife, Katherine Grey, daughter of the marquess of Dorset (<i>d</i>. 1530); also, with Margaret Acland, Lady Arundell (<i>d</i>. 1691), wife of John, second Baron Arundell of Trerice (<i>d</i>. 1697), of another branch of the great Arundell clan of Cornwall.<br>
<br>Mary died on 20/21 October 1557 at the earl of Arundel's house, Bath Place, London. Her funeral took place on 27/8 October at the church of St Clement Danes, where her body was buried. In 1847 a lead coffin, believed to contain the body of the countess, was discovered in a chapel at Arundel Castle. The coffin now lies beneath the floor of the main vault in the Fitzalan chapel at the castle.<br>
<br>Pamela Y. Stanton </div>
