<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Dixon,  Matthew</span>  (<em>d.</em> 1710), <span class="occ">portrait painter</span>, was a pupil of Sir Peter Lely. In her article &#145;Nicholas Dixon, limner, and Matthew Dixon, painter, died 1710&#146;, Mary Edmond noted that &#145;Vertue's &#147;Mr John Dixon&#148; was &hellip; non-existent&#146;, and that &#145;Walpole added to the confusion by combining details given by Vertue about Nicholas Dixon the limner with those about &#147;John&#148; Dixon, and attributing them to the latter&#146;  (Edmond, 611). Matthew Dixon &#145;drew in Crayons from the life &hellip; with great excellence. &hellip; There are several peices of Art of his performance in Crayons. the Dutches Cleveland, Portsmouth &hellip; after Lilly. &amp; his own picture excellently well done in a fine manner&#146;  (Vertue, <span class="italic">Note books</span>, 4.50). Vertue noted further that &#145;he left London &amp; the practice of painting &amp; retir'd to a Small estate in the Country. at Thwaite near Bungay in Suffolk&#146;  (ibid.). Mary Edmond established that Dixon bought the &#145;farm at Thwaite after 1666 and by Feb 1691/2 was one of the principal inhabitants of the village. One month later he had married Mary, daughter of John and Alice Gosling, members of a fairly prominent Norfolk family&#146;; Mary was described in the register as &#145;the last of the Goslings&#146; and was married on her eighteenth birthday. She was born on 22 March 1674 and baptized five days later. Matthew and Mary had six children: Catherine (<i>b</i>. 1692), Mary (<i>b</i>. 1694), John Gosling (<i>bap</i>. 28 Jan 1696), Alice (<i>bap</i>. March 1698), and Anne (<i>bap</i>. 1708); there was also a younger son, Matthew. Matthew Dixon was buried at Thwaite on 2 November 1710. His will records that he bequeathed his own picture to his wife; Sir Peter Lely's picture to his elder son, John; and to his daughters pictures including those of the duchesses of Portsmouth and Cleveland  (Edmond, 612). Whinney and Millar noted that Matthew Dixon was among the &#145;more shadowy figures in Lely's <i>atelier</i> &hellip; of whose wooden imitations of the later Lely a good example was at Ecton in the portrait of James Sotheby (1679)&#146;  (Edmond, 612).<br>
<br>Annette Peach </div>
