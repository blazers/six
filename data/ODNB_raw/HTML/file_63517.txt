<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Croker,  Margaret Sarah</span>  (<em>bap. </em>1773), <span class="occ">poet and novelist</span>, was baptized on 4 March 1773 at Holbeton in Devon, the daughter of Captain Richard Croker (<i>d</i>. 1816) and his wife, Mary. The high cost of a brother's education prompted Croker to contact the Royal Literary Fund just before her father's death in 1816, in the hope of earning a living by her pen.<br>
<br>Croker's published poems include <span class="italic">A Monody on the Lamented Death of Princess Charlotte Augusta</span> (1817), <span class="italic">A Tribute to the Memory of Sir Samuel Romilly</span> (1818), and <span class="italic">Monody on his Late Royal Highness the Duke of Kent</span> (1820), expressing her hopes for the future reign of his daughter Victoria. These verse tributes are &#145;well-written, feeling, but not remarkable&#146;  (Blain, Clements &amp; Grundy, <span class="italic">Feminist comp.</span>, 249). Croker also published a volume of verse, <span class="italic">Nugae canorae</span> (1818), by subscription.<br>
<br>Croker's only surviving novel, <span class="italic">The Question, who is Anna?</span> (3 vols., 1818), treats the question of illegitimacy with unusual frankness for its time. Its heroine, born to unmarried young parents, is portrayed sympathetically, rather than condemned. Croker may also have published two other novels, <span class="italic">Henry de Courtenay</span> and <span class="italic">The Widow of Wingfield</span>, and written a further unpublished novel in 1825  (see Blain, Clements &amp; Grundy, <span class="italic">Feminist comp.</span>, 249), but these cannot be traced. It is not known when she died.<br>
<br>Megan A. Stephan </div>
