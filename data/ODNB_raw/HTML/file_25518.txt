<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Sidley,  Samuel</span>  (1829&#150;1896), <span class="occ">portrait painter</span>, was born in York. He studied at the Manchester School of Art and the Royal Academy Schools, and first exhibited at the Royal Academy in 1855. By the end of his life he had exhibited thirty paintings at the Royal Academy and eleven at the Society (from 1887 Royal Society) of British Artists. He was best-known as a portrait painter and was commissioned to paint official and presentation portraits, including those of Professor Henry Fawcett, John Colenso, bishop of Natal (National Portrait Gallery, London), and the duke and duchess of Buckingham. His other paintings included <span class="italic">Alice in Wonderland</span> and <span class="italic">The Challenge</span>, which were engraved by C. A. Tomkins. He collaborated with the animal painter Richard Ansdell, and one of their joint paintings was of the children of Angus Holden, with a pony by Ansdell.<br>
<br>Sidley was elected a member of the Royal Society of British Artists in 1890, and he was an associate of the Royal Cambrian Academy. He died on 29 June 1896 at his home, 8 Victoria Road, Kensington, London, leaving a widow, Betty Ferns Sidley.<br>
<br>L. H. Cust<span class="italic">, rev. </span> Anne Pimlott Baker </div>
