<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Boswell,  Robert</span>  (1746&#150;1804), <span class="occ">Glasite preacher</span>, was a descendant of the Auchinleck family in Ayrshire. He received a classical education and became a writer to the signet in Edinburgh. While still young he became a Glasite, or Sandemanian, and was chosen by the church in Edinburgh to be one of their teaching elders. He published <span class="italic">The Book of Psalms, in Metre; from the Original</span> in 1784. In his preface he explains his intention of producing as literal a translation as possible, based on Benjamin Kennicott's edition of the Hebrew <span class="roman">Bible</span>.<br>
<br>Boswell was on a visit to friends in London when he died in a chapel while preaching a sermon on the text &#145;All flesh is as grass&#146; on Sunday 1 April 1804; this was probably the Sandemanian chapel, near the Barbican, where Michael Faraday later officiated as elder.<br>
<br>J. H. Thorpe<span class="italic">, rev. </span> Emma Major </div>
