<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Phillimore,  Greville</span>  (1821&#150;1884), <span class="occ">Church of England clergyman and author</span>, born in London on 5 February 1821, was the fifth son of <script>writeSeealsoLink('../view/article/22137/', "Joseph Phillimore (1775&#150;1855)")</script>, regius professor of civil law, and his wife, Elizabeth (<i>d</i>. 1859), daughter of the Revd Walter Bagot, and brother of <script>writeSeealsoLink('../view/article/22138/', "Sir Robert Joseph Phillimore")</script> and of <script>writeSeealsoLink('../view/article/22136/', "John George Phillimore")</script>. He was educated at Westminster School, Charterhouse, and Christ Church, Oxford, where he graduated BA in 1842 and MA in 1844. On taking holy orders, he was curate successively at Henley-on-Thames (where his family owned property) and at Shiplake. In 1851 he became vicar of Down Ampney, near Cricklade, and in 1867 he returned as rector to Henley, where he remained until, in July 1883, he accepted the crown living of Ewelme from Gladstone, his brother Robert's close friend. He married, on 16 April 1857, Emma Caroline, daughter of Captain Ambrose Lethbridge Goddard (1779&#150;1854) of The Lawn, Swindon, MP for Cricklade from 1837 to 1841, and his wife, Charlotte, daughter of Ayshford Sandford of Nynehead Court, Somerset. Unlike his brothers, he had little taste for the metropolitan world of affairs.<br>
<br>Phillimore was joint editor, with Hyde Wyndham Beadon and James Russell Woodford (later bishop of Ely), of the <span class="italic">Parish Hymn Book</span>, first issued in 1863, to which he contributed, besides translations, eleven original hymns, several of which have been reprinted in other collections. His <span class="italic">Parochial Sermons</span> was published in 1856 (2nd edn, 1885), and he was author of <span class="italic">Uncle Z</span>, a story of Triberg, in the Black Forest (1881), and <span class="italic">Only a Black Box, or, A Passage in the Life of a Curate</span> (1883). He died at the rectory, Ewelme, on 21 January 1884, his wife surviving him. A memorial volume, printed at Henley in 1884 and edited by his daughter Catherine, contains his hymns and a few sermons.<br>
<br>Thomas Seccombe<span class="italic">, rev. </span> H. C. G. Matthew </div>
