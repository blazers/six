<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Pringle,  Robert</span>  (<em>d.</em> 1736), <span class="occ">politician</span>, was the third son among nineteen children of Sir Robert Pringle, first baronet (<i>c</i>.1630&#150;1692), of Stichill, Roxburghshire, and his wife, Margaret, the daughter of Sir John Hope, Lord Craighall. After studying at the University of Leiden, which he entered on 19 November 1687, he served with William III in the invasion of 1688. He was called from his advocate's practice to become under-secretary of state for Scotland on 29 April 1695. He attended William on his later campaigns abroad as his man of business for Scotland, and kept office under Anne without becoming embroiled in the union controversy. He was promoted to under-secretary for the southern department in June 1710. Pringle married a Miss Law and had one son, Robert; the date of the marriage is unknown, but it ended with his wife's death before 1736. On 18 May 1718 he was appointed secretary at war on a salary of &pound;1000, plus &pound;500 rent for a London house at the king's desire following William's grant. He presented the annual army estimates to the Treasury in November, but was replaced on 24 December. Later he became registrar-general of shipping. Pringle resided at Hampstead, but kept up his Dutch links. He died at Rotterdam on 13 September 1736.<br>
<br>Timothy Venning </div>
