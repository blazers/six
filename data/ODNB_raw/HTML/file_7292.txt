<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Davis,  Nathan</span>  (1812&#150;1882), <span class="occ">traveller and excavator</span>, was born in 1812. Nothing is known of his education, save that he is referred to as Dr Davis. He was an American who for many years lived in an old Moorish palace, 10 miles from Tunis, where he extended his hospitality to various travellers. He published his experiences of northern Africa in: <span class="italic">Tunis, or, Selections from a Journal during a Residence in that Regency</span> (1841); <span class="italic">A voice from North Africa, or a narrative illustrative of the &hellip; manners of the inhabitants of that part of the world</span> (1844); and <span class="italic">Evenings in my tent, or, Wanderings in Balad, Ejjareed, illustrating the &hellip; conditions of various Arab tribes of the African Sahara</span> (2 vols., 1854). In 1852 he edited the <span class="italic">Hebrew Christian Magazine</span>, and afterwards became a nonconformist minister. From 1856 to 1858 he was engaged on behalf of the British Museum in excavations at Carthage and Utica and between 1857 and 1860 numerous cases of antiquities were sent by him to that museum where the chief antiquities discovered&#151;Roman mosaic pavements&#151;were preserved. His benefactions were particularly appreciated by the museum since he was an American. Davis described his explorations in <span class="italic">Carthage and her Remains</span> (1861) which, although rather unscholarly in presentation, was widely circulated. It was followed by <span class="italic">Ruined Cities within Numidian and Carthaginian Territories</span> (1862). He also published <span class="italic">Israel's True Emancipator</span> (two letters to Dr Adler, 1852), and, with Benjamin Davidson, <span class="italic">Arabic Reading Lessons</span> (n.d. [1854]). Shortly before his death Davis revisited Tunis, but the journey tried his strength, and he died at Florence on 6 January 1882 of congestion of the lungs.<br>
<br>W. W. Wroth<span class="italic">, rev. </span> Elizabeth Baigent </div>
