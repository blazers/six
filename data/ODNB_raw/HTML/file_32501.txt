<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Collingwood,  Cuthbert</span>  (1826&#150;1908), <span class="occ">naturalist</span>, was born on 25 December 1826, probably at Christchurch, Hampshire, the fifth of six sons of Samuel Collingwood, architect and contractor, of Wellington Grove, Greenwich, and his wife, Frances, daughter of Samuel Collingwood, printer to Oxford University. Educated at King's College School, London, he matriculated from Christ Church, Oxford, on 8 April 1845, graduating BA in 1849, MA in 1852, and MB in 1854. He subsequently studied at Edinburgh and Cambridge universities, and at Guy's Hospital, and spent some time in the medical schools of Paris and Vienna. From 1858 to 1866 he held the appointment of lecturer on botany to the Royal Infirmary medical school at Liverpool. Elected a fellow of the Linnean Society on 1 November 1853, he served on the council in 1868. He also lectured on biology at the Liverpool School of Science.<br>
<br>In 1866&#150;7 Collingwood served as surgeon and naturalist on board HMS <i>Rifleman</i> and HMS <i>Serpent</i> on voyages of exploration in the China seas, doing research in marine zoology. <span class="italic">Rambles of a Naturalist on the Shores and Waters of the China Seas</span> (1868)&#151;probably his best-known work&#151;was the result of this. On his return to Liverpool he became senior physician of the Northern Hospital and took a leading part in the intellectual life of the city.<br>
<br>Collingwood was through life a prominent member of the Swedenborgian or &#145;New&#146; Church. Besides some forty papers on natural history in scientific periodicals he published many expositions of his religious beliefs, of which the chief were: <span class="italic">A Vision of Creation</span> (1872), a poem with an introduction, critical and geological; <span class="italic">New Studies in Christian Theology</span> (published anonymously in 1883); and <span class="italic">The Bible and the Age, Principles of Consistent Interpretation</span> (1886).<br>
<br>Collingwood married Clara (<i>d</i>. 1871), daughter of Lieutenant-Colonel Sir Robert Mowbray of Cockavine; they had no children. In the later years of his life he lived in Paris, but returned to England in 1907. He died in Lewisham on 20 October 1908, aged eighty-two.<br>
<br>Robert Steele<span class="italic">, rev. </span> Giles Hudson </div>
