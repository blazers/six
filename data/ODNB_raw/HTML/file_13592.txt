<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Holmes,  George</span>  (<em>d.</em> 1721), <span class="occ">organist and composer</span>, may have been related to the cathedral musicians John Holmes (<i>d</i>. 1629) and Thomas Holmes (<i>d</i>. 1638). In 1698 he was organist to the bishop of Durham, and in 1704 succeeded Thomas Allinson as organist of Lincoln Cathedral. His surviving music includes two settings of the <span class="roman">Magnificat</span> for five voices, and two anthems, &#145;Arise, shine, o daughter of Zion&#146; (composed on the union of England with Scotland), and &#145;I will love thee, o Lord&#146;. These latter are in Tudway's collection in the British Library  (Harleian MS 7341). His setting of the burial sentences was in use in Lincoln Cathedral as late as 1937, and was republished by Novello, edited by G. J. Burnett. Holmes's <span class="italic">Ode for St Cecilia's Day</span> has been dated by its contents to between 1703 and 1713. He also published several songs: &#145;Tell me, little wanton boy&#146; and &#145;Celia's Invitation&#146; have been ascribed to him. The catches by a George Holmes in Hilton's <span class="italic">Catch as Catch can</span> (1652) are possibly by his father. Holmes died at Lincoln in 1721.<br>
<br>L. M. Middleton<span class="italic">, rev. </span> K. D. Reynolds </div>
