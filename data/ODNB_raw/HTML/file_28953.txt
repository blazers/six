<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Wedderburn, Sir  Alexander, of Blackness</span>  (1610&#150;1676), <span class="occ">politician</span>, was born on 22 March 1610, the eldest son of James Wedderburn (1589&#150;1627), town clerk of Dundee, and Margaret, daughter of James Goldman, a Dundee merchant. <script>writeSeealsoLink('../view/article/28961/', "Sir Peter Wedderburn")</script>, a future lord of session, was his younger brother. In 1628 Alexander Wedderburn married Matilda, daughter of James Fletcher, provost of Dundee; they had five sons and six daughters. Their second son, James (1649&#150;1696), was the grandfather of the Jacobite Sir John Wedderburn.<br>
<br>Wedderburn was educated for the law and was admitted advocate; but upon the death of his uncle Alexander of Kingennie, whose son was then a minor, he was appointed town clerk of Dundee in 1633, and held the office until 1675. He received a tack of the customs of Dundee from Charles I in 1639, and in 1640 was granted an annual pension of &pound;100 out of the customs, ratified in 1664. In September 1640 he was appointed one of the eight Scots commissioners to arrange the treaty of Ripon. He was knighted in 1642 and received a grant of the lands of Blackness in Forfarshire, which were later incorporated into a free barony.<br>
<br>Wedderburn represented Dundee in the Scottish parliament from 1644 to 1647 and from 1648 to 1651, and served in the protectorate parliaments of 1654 and 1656. He served on numerous committees of the estates, including the committees of war for 1645&#150;7 and 1651. At the Restoration in 1661 he was appointed one of the commissioners for regulating weights and measures. He died on 18 November 1676.<br>
<br>T. F. Henderson<span class="italic">, rev. </span> Sharon Adams </div>
