<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Lee,  Henry Augustus  Dillon-</span>, thirteenth Viscount Dillon  (1777&#150;1832), <span class="occ">writer</span>, eldest son of Charles Dillon-Lee, twelfth Viscount Dillon (<i>d</i>. 1813), KP, and his first wife, the Hon. Henrietta Maria Phipps (<i>d</i>. 1782), only daughter of Constantine, first Baron Mulgrave, was born in Brussels on 28 October 1777. He matriculated at Christ Church, Oxford, in 1795.<br>
<br>Trained as a soldier in the Surrey yeomanry and the Oxford militia, he became colonel in the Irish brigade on 1 October 1794, and was colonel of the duke of York's Irish regiment (101st foot) from August 1806 until its disbandment in 1817. In 1799 he was returned to parliament for the borough of Harwich. At the last general election of 1802 he was elected to one of the Mayo county seats and was re-elected in 1806, 1807, and 1812. He continued a member of the House of Commons until 9 November 1813, when he succeeded to his father's title. He spent most of his political career in opposition, and was an active proponent of Catholic relief.<br>
<br>Through his grandmother, Lady Charlotte Lee, daughter of the second of the extinct earls of Lichfield, Dillon inherited the estate of Ditchley Park, Oxfordshire, although latterly financial distress obliged him to live in Italy. In February 1807 he married Henrietta (1789&#150;1862), daughter of Dominick and Margaret Browne and sister of the first Lord Oranmore. They had six sons and five daughters, among them <script>writeSeealsoLink('../view/article/26271/', "Henrietta Maria Stanley (1807&#150;1895)")</script>, political hostess and campaigner for women's education. He died, after much suffering, on 24 July 1832, at Lower Brook Street, Grosvenor Square, London.<br>
<br>Dillon is best-known for two pamphlets advocating Catholic emancipation, published in 1801 and 1805. He also wrote on military and political affairs, and published two novels, one of which was set in the seventeenth century.<br>
<br>Robert Harrison<span class="italic">, rev. </span> Marie-Louise Legg </div>
