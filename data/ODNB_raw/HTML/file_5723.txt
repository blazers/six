<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Clulow,  William Benton</span>  (1802&#150;1882), <span class="occ">Congregational minister and author</span>, was a native of Leek, Staffordshire, and, after receiving a preliminary education in the grammar school there, entered Hoxton Academy. He became pastor of the Congregational church at Shaldon, Devon, where he remained for twelve years. In 1835 he became classical tutor at Airedale College, Bradford, but in 1843 he left in consequence of a disagreement with some influential supporters of the institution. He turned to writing, and published <span class="italic">Aphorisms and Reflections, a Miscellany of Thought and Opinion</span> (1843), <span class="italic">Essays of a Recluse, or, Traces of Thought, Literature and Fancy</span> (1865), and his most successful work, <span class="italic">Sunshine and Shadows, or, Sketches of Thought Philosophic and Religious</span> (1863), which ran to three editions over the next twenty years. After residing at Bradford for forty years he retired to Leek, where he died on 16 April 1882.<br>
<br>Thompson Cooper<span class="italic">, rev. </span> K. D. Reynolds </div>
