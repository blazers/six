<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Wastell,  Simon</span>  (<em>d.</em> 1632), <span class="occ">schoolmaster</span>, was from a family originating from Wasdale, Cumberland. He entered Queen's College, Oxford, about 1580, and graduated BA on 15 March 1585. Before 18 June 1592, when his son John was baptized at All Saints' Church, Northampton, he was appointed headmaster of the free school in that town. There he acquired considerable reputation as a teacher.<br>
<br>In 1623 Wastell published a work based on John Shaw's <span class="italic">Biblii summula</span> (1621), entitled <span class="italic">A True Christians Daily Delight</span> (1623), dedicated to Robert Spencer, first Baron Spencer of Wormleighton (<i>d</i>. 1627). It was a short summary in verse of the contents of the <span class="roman">Bible</span>, intended for children to commit to memory. To make the task easier the stanzas began with the successive letters of the alphabet. The first edition was reprinted in 1683 under the title <span class="italic">The Divine Art of Memory</span>, with a preface by T. B., and Wastell himself issued a second enlarged edition in 1629 entitled <span class="italic">Microbiblion, or, The Bibles Epitome in Verse</span>, dedicated to Spencer's son, Sir William Spencer. The summary of the <span class="roman">Old Testament</span> was entirely recast, but the summary of the <span class="roman">New Testament</span> was merely reprinted.<br>
<br>On 1 July 1626 Wastell's first wife, Elizabeth, with whom he had eleven children between 1592 and 1611, was buried, and he subsequently married another Elizabeth (<i>d</i>. 1639). In 1631 Wastell, or more probably his son, also Simon Wastell (<i>b</i>. 1602), was vicar of Daventry in Northamptonshire, but resigned the living before 22 September of that year. In his will, drawn up on 19 August 1631, Wastell left a house in Gold Street, Northampton, to trustees who included the physician and author Dr James Hart, to be sold for the benefit of his surviving children, Samuel (<i>b</i>. 1599), Simon, Hannah (<i>b</i>. 1609), and Mary (<i>b</i>. 1611). He died at Northampton, and was buried at All Saints' Church there on 31 January 1632. Probate was granted to his widow and son Simon on 27 February.<br>
<br>E. I. Carlyle<span class="italic">, rev. </span> S. E. Mealor </div>
