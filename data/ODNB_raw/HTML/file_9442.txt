<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Finch,  Robert Poole</span>  (1724&#150;1803), <span class="occ">Church of England clergyman</span>, son of the Revd Richard Finch, was born at Greenwich on 3 March 1724. He entered Merchant Taylors' School, London, in 1736, and in 1739 went to Peterhouse, Cambridge, graduating BA (1743), MA (1747), and DD (1772). He was ordained priest in 1748, and was chaplain of Guy's Hospital for thirty-seven years. He was a lecturer at St Bartholomew's Hospital from 1755 onwards. In 1771 he was appointed rector of St Michael Cornhill but resigned in 1784 on becoming rector of St John the Evangelist, Westminster. In 1781 he was made prebendary of Westminster, and remained so until his death.<br>
<br>Finch published numerous sermons; he was treasurer of the SPCK for over twenty years, and his <span class="italic">Considerations upon the Use and Abuse of Oaths</span> (1788) became a standard work among SPCK publications. He died at Westminster on 18 May 1803 and was buried in Westminster Abbey.<br>
<br>C. J. Robinson<span class="italic">, rev. </span> Emma Major </div>
