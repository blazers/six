<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Hershon,  Paul Isaac</span>  (1817&#150;1888), <span class="occ">Hebrew scholar</span>, was born of Jewish parents in Galicia but converted to Christianity at an early age. As a protestant missionary he became an active member of the London Society for Promoting Christianity amongst the Jews in England and the East. He worked successively as director of the House of Industry for Jews at Jerusalem and of the model farm at Jaffa.<br>
<br>In 1859 Hershon retired from the mission field in order to devote himself to work on the <span class="roman">Talmud</span> and <span class="roman">Midrashim</span>. He published a number of texts on the <span class="roman">Talmud</span>, including <span class="italic">Extracts from the Talmud</span> (1860); <span class="italic">The Pentateuch According to the Talmud &hellip; Genesis</span> (1878); and <span class="italic">A Talmudic miscellany &hellip; or, A thousand and one extracts [translated] from the Talmud, the Midrashim, and the Kabbalah</span> (1880), which was published as volume 19 in Tr&uuml;bner's <span class="roman">Oriental</span> series. He translated from the Judaeo-Polish (the language most widely spoken by Jews in Europe) Jacob ben Isaac of Janowa's rabbinical commentary on <span class="roman">Genesis</span> (1885). He also translated the <span class="roman">New Testament</span> into Judaeo-Polish, and compiled an unpublished digest of marginal references in Hebrew for the whole <span class="roman">Bible</span>, which became the property of the London Jews' Society. He died, comparatively suddenly, on 25 September 1888, at his home, 9 Park Avenue, Wood Green, Middlesex.<br>
<br>Gordon Goodwin<span class="italic">, rev. </span> Gerald Law </div>
