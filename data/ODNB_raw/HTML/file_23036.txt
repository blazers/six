<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Raithby,  John</span>  (1766&#150;1826), <span class="occ">lawyer</span>, was the eldest son of Edmund Raithby of Edenham, Lincolnshire. Originally a law stationer in Chancery Lane, at the start of the French Revolution he apparently published anonymous pamphlets advocating democracy. On 26 January 1795 he was admitted a member of Lincoln's Inn, and was subsequently called to the bar. He practised in the court of chancery. His legal writings obtained for him a commissionership of bankruptcy; he was also nominated a sub-commissioner on the public records. Raithby died at The Grove, Highgate, Middlesex, on 31 August 1826, leaving a widow; his only daughter died young.<br>
<br>Raithby published anonymously, in 1798, <span class="italic">The Study and Practice of the Law Considered</span>, which appeared in octavo form and was considered a work of great merit; for some time it was attributed to Sir James Mackintosh. An American edition appeared at Portland, Maine, in 1806, and the second English edition was issued at London in 1816 with the author's name. With Sir Thomas Edlyne Tomlins, Raithby issued a new edition in quarto form of the <span class="italic">Statutes at Large, from Magna Charta to the Union, 41 Geo. III</span> (10 vols., 1811). Tomlins co-operated in the edition down to 49 Geo. III, at which point he relinquished the task to Raithby and Nicholas Simons. Raithby compiled an index to the work, entitled <span class="italic">From Magna Charta to 49 Geo. III</span>, which appeared in 1814 in one volume in quarto form, and in three volumes in octavo form. He also compiled alphabetical and chronological indexes to the <span class="italic">Statutes of the Realm</span>, which were published by the record commissioners in 1824 and 1828, and he wrote <span class="italic">The Law and Principle of Money Considered</span> (1811). Apart from his work in the field of legal writing, Raithby also tried his hand at fiction and produced <span class="italic">Henry Bennet: a Novel</span>.<br>
<br>Gordon Goodwin<span class="italic">, rev. </span> Joanne Potier </div>
