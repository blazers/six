<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Fitzgerald,  Gerald fitz Maurice</span>  (<em>d.</em> 1204), <span class="occ">magnate</span>, was a son of <script>writeSeealsoLink('../view/article/9574/', "Maurice Fitzgerald (d. 1176)")</script>. He accompanied his father to Ireland and he and his brother Alexander were with him when the Norman garrison in Dublin was besieged in 1171 by Ruaidri &Oacute; Conchobair, king of Connacht and claimant to the high-kingship. John, son of Henry II, as lord of Ireland confirmed to fitz Maurice <i>c</i>.1185&#150;9 the half cantred of U&iacute; F&aacute;el&aacute;in which included U&iacute; M&aacute;el Rubae, Rathmore, Maynooth, Laraghbryan, Taghadoe, and Straffan (in Kildare), which had been granted to him by his brother William (<i>d</i>. <i>c</i>.1199); and also lands in U&iacute; Glaisin in the kingdom of Cork which had devolved to him as heir of his brother Alexander, who had been enfeoffed by Robert fitz Stephen. At some time between 1194 and 1204 Philip of Worcester made to fitz Maurice grants of land in what are now counties Limerick and Tipperary.<br>
<br>Gerald fitz Maurice married Eva (<i>d</i>. <i>c</i>.1225), daughter and heir of Robert of Bermingham, who had been granted U&iacute; Failge (Offaly) by Richard fitz Gilbert, earl of Pembroke and lord of Striguil (known as Strongbow), and succeeded to the lordship of Offaly in right of his wife. He died before 15 January 1204, when Meiler fitz Henry, justiciar, was ordered to give wardship of his heir, custody of his castles, and lands (including the castles of Lea and Geashill in U&iacute; Failge) to William (I) Marshal as lord of Leinster. He was to be succeeded by his son, <script>writeSeealsoLink('../view/article/9576/', "Maurice Fitzgerald (d. 1257)")</script>, who had come of age by 1215. His widow, Eva, married Geoffrey fitz Robert (<i>d</i>. 1211), lord of Kells, and Geoffrey de Marisco, justiciar. Gerald fitz Maurice Fitzgerald was ancestor of the earls of Kildare, later dukes of Leinster.<br>
<br>M. T. Flanagan </div>
