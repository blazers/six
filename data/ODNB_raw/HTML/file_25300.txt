<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Sheil, Sir  Justin</span>  (1803&#150;1871), <span class="occ">army officer in the East India Company and diplomatist</span>, son of Edward Sheil, merchant, and his wife, Catherine (<i>n&eacute;e</i> MacCarthy), of Spring House, co. Tipperary, and brother of <script>writeSeealsoLink('../view/article/25301/', "Richard Lalor Sheil")</script>, was born at Rathpatrick, co. Kilkenny, on 2 December 1803. Educated at Stonyhurst College, he was nominated to an East India cadetship. On arriving in India he was posted as ensign to the 3rd Bengal infantry (4 March 1820). Exchanged to the 35th Bengal infantry, of which he became adjutant, he was present at the siege of Bharatpur (1826). He became a captain on 13 April 1830, and on 4 July 1833 was appointed second in command of the disciplined troops in Persia under Colonel Pasmore, who had specially recommended him to Lord W. Bentinck for this service. Pasmore wrote that he was sensible, well-informed, and conciliatory. On 16 February 1836 he was appointed secretary to the British legation in Persia, and in 1844 he succeeded Sir John McNeill as envoy and minister at the shah's court, and continued such until he retired in 1854. He married in 1847, Mary Leonora (<i>d</i>. 1869), only daughter of the Rt Hon. Stephen Woulfe, chief baron of the Irish exchequer. He contributed to his wife's book, <span class="italic">Glimpses of Life and Manners in Persia</span> (1856), and two articles in the journal of the Royal Geographical Society. He had been promoted to the rank of major on 17 February 1841, and became a major-general in 1859. In 1848 he was created a CB, and in 1855 a KCB. He died at 13 Eaton Place, London, on 17 April 1871.<br>
<br>Stephen Wheeler<span class="italic">, rev. </span> James Lunt </div>
