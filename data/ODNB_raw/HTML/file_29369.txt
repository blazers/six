<span class="headword">Wigheard <span class="st"> [Wighard]</span></span>  (<em>d.</em> 664<span class="mult">x</span>7), <span class="occ">archbishop-elect of Canterbury</span>, was a Kentish priest and cleric under Archbishop Deusdedit. Following the death of Deusdedit in July 664, he was nominated as archbishop by Ecgberht, king of Kent, and sent to Rome, where he died of the plague before he could be consecrated. The existence of a letter from Pope Vitalian to Oswiu, king of Northumbria, apparently answering a request to appoint an archbishop, led Bede to infer that Wigheard had been sent jointly by both kings. This is unlikely. The pope did, however, probably consult some of Wigheard's companions on the question of filling the vacant see of Canterbury, to which he eventually appointed Theodore in March 668.<br>
<br>Mary Bateson<span class="italic">, rev. </span> Marios Costambeys </div>
