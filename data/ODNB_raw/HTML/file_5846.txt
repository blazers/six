<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Cole,  Charles Nalson</span>  (1722/3&#150;1804), <span class="occ">lawyer</span>, was the son of Charles Cole, rector of North Crowley, Buckinghamshire, and grandson of an apothecary of Ely. He was educated at St John's College, Cambridge, which he left after taking his BA in 1743, and became a student of the Inner Temple, where he was called to the bar.<br>
<br>Although not a practising barrister, Cole became registrar of the Bedford Level corporation and published <span class="italic">A collection of laws which form the constitution of the Bedford Level corporation, with an introductory history thereof</span> (1761). He also prepared the second edition of Sir William Dugdale's <span class="italic">History of imbanking and drayning of divers fenns and marshes, both in foreign parts and in this kingdom</span> (1772). Cole was a long-standing friend of the writer Soame Jenyns and became his literary executor after his death. Three years later Cole's edition of the <span class="italic">Works of Soame Jenyns</span> appeared, with a sketch of his life. He died in Edward Street, Cavendish Square, London, on 18 December 1804, aged eighty-one.<br>
<br>Thompson Cooper<span class="italic">, rev. </span> Robert Brown </div>
