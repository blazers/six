<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Evans,  John</span>  (<em>d.</em> 1779), <span class="occ">Church of England clergyman and biblical commentator</span>, was born at Meini Gwynion, Llanbadarn Odwyn (Llangeitho), Cardiganshire, and is said to have been educated at the University of Oxford. His first curacy was that of Llannarth, from where he moved to Portsmouth, also as curate. He is credited with being the John Evans who translated a work on the sacraments by Jabez Earle in 1735. In 1765 Evans published his <span class="italic">Cyssondeb y pedair efengyl</span> (&#145;Harmony of the four gospels&#146;). This was the first commentary in Welsh on any portion of the <span class="roman">Bible</span>, being five years earlier than the commentary of Peter Williams; a second edition was published in 1804. Evans is supposed to have seen through the press the Welsh <span class="roman">Bible</span> of 1769, which ran to 20,000 copies, and he also translated into Welsh Bishop Francis Gastrell's <span class="italic">The Christian Institutes</span> in 1773. He died at Portsmouth in 1779.<br>
<br>R. M. J. Jones<span class="italic">, rev. </span> Dylan Foster Evans </div>
