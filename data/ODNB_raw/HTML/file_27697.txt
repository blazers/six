<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Trench,  Francis Chenevix</span>  (1805&#150;1886), <span class="occ">Church of England clergyman and author</span>, was the eldest son of Richard Trench (1774&#150;1860), barrister, and his wife, <script>writeSeealsoLink('../view/article/27700/', "Melesina Trench")</script>. <script>writeSeealsoLink('../view/article/27702/', "Richard Chenevix Trench")</script> was his younger brother.<br>
<br>Trench entered Harrow School early in 1818, and matriculated from Oriel College, Oxford, on 12 November 1824, graduating BA in 1834 and MA in 1859. On 4 June 1829 he entered Lincoln's Inn to study law, but in 1834 he was ordained deacon and became curate of St Giles, Reading. The next year he was ordained priest, and on 13 September 1837 he was appointed perpetual curate of St John's, Reading. He married, on 6 December 1837, Mary Caroline, daughter of <script>writeSeealsoLink('../view/article/18116/', "William Marsh")</script>, the evangelical. They had one son, Richard William Francis (1849&#150;1860), and two daughters, Mary Melesina and Maria Marcia Fanny. From 1857 until 1875, when he retired to London, he was rector of Islip, Oxfordshire. He added the name &#145;Chenevix&#146; in 1873. Mary Caroline Chenevix Trench died on 4 March 1886 and her husband the next month, on 3 April, in Bursledon, Hampshire.<br>
<br>Trench was a scholarly parson, publishing many sermons, theological works, and travel books, such as <span class="italic">Diary of Travels in France and Spain</span> (1845), <span class="italic">Scotland: its Faith and Features</span> (1846), <span class="italic">A Walk around Mont Blanc</span> (1847), and <span class="italic">A Ride in Sicily, by Oxoniensis</span> (1851). He contributed to <span class="italic">Macmillan's Magazine</span> and <span class="italic">Notes and Queries</span>, published a series of papers as <span class="italic">Islipiana</span> (1869&#150;70), and wrote <span class="italic">A Few Notes from Past Life</span> (1862), which includes interesting autobiographical <i>memorabilia</i>.<br>
<br>E. I. Carlyle<span class="italic">, rev. </span> H. C. G. Matthew </div>
