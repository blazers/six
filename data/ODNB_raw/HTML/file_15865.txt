<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Lacy,  Walter</span> <span class="st"> [<em>real name </em>Walter Williams]</span>  (1809&#150;1898), <span class="occ">actor</span>, the son of a coach builder named Williams in Bristol, was educated for the medical profession and went as a young man to Australia. He was first seen on the stage in Edinburgh, in 1829, as Montalban in <span class="italic">The Honeymoon</span>, played there again in 1832, and acted also in Glasgow, Liverpool, and Manchester.<br>
<br>Lacy's London d&eacute;but was at the Haymarket, on 21 August 1838, as Charles Surface. On 22 June 1839 he married Harriett Deborah Taylor (1807&#150;1874), an actress [<i>see </i><script>writeSeealsoLink('../view/article/15850/', "Lacy, Harriett Deborah")</script>]. At Covent Garden he appeared, about 1841, as Captain Absolute opposite Madame Vestris and at Drury Lane as Wildrake in Sheridan Knowles's <span class="italic">The Love Chase</span>. He then joined Charles Kean at the Princess's for seven years. On 18 September 1852 he created the part of Rouble in Boucicault's <span class="italic">The Prima Donna</span>, and he made a great success as Ch&acirc;teau Renaud in <span class="italic">The Corsican Brothers</span>. Opposite Kean, he played John of Gaunt in <span class="italic">Richard II</span>, Edmund in <span class="italic">King Lear</span>, Gratiano, and Lord Trinket in Colman's <span class="italic">The Jealous Wife</span>. On 30 June 1860, at the Lyceum, he performed the Marquis of Saint Evr&eacute;mont in <span class="italic">A Tale of Two Cities</span>, and at Drury Lane, on 17 October 1864, was Cloten to Helen Faucit's Imogen. He appeared as Flutter in <span class="italic">The Belle's Stratagem</span> on 8 October 1866 at the St James's, where he was, on 5 November, the first John Leigh in Boucicault's <span class="italic">Hunted Down, or, Two Lives of John Leigh</span>. He played Mercutio in two Lyceum revivals of <span class="italic">Romeo and Juliet</span>. On 12 August 1868 he was, at the Princess's, the original Bellingham in Boucicault's <span class="italic">After Dark</span>.<br>
<br>Other parts in which Lacy was seen were Benedick, Comus, Faulconbridge, Malvolio, Touchstone, Prospero, Roderigo, Henry VIII, Young Marlow, Sir Brilliant Fashion, Goldfinch, Tony Lumpkin, Bob Acres, Dazzle, Flutter, Dudley Smooth, Megrim in <span class="italic">Blue Devils</span>, the Ghost in <span class="italic">Hamlet</span>, My Lord Duke in James Townley's farce <span class="italic">High Life below Stairs</span>, Jeremy Diddler, and Puff. After a long absence from the stage, occupied as professor of elocution at the Royal Academy of Music, Lacy reappeared at the Lyceum in April 1879 as Colonel Damas in Sir Henry Irving's revival of <span class="italic">The Lady of Lyons</span>. He died on 13 December 1898 at his home, 13 Marine Square, Brighton, and was buried at Brompton cemetery on the 17th.<br>
<br>Lacy was a respectable light comedian, but failed as an exponent of old men and was a wretched Sir Anthony Absolute. He was a familiar figure at the Garrick Club, and was almost to the last a man of much vivacity, and of quaint, clever, unbridled, and characteristic speech.<br>
<br>Joseph Knight<span class="italic">, rev. </span> Katharine Cockin </div>
