<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Yonge,  James</span>  (1792&#150;1870), <span class="occ">physician</span>, born in Devon, was the fourth son of Duke Yonge (1751/2&#150;1823), vicar of Otterton, Devon, and his wife, Catherina, daughter of Thomas Crawley-Boevey of Flaxley Abbey, Gloucestershire. He was a direct descendant of James Yonge (1647&#150;1721), a naval surgeon and medical writer. The novelist <script>writeSeealsoLink('../view/article/37065/', "Charlotte Mary Yonge (1823&#150;1901)")</script> was his niece. Yonge was briefly at Eton College in 1811, the year he matriculated from Exeter College, Oxford, whence he graduated BA on 13 May 1815, MA on 22 October 1817, BM on 8 June 1819, and DM on 20 June 1821. After leaving Oxford he studied at Edinburgh University before moving to London, where he assisted John Abernethy. He was elected a fellow of the Royal College of Physicians on 30 September 1822. Yonge moved to Plymouth in 1832 and became physician to the Plymouth Dispensary. In 1840 he went on to become physician to the Devon and East Cornwall Hospital, and he was for many years one of the most prominent physicians in the west of England. He married, in 1820, his cousin Margaret, daughter of Sir Thomas Crawley-Boevey, baronet. Yonge died at his home, The Crescent, Plymouth, on 3 January 1870.<br>
<br>Norman Moore<span class="italic">, rev. </span> Michael Bevan </div>
