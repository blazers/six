<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Welsh,  David</span>  (1793&#150;1845), <span class="occ">Free Church of Scotland minister and author</span>, youngest son of David Welsh, sheep farmer, of Earlshaugh and Tweedshaws, was born at Braefoot, Moffat, on 11 December 1793. He was educated at Moffat parish school, the high school of Edinburgh, and Edinburgh University, and on 7 May 1816 was licensed to preach by the presbytery of Lochmaben. On 22 March 1821 he was ordained minister of the parish of Crossmichael in Kirkcudbrightshire, and on 6 September 1827 he was translated to St David's Church, Glasgow. He married, probably in 1830, Isabel, sister of William Hamilton, provost of Glasgow. In 1825 Welsh published a biography of Thomas Brown MD, following it with an edition of Brown's philosophical lectures in 1834. In October 1831 he was appointed professor of ecclesiastical history in the University of Edinburgh, and, on leaving Glasgow, received from the university the degree of DD. He was a painstaking lecturer. In 1834 he visited Germany and in 1844 published <span class="italic">Elements of Church History</span>. He wrote articles on Jesus and on the Jews for the <span class="italic">Encyclopaedia Britannica</span> (1841).<br>
<br>At the meeting of the general assembly of 1842 Welsh was chosen moderator of the assembly, which adopted &#145;the claim of right&#146;, and he was one of the leaders of those who, on 18 May 1843, formed themselves into &#145;the general assembly of the Free Protesting Church of Scotland&#146;, with Dr Thomas Chalmers as its first moderator. It was Welsh who laid their protest on the table of the assembly. <span class="italic">The Limits and Extent of the Right of Private Judgement</span> (1843) records his opinions. He had to resign his chair, and his appointment as secretary to the Bible board, made in 1839, was cancelled. In two months he collected &pound;21,000 for building New College at Edinburgh for the education of Free Churchmen. In 1844 he was appointed librarian of the college and professor of church history; he left his books to the library. Also in 1844 he was first editor of the <span class="italic">North British Review</span>, the Free Church's journal. He died suddenly at Camis Eskin on the Clyde on 24 April 1845, survived by his wife and four children.<br>
<br>George Stronach<span class="italic">, rev. </span> H. C. G. Matthew </div>
