<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Low,  James</span>  (1791&#150;1852), <span class="occ">army officer in the East India Company and Siamese scholar</span>, was born at Kettle, Fifeshire, on 4 April 1791, the son of Alexander Low and his wife, Ann, <i>n&eacute;e</i> Thomson. He received a Madras army cadetship in 1811, and on 11 June 1812 was appointed ensign 25th Madras native infantry. He became lieutenant in 1817, and captain 46th native infantry in 1826. He retired as lieutenant-colonel 16th native infantry in 1845. He was for many years in civil charge of Wellesley Province, Penang, in the Straits Settlements, Malaya. He published <span class="italic">A Dissertation on the Soil and Agriculture of &hellip; Penang</span> (1828), <span class="italic">A Grammar of the T,Hai, or Siamese Language</span> (1828), <span class="italic">On Buddha and the Phrabat</span> (1831), and treatises on the literature and laws of Siam. Low, who was unmarried, died on 2 May 1852, at 10 Minto Street, Newington, Edinburgh. Copies of his Siamese drawings were deposited in the British Museum.<br>
<br>H. M. Chichester<span class="italic">, rev. </span> Roger T. Stearn </div>
