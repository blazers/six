<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Pilkington, Sir  Andrew</span>  (<em>c.</em>1767&#150;1853), <span class="occ">army officer</span>, obtained his first commission in the army on 7 March 1783, and was promoted lieutenant on 24 January 1791, captain on 2 March 1795, major on 31 March 1804, lieutenant-colonel on 5 October 1809, colonel on 12 August 1819, major-general on 22 July 1830, and lieutenant-general on 23 November 1841. Pilkington saw much and varied service. With the Channel Fleet in 1793&#150;4 he commanded a company of the Queen's Royals on board the <i>Royal George</i> at the battle of 1 June 1794 in the north Atlantic, when Lord Howe defeated the French off Ushant. Pilkington received two splinter wounds. He was next employed in the West Indies, and was present at the capture of Trinidad in 1795&#150;7. He served in Ireland during the suppression of the 1798 uprising, and was with the expeditions to The Helder in 1799 and 1805. In 1800 he was severely wounded in defending the <i>Kent</i>, an East Indiaman, against a French privateer while <i>en route</i> for India. He served on the staff at the Horse Guards in 1807&#150;8. On 9 May 1808 he married Maria Elizabeth, daughter of <script>writeSeealsoLink('../view/article/10608/', "Sir Vicary Gibbs")</script>, at Hayes; they had two daughters.<br>
<br>Pilkington served in Nova Scotia from 1809 to 1815. During the Anglo-American War, he subdued the islands in Passamaquody Bay, between New Brunswick and Maine. He was created KCB on 19 July 1838. Pilkington died on 23 February 1853 at his home, Catsfield Place, Battle, Sussex, a property he had purchased from James Eversfield. His wife and daughters survived him. One of his daughters, Maria Georgina, married Burrell Hayley, rector of Catsfield in Sussex, on 18 July 1848; the other, Louisa Elizabeth, married Richard Thomas Lee on 1 September 1853.<br>
<br>William Broadfoot<span class="italic">, rev. </span> David Gates </div>
