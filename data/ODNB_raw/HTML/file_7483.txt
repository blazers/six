<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Denholm,  James</span>  (1772&#150;1818), <span class="occ">topographer and painter</span>, was born in Glasgow and educated at Glasgow Academy. He was taught drawing and geography and became a painter of miniatures and landscapes. He made his publishing d&eacute;but in 1797, with <span class="italic">An Historical and Topographical Description of the City of Glasgow</span>. An enlarged edition, including &#145;a sketch of a tour to Loch Lomond and the falls of the Clyde&#146;, appeared in 1798 and a third edition followed in 1804, with &#145;a sketch of a tour of the principal Scotch and English lakes&#146; appended. The <span class="italic">Sketch</span> was also published separately in the same year.<br>
<br>In 1803 Denholm became a member of the Philosophical Society of Glasgow, founded in 1802; he was president from 1811 to 1814. He was also a member of the Philotechnical Society of Glasgow and the Manchester Literary and Philosophical Society. Denholm was married with children; he died in Glasgow on 20 April 1818. Although now fallen into obscurity, his artistic work was valued by his contemporaries. Compilers of nineteenth-century local histories and guidebooks paid him the compliment of plundering his <span class="italic">Description</span>; in <span class="italic">Glasgow, Past and Present</span> (2nd edn, 1884) he was described as &#145;one of the most interesting and able of our local chroniclers&#146;  (Pagan and Robertson, 1.62).<br>
<br>Joanne Potier </div>
