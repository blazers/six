<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Tisdale,  John</span>  (<em>b. </em>1530/31, <em>d.</em> in or after 1563), <span class="occ">printer</span>, freed in 1555, aged twenty-four, began to print in partnership with John Charlewood in 1557 or 1558 at the Saracen's Head, near Holborn conduit. His next book, in 1558 or 1559, he apparently printed alone at the sign of the Mitre in Smithfield. In 1560&#150;61 he was in Knightrider Street, south of St Paul's Cathedral, and in 1561&#150;3 at the sign of the Eagle's Foot in All Hallows churchyard, Lombard Street. He was an original member of the Stationers' Company, and is mentioned in its first charter, on 4 May 1557. The first entry to him in the register is in 1558 for a licence &#145;to prynte an A B C in laten for Rycharde Jugge, John Judson, and Anthony Smythe&#146;, which is the &#145;first instance recorded in the &#147;Register&#148; of one printer printing for another&#146;  (Arber, <span class="italic">Regs. Stationers</span>, 1.95). He is also known to have printed for Rafe Newbery and Francis Coldocke.<br>
<br>Tisdale married, on 5 November 1559, Katherine Master. He began to take apprentices in the same year, on 25 December. One of his devices was an angel driving Adam and Eve out of paradise; another was Abraham's sacrifice. He printed several of Bishop Bale's treatises. His last production is dated 1563, and the latest entry referring to him is one for taking an apprentice on 25 June of the same year  (Arber, <span class="italic">Regs. Stationers</span>, 1.227).<br>
<br>H. R. Tedder<span class="italic">, rev. </span> Anita McConnell </div>
