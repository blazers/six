<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Prior,  Thomas</span>  (1681&#150;1751), <span class="occ">author and a founder of the Dublin Society</span>, was born at Garriston, near Rathdowney, Queen's county, the second son of Colonel Thomas Prior (<i>d</i>. 1700) and grandson of Captain Thomas Prior of Ely, who settled in Ireland with his regiment in 1636. He was educated at the public school at Kilkenny between January 1697 and April 1699; there he formed what became a lifelong friendship with George Berkeley, subsequently bishop of Cloyne, to whom he later served as legal adviser and Dublin agent. Prior entered Trinity College, Dublin, obtained a scholarship in 1701, and graduated BA in 1703. He subsequently devoted himself to the promotion of trade and industry among the protestant population in Ireland.<br>
<br>In 1729 Prior published his <span class="italic">List of the Absentees of Ireland</span>, which contained details of estates and incomes from rents. In this popular and often reprinted pamphlet he estimated that up to &pound;600,000 went overseas in remitted rents, a figure which has since been identified as an over-estimate  (Cullen, 172). In 1730 there appeared his <span class="italic">Observations on Coin</span>. In the following year Prior and twelve other associates including Samuel Madden, philanthropist, Sir Thomas Molyneux, physician, Francis Bindon, architect and portrait painter, and Patrick Delany, dean of Down, succeeded in establishing the Dublin Society for the Promotion of Agriculture, Manufactures, Arts and Sciences. It was duly incorporated, and in 1749 received a grant of &pound;500 per annum from parliament; it subsequently became the Royal Dublin Society. Prior dedicated his <span class="italic">Authentic Narrative of the Success of Tar-Water in Curing a Great Number and Variety of Distempers</span> (1746) to Lord Chesterfield, with whom he had corresponded during the latter's spell as viceroy to Ireland (1745&#150;6). An essay by Prior, advocating the encouragement of the linen manufacture in Ireland, was also published at Dublin in 1749.<br>
<br>After a long illness Prior died, unmarried, on 21 October 1751 at Rathdowney, where he was buried on 25 October. A monument erected by subscription to his memory in Christ Church, Dublin, carried an inscription by Bishop Berkeley, who styled him &#145;Societatis Dubliniensis auctor, institutor, curator&#146;.<br>
<br>J. T. Gilbert<span class="italic">, rev. </span> Philip Carter </div>
