<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Davis,  John Philip</span> <span class="st"> [<em>called </em>Pope Davis]</span>  (1784&#150;1862), <span class="occ">portrait painter</span>, of whose parents nothing is known, first exhibited at the Royal Academy in 1811. Then, and for ten years following, his contributions consisted of portraits in oil. In 1824 he went to Rome, where he painted a large picture, <span class="italic">The Talbot Family Receiving the Benediction of the Pope</span> (hence his nickname, Pope Davis). In the year following he was awarded a premium of &pound;50 by the directors of the British Institution. In 1826, after his return to London, he exhibited at the Royal Academy <span class="italic">Canova Crowned by the Genius of Sculpture</span>. Thenceforward until 1843 he was an occasional exhibitor from a variety of London addresses. With his friend B. R. Haydon, Davis was a vigorous opponent of the Royal Academy. In 1843 he published <span class="italic">Facts of Vital Importance Relative to the Embellishment of the Houses of Parliament</span>; in 1858 <span class="italic">The Royal Academy and the National Gallery: what is the State of these Institutions?</span> Davis died on 28 September 1862 at 67 Great Russell Street, Bloomsbury, London. A volume of his essays, <span class="italic">Thoughts on Great Painters</span>, was published posthumously in 1866.<br>
<br>Ernest Radford<span class="italic">, rev. </span> Annette Peach </div>
