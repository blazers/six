<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Fane, Sir  Edmund Douglas Veitch</span>  (1837&#150;1900), <span class="occ">diplomatist</span>, was the eldest son of Arthur Fane (<i>d</i>. 1872) of Boyton, Codford, Wiltshire, prebendary of Salisbury, and his wife, Lucy, daughter of J. Benett of Peyt House, Wiltshire. He matriculated from Merton College, Oxford, on 28 May 1855, but did not graduate, entered the diplomatic service and lived the life of a competent but undistinguished diplomatist. He was appointed attach&eacute; at Tehran in 1858. In 1863 he was transferred to Turin, and from Turin to St Petersburg in 1866 as second secretary. During 1867&#150;78 his employment was extremely varied, involving brief sojourns at Washington, Florence, Munich, Brussels, Vienna, and Bern. He was secretary of legation at Copenhagen (1879&#150;81); secretary of embassy at Madrid (1881&#150;5), Brussels (March&#150;December 1885), and Constantinople (1886&#150;93); and minister at Belgrade (1893&#150;8) and at Copenhagen once more (1898&#150;1900). He negotiated the treaty of commerce with Serbia of 10 July 1893. In 1897 he received the jubilee medal, and in 1899 was created KCMG. He was lord of the manor of Boyton, Wiltshire, and a deputy lieutenant and justice of the peace for the county. He married, in 1875, Constantia Eleanor, daughter of General R. Blucher Wood. He died on 20 March 1900 at Copenhagen, his wife surviving him.<br>
<br>J. M. Rigg<span class="italic">, rev. </span> H. C. G. Matthew </div>
