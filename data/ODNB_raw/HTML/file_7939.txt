<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Hamilton,  William Alexander Anthony Archibald  Douglas-</span>, eleventh duke of Hamilton and eighth duke of Brandon  (1811&#150;1863), <span class="occ">aristocrat</span>, was the only son of <script>writeSeealsoLink('../view/article/7857/', "Alexander Douglas-Hamilton, the tenth duke (1767&#150;1852)")</script>, and Susan Euphemia Beckford (1786&#150;1859). He was born on 19 February 1811, at Grosvenor Place, London, and was known as marquess of Douglas until he succeeded to his father's several titles and estates in 1852. He was educated at Eton College and at Christ Church, Oxford. He was knight marischal of Scotland, colonel of the Lanarkshire militia, lord lieutenant of that county, deputy lieutenant of the county of Bute, major-commandant of the Glasgow yeomanry from 1849 to 1857, and grand master of the society of freemasons. On 23 February 1843 he married the Princess Marie Am&eacute;lie (1818&#150;1888), youngest daughter of Karl Ludwig Friedrich, grand duke of Baden, and his wife, Stephanie de Beauharnais, and a cousin of Napoleon III. They had a son and a daughter. After his marriage he lived chiefly in Paris and Baden Baden, and was frequently a guest at the Tuileries, taking very little interest in British politics. He died on 15 July 1863 from the effects of a fall after a supper at the Maison Dor&eacute;e, boulevard des Italiens, Paris. He inherited the pride for which his family was noted, and was described by the lord chancellor, Lord Brougham, as &#145;Very Duke of Very Duke&#146;  (GEC, <span class="italic">Peerage</span>).<br>
<br>L. C. Sanders<span class="italic">, rev. </span> K. D. Reynolds </div>
