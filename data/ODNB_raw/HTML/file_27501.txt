<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Toller, Sir  Samuel</span>  (1764&#150;1821), <span class="occ">lawyer in India</span>, was born on 10 November 1764, the eldest son of Thomas Toller (1732&#150;1795), dissenting minister, and his wife, the daughter of Samuel Lawrence, dissenting minister. Thomas Toller was the youngest child of Hugh Toller, attorney, of Taunton; he attended Mile End Academy, Middlesex, was ordained in 1754, and in 1760 became a pastor at Monkwell Street Chapel, London, succeeding his deceased father-in-law. Following a quarrel with another pastor, he left about 1775, ministered in Hoxton, and died on 3 March 1795.<br>
<br>Samuel Toller was educated at Charterhouse School, Smithfield, London, from March 1775 to April 1782. He was admitted at Lincoln's Inn on 27 March 1781, and called to the bar. In 1793 Toller married Miss Cory of Cambridge, sister of Robert Towerson Cory, master of Emmanuel College, Cambridge, from 1797 to 1835; they had at least one child.<br>
<br>In March 1812 Toller was appointed advocate-general at Madras, and was subsequently knighted. He published two valued legal works: <span class="italic">The Law of Executors and Administrators</span> (1800; 7th edn by Francis Whitmarsh, 1838; 2nd American edn by T. F. Gordon, 1824; 3rd American edn by E. D. Ingraham, 1834) and <span class="italic">Treatise of the Law of Tithes: Compiled in Part from some Notes of Richard Wooddeson</span> (1808; 3rd edn, 1822).<br>
<br>Toller died in India on 19 November 1821, on his way to Bangalore, where he was going for his health.<br>
<br>E. I. Carlyle<span class="italic">, rev. </span> Roger T. Stearn </div>
