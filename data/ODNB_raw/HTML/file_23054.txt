<div class="para">
<span class="headword">St Albans,  Ralph  of</span>  (<em>fl. </em>1195<span class="mult">x</span>1214?), <span class="occ">Benedictine monk and hagiographer</span>, was probably a native of Dunstable and a monk of St Albans. By some writers he is called Robert. He is possibly to be identified with the Ralph who was prior of Wymondham, a cell of St Albans, at some point between 1195 and 1214. At the request of William, another monk of St Albans, he wrote a metrical life of St Alban, which amplified and versified William's Latin prose lives of St Alban and St Amphibalus. Copies of Ralph's work are in four St Albans manuscripts: BL, Cotton MS Julius D.iii, part 2, folios 125&#150;58, and Claudius E.iv, folios 47&#150;58<i>v</i>, and in Dublin, Trinity College, MS 177 (formerly E.i.40), folios 3&#150;20, and Cambridge, Gonville and Caius College, MS 230, folios 172ff. (incomplete). In a treatise &#145;On the foundation and merits of the monastery of St Albans&#146; composed probably by Thomas of Walsingham, Ralph is compared to Virgil.<br>
<br>Mary Bateson<span class="italic">, rev. </span> Rebecca Reader </div>
