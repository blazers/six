<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Lookup,  John</span>  (<em>fl. </em>1739&#150;1740), <span class="occ">theologian</span>, the details of whose upbringing and parentage are unknown, was a disciple of the philosopher John Hutchinson (1674&#150;1737). In 1739 Lookup published <span class="italic">The erroneous translations in the vulgar versions of the scriptures detected in several instances taken from the original, with a previous essay upon the doctrine of the Trinity</span>, the &#145;Essay&#146; having been written in deprecation of Edward Merchant Johnson's pamphlet, <span class="italic">A Plain Account of the Trinity from Scripture and Reason</span> (1739). In the following year Lookup produced his translation, <span class="italic">Berashith, or, The First Book of Moses, Call'd Genesis</span>, which is inscribed to John Potter, archbishop of Canterbury. Lookup had previously shocked the archbishop by his &#145;incorrect sentiments&#146; on the doctrine of the Trinity. His translation shows him to have possessed a creditable knowledge of Hebrew.<br>
<br>Gordon Goodwin<span class="italic">, rev. </span> Philip Carter </div>
