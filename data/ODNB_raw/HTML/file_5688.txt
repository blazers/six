<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Clipston <span class="st"> [Clipstone]</span>,  John</span>  (<em>d.</em> <em>c.</em>1378), <span class="occ">prior of Nottingham and theologian</span>, joined the Carmelite order in Nottingham. He studied at Cambridge, where he incepted as DTh and lectured in the university. On 7 December 1349 he was granted permission to hear confessions in the Ely diocese. He was prior at Nottingham for some years and died there <i>c</i>.1378. Clipston was a noted scripture scholar and preacher, but sadly none of his works survives. Bale preserves the titles and incipits of ten of his compositions, which include a &#145;large work&#146; describing the personages of the <span class="roman">Bible</span> in alphabetical order. About 1550 Bale saw five volumes of his sermons which had once been in the possession of the monastery at Syon.<br>
<br>Richard Copsey </div>
