<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Deane,  William John</span>  (1823&#150;1895), <span class="occ">Church of England clergyman and biblical scholar</span>, born on 6 October 1823, was the third son of John Deane of Lymington in Hampshire. After attending Rugby School he matriculated from Oriel College, Oxford, on 20 October 1843, graduating BA in 1847 and MA in 1872. He was ordained deacon in 1847 and priest in 1849. He was successively curate of Rugby (1847&#150;9), curate of Wick Rissington in Gloucestershire (1849&#150;52), and rector of South Thoresby in Lincolnshire (1852&#150;3). In 1853 he was presented by the chancellor of the duchy of Lancaster to the rectory of Ashen in Essex, which he retained until his death.<br>
<br>Deane was the author of a number of exegetical works, written in a clear and interesting manner. In 1881 he edited the Greek, Latin, and English texts of the <span class="roman">Wisdom of Solomon</span> for the Clarendon Press, Oxford, with critical notes, and in 1891 he published <span class="italic">Pseudepigrapha</span>, a well-written description and estimate of the apocryphal books. He also published biographies of the prophets in the <span class="roman">Men of the Bible</span> series and introductions to <span class="roman">Old Testament</span> books in the <span class="roman">Pulpit Commentary</span>. He was a frequent contributor to <span class="italic">The Thinker</span>, and published on holy days (1850 and 1864), also editing a volume entitled <span class="italic">Lyon sanctorum</span> (1850). He died at Ashen rectory on 30 May 1895, leaving a widow, three sons, and three daughters. He was buried on 4 June in Ashen churchyard, under the east window of the chancel.<br>
<br>E. I. Carlyle<span class="italic">, rev. </span> H. C. G. Matthew </div>
