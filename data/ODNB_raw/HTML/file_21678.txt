<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Peacock,  Lucy</span>  (<em>fl. </em>1785&#150;1816), <span class="occ">bookseller and children's writer</span>, was probably born in London, where she kept a shop in Oxford Street. Although little is known of her life, it is likely that she was married, as the publisher's imprint on some of her works states that they were &#145;printed for R. and L. Peacock&#146;. Her children's books were published for the most part anonymously. Among the earliest of these was <span class="italic">The adventures of the six princesses of Babylon in their travels to the temple of virtue: an allegory</span> (1785), of which five editions were printed, and which was &#145;dedicated, by permission to her Royal Highness the Princess Mary&#146;. It was followed by <span class="italic">The Rambles of Fancy, or, Moral and Interesting Tales</span> and <span class="italic">Friendly Labours</span> in 1786. In the following years Peacock contributed similar moral tales to the <span class="italic">Juvenile Magazine</span>, and other periodicals. Among her publications were the allegorical narrative <span class="italic">The Knight of the Rose</span> (1793); <span class="italic">The Little Emigrant: a Tale</span> (1799); and <span class="italic">Emily, or, The Test of Sincerity</span> (1816). <span class="italic">The Visit for a Week</span> (1794) was her most popular work, running into nine editions. Typically didactic, it deals with the visit of two children to their aunt, who teaches them that learning can be exciting, with her discourses on many varied topics. <span class="italic">The Visit</span> was translated into French in 1817 by J. E. Lefebvre.<br>
<br>Peacock also translated from the French <span class="italic">Ambrose and Eleanor, or, The Adventures of Two Children Deserted on an Uninhabited Island</span> (1796, 1812), an adaptation of <span class="italic">Fanfan et Lolotte</span>; Veyssi&egrave;re de La Croze's <span class="italic">Grammaire historique</span> (1800); and <span class="italic">Abr&eacute;g&eacute; chronologique de l'histoire universelle</span> (1807). Many of her works were reprinted in America.<br>
<br>G. Le G. Norgate<span class="italic">, rev. </span> M. Clare Loughlin-Chow </div>
