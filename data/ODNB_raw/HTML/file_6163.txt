<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Cooke,  George Boughey</span>  (1807&#150;1863), <span class="occ">actor</span>, was born in Manchester on 7 March 1807. In early life he was placed with the mercantile firm of Hoyle &amp; Co., but his interests lay in the theatre. After performing the role of Othello in amateur theatricals, he left the firm and in March 1828 began his professional career at Walsall theatre, under the management of Chamberlayne. He remained with Chamberlayne for eighteen months, playing in Coventry, Lichfield, and Leamington. He then joined other managements and appeared successfully at Margate, at Doncaster in 1832, and at Edinburgh in October 1835, as Old Crumbs in Douglas Jerrold's <span class="italic">The Rent Day</span>. In July 1837 he was at the Strand in London, then under the management of W. J. Hammond, playing Mr Wardle in W. T. Moncrieff's adaptation <span class="italic">Sam Weller, or, The Pickwickians</span>. He accompanied Hammond to Drury Lane in October 1839 but had rather a disastrous season at that theatre.<br>
<br>Cooke married in 1840 Elizabeth Stuart, the sister of a well-known actor. After playing at Liverpool, Manchester, and Birmingham, he appeared at the Marylebone in 1847, when that theatre was under the management of Mary Amelia Warner. Here he played the Old Shepherd in <span class="italic">The Winter's Tale</span>, Sir Oliver Surface in <span class="italic">The School for Scandal</span>, and Colonel Damas in <span class="italic">The Lady of Lyons</span>. He later enjoyed a long engagement at the Olympic Theatre, where he remained until his death, and became a favourite with the public in the original comic roles he played in Wilkie Collins's <span class="italic">The Lighthouse</span> (1857), John Oxenford's <span class="italic">The Porter's Knot</span> (1858), <span class="italic">Retained for the Defence</span> (1859), and <span class="italic">Uncle Zachary</span> (1860), and many other plays. He died on 5 March 1863, committing suicide by cutting his throat at his residence, 51 Cambridge Street, Pimlico, London. He had been suffering for a long time from &#145;a dropsical disease&#146;.<br>
<br>Cooke was a genial actor, skilled in the impersonation of old men, generous uncles, good-natured guardians, and similar such sketches of character.<br>
<br>Joseph Knight<span class="italic">, rev. </span> Nilanjana Banerji </div>
