<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Hatton,  Edward  Finch-</span>  (1697?&#150;1771), <span class="occ">diplomatist</span>, was born Edward Finch, the fifth son of <script>writeSeealsoLink('../view/article/9427/', "Daniel Finch, seventh earl of Winchilsea and second earl of Nottingham (1647&#150;1730)")</script>, politician, and Anne Hatton (1668&#150;1743). After attending Mr Ellis's school in Isleworth, Middlesex, he was admitted fellow-commoner of Trinity College, Cambridge, in 1713 and he proceeded MA in 1718. He went on the grand tour to France, Italy, and Hanover from 1720 to 1723 and his hopes of a diplomatic career were met with his appointment as envoy-extraordinary to the imperial diet of Regensburg (September 1724&#150;February 1725). He served in succession as envoy to the Polish court from 1725 to 1727, to the Swedish court from 1728 to 1739, and to the Russian court from 1740 to 1742.<br>
<br>In 1727 Finch was elected MP for Cambridge University, which he represented until 1768. A fellow of Christ's College, Cambridge (1728&#150;46), he and his fellow MP Thomas Townshend instituted the members' prizes in the university for essays in Latin prose. On his return from abroad in 1742 he declared his support for Lord Carteret and was appointed groom of the royal bedchamber; in spite of the subsequent changes in government, the king ensured that Finch remained in post until 1756. On 6 September 1746 he married Elizabeth Palmer, daughter of Sir Thomas Palmer, fourth baronet, of Wingham, Kent; they had three daughters and two sons. He assumed in 1764 the additional name of Hatton, under the terms of the will of his great-aunt, Anne Hatton, daughter of Christopher, Viscount Hatton. He was appointed master of the robes in June 1757 and surveyor of the king's private roads in November 1760; he retired from the Commons before the general election of 1768. Finch-Hatton died on 16 May 1771.<br>
<br>[Anon.]<span class="italic">, rev. </span> R. D. E. Eagles </div>
