<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Martin,  William Charles Linnaeus</span>  (1798&#150;1864), <span class="occ">writer on natural history</span>, was baptized on 23 February 1798, at Burton upon Trent, Staffordshire, the son of <script>writeSeealsoLink('../view/article/18216/', "William Martin (1767&#150;1810)")</script>, the naturalist, and his wife, Mary, an actress whose former married name was Adams. From October 1830 to 1838 he was superintendent of the museum at the Zoological Society of London.<br>
<br>Martin's earliest works were <span class="italic">A Natural History of Quadrupeds</span>, of which only 544 pages were issued in 1840, <span class="italic">A General Introduction to the Natural History of Mammiferous Animals</span> (1841), <span class="italic">The History of the Dog</span> (1845), and <span class="italic">The History of the Horse</span> (1845). These were followed, between 1847 and 1858, by a series of works on fish, poultry, cattle, pigs, and sheep, which appeared either separately or as volumes in the <span class="italic">Farmer's Library</span>, <span class="italic">Books for the Country</span>, and <span class="italic">The Country House</span>. In 1857 the writer Samuel Sidney edited and rewrote Martin's <span class="italic">The Pig</span>.<br>
<br>Martin was also author of a number of ornithological works: <span class="italic">An Introduction to the Study of Birds &hellip; with a Particular Notice of the Birds Mentioned in Scripture</span> (1850), and <span class="italic">A General History of Humming-Birds &hellip; with &hellip; Reference to the Collection of J. Gould</span> (1852). He also edited a fourth edition of Robert Mudie's <span class="italic">Feathered Tribes of the British Islands</span> (1854) for Henry George Bohn's <span class="italic">Illustrated Library</span> and, in conjunction with Francis Trevelyan Buckland and others, contributed papers to <span class="italic">Birds and Bird Life</span> (1863). Forty-five papers were also read by Martin before the Zoological Society, and appeared in its <span class="italic">Proceedings</span>.<br>
<br>Martin died on 15 February 1864, at his home, 10 Dacre Park Terrace, Lee, in Kent. He was survived by his wife, Mary Jane.<br>
<br>B. B. Woodward<span class="italic">, rev. </span> Yolanda Foote </div>
