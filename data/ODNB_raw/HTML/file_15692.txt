<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Kite,  Charles</span>  (<em>bap. </em>1760?, <em>d.</em> 1811), <span class="occ">surgeon</span>, may have been the son of Samuel Kite baptized at Gravesend, Kent, on 1 October 1760. Kite was a member of the Company of Surgeons in London, and practised at Gravesend, Kent. Little else is known about his life. Besides contributing to the <span class="italic">Memoirs of the London Medical Society</span> and other medical journals, he wrote: <span class="italic">An Essay on the Recovery of the Apparently Dead</span> (1788), for which he was awarded the silver medal of the Humane Society, and <span class="italic">Essays and observations, physiological and medical, on the submersion of animals, and on the resin of the aeoroides resinifera, or yellow resin of Botany Bay</span> (1795). He died in Gravesend in 1811.<br>
<br>Gordon Goodwin<span class="italic">, rev. </span> Patrick Wallis </div>
