<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Holmes,  William Anthony</span>  (1782&#150;1843), <span class="occ">Church of Ireland clergyman</span>, was born in Drogheda, co. Louth, the son of Joseph Holmes. He was educated privately, and then entered Trinity College, Dublin, on 7 January 1799 and was elected a scholar in 1801. He graduated BA in 1803 and BD and DD in 1834. Having been ordained a priest in 1806, he became incumbent of Holywood, co. Down, in 1810. While there he played an important part in establishing an institute for beggars in Belfast by publishing the tract <span class="italic">A Plan for a Mendicity Institution</span> (<i>c</i>.1817&#150;1819). In 1818 he was promoted to the rectory of Ballyroan in the diocese of Leighlin; for some years he was preacher of Cashel Cathedral, and in 1822 became rector of Hore Abbey in the diocese of Cashel. On 22 May 1832 he was collated to the chancellorship of Cashel, and in 1837 to the rectory of Templemore. He was described by a contemporary archdeacon as &#145;an eloquent preacher, and a person of active mind and literary habits&#146;.<br>
<br>Besides sermons and contributions to periodicals, Holmes published several works including <span class="italic">The Time of the End; being a Series of Lectures on Prophetical Chronology</span> (1833) and <span class="italic">The Queen's Declaration Against Popery, and the Coronation Oath Discussed</span> (1843). He was married to Caroline (<i>d</i>. 1838), daughter of John Bond, of Newbridge House, near Bath, and they had two sons. He died at the rectory, Templemore, on 30 December 1843, and was buried in St John the Baptist's churchyard, Cashel.<br>
<br>B. H. Blacker<span class="italic">, rev. </span> David Huddleston </div>
