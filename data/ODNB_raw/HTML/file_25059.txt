<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Sellon,  Baker John</span>  (1762&#150;1835), <span class="occ">serjeant-at-law</span>, was born in London on 14 March 1762, the second son of William Sellon (<i>d</i>. 1790), perpetual curate of St James's, Clerkenwell. He was admitted into Merchant Taylors' School on 2 November 1773, and entered St John's College, Oxford, on 11 June 1779. He graduated BCL on 24 October 1785. On 24 January 1788 he married Charlotte (<i>d</i>. 1832), daughter of Rivers Dickinson of St John Street, Clerkenwell; they had a large family, of whom three daughters and one son survived infancy. Their second daughter, Maria Ann, married John James Hall, and their third daughter, Anne, married Sir Benjamin Collins Brodie.<br>
<br>Sellon wished to become a clergyman, but, at his father's request, he studied law, and was called to the bar from the Inner Temple on 10 February 1792. While at the Inner Temple, Sellon published his <span class="italic">Analysis of the practice of the courts of king's bench and common pleas, with some observations on the mode of passing fines and suffering recoveries</span> (1789). On being admitted to the bar, he worked on <span class="italic">The Practice of the Courts of King's Bench and Common Pleas</span> (2 vols., 1792&#150;6). He also wrote <span class="italic">A Treatise on the Deity and the Trinity</span>, which was published posthumously in 1847 and edited by W. Marsh.<br>
<br>After practising for several years with distinction, Sellon was admitted a serjeant-at-law in the Easter term of 1798, and he later became leader of the Norfolk circuit. Increasing deafness, however, obliged him to refuse a judgeship, and finally to retire from the bar. In 1814, at his own request (he was apparently short of money), Henry Addington, Viscount Sidmouth, appointed him police magistrate at Union Hall; in January 1819 he was transferred to the Hatton Garden office. There he continued to act until his retirement in 1834. He died at his home in Hampstead, after a period of paralysis, on 19 August 1835.<br>
<br>Gordon Goodwin<span class="italic">, rev. </span> Joanne Potier </div>
