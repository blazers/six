<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Petersdorff,  Charles Erdman</span>  (1800&#150;1886), <span class="occ">legal writer</span>, was born in London on 4 November 1800, the third son of Christian Frederick Petersdorff, furrier, who began trading in London in the 1790s and later lived at Ivy House, Tottenham. He became a student of the Inner Temple on 24 September 1818, and after practising as a special pleader and going on the home circuit, was called to the bar on 25 January 1833.<br>
<br>Petersdorff was for some time one of the counsel to the Admiralty, and by order of the lords of the Admiralty he compiled a complete collection of the statutes relating to the navy, to shipping, ports, and harbours. He had already, when only twenty, produced <span class="italic">A General Index to the Precedents in Civil and Criminal Proceedings from the Earliest Period</span>, followed in 1824 by <span class="italic">A Practical Treatise on the Law of Bail</span> and between 1825 and 1830 the fifteen volumes of <span class="italic">A practical and elementary abridgement of cases in the king's bench, common pleas, exchequer and at nisi prius from the Restoration</span>. Among his later writings, the most important was <span class="italic">A Practical and Elementary Abridgement of the Common Law</span> (five vols., 1841&#150;4). In its synthesis of common law and statutory modifications, this work looked ahead to later legal encyclopaedias such as <span class="italic">Halsbury's Laws</span>.<br>
<br>On 15 November 1847 Petersdorff married Mary Anne, widow of James Mallock, a London solicitor. He was created a serjeant-at-law on 14 June 1858, and on 1 January 1863 nominated a judge of the county courts for north Devon and Somerset, an appointment which he resigned in December 1885 at the age of eighty-five. He died from a fall into the area of his house, 23 Harley Street, London, on 29 July 1886.<br>
<br>G. C. Boase<span class="italic">, rev. </span> Patrick Polden </div>
