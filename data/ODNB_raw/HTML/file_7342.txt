<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Dawson,  Abraham</span>  (1713?&#150;1789), <span class="occ">biblical scholar</span>, was probably born at Horton, near Bradford, into a well-established Yorkshire nonconformist family. His father was Eli Dawson (<i>d</i>. 1744), who served as a Presbyterian minister at Morley, near Halifax, then at Horton, and lastly, from 1728, at Halifax; his mother was Alice Taylor. His grandfather Joseph Dawson (1635/6&#150;1709) had also been a Presbyterian minister and was ejected from Thornton Chapel, near Bradford. Abraham had six brothers, including <script>writeSeealsoLink('../view/article/7344/', "Benjamin Dawson (1729&#150;1814)")</script> and <script>writeSeealsoLink('../view/article/7356/', "Thomas Dawson (c.1725&#150;1782)")</script>. All but one of his brothers were, like Abraham, educated for and entered the church; all in time left the ministry, Benjamin becoming a philologist, and Thomas a physician.<br>
<br>Abraham Dawson served as a Presbyterian minister for several years before he conformed to the Church of England (as did four of his siblings). In July or August 1754 he was instituted rector of Ringsfield, near Beccles in Suffolk. While there he published <span class="italic">A New English Translation of the First Three Chapters of Genesis</span> (1763), followed by his translation of the fourth and fifth chapters (1772) and the sixth and eleventh (1786). Dawson, who was married, died at Ringsfield on 3 October 1789 and was buried in the church five days later.<br>
<br>Alexander Gordon<span class="italic">, rev. </span> Philip Carter </div>
