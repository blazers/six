<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Pink,  Charles Richard</span>  (1853&#150;1889), <span class="occ">architect</span>, was born on 4 July 1853 at Wood End House, Soberton, Hampshire, the son of Charles Pink, a land and estate surveyor. From 1864 to 1869 he studied drawing with J. G. Hart, after which, in 1871, he was articled to Thomas Henry Watson for four years. In 1872 he was a student at the RIBA and in 1873&#150;4 he attended Professor T. Hayter Lewis's classes of fine art and construction at University College, London, where he won a first prize in ancient and medieval art, and a second in ancient and modern construction.<br>
<br>In 1875 Pink was employed in Winchester, Hampshire, designing the Chilworth and North Baddesley schools. In 1876 he became an associate of the Royal Institute of British Architects (RIBA). He designed a number of houses and schools, and a few churches, mostly in Hampshire. Pink was particularly knowledgeable about architectural heraldry, and a number of his heraldic sketches were reproduced after his death in a volume called <span class="italic">The Pink Memorial</span> (1889). In addition he published <span class="italic">Notes on Heraldry</span> in 1884, and a privately printed paper, <span class="italic">Architectural Education</span>, in 1886.<br>
<br>Pink also took a keen interest in the professional education of architects. He served on the committee of the Architectural Association until 1885, when he was elected president, and in 1886 he was elected fellow of the RIBA. Pink married Florence Anna, <i>n&eacute;e</i> Browne, and their son, <script>writeSeealsoLink('../view/article/76073/', "Richard Charles Montagu Pink")</script>, was born in November 1888. However, Charles Pink died of bronchitis at Hyde, near Winchester, on 25 February 1889.<br>
<br>Laurence Binyon<span class="italic">, rev. </span> John Elliott </div>
