<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Kent,  James</span>  (1700&#150;1776), <span class="occ">organist and composer</span>, was born at Winchester on 13 March 1700, the son of a glazier. He was a chorister at Winchester Cathedral (1711&#150;14) and then at the Chapel Royal (1714&#150;18) under Dr William Croft. Through the influence of the subdean, John Dolben, he was appointed organist to the parish church of Finedon, Northamptonshire, in 1718. He resigned in 1731 on being elected organist of Trinity College, Cambridge, remaining there until he succeeded John Bishop as organist of Winchester Cathedral and Winchester College. He was married to Elizabeth, daughter of John Freeman, a singer in the theatre and in the choirs of the Chapel Royal, St Paul's, and Westminster Abbey. He resigned in 1774 and died in Winchester on 6 May 1776.<br>
<br>Kent published <span class="italic">Twelve Anthems</span> (1773), and after his death <span class="italic">A Morning and Evening Service with Eight Anthems</span> (<i>c</i>.1777) was edited by Joseph Corfe. He had assisted William Boyce in the preparation of his <span class="italic">Cathedral Music</span> (1760). His anthem &#145;Hear my prayer&#146;, for two solo trebles, may be taken as representative of his work, which was both extravagantly praised and excoriated: his work drew so heavily from G. B. Bassani and others as to open him to charges of unscrupulous plagiarism.<br>
<br>R. F. Sharp<span class="italic">, rev. </span> K. D. Reynolds </div>
