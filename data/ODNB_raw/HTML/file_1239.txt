<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Ballard,  Volant  Vashon</span>  (1774?&#150;1832), <span class="occ">naval officer</span>, a nephew of Admiral James Vashon, served as a midshipman with George Vancouver in his voyage to the north-west coast of America. Shortly after his return to England he was made a lieutenant, on 6 June 1795; and on 25 December 1798, while commanding the sloop <i>Hobart</i>, on the East India station, was posted into the frigate <i>Carysfort</i>. He subsequently commanded the frigate <i>Jason</i>, the <i>De Ruyter</i> (68 guns), and the <i>Beschermer</i> (50 guns), but without opportunity of distinction. In 1807, while commanding the 32-gun frigate <i>Blonde</i>, he cruised with great success against privateers, capturing seven in a few months. In 1809&#150;10, still in the <i>Blonde</i>, he served under the command of his namesake, Commodore Ballard of the <i>Sceptre</i>, at the capture of the French frigates in Anse-la-Barque and the capture of Guadeloupe for which he was honourably mentioned by both the naval and the military commanders-in-chief. On 18 September 1811 he married Isabella Sarah, eldest daughter of James Crabb of Shidfield Lodge, Hampshire. He obtained his flag rank on 27 May 1825. He died at Bath on 12 October 1832, and was buried at the church of St Saviour, Walcot, Wiltshire.<br>
<br>J. K. Laughton<span class="italic">, rev. </span> Andrew Lambert </div>
