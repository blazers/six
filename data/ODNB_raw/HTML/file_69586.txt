<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Harvey,  Stephen</span>  (1655&#150;1707), <span class="occ">lawyer and poet</span>, was born on 20 October 1655 at Walthamstow, the son of Stephen Harvey (1622&#150;1688), of East Beechworth, a barrister of the Middle Temple, and his wife, Dorothy (1631&#150;1694), daughter of William Conyers, of Walthamstow, sergeant-at-law. He had two younger sisters, Dorothy and Elizabeth. After attending Merchant Taylors' School, 1668&#150;71, he matriculated as Merchant Taylor fellow and Sir Thomas White scholar at St John's College, Oxford, on 27 June 1671, was admitted to the Middle Temple on 12 February 1673, and was called to the bar on 21 May 1680. He married his cousin Anne, daughter of John Harvey, of St Mary-at-Hill, on 15 August 1693. They had seven children: John, admitted to the Middle Temple on 17 May 1709, Elizabeth, Stephen, born at East Beechworth on 14 February 1700, Ann, Dorothy, Frances, Martin, and William.<br>
<br>Harvey made three contributions as a verse translator to the publishing ventures of Jacob Tonson. His translation of Juvenal's notorious ninth satire appeared in <span class="italic">The Satires of Decimus Junius Juvenalis, Translated &hellip; by Mr. Dryden and Several other Eminent Hands</span> (1693 [i.e. 1692]). His translation of &#145;The Passion of Byblis&#146; from the ninth book of Ovid's <span class="italic">Metamorphoses</span>, which borrows some phrases from John Oldham's translation of the same episode, and of &#145;Jupiter and Europa&#146; from the fourth book, both appeared in <span class="italic">The Annual Miscellany: for the Year 1694</span> (1694). All three poems treat of an unusual sexual situation without prurience, and Harvey shows himself to be a fluent versifier whose competence occasionally rises to excellence.<br>
<br>Harvey's friendship with John, Baron Somers, led to his being appointed steward of the royal manor of Reigate when it was granted to Somers on his becoming lord chancellor in 1697, and the following year he became MP for the borough of Reigate, holding both positions until his death. Harvey acted as deputy steward or steward for other manors, including John Evelyn's Surrey estates, where he is recorded as serving as steward in September 1701. In May 1706 he was appointed a puisne justice of the Anglesey circuit, and on 25 October that year he became a master of the bench of the Middle Temple. Harvey died on 24 May 1707.<br>
<br>Alexander Lindsay </div>
