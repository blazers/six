<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Booth,  Thomas</span>  (<em>fl. </em>1594&#150;1616), <span class="occ">Church of England clergyman</span>, was educated at Trinity College, Cambridge, where he entered as a sizar in 1594, and graduated BA in 1598, MA in 1601, and BD in 1609. He became rector of Langdon Hills in Essex in 1611 and a prebendary of Lichfield Cathedral in 1615. The dates when his successors were appointed to both positions imply that he died in 1616. In 1611 he published (under his initials) <span class="italic">Concio ad clerum jamdudum Cantabrigiae habita in Luc. cap. 5 ver. 10</span>. No copy of this sermon is known to survive in any major library.<br>
<br>Thompson Cooper<span class="italic">, rev. </span> Tim Wales </div>
