<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Mant,  Walter Bishop</span>  (1807&#150;1869), <span class="occ">Church of Ireland clergyman</span>, was the eldest son of <script>writeSeealsoLink('../view/article/18001/', "Richard Mant (1776&#150;1848)")</script>, bishop of Down, Connor, and Dromore, and Elizabeth Wood (<i>d</i>. 2 April 1846). He was born on 25 June 1807 at Buriton, Hampshire. He matriculated at Oriel College, Oxford, on 6 February 1824, and graduated BA in 1827, proceeding MA in 1830. In 1831 he took holy orders, and was appointed archdeacon of Connor by his father. He married the Hon. Marianne Blackwood, daughter of Hans, third Lord Dufferin and Claneboye, on 15 October 1831. They had two daughters before her death on 22 February 1845. In 1832 he attended Trinity College, Dublin. He became rector of Hillsborough, co. Down, in October 1834, and was appointed archdeacon of Down.<br>
<br>For many years Mant was provincial grand master, and afterwards provincial grand chaplain, of the freemasons of Down and Antrim. On 5 January 1847 he remarried; his new wife was Emily Neville (<i>d</i>. 1865), daughter of Lieutenant-Colonel Marcus Corry, of Hillsborough, co. Down. They had two sons. Mant wrote poetry and was interested in antiquarian subjects, contributing to the <span class="italic">Proceedings</span> of local societies. His published works include <span class="italic">Horae apostolicae</span> (1839), <span class="italic">Memoirs of &hellip; Richard Mant</span> (1857), <span class="italic">Christophoros and other Poems</span> (1861), and <span class="italic">Bible Quartets</span> (1862). He died on 6 April 1869 at the archdeaconry, Hillsborough, where he was buried in the churchyard on 10 April. A tablet to his memory was erected in the village church there.<br>
<br>Alexander Gordon<span class="italic">, rev. </span> David Huddleston </div>
