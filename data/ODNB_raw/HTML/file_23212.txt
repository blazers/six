<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Rea,  John</span>  (<em>d.</em> 1681), <span class="occ">nursery gardener</span>, lived with his daughter, Minerva, and her husband, Samuel Gilbert, author of the <span class="italic">Florist's vademecum</span> (1682), at Kinlet, Shropshire, near Bewdley, Worcestershire. Rea describes the place in his <span class="italic">Flora</span> (1676) as &#145;a rural district where it was my unhappiness to plant my stock&#146;. He was said to have had the largest collection of tulips in England and it was he who introduced <i>Corylus colurna</i> in 1665. Rea planned the gardens at Gerard's Bromley, Staffordshire, the seat of Charles, fourth Baron Gerard.<br>
<br>Rea published <span class="italic">Flora, seu, De florum cultura</span>, with a second engraved title-page, <span class="italic">Flora, Ceres, and Pomona</span>, in 1665 (3rd edn 1702); the work was dedicated to Sir Thomas Hanmer and members of his family, and to Baron Gerard's son. This was intended as a revision of John Parkinson's <span class="italic">Paradisi in sole paradisus terrestris</span> (1629), and includes designs for gardens, and many pages on fruit and tulips.<br>
<br>Rea died at Kinlet in November 1681, leaving his holding to his daughter.<br>
<br>G. S. Boulger<span class="italic">, rev. </span> Anne Pimlott Baker </div>
