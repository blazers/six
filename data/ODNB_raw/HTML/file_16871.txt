<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Llywarch ap Llywelyn</span> <span class="st"> [<em>called </em>Prydydd y Moch]</span>  (<em>fl. </em><em>c.</em>1180&#150;<em>c.</em>1220), <span class="occ">poet</span>, was the chief court poet of Gwynedd. He held lands and a mill, probably by Llywelyn ab Iorwerth's gift, in Rhos is Dulas. His father was presumably named Llywelyn, but nothing more is known of his family history; he himself hints that his forebears were not poets. The sobriquet attributed to him resulted from a daring reference to casting pearls before swine in a poem rebuking Gruffudd ap Cynan; however, doubt has been cast on the identification of Llywarch with Prydydd y Moch. Of Llywarch's poetic output thirty works survive, a total of 1780 lines. His earliest extant poems (probably of <i>c</i>.1180) were addressed to Dafydd and Rhodri ab Owain Gwynedd. His chief patron, however, was Llywelyn ab Iorwerth, for whom nine poems survive, tracing the fortunes of that redoubtable prince from <i>c</i>.1183 to <i>c</i>.1220. Others of his poems are addressed to some of Llywelyn's client princelings. Llywarch also composed an intriguing poem to the ordeal by fire, and a light-hearted <i>awdl</i>, which purports to be that of an ardent lover, for Gwenll&iuml;an ferch Hywel of Gwynll&#373;g.<br>
<br>R. Geraint Gruffydd </div>
