<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Pagan,  Isobel</span>  (1741/2&#150;1821), <span class="occ">poet</span>, was a native of New Cumnock, Ayrshire, and passed her life mainly in the neighbourhood of Muirkirk in that county. Born with a deformed foot, she was abandoned early by her parents. In one of her poems she describes her education as &#145;ten weeks when I was seven years old&#146;  (<span class="italic">Collection of Songs</span>). However, she read in her spare time, especially the <span class="roman">Bible</span>.<br>
<br>A charitable landlord allowed Pagan to live rent-free in an old brick store in Muirkirk. In what was essentially a hovel, she sold whisky to both peasants and gentry and entertained them with her dramatic monologues and bawdy songs. Pagan reputedly had a beautiful singing voice, but she was habitually drunk and unashamedly sexually promiscuous. Her sarcasm was formidable. A clergyman once said she was &#145;the most perfect realisation of a witch or hag that I ever saw&#146;  (Blain, Clements &amp; Grundy, <span class="italic">Feminist comp.</span>).<br>
<br>Since Pagan could not write, her poems were transcribed by her friend William Gemmell, a tailor. In 1805 her <span class="italic">Collection of Songs and Poems on Several Occasions</span> was published in Glasgow. Two songs attributed to her but not included in the <span class="italic">Collection</span>, &#145;Ca' the Yowes to the Knowes&#146;&#151;a realistic tale of lovers' tricks&#151;and &#145;Crook and Plaid&#146;&#151;a more dignified appraisal of the virtues of the lowly&#151;demonstrate her lyrical abilities. Her satirical skills led to her being nicknamed Wicked Tibbie. Having heard &#145;Ca' the Yowes to the Knowes&#146;, Robert Burns created his own version by adding a verse of his own, but he did not acknowledge Pagan as the original source (see <span class="italic">The Scots Musical Museum</span>, 1787&#150;1803). Isobel Pagan died on 3 November 1821 in Muirkirk and was buried in Muirkirk churchyard.<br>
<br>T. W. Bayne<span class="italic">, rev. </span> Jane Potter </div>
