<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Shalders,  George</span>  (<em>c.</em>1825&#150;1873), <span class="occ">watercolour painter</span>, was born in Portsmouth and began to exhibit in London at the Royal Academy and the Society of British Artists in Suffolk Street in 1848. He showed fifteen paintings at the Royal Academy, the last in 1864. In 1863 he became an associate of the New Society of Painters in Water Colours, and a member in 1864, and subsequently he exhibited only there. He painted landscapes, usually views in Hampshire, Surrey, Yorkshire, Wales, and Ireland, often containing cattle or sheep. Reviewing the 1865 exhibition at the New Watercolour Society, in which his <span class="italic">Fresh Pasture</span> was exhibited, the critic writing for the <span class="italic">Art Journal</span> commented that &#145;Sheep are folded and driven to field by Mr Shalders with a truth and beauty which finds no rivals&#146;  (<span class="italic">Art Journal</span>, 27, 1 June 1865, 176). His <span class="italic">Cows in a Landscape</span> is in the Victoria and Albert Museum, London. Shalders died a widower on 27 January 1873 at his home, 10 Berkeley Gardens, Campden Hill, London, leaving three young daughters. His early death was said to have been caused by overwork, following the death of his wife two years earlier.<br>
<br>F. M. O'Donoghue<span class="italic">, rev. </span> Anne Pimlott Baker </div>
