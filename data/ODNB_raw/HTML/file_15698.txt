<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Knapp,  William</span>  (1698&#150;1768), <span class="occ">composer</span>, was born at Wareham, Dorset. A glover and property owner, he became one of the burgesses of Poole, and was parish clerk at St James's, Poole, for thirty-nine years. Knapp was apparently a &#145;difficult personality&#146;  (<span class="italic">New Grove</span>), and trained the choirs of several Dorset churches. He published two popular collections of church music, <span class="italic">A Sett of New Psalm Tunes and Anthems</span> (1738; 8th edn, 1770) and <span class="italic">New Church Melody</span> (1753; 5th edn, 1764). Both works contained music from earlier collections, as well as original compositions by Knapp. One of his psalm tunes, &#145;Wareham&#146;, appeared in both volumes: it continued in use throughout the twentieth century. Knapp's tunes and anthems were widely collected in Britain and the American colonies, and reveal his &#145;undoubted flair for effective melody&#146;  (<span class="italic">New Grove</span>). Knapp died in Poole and was buried there on 26 September 1768.<br>
<br>J. C. Hadden<span class="italic">, rev. </span> K. D. Reynolds </div>
