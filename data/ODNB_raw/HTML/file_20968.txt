<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Overend,  Marmaduke</span>  (<em>d.</em> 1790), <span class="occ">organist and composer</span>, was organist of Isleworth, Middlesex, from 1760 until his death. He studied with William Boyce, and corresponded with him until Boyce's death in 1779, about ancient Greek musical doctrines and the mathematical calculation of musical intervals. Following Boyce's death, Overend purchased his manuscript treatise on music. Based on it, he published <span class="italic">A Brief Account of, and Introduction to, Eight Lectures in the Science of Music</span> (1781), containing a method of finding musical ratios; it does not appear that the lectures were ever delivered. He also wrote music, much of which remained in manuscript. His published music includes an <span class="italic">Epithalamium</span> on the marriage of George III and Queen Charlotte (1761), <span class="italic">Twelve Sonatas</span> for two violins and cello (1762), and <span class="italic">A Hunting Cantata</span> (<i>c</i>.1780). Overend was buried at Isleworth on 25 June 1790, describing himself in his will as &#145;Student in Music&#146;. His library was sold in 1791, when his manuscripts and that of Boyce were acquired by J. W. Callcott. They were eventually purchased by the Bodleian Library in 1972.<br>
<br>L. M. Middleton<span class="italic">, rev. </span> K. D. Reynolds </div>
