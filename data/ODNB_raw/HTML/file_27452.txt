<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Tilney,  John</span>  (<em>d.</em> after 1455), <span class="occ">prior of Yarmouth and theologian</span>, joined the Carmelite order in Yarmouth, Norfolk. He undertook his studies at Cambridge and incepted as a doctor of theology <i>c</i>.1430. He returned to Yarmouth, where his name occurs as prior in 1435 and 1437, and he was still in office in 1455. It was probably during his time lecturing in Cambridge that Tilney wrote a commentary on the <span class="roman">Apocalypse</span> which Bale saw and for which he recorded the incipit. Bale describes how Tilney taught that the remission of sins is due to the free grace of Christ and that his death brings reconciliation with God the Father. However, this is simply an expansion of the second half of the incipit which Bale had copied in one of his early notebooks. Bale also notes a compendium on <span class="italic">The Sentences</span> with incipit and a collection of forty-four sermons. Later he added the usual &#145;Scholastic lectures&#146;. None of these works survives, and although an exposition on <span class="roman">St John's gospel</span>, found in Cambridge, Gonville and Caius College, MS 357, has been attributed to Tilney, it also survives in an anonymous thirteenth-century copy  (Bodl. Oxf., MS Bodley 20, fols. 113&#150;153<i>v</i>), and so cannot be by him.<br>
<br>Richard Copsey </div>
