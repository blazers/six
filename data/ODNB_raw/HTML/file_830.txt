<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Astry,  Richard</span>  (1630/31&#150;1714), <span class="occ">antiquary</span>, was the son of Thomas Astry of Toseland, a Huntingdon yeoman. Astry was admitted to Queens' College, Cambridge, in March 1648, and matriculated in 1650, graduating BA in 1651 and probably MA in 1654.<br>
<br>He was identified by Thompson Cooper, author of <span class="italic">A New Biographical Dictionary</span> (1873), as responsible for a manuscript volume (now in the British Library) containing a parochial history of Huntingdonshire. It is the work of more than one hand, in which the parishes are listed in alphabetical order together with historical details of each. An eighteenth-century inscription reads: &#145;Some part of this book is in the handwriting of Sir Robert Cotton, the rest is the collection of Mr Astry of Huntingdonshire&#146;. It would seem that this was added after much of the contents was transcribed by Thomas Baker (1656&#150;1740) who made no mention of Astry but noted that the handwriting was &#145;said to be Sir Robert Cotton's. But if it were his, the additions are more than the original MS&#146;  (<span class="italic">Catalogue</span>, ed. Luard, 437). The British Library catalogue describes the work as being &#145;in the handwriting of Sir Robert Cotton with some interleaved additions by Mr Astry&#146;. The basis on which Cooper dismissed Cotton's authorship is unclear.<br>
<br>Cooper also identified Astry as the author of three manuscript volumes containing &#145;Alphabetical Catalogues of English Surnames with the arms belonging to them, and the particular time that the persons recorded lived&#146;, said once to have been in possession of the Revd Henry Freeman of Norman Cross, Huntingdonshire. Astry died an alderman of Huntingdon and was buried at St Mary's Church in that town on 11 August 1714, aged eighty-three.<br>
<br>Piers Wauchope </div>
