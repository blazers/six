<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Fell,  (Walter) William</span>  (1761/2&#150;1848), <span class="occ">writer</span>, was probably born near Brampton, Cumberland. He was a schoolmaster successively at Manchester, Wilmslow, and Lancaster, and was also an industrious writer for the press. His published works included <span class="italic">Hints on the Instruction of Youth</span>, published anonymously in Manchester in 1798; <span class="italic">Hints on the Causes of the High Prices of Provisions</span> (1800); and <span class="italic">A System of Political Economy</span> (1808). He also produced <span class="italic">A Sketch of the Principal Events in English History</span> in 1811; it ran into a second edition in 1813.<br>
<br>After his retirement, Fell lived at Clifton, near Lowther, Westmorland. He died on 27 March 1848, aged eighty-six, at Shap, in the same county, leaving his property to the children of his elder brother, John Fell of Swindale Head, thereby disinheriting his only surviving son, Henry, who was at that time living in Denmark.<br>
<br>C. W. Sutton<span class="italic">, rev. </span> Megan A. Stephan </div>
