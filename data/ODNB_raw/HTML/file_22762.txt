<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Price,  Richard</span>  (1790&#150;1833), <span class="occ">literary scholar</span>, was the eldest son of Richard Price, merchant. He entered at the Middle Temple on 29 May 1823, and went on to practise on the western circuit.<br>
<br>In 1824 Price superintended an edition of Warton's <span class="italic">History of Poetry</span>, and provided a long preface, which was reprinted in the editions of R. Taylor (1840) and W. C. Hazlitt (1871). Price incorporated the notes of Ritson, Ashby, Douce, and Park, besides adding some of his own. The edition proved valuable to later editors, although Price retained many of Warton's mistakes, and made some of his own. In 1830, two years after he was called to the bar, he revised and brought up to date, in four volumes, Edward Christian's edition of Blackstone's <span class="italic">Commentaries</span> of 1809. He also assisted Henry Petrie in his edition of the &#145;Saxon Chronicle to 1066&#146;, in the first volume of <span class="italic">Monumenta historica Britannica</span> (1848).<br>
<br>Price was a sub-commissioner of the Public Record Commission, a body which sponsored the publication of works tending to advance historical and antiquarian knowledge. He had a wide knowledge of German and Scandinavian literature, to which testimony was later borne by Dr James Grimm, Dr J. J. Thorkelin, and Edgar Taylor, translator of Wace's <span class="italic">Chronicle</span>. Thorpe, in the preface to his <span class="italic">Ancient Laws and Institutes of England</span> (published for the Record Commission, 1840), claimed that his labours had been considerably lightened by Price, whom he called &#145;a good man and highly accomplished scholar&#146;  (Warton, 1840). Price died of dropsy on 23 May 1833, at his home at Branch Hill, Hampstead, Middlesex.<br>
<br>G. Le G. Norgate<span class="italic">, rev. </span> P. J. Connell </div>
