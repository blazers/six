<div class="para">
<span class="headword">&AElig;thelberht</span>  (<em>d.</em> 865), <span class="occ">king of the West Saxons</span>, was the third of the five sons of <script>writeSeealsoLink('../view/article/8921/', "&#198;thelwulf (d. 858)")</script>, king of the West Saxons, and his first wife, <script>writeSeealsoLink('../view/article/20887/', "Osburh (fl. 839)")</script>, herself descended from the West Saxon royal line. Shortly before &AElig;thelwulf went to Rome in 855, &AElig;thelberht was appointed sub-king of Kent (really modern Kent with Essex, Surrey, and Sussex), a position he kept until 860. On &AElig;thelwulf's return in 856, the eldest surviving son, <script>writeSeealsoLink('../view/article/8901/', "&#198;thelbald")</script>, was able to rule the western part of Wessex and &AElig;thelwulf himself took the eastern part; &AElig;thelberht was allowed to retain his position in Kent. &AElig;thelwulf seems to have intended &AElig;thelberht to establish a cadet branch of West Saxon kings in Kent. However, when &AElig;thelbald died in 860 the kingship of the West Saxons passed to &AElig;thelberht, presumably because his brothers <script>writeSeealsoLink('../view/article/8913/', "&#198;thelred")</script> and <script>writeSeealsoLink('../view/article/183/', "Alfred")</script> were considered too young to lead a country facing viking attacks (Alfred was only eleven).<br>
<br>It was under &AElig;thelberht that Wessex and its south-eastern extension became a united kingdom. Unlike his predecessors, &AElig;thelberht did not appoint another member of his family as sub-king of Kent. Further, a charter  (<span class="italic">AS chart.</span>, S 327) issued in the first year of &AElig;thelberht's reign reflects an extraordinary new kind of assembly: it was the first charter of a West Saxon king to include a full complement both of West Saxon and of Kentish witnesses.<br>
<br>The <span class="roman">Anglo-Saxon Chronicle</span> describes &AElig;thelberht's reign as one of good harmony and great peace. Although this was true of internal affairs, the vikings remained a threat, unsuccessfully storming Winchester and ravaging all eastern Kent. &AElig;thelberht died in the autumn of 865 and was buried at Sherborne beside his brother &AElig;thelbald.<br>
<br>Sean Miller </div>
