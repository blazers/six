<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Finingham,  Robert</span>  (<em>fl. </em><em>c.</em>1378), <span class="occ">canonist</span>, presumably came from Finingham, Suffolk, and became a Franciscan friar at Norwich. His name and convent are given by Bale, who in his <span class="italic">Index</span> attributes to him a work <span class="italic">De excommunicationibus</span>, found as a single work in a Norwich Cathedral priory manuscript and in several sections under separate headings in a copy in Richard Kele's printing shop. Since the first manuscript is extant, as CUL MS Ee.5.11, and has no contemporary ascription, Bale may have found the title in Kele's text. Two other copies, both without ascriptions, survive. All the manuscripts were written in the late fourteenth and early fifteenth century, and reference to the practice of the Norwich diocese on excommunication, as well as the inclusion of a section on Franciscan privileges, confirm Bale's description in his <span class="italic">Index</span>. Finingham was clearly a trained canonist capable of citing a wide range of authorities, the latest of which are of the early fourteenth century, except for a reference to Pope Gregory &#145;of happy memory&#146;, probably Gregory XI (<i>r</i>. 1370&#150;78). He set out the standard texts and papal decrees relevant to English practice in a handbook possibly most useful for religious orders. Tanner's date, <i>claruit</i> (&#145;flourished&#146;) 1460, is unsupported speculation.<br>
<br>Jeremy Catto </div>
