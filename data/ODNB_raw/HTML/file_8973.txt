<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Evans,  Lewis</span>  (1755&#150;1827), <span class="occ">mathematician</span>, was the son of the Revd Thomas Evans of Basaleg, Monmouthshire. He matriculated at Merton College, Oxford, on 16 December 1774, but left the university without a degree. In 1777 he was ordained by the bishop of Lichfield and Coventry, and was for a year curate at Ashbury, Berkshire. He then served for ten years as residential curate of Compton, Berkshire, during which time he married Ann, the daughter of Thomas Norman. They had at least two sons, <script>writeSeealsoLink('../view/article/8986/', "Thomas Simpson Evans")</script> and <script>writeSeealsoLink('../view/article/8941/', "Arthur Benoni Evans")</script>, and two daughters. In 1788 Evans moved to the vicarage at Froxfield, Wiltshire, and he retained the living until his death. In 1799 he was appointed first mathematical master at the Royal Military Academy, Woolwich, a post he held until 1820.<br>
<br>In later life Evans devoted much of his time to astronomy. He possessed several valuable instruments, notably the fine transit by Edward Troughton, later known as the &#145;Lee circle&#146; and preserved by the Royal Astronomical Society. For many years he employed himself as a skilful and successful observer, and he had his own private observatories at Froxfield (1788&#150;99) and at Woolwich (1799&#150;1820). He contributed several papers to the <span class="italic">Philosophical Magazine</span>. Evans was elected FRS on 29 May 1823, and was an early fellow of the Astronomical Society. He died at the vicarage, Froxfield, on 19 November 1827.<br>
<br>Gordon Goodwin<span class="italic">, rev. </span> Anita McConnell </div>
