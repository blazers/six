<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Pratt,  John Burnett</span>  (1799&#150;1869), <span class="occ">Scottish Episcopal clergyman and antiquary</span>, born at Cairnbanno, New Deer, Aberdeenshire, was the son of a working tradesman. After graduating MA at Aberdeen University he took orders in the Scottish Episcopal church, and obtained a living at Stuartfield in 1821. In 1825 he was elected to St James's Church, Cruden, Aberdeenshire, where he remained until his death. He was also examining chaplain to the bishop of Aberdeen and domestic chaplain to the earl of Erroll. Aberdeen University conferred on him the honorary degree of LLD in 1865. Pratt edited <span class="italic">The Communion Office for the Use of the Church of Scotland</span> (1866), and, in addition to a volume of sermons, published <span class="italic">The Old Paths, where is the Good Way</span> (3rd edn, 1840), <span class="italic">Buchan</span> (1858), which embodied the results of many years of antiquarian and topographical research in the district, and <span class="italic">The Druids</span> (1861); his <span class="italic">Letters on the Scandinavian Churches, their Doctrine, Worship, and Polity</span> (1865) reflected an unusual interest. Pratt died at the parsonage, Cruden, on 20 March 1869.<br>
<br>E. I. Carlyle<span class="italic">, rev. </span> H. C. G. Matthew </div>
