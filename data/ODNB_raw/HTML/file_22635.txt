<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Poulson,  George</span>  (1783&#150;1858), <span class="occ">topographer</span>, produced his first publication, the copiously illustrated <span class="italic">Beverlac, or, The antiquities and history of the town of Beverley &hellip; and the collegiate establishment of St. John's</span>, in 1829, with the assistance of Charles Frost (1781/2&#150;1862). This was followed by his principal work, <span class="italic">The history and antiquities of the seigniory of Holderness, in the East Riding of the county of York, including the abbies of Meaux and Swine, with the priories of Nunkeeling and Burstall</span> (1840&#150;41). This was based on original records and the unpublished manuscripts of the Revd William Dade (1740&#150;1790) in the library of Burton Constable, and it contains valuable, if not always accurately transcribed, material which is now lost. Poulson also edited Henry William Ball's <span class="italic">Social History and Antiquities of Barton-upon-Humber</span> (1856). He died at Barton upon Humber on 12 January 1858.<br>
<br>Thompson Cooper<span class="italic">, rev. </span> William Joseph Sheils </div>
