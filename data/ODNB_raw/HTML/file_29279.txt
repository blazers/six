<div class="para">
<span class="headword">White,  William</span>  (<em>bap. </em>1604, <em>d.</em> 1678), <span class="occ">Church of England clergyman</span>, was baptized on 21 June 1604 at Witney, Oxfordshire, the fifth child of Henry White (<i>d</i>. 1631), who was possibly a fuller. He entered Wadham College, Oxford, matriculating on 13 July 1621, graduated BA on 25 February 1625, and proceeded MA on 25 June 1628. He became master of Magdalen College School in 1632, and vicar of Wargrave, Berkshire, thanks to Archbishop Laud, in 1637. At Magdalen School he taught Greek well and &#145;framed Latine sentences in the belly of each other, as he phrased it&#146;  (Stanier, 103). In 1648 the parliamentary commissioners ejected him, but Bishop Brian Duppa of Salisbury procured him the rectorship of Pusey, Berkshire, which he kept &#145;by the favour of his Friends, and the smallness of its Profits&#146;  (Wood, <span class="italic">Ath. Oxon.</span>, 2.618). In 1662 his former pupil Thomas Pierce, now president of Magdalen, made him also rector of Appleton, Berkshire.<br>
<br>As Guilielmus Phalerius, White wrote a brief teaching manual, <span class="italic">Ad grammaticen ordinarium supplementa</span> (1648), and a tract urging church unity, <span class="italic">Via ad pacem ecclesiasticam</span>, (1660). A third small book, on doctrine <span class="italic">Paraphrasis &hellip; catechismi Anglicani</span> (1674), is ascribed to him by his pupil and biographer William Fulman. White died at Pusey, &#145;a little after midnight before Saturdaie Jun. 1. 1678&#146;  (Fulman); he was buried there on 5 June. His punctiliousness appears in his will, dated 26 October 1677: his daughter and heir Elizabeth Pusey, wife of Robert Pusey, was instructed to give Cornelius Yates, vicar of St Mary's, Marlborough, Wiltshire, and Yates's successors &pound;5 annually so long as they catechized their parishioners and advocated the order of family prayers White specified. The dean of Salisbury was to ratify this. White left the vicars his books and papers on condition that his marginalia be favourably construed and that his diaries, kept since 1628, all be burnt. The Marlborough Vicars' Library, which includes books given to White by Henry Hammond, is now in the Bodleian Library, Oxford. Nothing is known of his wife.<br>
<br>Hugh de Quehen </div>
