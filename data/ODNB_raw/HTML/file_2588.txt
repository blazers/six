<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Blakely,  Johnston</span>  (1781&#150;1814), <span class="occ">naval officer in the United States service</span>, was born in Dublin in October 1781. When he was an infant, his parents emigrated to North Carolina. In 1800 he entered the United States Navy and, when the Anglo-American War broke out in 1812, had attained the rank of master commandant. Commanding the <i>Wasp</i>, a new, large, heavily armed sloop, he sailed from Portsmouth, New Hampshire, on 1 May 1814. He crossed the Atlantic, and in the channel on 28 June fought and captured the much weaker British brig <i>Reindeer</i>, for which congress voted him a gold medal. In September 1814, on another cruise, he captured a merchant vessel and defeated the much weaker British brig <i>Avon</i>. When other British warships approached, he ran. The <i>Wasp</i> sailed south, captured two or three merchantmen, and was last seen in the Atlantic, west of Portugal, on 9 October; her fate was unknown. For one who fought so few actions and only against decisively weaker opponents, Blakely gained an apparently exaggerated reputation among his compatriots.<br>
<br>J. K. Laughton<span class="italic">, rev. </span> Andrew Lambert </div>
