<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Watson,  Peter William</span>  (<em>bap. </em>1761, <em>d.</em> 1830), <span class="occ">botanist</span>, was born at Hull and baptized at Holy Trinity Church there on 26 August 1761. He was educated at the grammar school under Joseph Milner (1744&#150;1797), and occupied in early life in trade; he was an enthusiastic student of botany, entomology, chemistry, and mineralogy, and a skilful landscape painter. In 1812 he took an active part in the establishment of the Hull Botanic Garden. In his <span class="italic">Dendrologia Britannica, or, Trees and shrubs that will live in the open air of Britain throughout the year</span>, published in twenty-four parts in 1824&#150;5, he alludes (pt 1, p. xii) to his<blockquote>own endeavours to furnish the institution with many indigenous plants, which I collected at considerable expense and labour, by traversing the whole East Riding &hellip; in my gig, with proper apparatus for cutting up roots, collecting seeds, &amp;c. of the rarer sorts, whose habitats had been rendered familiar to me from numerous previous herborisations.</blockquote>
<span class="italic">Dendrologia Britannica</span> was described by John Claudius Loudon in his <span class="italic">Arboretum Britannicum</span> of 1833 (p. 188) as &#145;the most scientific work devoted exclusively to trees which has hitherto been published in England&#146;. It contained an introduction to descriptive botany, occupying seventy-two pages, and 172 coloured plates of exotic trees and shrubs, each accompanied by a page of technical description.<br>
<br>Watson was elected a fellow of the Linnean Society in 1824. He died at Cottingham, near Hull, on 1 September 1830.<br>
<br>G. S. Boulger<span class="italic">, rev. </span> Giles Hudson </div>
