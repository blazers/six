<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Pryor,  Alfred Reginald</span>  (1839&#150;1881), <span class="occ">botanist</span>, was born at Hatfield, Hertfordshire, on 24 April 1839, the eldest son of Alfred Pryor, brewer, and Jane Anne (<i>n&eacute;e</i> Pryor). He was educated at Tonbridge School and at University College, Oxford, whence he graduated BA on 26 June 1862. While at Oxford, in 1858, he converted to Catholicism. He was interested in botany by 1873 when the first of his papers appeared in <span class="italic">The Journal of Botany, British and Foreign</span>. In the following year he began work, with R. H. Webb, on a supplement to Coleman's <span class="italic">Flora Hertfordiensis</span> (1849), but this soon gave way to a plan for a new county flora. He worked on this for several years, before being compelled by bad health to winter abroad in 1879&#150;80. His health never really recovered and he died, unmarried, at Baldock, Hertfordshire, on 18 February 1881. He left his herbarium, books, and manuscript flora to the Hertfordshire Natural History Society, with a small sum of money to enable the society to print the manuscript, which appeared as <span class="italic">A Flora of Hertfordshire, edited &hellip; by B. Daydon Jackson, with an introduction &hellip; by John Hopkinson and the editor</span>, in 1887. However, the residue of his estate was to go (after supporting his mother until her death) to Cardinal Manning.<br>
<br>B. D. Jackson<span class="italic">, rev. </span> Peter Osborne </div>
