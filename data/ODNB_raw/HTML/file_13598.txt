<span class="headword">Holmes,  John Beck</span> <span class="st"> [<em>formerly </em>Johanes Holm]</span>  (1767&#150;1843), <span class="occ">Moravian bishop</span>, was born at Copenhagen on 3 November 1767. In 1780 he was sent to the academy at Nisky in Silesia, and subsequently attended the Moravian Academy at Barby in Wittenberg. In 1791 he was appointed a teacher at the Seminary of the United Brethren, Fulneck, near Leeds, where he remained until 1799, also serving as minister to the single men of the community. He then became minister of one of the small churches of the district at Wyke, and about this time he married. In 1812 he was appointed minister of the congregation at Dublin, and then on 18 August 1825 he was ordained a bishop of the church at Herrnhut in Saxony, being stationed at Fulneck and serving there as minister to the Moravian church's most significant congregation in the north of England. He presided at the 1835 British provincial synod of the church. While he was in Dublin he studied the history of the Moravians, and several of his works, including <span class="italic">Historical sketches of the missions of the United Brethren for propagating the gospel among the heathen</span> (1818) and <span class="italic">History of the Protestant Church of the United Brethren</span> (2 vols., 1825&#150;30), became standard accounts. He died at Fulneck on 3 September 1843, and was buried there.<br>
<br>Peter J. Lineham </div>
