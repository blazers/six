<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Cooke,  John</span>  (1738&#150;1823), <span class="occ">Church of England clergyman</span>, was educated at Trinity College, Cambridge (BA 1761, MA 1764), and was presented to the rectory of Denton, Buckinghamshire, on 2 August 1773. He was also chaplain to Greenwich Hospital, and a commissioner from 1773 until his death on 4 May 1823 at Greenwich Hospital.<br>
<br>Cooke published a number of works, including <span class="italic">An Historical Account of the Royal Hospital for Seamen at Greenwich</span> (1789). The sermon he preached at the opening of the chapel of the hospital on 20 September 1789, on the suitable theme of the preservation of St Paul from shipwreck on the island of Melita, was also published. In 1799 he wrote an account of the voyage of John, earl of Sandwich, round the Mediterranean (together with a memoir of the earl's life). He was survived by his wife, whose name is not known.<br>
<br>J. M. Rigg<span class="italic">, rev. </span> Robert Brown </div>
