<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Collins,  Charles James</span>  (1820&#150;1864), <span class="occ">writer</span>, was connected with the London press for more than twenty years, during which time he was on the parliamentary staff of <span class="italic">The Sun</span>, <span class="italic">Daily Telegraph</span>, and <span class="italic">The Standard</span>. He edited the <span class="italic">Comic News</span> from 1847 and helped to found, and edited (from 1861), the <span class="italic">Racing Times</span>. He died at his home, 9 Manor Terrace, Brixton, on 31 December 1864, leaving a widow, Phoebe.<br>
<br>Collins was the author of <span class="italic">Kenilworth</span>, a burlesque, and other dramas of a similar character, and of novels such as <span class="italic">Dick Diminy, or, The Life and Adventures of a Jockey</span> (1855; 2nd edn, 1875), <span class="italic">Sackville Chase</span> (3 vols., 1863&#150;5), <span class="italic">Matilda the Dane: a Romance of the Affections</span> (1863), <span class="italic">Singed Moths: a City Romance</span> (3 vols., 1864), and <span class="italic">The Man in Chains</span> (3 vols., 1864).<br>
<br>Thompson Cooper<span class="italic">, rev. </span> Julian Lock </div>
