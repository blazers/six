<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Dodd,  Daniel</span>  (<em>fl. </em>1752&#150;<em>c.</em>1780), <span class="occ">painter</span>, principally of portraits and fashionable scenes, also produced subject and flower paintings. He is first mentioned in the <span class="italic">Daily Advertiser</span> of 28 November 1752, as having etched a portrait of Mr Leveridge in the style of Rembrandt.<br>
<br>Dodd was a member of the Free Society of Artists, first exhibiting there in 1761. He continued to contribute many works to the same exhibition up to 1780 from various London addresses. His works were mainly portraits in crayon or oils on a small scale. Among them may be mentioned a portrait in crayon of <span class="italic">Garrick between Tragedy and Comedy</span>, portraits of <span class="italic">Mr. Darley</span>, <span class="italic">Mr. Fielding</span>, and of Buckhorse, the pugilist, &#145;the notorious bruiser&#146;  (&#145;Anstey, Christopher&#146;, <span class="italic">DNB</span>), of whom he exhibited both a crayon and a mezzotint portrait in 1769.<br>
<br>Dodd was also known for his large scenes of fashionable life, crowded with figures, such as <span class="italic">A View of the Ball at St. James's on Her Majesty's Birthnight</span> (engraved by Tukey), <span class="italic">A View of the Exhibition of the Royal Academy at Somerset House</span> (engraved by William Angus), and <span class="italic">The Royal Procession to St. Paul's</span>, among others. He also provided grey wash illustrations for the <span class="roman">Bible</span>, the <span class="italic">Novelist's Magazine</span>, published by J. Harrison &amp; Co., G. F. Raymond's <span class="italic">A new, universal and impartial history of England &hellip; embellished with upwards of 120 beautiful copper-plate engravings, taken from the original drawings of Messrs. Metz, Stothard, and Samuel Wale esq.</span> (<i>c</i>.1786), and other similar publications.<br>
<br>A pastel portrait by Dodd of Sir Watkin Lewes is in the National Portrait Gallery, London. The Victoria and Albert Museum possesses a wash drawing of Joseph George Holman, the actor. D. P. Dodd and Miss Dodd, who both exhibited with the Free Society of Artists, were his son and daughter. A &#145;Master Smart&#146; exhibited as his pupil at the Free Society of Artists in 1780 and the miniaturist Thomas Day exhibited from &#145;Mr Dodds, Great Portland St&#146; from 1768 to 1771  (Foskett, <span class="italic">Miniatures</span>, 525). Both are believed to have been his pupils.<br>
<br>L. H. Cust<span class="italic">, rev. </span> Jill Springall </div>
