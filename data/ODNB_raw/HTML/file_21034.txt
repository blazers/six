<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Owen,  Thomas</span>  (1749&#150;1812), <span class="occ">Church of England clergyman and translator</span>, was born in Anglesey, the son of Thomas Owen. He matriculated from Jesus College, Oxford, on 20 March 1767, graduating BA in 1770, and proceeding MA from Queen's College in 1773. In 1779 he was presented to the living of Upton Scudamore, Wiltshire. Owen's translations included <span class="italic">The Three Books of M. Terentius Varro Concerning Agriculture</span> (1800), <span class="italic"><i>Ge&#333;ponika</i>, Agricultural Pursuits</span> (2 vols., 1805&#150;6), and a translation of <span class="italic">The Fourteen Books of Palladius on Agriculture</span> (1807). Owen died in Anglesey in May 1812.<br>
<br>A. F. Pollard<span class="italic">, rev. </span> Anne Pimlott Baker </div>
