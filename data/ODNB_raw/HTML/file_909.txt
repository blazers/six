<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Austin,  John</span>  (1717&#150;1784), <span class="occ">Jesuit</span>, was born in Dublin on 12 April 1717. Said to be a prot&eacute;g&eacute; of Jonathan Swift, he entered the Society of Jesus in Champagne on 27 November 1735. After completing the higher studies he was for several years employed in teaching humanities and held the office of prefect of the Irish College at Poitiers. In 1750 he returned to Dublin, where he obtained renown as a preacher. He was professed of the four vows on 2 February 1754. Topham Bowden, an English protestant, remarked in his <span class="italic">Tour through Ireland</span> (1791): &#145;Austin was a very remarkable character, of extraordinary learning and piety; he was a great preacher, and injured his health by his exertions in the pulpit&#146;. He opened a seminary in Saul's Court, Dublin, where many distinguished men were educated. A collection of his <span class="italic">Devotions in the Ancient Way of Offices</span> was published in Edinburgh in 1789.<br>
<br>Austin died in Dublin on 29 September 1784 and was buried in St Kevin's churchyard. The inscription over his grave described him as &#145;pius, doctus, indefessus operarius, apostolicis confectus laboribus. Divites admonuit, pauperes sublevavit, juventutem erudivit, orphanis loco parentis fuit, de omni hominum genere pr&aelig;clare meruit, omnibus omnia factus ut omnes Christo lucrifaceret&#146;.<br>
<br>Thompson Cooper<span class="italic">, rev. </span> Robert Brown </div>
