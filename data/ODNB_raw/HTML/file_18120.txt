<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Marshal,  Ebenezer</span>  (<em>d.</em> 1813), <span class="occ">historian and Church of Scotland minister</span>, was licensed as a preacher by the presbytery of Edinburgh on 30 October 1776, and ordained on 3 April 1782 as chaplain to the Scottish regiment in the Dutch service. On 22 November 1782 he was presented to the living of Cockpen in the presbytery of Dalkeith, Edinburghshire. On 29 December 1784 he married Christian Goodsman (<i>d</i>. 1824); they had two children, Archibald, who later became an accountant in Edinburgh, and Susan Gloag.<br>
<br>In later years Marshal published <span class="italic">The History of the Union of Scotland and England</span> and <span class="italic">An Abridgment of the Acts of Parliament Relating to the Church of Scotland</span> (both 1799), followed by <span class="italic">A Treatise on the British Constitution</span> (1812). He also contributed an account of the parish of Cockpen to the first edition of Sir John Sinclair's <span class="italic">Statistical Account of Scotland</span> (1791&#150;9). Marshal died at Cockpen on 19 May 1813 and was survived by his wife, who died on 13 August 1824.<br>
<br>Gordon Goodwin<span class="italic">, rev. </span> Philip Carter </div>
