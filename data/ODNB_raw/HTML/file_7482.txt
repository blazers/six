<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Denham,  Michael Aislabie</span>  (1800/01&#150;1859), <span class="occ">collector of folklore</span>, was a native of Bowes, Yorkshire, who worked at Hull in the early part of his life and ultimately settled as a general merchant at Piercebridge, near Gainford, co. Durham, where he was a member of the Society of Friends.<br>
<br>From 1825 Denham industriously collected local proverbial lore, beginning with <span class="italic">A collection of proverbs and popular sayings relating to the seasons, the weather, and agricultural pursuits, gathered chiefly from oral tradition</span> (1846), printed by the Percy Society. Other folklore compilations included <span class="italic">Cumberland Rhymes, Proverbs, and Sayings</span> (published in four parts, the last in 1854); <span class="italic">Folklore of the North</span> (six parts, the last in 1856), a similar work relating to Westmorland (two parts, 1858); and <span class="italic">Folklore, or, A collection of local rhymes, proverbs, sayings, prophecies, slogans, &amp;c., relating to Northumberland, Newcastle-on-Tyne, and Berwick-on-Tweed</span> (1858). From 1849 to about 1854 twenty <span class="italic">Minor Tracts on Folklore</span> were published; a distinctive volume was <span class="italic">The Slogans, and War and Gathering Cries of the North of England</span> (1850, 1851). Denham listed his works in <span class="italic">A Classified Catalogue of the Antiquarian Tomes, Tracts, and Trifles</span> (1859). He died at Piercebridge on 10 September 1859, aged fifty-eight.<br>
<br>Thompson Cooper<span class="italic">, rev. </span> John D. Haigh </div>
