<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Barclay,  Hugh</span>  (1799&#150;1884), <span class="occ">judge</span>, was born on 18 January 1799 in the Gorbals, Glasgow, son of John Barclay, merchant, and Jean McKinlay. After serving his apprenticeship as a law agent he was admitted a member of the Faculty of Advocates in 1821. In 1829 he was appointed sheriff-substitute of Dunblane and in 1833 sheriff-substitute of Perthshire. He wrote <span class="italic">A digest of the law of Scotland, with special reference to the office and duties of the justice of the peace</span> (2 vols.; 2nd edn, 1852&#150;3), a work which passed into several editions and proved very useful to legal practitioners. Besides editions of various other legal works, he also published on a range of miscellaneous legal matters, from the law of highways to the Scots law against profaning the sabbath. He was a frequent contributor to the <span class="italic">Journal of Jurisprudence</span> and other legal periodicals, and various of his papers, including &#145;Curiosities of the game laws&#146; and &#145;Curiosities of legislation&#146;, were also published by him in a collected form.<br>
<br>Barclay was married to Margaret Buchanan, and they had at least one son and one daughter. For many years he was a prominent member of the general assembly of the Church of Scotland, and, taking an active interest in ecclesiastical and philanthropic matters, he published <span class="italic">Thoughts on Sabbath Schools</span> (1855) and a few other small works of a similar kind. Barclay died at his residence at Early Bank, Craigie, near Perth, on 1 February 1884, having for several years been the oldest judge in Scotland. He was survived by his wife.<br>
<br>T. F. Henderson<span class="italic">, rev. </span> Eric Metcalfe </div>
