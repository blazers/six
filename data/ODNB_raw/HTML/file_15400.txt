<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Kenneth III</span> <span class="st"> [Cin&aacute;ed mac Duib]</span>  (<em>d.</em> 1005), <span class="occ">king in Scotland</span>, was the son of <script>writeSeealsoLink('../view/article/8166/', "King Dubh (d. 966)")</script>. When he killed <script>writeSeealsoLink('../view/article/6116/', "Constantine III")</script> [<i>see under </i><script>writeSeealsoLink('../view/article/6870/', "Culen")</script>] in 997 and became king, his victory represented the final demise of the rival branch of the royal dynasty, the descendants of King Aed (<i>d</i>. 878). After Kenneth's triumph competition for the kingship narrowed to rival descendants of Malcolm I. In 1005 Kenneth III was killed in battle at Monzievaird (Perthshire) by his cousin <script>writeSeealsoLink('../view/article/17858/', "Malcolm II (d. 1034)")</script>, who succeeded him. A late (and debatable) source claims that he was buried on Iona. Boite mac Cinaeda, whose son or grandson was killed by Malcolm II, may have been Kenneth III's son, but was more probably the son of Kenneth II. Kenneth III's supposed son Giric was apparently the product of a copyist's error, but Kenneth probably did have a son or sons whose descendants became the <i>clann</i> Duib (later Macduff).<br>
<br>Dauvit Broun </div>
