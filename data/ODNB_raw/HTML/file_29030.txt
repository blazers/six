<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Welsted,  Robert</span>  (1670/71&#150;1735), <span class="occ">physician</span>, was the son of Leonard Welsted of Bristol. He matriculated from St Edmund Hall, Oxford, on 4 December 1687, aged sixteen, and was elected in 1689 to a demyship at Magdalen College, which he held until 1698, graduating BA on 25 June 1691, and MA on 12 May 1694. He was admitted an extra-licentiate of the Royal College of Physicians on 11 December 1695. <br>
<br>Welsted was then practising medicine at Bristol, where he remained for some years, but eventually moved to London, and was admitted a licentiate of the college on 3 September 1710. Welsted was one of &#145;the oldest and heartiest&#146; friends of Bishop Hugh Boulter, primate of Ireland, who described him as a true Hanoverian and supporter of the revolution, and thought that because of this &#145;he has been distressed in his business by the disaffected&#146;  (<span class="italic">Letters</span>, 1.94). He was the author of a number of philosophical works and was admitted a fellow of the Royal Society on 20 March 1718. With Richard West (1671&#150;1716), the father of Gilbert West (1703&#150;1756), Welsted edited the works of Pindar with notes and a paraphrase (1692). His practice was not successful in his later years and he was obliged to rely on the generosity of Bishop Boulter, who allowed him &pound;200 a year and maintained his son at Hart Hall, Oxford  (<span class="italic">Letters</span>, 1.94). Welsted died at Tavistock Street, London, on 1 February 1735.<br>
<br>E. I. Carlyle<span class="italic">, rev. </span> Patrick Wallis </div>
