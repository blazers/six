<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Singleton,  Robert Corbet</span>  (1810&#150;1881), <span class="occ">hymn writer</span>, was born on 9 October 1810, the second son of Francis Corbet of Aclare, co. Meath. His father added Singleton to his name in 1820. After attending a series of schools in Dublin, Robert entered Trinity College, where he graduated BA in 1830 and MA in 1833. After his ordination he was appointed first warden of St Columba's College, Rathfarnham, near Dublin, which was opened in 1843. He then proceeded to St Peter's College, Radley, of which he was the first warden (1847&#150;51), and there inaugurated for the boys a rigorous system of religious discipline. While at Radley he published <span class="italic">The Psalter Arranged for Chanting</span> (1846) and discourses entitled <span class="italic">Uncleanness, the Ruin of Body and Soul</span> (1850). In 1847 he was admitted <i>ad eundem</i> to Trinity College, Oxford. In 1868 he edited, in conjunction with Dr E. G. Monk, <span class="italic">The Anglican Hymn-Book</span>, which contained nearly thirty original hymns by him, most notably &#145;With gladsome feet we press&#146;, as well as numerous translations from the German and Latin. He died at Petergate, York, on 7 February 1881, and was buried on the 12th in the Corbet vault, St Patrick's Cathedral, Dublin.<br>
<br>D. J. O'Donoghue<span class="italic">, rev. </span> Leon Litvack </div>
