<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Main,  Thomas John</span>  (1818&#150;1885), <span class="occ">mathematician</span>, was the son of Thomas and Elizabeth Main and a younger brother of the <script>writeSeealsoLink('../view/article/17806/', "Revd Robert Main")</script>. He entered St John's College, Cambridge, on 14 February 1834, graduating in 1838 as senior wrangler and first Smith's prizeman. Elected a fellow of his college that year, he proceeded MA in 1841. He joined the Royal Astronomical Society on 10 January 1840. He took orders and was appointed as a chaplain in the Royal Navy in 1842, serving on HMS <i>Excellent</i>, a ship moored permanently in Portsmouth harbour, which was used as a school for instruction in gunnery for officers and men. He was placed on the retired list in 1869. He also served as professor of mathematics for thirty-four years at the Royal Naval College, Portsmouth. He wrote, with Thomas Brown RN, <span class="italic">The Indicator and Dynamometer</span> (1847; 3rd edn, 1857), <span class="italic">The Marine Steam Engine</span> (1849; 5th edn, 1865), which was translated into German in 1868, and <span class="italic">Questions on Subjects Connected with the Marine Steam Engine</span> (1857 and 1863). He married, on 30 June 1842, Emma Louisa Berry, the daughter of Sir John Theophilus Lee of Mount Radford. He died at his home, 15 Elsworthy Road, Hampstead, on 28 December 1885, aged sixty-seven. At least two sons survived him.<br>
<br>A. M. Clerke<span class="italic">, rev. </span> Adrian Rice </div>
