<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Sacleux,  Charles Joseph</span>  (1856&#150;1943), <span class="occ">Swahili scholar and missionary</span>, was born on 5 July 1856 at Enquin, Pas-de-Calais, France, the second of three children, all sons, of Auguste Sacleux, a tobacconist, and his wife Marie Firmine Bayart. His father died when he was five and it was his widowed mother who had the responsibility of bringing up the family. In 1869 he entered the junior seminary at Arras, and in 1874 he spent a year in the senior seminary. The following year he joined the Holy Ghost Fathers and was ordained priest in 1878 and made his profession in 1879, when he left for Zanzibar where his community had their east African headquarters. In due course he was posted to Bagamoyo, then an important coastal town. It was there that he began to study Swahili, the language of Swahililand, already the lingua franca for much of the east African interior and soon the chief local language of British administration. Altogether he was to spend nearly twenty years on the east African coast, devoting a great deal of his time to the Swahili language and to botany.<br>
<br>As a savant of exceptional ability (he was elected to membership of the Soci&eacute;t&eacute; Linguistique de Paris in 1894) Sacleux was fortunate to have studied Swahili before it was influenced by standardization and, later, by English language patterns. Two of his publications are outstanding: his <span class="italic">Grammaire des dialectes swahilis</span> (1909), still the best one-volume study of the Swahili dialects, and his <i>chef-d'&#339;uvre</i>, <span class="italic">Dictionnaire swahili-fran&ccedil;ais</span> (1939), which immediately became, and is likely to remain, the definitive Swahili lexicon.<br>
<br>Sacleux returned to France in 1898, and for the next forty years taught in the community's mother house at Chevilly. Just before the German occupation of Paris he was (with others) evacuated to Cellule and thence to Grasse, on the C&ocirc;te d'Azur, where he died on 16 May 1943 and was buried.<br>
<br>P. J. L. Frankl </div>
