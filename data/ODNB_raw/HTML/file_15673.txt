<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Kirkman,  Jacob</span>  (<em>d.</em> 1812?), <span class="occ">composer</span>, was a nephew of the <script>writeSeealsoLink('../view/article/55529/', "Jacob Kirkman")</script> who had a harpsichord-making business in Broad Street, Golden Square, London, about 1770. The family was of Alsatian origin. The younger Kirkman built up a reputation in London before the end of the eighteenth century as a pianist and composer of works for the piano. He may have been the Jacob Kirkman who was organist of St George's Church, Hanover Square, and he may also have been the Jacob Kirkman who died in Upper Guilford Street on 29 April 1812, at the age of sixty-seven.<br>
<br>Kirkman's published works included piano duets, sonatas for harpsichord and violin, eight ballads dedicated to the marchioness of Salisbury, and a collection of six voluntaries for the organ, harpsichord, or piano. With John Keeble he published <span class="italic">Forty Interludes to be Played between the Verses of the Psalms</span>.<br>
<br>R. H. Legge<span class="italic">, rev. </span> Anne Pimlott Baker </div>
