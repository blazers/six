<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Banyer,  Henry</span>  (1690&#150;1749), <span class="occ">physician</span>, was the nephew of Lawrence Banyer (<i>d</i>. 1720), apothecary of Wisbech, Cambridgeshire. He studied at St Thomas's Hospital, and practised as a surgeon and then as a physician (extra-licentiate of the Royal College of Physicians, 1736) at Wisbech. He translated with additions Joannes van Horne's <span class="italic">Micro-techne, or, A Methodical Introduction to the Art of Chirurgery</span> (1717), and wrote a <span class="italic">Pharmacopoeia</span> (4 edns, 1721&#150;39). He was married and appears to have been prosperous. Banyer died in Wisbech in 1749: he was survived by his wife, who died in 1759.<br>
<br>[Anon.]<span class="italic">, rev. </span> Jean Loudon </div>
