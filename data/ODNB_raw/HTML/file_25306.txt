<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Sheldon,  Ralph</span>  (1623&#150;1684), <span class="occ">antiquary</span>, was born on 1 August 1623 at Beoley, Worcestershire, the eldest son of William Sheldon (1589&#150;1659) of Beoley and Weston in Long Compton, Warwickshire, and Elizabeth (1592&#150;1656), daughter of William, second Lord Petre. He was a nephew of Edward Sheldon the translator. The Sheldons were one of the wealthiest gentry families in the region, but their access to public life was limited by their Catholicism. Sheldon left England for France and Italy in 1642, returning shortly before his marriage in 1647 to Lady Henrietta Maria, daughter of John Savage, second Earl Rivers (<i>c</i>.1603&#150;1654). In the civil war the house at Beoley was apparently burnt to prevent it falling to the parliamentarian forces and the estate was sequestrated. Following the Restoration Sheldon was nominated as a member of the contemplated order of the Royal Oak for his family's devotion to the royalist cause.<br>
<br>After his wife's death, childless, in 1663 Sheldon devoted himself to the study of genealogy, heraldry, and antiquities, drawing up a &#145;Catalogue of the nobility of England since the Norman Conquest&#146; (CUL, Add. 8138). He created a fine library at Weston, catalogued by Anthony Wood; he also had a cabinet of curiosities. In 1667 he again travelled to Rome, where over three years he extended his collection. Described by Wood as &#145;a munificent favourer of learning and learned men&#146;  (Hamper, 434&#150;5), he granted a pension to the antiquary John Vincent, from whom he purchased an important collection of manuscripts belonging to his father, the herald Augustine Vincent. On his own death he bequeathed this collection to the College of Arms. Despite his Catholicism, which led to his arrest in 1678, Sheldon was apparently popular locally and known as &#145;Great Sheldon&#146;  (Nash, 1.68). He died at Weston on 24 June 1684; 500 people viewed the body before its burial at the family chapel at Beoley.<br>
<br>Jan Broadway </div>
