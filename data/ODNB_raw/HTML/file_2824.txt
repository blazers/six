<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Bond,  Henry John Hayles</span>  (1801&#150;1883), <span class="occ">physician</span>, younger son of the Revd William Bond (1745&#150;1832), fellow of Gonville and Caius College, Cambridge, and rector of Wheatacre, Norfolk, and his wife, Martha Hayles, was born in Wheatacre on 22 December 1801. He was educated at Norwich grammar school under Edward Valpy. He studied medicine at Cambridge, London, Edinburgh, and Paris, and graduated MB at Corpus Christi, Cambridge, in 1825, and MD in 1831.<br>
<br>By 1831 Bond had settled in Cambridge, where he soon established a large practice. On 11 December 1834 he married Mary, daughter of William Carpenter, of Toft Marks, Norfolk, and niece of Sir Edward Berry, bt.; they had a large family. In 1835 he was elected a fellow of the Royal College of Physicians, and in 1851 he was appointed regius professor of physic at Cambridge in succession to John Haviland. From 1858 to 1863 he was a member of the General Medical Council. He resigned his professorship in 1872, having largely retired from practice some time before.<br>
<br>Bond published nothing except an excellent syllabus of his lectures, but his tenure of office was contemporary with a great rise in the reputation of the medical school at Cambridge. He was a man of great integrity and ability, but was shy and retiring, and an enemy of ostentation. He died at his home, 4 Regent Street, Cambridge, on 3 September 1883, and was buried in Cambridge cemetery. He was survived by his wife.<br>
<br>E. S. Shuckburgh<span class="italic">, rev. </span> Patrick Wallis </div>
