<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Ashe,  Robert  Hoadly-</span>  (1750/51&#150;1826), <span class="occ">Church of England clergyman</span>, was born Robert Ashe, the son of Robert Ashe, a prebendary of Winchester Cathedral. He was educated at Pembroke College, Oxford, where he graduated BA in 1773. Ashe proceeded MA in 1793, and BD and DD in 1794. He was ordained in 1773, and under the patronage of Philip Yorke, second earl of Hardwicke, became a curate in Dunstable, Bedfordshire. Hardwicke knew of him through the poet <script>writeSeealsoLink('../view/article/13377/', "John Hoadly (1711&#150;1776)")</script>, who had married Ashe's aunt Elizabeth. From 1775 to 1826 Ashe held the living of Crewkerne, Somerset, and also served as master of the grammar school there. In 1786 Ashe edited <span class="italic">Poetical Translations from Various Authors</span> by &#145;Master John Browne of Crewkerne, a boy of twelve years old&#146;. Ashe took the additional surname of Hoadly in 1793 upon inheriting the Hoadly estate from his aunt. In 1799 he published a letter to the Roman Catholic priest John Milner which sought to vindicate Bishop Benjamin Hoadly, father of John Hoadly, from &#145;false and illiberal aspersions&#146;  (<span class="italic">GM</span>, 181) included in Milner's history of Winchester. He died on 3 May 1826.<br>
<br>[Anon.]<span class="italic">, rev. </span> Robert Brown </div>
