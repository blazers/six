<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Ashburne,  Thomas</span>  (<em>fl. </em>1384), <span class="occ">Carmelite friar</span>, was a member of the order in Northampton in 1384 when he wrote a short poem in English &#145;On Despising the World&#146;, which was given the Latin title <span class="italic">De contemptu mundi</span>. This composition was in three parts, and described the situation of the world of his day, its twelve defects, the signs in the heavens, and the lack of religious devotion among rulers and their subjects. The poem existed in the Cotton Library, MS Vitellius F.xiii.1, but was unfortunately lost in the fire of 1731. Ashburne is probably to be identified with the Carmelite from Stamford of the same name who was licensed to hear confessions in the diocese of Lincoln on 11 February 1350. Nothing further is known about his life, and the attribution of a version of <span class="italic">The Prick of Conscience</span> found in another Cotton manuscript, MS App.vii.1, is generally rejected.<br>
<br>Richard Copsey </div>
