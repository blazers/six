<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Thomson,  Alexander</span>  (1763&#150;1803), <span class="occ">poet</span>, was born on 7 August 1763. He resided in Edinburgh and was a close friend of Robert Anderson (1750&#150;1830). Thomson wrote <span class="italic">The Choice</span> (1788), and (his two best-known works) <span class="italic">Whist</span> (1791) and <span class="italic">An Essay on Novels</span> (1793). His other publications included <span class="italic">The Paradise of Taste</span> (1796) and <span class="italic">The British Parnassus at the Close of the Eighteenth Century</span> (1801). He also published <span class="italic">The German Miscellany</span> (1796), consisting of translations from Kotzebue and Meissner, and translated Kotzebue's comedy, <span class="italic">The East Indian</span> (1799). Anderson was with Thomson at his house in Buccleuch Street, Edinburgh, when he suffered a stroke from which he died on 7 November 1803, leaving a widow and six daughters. At the time of his death he had been working on a history of Scottish poetry which remained unpublished.<br>
<br>E. I. Carlyle<span class="italic">, rev. </span> Sarah Couper </div>
