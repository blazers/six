<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Lord,  Thomas</span>  (<em>fl. </em>1791&#150;1796), <span class="occ">ornithologist</span>, was a prot&eacute;g&eacute; of the painter the Revd Matthew William Peters RA (1742&#150;1814), and under his &#145;inspection and patronage&#146; published at London, in folio numbers, from 1791 until 1796, a work entitled <span class="italic">Lord's Entire New System of Ornithology, or, Oecumenical History of British Birds</span>. This consisted of 114 plates painted and engraved by Lord himself, with a brief descriptive text revised by Dr Dupree, master of Berkhamsted grammar school. The figures were mostly life size, and the book is now very rare. In October 1796 Lord was living at 6 Lambeth Road, near the Obelisk in London.<br>
<br>Gordon Goodwin<span class="italic">, rev. </span> Yolanda Foote </div>
