<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Bisset, Sir  John</span>  (1774&#150;1854), <span class="occ">commissariat officer</span>, was born in Perth. He served in the commissariat (the civil department, which provided the army with transport, food, and forage, and kept the stores) in Britain from 1795 to 1800, in Germany from May 1800 to June 1802, in Britain again from 1802 to 1806, in South America in 1806&#150;7, and at the Scheldt in 1809. He was commissary-general from 31 July 1811 until 1819, when he was placed on half pay. He had charge of the commissariat of Wellington's army at a crucial period of the Peninsular War, before and after the battle of Salamanca. Bisset, who was made a knight bachelor and knight commander of the Royal Guelphic Order in 1832 and KCB in August 1850, was granted a pension of &pound;550. He published <span class="italic">Memoranda Regarding the Duties of the Commissariat on Field Service Abroad</span> (1846). He died at Perth, where he had resided for many years, on 3 April 1854.<br>
<br>H. M. Chichester<span class="italic">, rev. </span> David Gates </div>
