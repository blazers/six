<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Cobbold,  John Spencer</span>  (1768&#150;1837), <span class="occ">Church of England clergyman</span>, son of the Revd Thomas Cobbold (1742&#150;1831) and his wife, Anne, <i>n&eacute;e</i> Rust, was born at Occold, Suffolk, on 24 July 1768. He attended school in Ipswich and matriculated from Caius College, Cambridge, in 1786. He graduated BA as seventh wrangler in 1790, MA in 1793, and was a fellow of Caius, 1790&#150;98. He taught at the Perse School, Cambridge, 1793&#150;4, and at Nuneaton School from 1794. He was curate at Wilby, Suffolk, to his father, and followed him in 1805 to Woolpit, also as curate; on his father's death he became rector of Woolpit, where he spent the remainder of his life. He also held the vicarage of Shelland, Suffolk, to which he was instituted in 1793. In December 1798 he married Dorothy, daughter of <script>writeSeealsoLink('../view/article/13651/', "Henry Homer")</script>, rector of Birdingbury. Cobbold published several sermons, two essays&#151;on immortality (1793) and on revelation (1797)&#151;and discourses on predestination (1801), which reject both predestination and religious enthusiasm. He died at Woolpit, Suffolk, on 3 April 1837.<br>
<br>H. C. G. Matthew </div>
