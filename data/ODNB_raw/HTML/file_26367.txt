<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Tours <span class="st"> [Mar&ccedil;ay]</span>,  &Eacute;tienne  de</span>  (<em>d.</em> 1193), <span class="occ">administrator</span>, was a member of the lesser baronage of the south Touraine. He appears as a royal chamberlain in the pipe roll of 1158, and was keeper of the king's treasure at Chinon. He became seneschal of Anjou in 1163, and still held that post on 12 June 1189, when he fired Le Mans to defend it from Philip Augustus. Richard I, on his accession, imprisoned &Eacute;tienne and compelled him to surrender the royal treasure in his charge. Richard of Devizes, who calls him &Eacute;tienne de Mar&ccedil;ay, says that he was imprisoned at Winchester, and had to pay a heavy fine for his release  (<span class="italic">Chronicon Richardi Divisensis</span>, 4&#150;5). William of Newburgh relates that he had been raised from a humble position by Henry II, and was after his release kept in authority by Richard I. Believing that Richard would never return from the crusades, and relying on the prophecy of a wizard, &Eacute;tienne exercised his power in an arbitrary fashion. The wizard foretold that he would die &#145;in pluma&#146;, and he duly met his death at a fortress of that name in 1193, shortly before Richard's return. &Eacute;tienne has sometimes been identified wrongly with <script>writeSeealsoLink('../view/article/27885/', "Stephen of Thornham (d. 1213/14)")</script>.<br>
<br>C. L. Kingsford<span class="italic">, rev. </span> Thomas K. Keefe </div>
