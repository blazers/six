<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Maunder,  Samuel</span>  (1785&#150;1849), <span class="occ">compiler of reference works</span>, belonged to a Devon family settled near Barnstaple. His sister Ann married <script>writeSeealsoLink('../view/article/22304/', "William Pinnock")</script>, well known in his time as the promoter of the educational <span class="italic">Catechisms</span>, published in eighty-three parts between 1837 and 1849. Maunder took part in their preparation, although Pinnock's name alone appears on their title-page. The two were also partners in a publishing business in London, and published for two or three years the <span class="italic">Literary Gazette</span>. Pinnock's rash business speculations led to his financial ruin, and Maunder, under his own name, then compiled and issued numerous dictionaries and compact encyclopaedias, chiefly for educational purposes. They were very popular, had large sales, and were frequently revised and reprinted. William Jerdan, who knew Maunder, wrote of &#145;his indefatigable, painstaking and diligent research, his absence of ambition and contentment with accuracy and solidity of information &hellip; an honourable and worthy man&#146;  (Jerdan, 343).<br>
<br>Maunder's first book was <span class="italic">The Little Lexicon, or, Multum in parvo of the English Language</span> (1825). His next book, <span class="italic">The Treasury of Knowledge and Library of Reference</span> (1830), sold over 200,000 copies. <span class="italic">The Biographical Treasury</span> (1838) was frequently revised and reprinted and was brought up to date by W. L. R. Cates (1873, 1882). An interesting feature, used elsewhere, is the snippets of information and moral maxims printed in the four margins of each page. Other <span class="roman">Treasuries</span> were devoted to belles lettres (1841), history (1844), natural history (1848), and geography (1856 and 1860).<br>
<br>Maunder also prepared a school edition of R. Montgomery's <span class="italic">Omnipresence of the Deity</span>, a revised edition of Shakespeare's plays (1851), and one of <span class="italic">Geography and History</span> by E. R. (22nd edn, 1859).<br>
<br>Maunder died at his home, 67 Gibson Square, Islington, on 30 April 1849.<br>
<br>G. Le G. Norgate<span class="italic">, rev. </span> John D. Haigh </div>
