<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Babion <span class="st"> [Babyon]</span>,  Peter</span>  (<em>supp. </em><em>fl. </em>1150), <span class="occ">supposed religious writer</span>, is the creation of the bibliographer John Bale (<i>d</i>. 1563). In his notebook, the <span class="italic">Index Britanniae scriptorum</span>, Bale conflated two entries in the <span class="italic">Catalogus</span> of Henry Kirkestede (<i>d</i>. in or after 1378) and two in the early fourteenth-century union catalogue of books on the British mainland compiled by the Oxford Franciscans, known as the <span class="italic">Registrum Anglie</span>. Kirkestede had listed an exposition of <span class="roman">Matthew's gospel</span> and numerous sermons under the name Babio. These were certainly by Geoffroi Babion de Loroux (<i>d</i>. 1158), archbishop of Bordeaux. Under the heading <span class="italic">Opera Petri Babilon</span>, the <span class="italic">Registrum</span> gives a treatise on the mass and a collection of sermons. The archbishop of Bordeaux may have written the former, though it has also been attributed to an otherwise unknown Peter, while the sermons are almost certainly those listed by Kirkestede. Bale noted that the two sets of sermons were the same, but failed to identify their author as Geoffroi Babion, and attributed all three works to a Petrus Babion (or Babylon).<br>
<br>The final item in Bale's list is the late twelfth-century comic play <span class="italic">Babio</span>, which he entered under Geoffroi Babion in his notebook, but under Peter Babion in his published work, known as the <span class="italic">Catalogus</span>. Earliest extant in Bodl. Oxf., MS Digby 53, written at the end of the twelfth century, <span class="italic">Babio</span> is anonymous, though it has tentatively been attributed to the late twelfth-century writer Nigel of Canterbury (also known as Nigel Wireker). This may be doubted, but it was certainly composed in England, which may explain why Bale thought that his Petrus Babion was British.<br>
<br>Marios Costambeys </div>
