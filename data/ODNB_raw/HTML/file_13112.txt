<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Hervey,  George William</span>, second earl of Bristol  (1721&#150;1775), <span class="occ">diplomatist</span>, born on 3 August 1721, was the eldest son of <script>writeSeealsoLink('../view/article/13116/', "John Hervey, Baron Hervey of Ickworth (1696&#150;1743)")</script>, politician, and Mary Lepell (1699/1700&#150;1768), political hostess [<i>see </i><script>writeSeealsoLink('../view/article/13118/', "Hervey, Mary")</script>]. He became ensign in the 38th, or duke of Marlborough's, regiment of foot on 2 June 1739 and ensign in the 1st regiment of foot guards on 11 May 1740. He was travelling in Italy when he was recalled on his promotion to captain in the 48th, or Cholmondeley's, regiment of foot, and he returned home in May 1741 but resigned this commission in August 1742. On 5 August 1743 Hervey succeeded his father as third Baron Hervey of Ickworth, and on the following 1 December he took his seat in the House of Lords. He became second earl of Bristol on the death of his grandfather <script>writeSeealsoLink('../view/article/13117/', "John Hervey (1665&#150;1751)")</script> on 20 January 1751, and hereditary high steward of Bury St Edmunds.<br>
<br>Bristol was gazetted envoy-extraordinary to Turin on 5 April 1755; he resigned his post in August 1758 on being appointed ambassador-extraordinary and plenipotentiary to Madrid. On the ratification of the family compact between the houses of Bourbon he left Madrid without taking leave on 17 December 1761. A court whig, he was nominated lord lieutenant of Ireland and a privy councillor on 26 September 1766, but he threw up the post of lord lieutenant the next year without visiting Ireland, although he received the usual allowance of &pound;3000 for the voyage, as well as the annual salary of &pound;16,000. On 2 November 1768 he was chosen lord keeper of the privy seal, an office he retained until 29 January 1770 when he became groom of the stole and first lord of the bedchamber to the king. He died, unmarried, in Bath on 18 March 1775 of palsy, and was buried on 26 March at Ickworth. He was succeeded by his brother <script>writeSeealsoLink('../view/article/13109/', "Augustus John Hervey")</script> as third earl.<br>
<br>Gordon Goodwin<span class="italic">, rev. </span> R. D. E. Eagles </div>
