<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Blair,  William</span>  (1741&#150;1782), <span class="occ">naval officer</span>, the son of Daniel Blair of Edinburgh, went to sea in merchant ships and, as surgeon's servant, able seaman, and midshipman, in the <i>Windsor</i>, <i>Firebrand</i>, <i>Amazon</i>, <i>Aleide</i>, <i>Hussar</i>, and <i>Eolus</i>, before passing his lieutenant's examination on 30 May 1760. From 9 October 1760 he served as lieutenant in the <i>Grenado</i>, <i>Superb</i>, <i>Centurion</i>, <i>Salisbury</i>, and <i>Grafton</i>, and then from 6 December 1777 commanded the sloop <i>Wasp</i>.<br>
<br>Blair was promoted post captain on 18 April 1778; he was captain of John Byron's flagship, <i>Princess Royal</i> (90 guns), at the action off Grenada on 6 July 1779, and was later appointed to the <i>Nonsuch</i>. From 2 March 1781 he commanded the <i>Dolphin</i> (44 guns). In the hard-fought but indecisive battle of Dogger Bank on 5 August 1781 the <i>Dolphin</i>, notwithstanding her small size, took her place in the line of battle. Blair's conduct won for him the approval of the Admiralty and his appointment on 23 August to the <i>Anson</i>, a new 64-gun ship, then fitting for service in the West Indies.<br>
<br>In January 1782 Blair sailed in the <i>Anson</i> as part of Sir George Rodney's fleet. On 12 April he was killed in action at the battle of the Saintes, the <i>Anson</i>, in the leading squadron, having been heavily engaged from the very beginning of the battle.<br>
<br>J. K. Laughton<span class="italic">, rev. </span> Randolph Cock </div>
