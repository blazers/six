<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Cockburn,  Archibald</span>  (<em>fl. </em>1722), <span class="occ">Church of England clergyman and writer</span>, is a figure about whom little is known. In his only published work he describes himself as being &#145;M.A., rector of the parishes of St Mary Cayon and Christ's Church, Nichola Town, in St. Christopher's&#146; in the Caribbean. Cockburn recounts that at the request of the Hon. William Matthew, lieutenant-governor of the Leeward Islands, he wrote <span class="italic">A Philosophical Essay Concerning the Intermediate State of Blessed Souls</span> (1722). In this curious work Cockburn discussed the souls of the dead, and especially those which made a physical appearance to the living. As a later commentator noted, the author was very metaphysical, &#145;and firmly believed in ghosts and apparitions&#146;  (Noble, 3.141). It is not known when he died.<br>
<br>Gordon Goodwin<span class="italic">, rev. </span> Robert Brown </div>
