<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Lanza,  Gesualdo</span>  (1779&#150;1859), <span class="occ">singing teacher</span>, was born in Naples, the son of Francesco Giuseppe Lanza (<i>c</i>.1750&#150;<i>c</i>.1812), composer and the author of <span class="italic">6 arie notturne con accomp. di chitarra franc. e v. a piac.</span> (1792) and of six trios, op. 13, and six canzonets with recitative, op. 14, who moved to London in the early 1790s and for some time was a private musician to the marquess of Abercorn. Lanza was taught music by his father, and soon became known in London as a singing teacher. Among his pupils were Catherine Stephens, later countess of Essex, and Anna Maria Tree, the sister-in-law of Charles Kean. He also had a music-selling business at Chesterfield Street, St Pancras New Road. In 1842 Lanza started singing classes at 75 Newman Street. Later in the same year he announced a series of lectures, &#145;The national school for singing in classes, free to the public&#146;, and on 5 December 1842 he delivered a lecture at the Westminster Literary and Scientific Institution illustrative of his new system of teaching singing in classes.<br>
<br>In 1813 Lanza published <span class="italic">The Elements of Singing</span>, which was highly praised. His other publications include <span class="italic">The Elements of Singing in the Italian and English Styles</span> (1809), <span class="italic">Sunday Evening Recreations</span> (1840), and <span class="italic">Signor Lanza's New Method of Teaching Class Singing</span> (1843). Lanza composed the music for <span class="italic">The Deserts of Arabia</span> (1806), an operatical entertainment written by F. Reynolds, a masque, <span class="italic">Spirits of Dew</span>, a <span class="italic">Stabat mater</span>, and a <span class="italic">Grand missa di gloria</span> (1835), as well as songs and ballads. He died in London on 12 March 1859 and was buried in Highgate cemetery. His daughter, Rosalie Lanza, was a well-known opera singer.<br>
<br>R. H. Legge<span class="italic">, rev. </span> Anne Pimlott Baker </div>
