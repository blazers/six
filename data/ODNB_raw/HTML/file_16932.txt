<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Forster <span class="st"> [<em>married name </em>Lofthouse]</span>,  (Emma Judith) Mary</span>  (1853&#150;1885), <span class="occ">watercolour painter</span>, was born at 20 Marlborough Buildings, Bath, on 6 December 1853, the daughter of Thomas Barton Watkin Forster (1822&#150;1887) and his wife, Emma Stewart Galbraith (<i>d</i>. 1882). Until her marriage she lived at Holt Manor, near Bradford-on-Avon, Wiltshire, the seat of her father, an amateur landscape painter. Father and daughter painted in Wales and France as well as locally, and Mary Forster also exhibited scenes of Yorkshire, Norfolk, and Hampshire. She may have visited Switzerland with her father and probably knew Scotland, her mother being a Galbraith from Macrihanish, Argyll. From the age of twenty Mary Forster exhibited in the annual general exhibitions at the Dudley Gallery, London; she also showed at the Royal Academy in 1876, 1878, and 1880. In early 1884 she was elected an associate of the Royal Society of Painters in Water Colours, but her art, in J. L. Roget's words, was:<blockquote>too delicate and refined to attract the attention which it deserved during the two years only in which her landscapes were accorded a place in the gallery. But they afforded fair ground for anticipation that had she lived longer they would have become a much more important feature in the annual gatherings. (Roget, 2.426)</blockquote>On 3 June 1884, at Bradford-on-Avon, Mary Forster married Samuel Hill Smith Lofthouse (<i>b</i>. 1841), a barrister, who had himself exhibited a winter landscape at the Society of British Artists in 1874. For her final exhibits she used her married name. She died in childbirth at their home, Elmbank, Lower Halliford, Walton-on-Thames, Surrey, on 2 May 1885, during the summer exhibition of the Royal Watercolour Society, at which her <span class="italic">Pembroke Castle</span> was attracting very favourable attention. A collection of twenty-six frames of her studies and sketches was hung in the society's winter show that year as a memorial.<br>
<br>Huon Mallalieu </div>
