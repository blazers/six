<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Boyle,  Michael</span>  (1580?&#150;1635), <span class="occ">Church of Ireland bishop of Waterford and Lismore</span>, was born in London, the third son of Michael Boyle (<i>d</i>. 1596), a merchant, and his wife, Jane Peacock, and younger brother of <script>writeSeealsoLink('../view/article/3134/', "Richard Boyle")</script>, archbishop of Tuam. He entered Merchant Taylors' School, London, in 1587, and proceeded to St John's College, Oxford, in 1593. He graduated BA (5 December 1597), MA (25 June 1601), BD (9 July 1607), and DD (2 July 1611), and became a fellow of his college. He was appointed vicar of Finedon in Northamptonshire. Though esteemed for his learning and prudence, Boyle's subsequent rise in Irish ecclesiastical circles owed much to his relative Richard Boyle, first earl of Cork. In 1613 he was appointed to the archdeaconries of Cork and Cloyne and the prebend of Glanworth. In 1614 he became dean of Lismore and by 1619 was bishop of Waterford and Lismore. He married twice; his first wife was Dorothy Fish, daughter of George Fish of South Hill, Bedfordshire, and his second Christian, daughter of Thomas Bellott of Chester, but he had no children by either marriage. He held until death the chancellorship of Lismore and Cashel and the treasurership of Waterford. Boyle died at Waterford on 27 December 1635, where he was buried in the cathedral of the Blessed Trinity.<br>
<br>J. T. Gilbert<span class="italic">, rev. </span> Elizabethanne Boran </div>
