<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Atkinson,  Miles</span>  (1741&#150;1811), <span class="occ">Church of England clergyman</span>, the son of the Revd Christopher Atkinson (<i>b</i>. 1712/13), rector of Thorp Arch, Yorkshire, was born at Ledsham on 28 September 1741. He was the elder brother of the clergyman and poet <script>writeSeealsoLink('../view/article/861/', "William Atkinson (1758&#150;1846)")</script>. He went to school at Thorp Arch, before going up to Peterhouse, Cambridge (BA 1763). He was ordained deacon on 18 March 1764, priest on 25 May 1766, and became curate of the parish church of Leeds. He served as headmaster of the school of Drighlington, near Leeds (1764&#150;70). He married Mary, daughter of Edward Kenion of Leeds, on 5 April 1768. The couple had at least two sons.<br>
<br>In 1769 Atkinson served as lecturer of the parish church of Leeds, and in 1783 became vicar of Kippax, near Leeds. At a cost of nearly &pound;10,000 he founded St Paul's Church, Leeds, of which he was appointed minister in 1793. He died on 6 February 1811, and was buried at St Paul's, Leeds. A collection of his <span class="italic">Practical Sermons</span> was published in London in two volumes in 1812.<br>
<br>Thompson Cooper<span class="italic">, rev. </span> Robert Brown </div>
