<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Stirling, Sir  Walter</span>  (1718&#150;1786), <span class="occ">naval officer</span>, only son of Walter Stirling (1686&#150;1732) of Sheva, in Stirlingshire, and Janet, daughter of William Ruthven of Torryburn, Fife, was born in Scotland on 18 May 1718. He was promoted lieutenant on 18 February 1745 and commander on 9 December 1756. In 1753 he married Dorothy (<i>d</i>. 1782), daughter of Charles Willing of Philadelphia. The couple had one daughter, Anne, mother of <script>writeSeealsoLink('../view/article/26532/', "Sir James Stirling")</script>, first governor of Western Australia, and two sons: Walter, who was made baronet in 1800 and who died on 26 August 1832, and <script>writeSeealsoLink('../view/article/26529/', "Charles Stirling")</script>, naval officer.<br>
<br>On 10 January 1759 Stirling was posted to the <i>Lynn</i>, which he commanded for two years; he was then moved to the <i>Lowestoft</i>, in which he remained until the peace of 1763. From 1764 to 1766 he commanded the <i>Rainbow</i> (44 guns) on the North American station, and in 1770 he was appointed to the <i>Dunkirk</i> as flag-captain to the commodore, George Mackenzie, at Jamaica. In 1771 he was moved to the <i>Portland</i>, in which he returned to England. In 1780 he was appointed to the <i>Gibraltar</i>, and sailed to the West Indies with Sir Samuel Hood. After the capture of St Eustatius he was sent home with the dispatches and was knighted (1781). In 1782 he was commodore at the Nore. He had no further employment, and died on 24 November 1786.<br>
<br>J. K. Laughton<span class="italic">, rev. </span> P. L. C. Webb </div>
