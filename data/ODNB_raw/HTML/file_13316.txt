<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Hill,  William  Noel-</span>, third Baron Berwick  (1773&#150;1842), <span class="occ">diplomatist</span>, was the second son of Noel Hill, first Baron Berwick, great-nephew of <script>writeSeealsoLink('../view/article/13289/', "Richard Hill (1655/6&#150;1727)")</script>, and his wife, Anne, daughter of Henry Vernon of Hilton, Staffordshire. Born on 21 October 1773, he was educated at Rugby School and Jesus College, Cambridge (1791&#150;3), and was MP for Shrewsbury (1796&#150;1812) and Marlborough (1814&#150;18), sitting as a silent Pittite. He courted Lady Hester Stanhope, ultimately unsuccessfully, though he was briefly engaged to her. He was appointed envoy to Regensburg in 1805&#151;&#145;a little bit of a job&#146;, Lady Bessborough thought  (HoP, <span class="italic">Commons</span>)&#151;but war prevented him from going there. He was envoy to Sardinia from 1807 to 1824. In 1822, he rejected Canning's offer of the under-secretaryship of foreign affairs. In 1824 he assumed the additional name of Noel; in the same year he was created a privy councillor, and went as envoy to Naples, where he served until 1833. There he was, Harold Acton thought, &#145;a middle-aged sybarite who loved good cheer though some thought him heartless. His reputation for wit was not justified by his despatches&#146;  (Acton, 6). In 1832 he succeeded his elder brother, Thomas Noel Hill, as third Baron Berwick. He was an FSA. He died unmarried at Redrice, near Andover, on 4 August 1842. His large library was dispersed by sale.<br>
<br>H. C. G. Matthew </div>
