<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Adams,  William Davenport</span>  (1851&#150;1904), <span class="occ">journalist and compiler of reference works</span>, was born at Park Terrace, New Park Road, Brixton, on 28 December 1851, the elder surviving son of <script>writeSeealsoLink('../view/article/141/', "William Henry Davenport Adams (1828&#150;1891)")</script>, then a private tutor, and his wife, Sarah Esther, <i>n&eacute;e</i> Morgan. He entered Merchant Taylors' School in January 1863, and proceeded to Glasgow Academy and Edinburgh University, where poor health prevented him from getting a good degree. Becoming a journalist, he was appointed in 1875 as leader writer and literary and drama critic for the <span class="italic">Glasgow Daily News</span>; later he edited the evening and weekly editions. On 19 October 1875 he married Caroline Estelle, the daughter of John K&ouml;rner, a Polish exile of a noble family. From 1878 to 1880 he was the editor of the <span class="italic">Greenock Advertiser</span>, from 1880 to 1882 the acting editor of the <span class="italic">Nottingham Guardian</span>, from 1882 to 1885 the editor of the <span class="italic">Derby Mercury</span>, and from 1885 until his death the literary editor and drama critic of the London <span class="italic">Globe</span>. In addition to his many newspaper and journal articles, Adams published: <span class="italic">A Dictionary of English Literature, being a Comprehensive Guide to English Authors and their Works</span> (1878), <span class="italic">By-Ways in Book-Land</span> (1888), <span class="italic">A Book of Burlesque</span> (1891), and <span class="italic">With Poet and Player</span> (1891).<br>
<br>Adams's main interest was drama, and he spent twenty years compiling his <span class="italic">A Dictionary of the Drama</span>, which was to be &#145;a guide to the plays, playwrights, players, and playhouses of the United Kingdom and America, from the earliest times to the present day&#146;. Only the first of the two projected volumes (A&#150;G) was completed when he died, on 26 July 1904 at 17 Burstock Road, Putney. He was buried at Putney Vale cemetery.<br>
<br>Lewis Melville<span class="italic">, rev. </span> Nilanjana Banerji </div>
