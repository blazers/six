<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Pembrooke,  Thomas</span>  (1658<span class="mult">x</span>62&#150;<em>c.</em>1690), <span class="occ">history and portrait painter</span>, was possibly born near Canterbury, Kent. Of his parents, nothing is known. He was a pupil of Marcellus Laroon (whether the elder or the younger is uncertain) and executed several works for Charles Granville, earl of Bath, who became his patron. A picture by Pembrooke, <span class="italic">Hagar and Ishmael</span>, was engraved in mezzotint by John Raphael Smith. Pembrooke died young <i>c</i>.1690.<br>
<br>L. H. Cust<span class="italic">, rev. </span> Susan Cooper Morgan </div>
