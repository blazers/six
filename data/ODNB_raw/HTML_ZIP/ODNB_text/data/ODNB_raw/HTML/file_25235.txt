<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Sharpe,  Lewis</span>  (<em>fl. </em>1640), <span class="occ">playwright</span>, is unknown except as the author of <span class="italic">The Noble Stranger</span>, a comedy which was first acted &#145;at the Private House in Salisbury Court, by Her Majestie's servants&#146; some time between 1638 and 1640, the year in which it was published. It is dedicated to &#145;the Worthy Knight, Sir Edmund Williams&#146; and is prefaced with eulogistic verses by Richard Woolfall. From these it appears to have been a popular piece and frequently acted.<br>
<br>A contemporary, <a name="cosubject_25235"></a><span class="headword">Roger Sharpe</span>  (<em>fl. </em>1610), <span class="occ">poet</span>, is known only as the author of <span class="italic">More Fooles Yet, Written by R. S.</span>, a collection of epigrams published in 1610; an address to the reader is signed Roger Sharpe.<br>
<br>E. I. Carlyle<span class="italic">, rev. </span> Matthew Steggle </div>
