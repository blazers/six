<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Orival <span class="st"> [Orivalle]</span>,  Hugh  de</span>  (<em>d.</em> 1085), <span class="occ">bishop of London</span>, was consecrated by Archbishop Lanfranc in 1075, being the first bishop appointed to that see by William I and probably previously a royal chaplain. Although nothing is known of his background, it is pretty certain that, like William's other bishops, he was a Norman. He was afflicted with leprosy and resorted to the drastic cure of castration, but without effect. He died of the disease on 12 January 1085. The location of his burial is unknown.<br>
<br>Edmund Venables<span class="italic">, rev. </span> Marios Costambeys </div>
