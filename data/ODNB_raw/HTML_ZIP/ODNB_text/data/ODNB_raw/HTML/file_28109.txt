<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Son,  Jan Frans  van</span>  (<em>b. </em>1658, <em>d.</em> in or after 1705), <span class="occ">painter</span>, born at Antwerp on 16 August 1658, was son of the still-life painter Joris van Son (1623&#150;1667) and Cornelia van Heulem. Van Son was too young to have learned much from his father before the latter's death, but he may have studied with his father's friend, the still-life specialist Jan Pauwel Gillemans the elder. His name does not appear in the Antwerp guild registers, and so it seems likely that&#151;as Vertue claimed&#151;he came young to England, perhaps not long after Gillemans's death in 1675. He painted flowers, fruit, and game for a wealthy London clientele which included the earls of Dover and Ranelagh, and he decorated the earl of Radnor's house in St James's Square. He married the niece of Robert Streater, the king's sergeant-painter, and, according to Walpole, inherited much business after Streater's death in 1680. He signed his paintings J. van Son, which has led to confusion with his father's work, albeit that Jan's style is less polished. Vertue maintained that van Son died in St Alban's Street in 1700, at the age of about fifty. Weyerman went to London in late 1702 or early 1703, and wrote that the flower and fruit painter &#145;N. van Zon&#146; had died shortly before. This, apparently, was the result of a heart broken with grief by the death of a daughter. Both sources are probably wrong, since a painting in Lille is signed and dated 1705; and van Son may not have died in St Alban's Street since his name has not been found in the burial register of St James's Piccadilly.<br>
<br>Paul Taylor </div>
