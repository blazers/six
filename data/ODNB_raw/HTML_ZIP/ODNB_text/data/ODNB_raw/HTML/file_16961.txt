<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Long <span class="st"> [<em>n&eacute;e </em>Walpole]</span>, Lady  Catharine</span>  (1797&#150;1867), <span class="occ">religious writer</span>, was the youngest child of the seven daughters and four sons of Horatio Walpole, second earl of Orford (1757&#150;1822), and his wife, Sophia, <i>n&eacute;e</i> Churchill (<i>d</i>. 1797). Little is known of her early life, but on 25 July 1822 she married Henry Lawes Long of Hampton Lodge, Surrey. They had seven daughters and a son, and most of her writing appears to date from after her marriage.<br>
<br>Long's novels were religious in focus, although this did not insure her against adverse criticism. Her <span class="italic">Sir Roland Ashton: a Tale of the Times</span> (1844), a novel directed against the Tractarian movement, was described as &#145;feeble and second-rate&#146; by an <span class="italic">Athenaeum</span> reviewer who felt that fiction was not &#145;a good medium for religious instruction&#146;  (<span class="italic">Athenaeum</span>, 24 Aug 1844, 771). She continued to work in this genre with some popularity, however, as she went on to write novels such as <span class="italic">The First Lieutenant's Story</span> (1853), favourably reviewed by the <span class="italic">London Daily News</span>. Both <span class="italic">Roland Ashton</span> and this later novel were reprinted in Routledge's cheap series in the mid 1850s.<br>
<br>Long also wrote pieces of music and poetry such as an <span class="roman">Agnus Dei</span> for four or five voices in 1848, and &#145;He is not dead, he cannot die&#146;, an elegy set to music upon the death of Prince Albert. Her collection of inspirational praise and verse, <span class="italic">The Midsummer Souvenir</span> (1846), contained original pieces, as well as selections from Felicia Hemans, Sarah Stickney Ellis, and other popular authors.<br>
<br>Lady Catharine Long died suddenly from heart failure after being alarmed by a thunderstorm at Landthorne Hatch, Farnham, Surrey, on 20 August 1867.<br>
<br>A. F. Pollard<span class="italic">, rev. </span> Megan A. Stephan </div>
