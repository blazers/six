<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Pitman,  John Rogers</span>  (1782&#150;1861), <span class="occ">Church of England clergyman and author</span>, was born on 25 June 1782, and educated at Christ's Hospital and Pembroke College, Cambridge. He matriculated in 1800, graduating BA in 1804 and MA in 1815. After taking holy orders he was classics master at Christ's Hospital (1816&#150;20), and was appointed perpetual curate of Berden and vicar of Ugley, Essex, on 18 February 1817. He became well known as a preacher in London, at Berkeley and Belgrave chapels, and at the Foundling and Magdalene hospitals before 1830. In 1833 he was presented to the perpetual curacy of St Barnabas, Kensington, by the vicar, J. H. Pott. He resigned his Essex livings in 1846, and Kensington in 1848, becoming domestic chaplain to the duchess of Kent.<br>
<br>Pitman was a prolific writer, compiler, and editor, producing annotated editions of the works of Jeremy Taylor (1820&#150;22), J. Lightfoot (1822&#150;5), and E. Reynolds (1826), of N. Hooke's <span class="italic">Roman History</span> (1821), of W. Lowth's <span class="italic">Commentaries</span> (1822), and of J. Bingham's <span class="italic">Origines ecclesiasticae</span> (1840). Besides numerous sermons, he also published <span class="italic">Excerpta ex variis Romanis poetis</span> (1808), <span class="italic">The School Shakespeare</span> (1822), <span class="italic">Sophocles Ajax</span> (1830), and two sets of biblical commentaries (1821 and 1852). Pitman died at 8 Green Park, Bath, on 27 August 1861, shortly after his royal patron. He was survived by his wife, Elizabeth.<br>
<br>E. G. Hawke<span class="italic">, rev. </span> H. C. G. Matthew </div>
