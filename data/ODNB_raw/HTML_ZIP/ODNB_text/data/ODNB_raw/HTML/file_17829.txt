<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Maitland,  John Gorham</span>  (1818&#150;1863), <span class="occ">civil servant</span>, was born on 27 October 1818 at Taunton, Somerset, the son of <script>writeSeealsoLink('../view/article/17834/', "Samuel Roffey Maitland (1792&#150;1866)")</script>, historian and religious controversialist, and his wife, Selina Stephenson. He was educated privately before being admitted to Trinity College, Cambridge, in 1835, where he was elected a fellow after gaining the third place in the classical and the seventh in the mathematical tripos of 1839. In 1840 he was admitted at Lincoln's Inn and was called to the bar in 1843, but was not a successful practitioner. He was elected FRS in 1847. He was the author of two pamphlets, <span class="italic">Church Leases</span> (1849) and <span class="italic">Property and Income Tax</span> (1853), a critique of Disraeli's 1852 budget. On 12 August 1847 he married Emma (<i>d</i>. 1851), second daughter of the physicist <script>writeSeealsoLink('../view/article/7124/', "John Frederic Daniell")</script>. He was secretary to the civil service commission in succession to his friend, and fellow member of the Cambridge Apostles, James Spedding from 1855 until his death at his London home, 51 Rutland Gate, Knightsbridge, on 27 April 1863. He was survived by his only son, <script>writeSeealsoLink('../view/article/34837/', "Frederic William Maitland")</script>, and two daughters.<br>
<br>M. C. Curthoys </div>
