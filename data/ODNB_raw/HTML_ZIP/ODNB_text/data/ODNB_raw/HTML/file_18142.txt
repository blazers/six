<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Marshall,  John</span>  (1784?&#150;1837), <span class="occ">naval officer and author</span>, himself recorded that he went to sea at nine years of age, and served throughout the French Revolutionary and Napoleonic wars in vessels of a class to which no schoolmaster was allowed, that is, in sloops, cutters, or other small craft. He was therefore probably born in 1784, and first went to sea in 1793. At the conclusion of the war he was promoted to the rank of lieutenant, on 14 February 1815, but had no further service in the Royal Navy.<br>
<br>In 1823 Marshall began the publication of <span class="italic">Royal Naval biography, or, Memoirs of the services of all the flag-officers &hellip; post captains, and commanders whose names appeared on the Admiralty list of sea officers at the commencement of the present year (1823), or who have since been promoted</span>. The work was continued until 1835, extending to twelve octavo volumes. This collection of biographies had no pretensions to literary merit, and the author seldom attempted any critical judgement of the conduct he described. Many of the lives were evidently contributed by the officers themselves; though events were thus sometimes described in too favourable manner, entries commonly included copies of official or private letters, and other documents, which added value to the work. Marshall died early in 1837 at Haslar Royal Naval Hospital, Gosport, Hampshire.<br>
<br>J. K. Laughton<span class="italic">, rev. </span> Roger Morriss </div>
