<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Higson,  John</span>  (1825&#150;1871), <span class="occ">topographer</span>, was born on 25 July 1825 at Whitely Farm, Gorton, Lancashire, the son of Daniel and Letitia Higson. He lived from <i>c</i>.1845 to <i>c</i>.1865 at Droylsden, where he was employed for almost twenty years as cashier at the Victoria spinning mills and subsequently at the Springhead Cotton-spinning Company. He was an enthusiastic supporter of the Droylsden Mechanics' Institute, and an active church worker for many years. He married Elizabeth Caroline Green on 6 November 1848; they had six sons and two daughters.<br>
<br>To a great extent self-taught, Higson was &#145;a tireless inquirer&#146; into the history of Droylsden and surrounding areas, and he spent many years gathering material for his published works, which included <span class="italic">The Gorton Historical Recorder</span> (1852) and <span class="italic">Historical and Descriptive Notices of Droylsden</span> (1859). He and John Owen rescued and preserved from destruction the first parochial register of Newton Chapel (to 1666), and Higson himself was responsible for the safeguarding for posterity of much material relating to the history of the area. With some associates he also started an unsuccessful Droylsden paper on liberal conservative lines. He was a contributor to the <span class="italic">Ashton Reporter</span> from its foundation in 1855, under the signature H. At the time of his death on 13 December 1871 at his home, Lees, Ashton under Lyne, he was working on a glossary of Lancashire idioms. He was survived by his wife.<br>
<br>Alan G. Crosby </div>
