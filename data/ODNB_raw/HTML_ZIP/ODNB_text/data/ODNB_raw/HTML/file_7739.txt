<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Dodd,  Philip Stanhope</span>  (1775&#150;1852), <span class="occ">Church of England clergyman</span>, was the son of the Revd Richard Dodd (1735/6&#150;1811), rector of Cowley, Middlesex, author of a translation of Formey's <span class="italic">Ecclesiastical History</span>, and his wife 
    (<i>n&eacute;e</i> Wynant; <i>d</i>. 1802)
  . He was educated at Tonbridge School, and, having entered Magdalene College, Cambridge, in 1792, was elected a fellow, and proceeded BA in 1796 and MA in 1799. In 1796 he published anonymously <span class="italic">Hints to Fresh-Men, from a Member of the University of Cambridge</span>, of which the third edition was printed in 1807. He was ordained by the bishop of London in 1797 and was for some years curate of Camberwell, Surrey, which he exchanged in 1803 for the ministry of Lambeth Chapel, retaining the afternoon lecture at Camberwell.<br>
<br>In 1806 Dodd was chaplain to the lord mayor, Sir William Leighton, and published five sermons preached in that capacity. The fourth of these, <span class="italic">The Lawfulness of Judicial Oaths and on Perjury</span>, preached at St Paul's Cathedral on 31 May 1807, produced a reply by Joseph Gurney Bevan. He was rewarded for his civic services by the valuable rectory of St Mary-at-Hill in the City of London in 1807, where he was one of the most popular clergymen of the metropolis.<br>
<br>In 1812 Dodd was presented by his college to the sinecure rectory of Aldrington in Sussex, the church of which had been destroyed. Sir J. S. Sidney, bt, in 1819 gave him the rectory of Penshurst, Kent, worth &pound;766 per annum, and he became rural dean of Malling in 1846. He married on 4 August 1814 Martha, second daughter of the late Lieutenant-Colonel Wilson, deputy treasurer of Chelsea Hospital. In 1837 Dodd wrote <span class="italic">A View of the Evidence afforded by the Life and Ministry of St Paul to the Truth of the Christian Revelation</span>. He died at Penshurst rectory on 22 March 1852.<br>
<br>G. C. Boase<span class="italic">, rev. </span> M. C. Curthoys </div>
