<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Bouchier <span class="st"> [<em>formerly </em>Boucher]</span>,  Barton</span>  (1795&#150;1865), <span class="occ">religious writer</span>, born in Epsom, Surrey on 19 November 1795, was a younger son of the vicar of Epsom, the <script>writeSeealsoLink('../view/article/2972/', "Revd Jonathan Boucher (1738&#150;1804)")</script>, and his third wife, Elizabeth Hodgson, widow of the Revd James of Arthuret. Barton changed his name from Boucher to Bouchier after 1822. He was educated at Balliol College, Oxford, proceeding BA in 1822 and MA in 1827. In 1816 he married Mary, daughter of the Revd Nathaniel Thornbury of Avening, Gloucestershire. Bouchier had read for the bar at Lincoln's Inn in 1813, but was ordained in 1819 by the bishop of Hereford and became curate at Monmouth. A sermon preached by him at Usk in 1822 for the Christian Knowledge Society was published by request. Bouchier held curacies later at Old, Northamptonshire, and (before 1834) at Cheam, Surrey; while resident there he published an edition of Lancelot Andrewes's <span class="italic">Prayers and Offices of Private Devotion</span> (1834). In 1839 he became rector of Fonthill Bishop, diocese of Salisbury, but remained in Cheam until 1856.<br>
<br>Bouchier was the author of biblical commentaries and devotional works, including <span class="italic">Prophecy and its Fulfilment</span> (1836) and <span class="italic">Manna in the House</span> (1852&#150;8). In 1853 he wrote a <span class="italic">Letter</span> to the prime minister, Lord Aberdeen, against opening the Crystal Palace on Sundays, following up this appeal in 1854 by <span class="italic">The Poor Man's Palace and the Poor Man's Duty</span>, a pamphlet addressed to the Crystal Palace directors. He also published his <span class="italic">Farewell Sermon</span> to his Cheam flock, having preached it on 28 September 1856. Bouchier died at the rectory at Fonthill Bishop on 28 December 1865. The editorship of <span class="italic">The Vision</span>, a humorous illustrated poem on Jonathan Boucher's philological studies, written by Sir Frederick Morton Eden, bt, and published in 1820, has been wrongly attributed to Bouchier.<br>
<br>Jennett Humphreys<span class="italic">, rev. </span> Mari G. Ellis </div>
