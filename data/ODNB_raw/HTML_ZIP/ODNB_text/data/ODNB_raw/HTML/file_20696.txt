<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Oldys,  Valentine</span>  (1620&#150;1685), <span class="occ">poet</span>, was born in Oxford, the son of Valentine Oldis, a London merchant. He went to school in Newport, Shropshire, and was admitted pensioner at Sidney Sussex College, Cambridge, on 19 September 1650.<br>
<br>Oldys published <span class="italic">A Poem on the Restoration of King Charles</span> in 1660, and was a patron of literature and men of letters. He is among the contributors of commendatory verses to Henry Bold's <span class="italic">Poems Lyrique, Macaronique, Heroique, &amp;c.</span> (1664) and one of the poems in the volume is addressed to him. He also contributed to Alexander Brome's <span class="italic">Songs, and other Poems</span> (1664). John Phillips dedicated to Oldys his <span class="italic">Macaronides, or, Virgil Travesty</span> (1673).<br>
<br>Oldys was made MD of Cambridge <i>per literas regias</i> on 6 October 1671, and honorary member of the College of Physicians on 30 September 1680. He died in 1685, and was buried near his father in St Helen, Bishopsgate, by St Mary Axe.<br>
<br>Ronald Bayne<span class="italic">, rev. </span> Joanna Moody </div>
