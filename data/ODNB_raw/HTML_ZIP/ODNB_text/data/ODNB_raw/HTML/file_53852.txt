<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Smith,  Robert Cross</span> <span class="st"> [<em>pseud. </em>Raphael]</span>  (1795&#150;1832), <span class="occ">astrologer and journalist</span>, was born on 19 March 1795 to respectable parents in Abbots Leigh near Bristol. By 1820 he had found work as a clerk in London. In the summer of that year he married Sarah Lucas, with whom he had six children. Known to his readers as Raphael, or Merlinus Anglicus Jun., he was a key figure in the revival of astrology in the nineteenth century. He edited the first weekly astrological journal in 1824 and from 1826 produced a successful almanac, the <span class="italic">Prophetic Messenger</span>, whose copyright was keenly sought after his death. His style was popular and dramatic: he wrote of astrology as an exclusive and learned practice, but his work linked it to popular tales of the supernatural. Of delicate health, Smith died of consumption at his home in Castle Street East, London, on 26 February 1832.<br>
<br>Katharine Anderson </div>
