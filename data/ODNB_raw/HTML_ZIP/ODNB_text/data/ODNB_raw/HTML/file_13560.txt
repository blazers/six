<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Hollings,  John</span>  (<em>bap. </em>1682, <em>d.</em> 1739), <span class="occ">physician</span>, was baptized at St Chad, Shrewsbury on 14 November 1682, the son of John Hollings (1635?&#150;1712?), a physician of that town, and his wife, Anne. After attending Shrewsbury School, he entered Magdalene College, Cambridge, as a pensioner, on 27 March 1700, and graduated MB in 1705 and MD in 1710. He was admitted a candidate of the Royal College of Physicians on 25 June 1725, and became a fellow on 25 June 1726, having on 9 March previously been elected FRS.<br>
<br>Hollings rose to be physician-general to the army and physician-in-ordinary to the king. With his wife, Jane, he had two sons, John (<i>d</i>. 1739), a physician, and Richard (<i>d</i>. 1741), solicitor-general to the prince of Wales from 1736 to 1741, and two daughters. Hollings died at his home in Pall Mall, London, on 10 May 1739.<br>
<br>Hollings's reputation for classical scholarship and general culture was considerable among his contemporaries. His only publication was the Harveian oration for 1734, entitled <span class="italic">Status humanae naturae expositus in oratione coram medicis Londinensibus habita</span> (1734), of which an English translation appeared the same year.<br>
<br>Gordon Goodwin<span class="italic">, rev. </span> Patrick Wallis </div>
