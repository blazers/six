<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Hill, Sir  Thomas Noel</span>  (1784&#150;1832), <span class="occ">army officer</span>, seventh son of Sir John Hill, third baronet (1740&#150;1824), of Hawkstone, Shropshire, and his wife, Mary, <i>n&eacute;e</i> Chambre (<i>d</i>. 8 March 1806), and younger brother of <script>writeSeealsoLink('../view/article/13298/', "Rowland Hill, Viscount Hill")</script>, was born on 14 February 1784. He entered the army on 25 September 1801 as cornet in the 10th light dragoons, becoming lieutenant in 1803 and captain in 1805. He exchanged to the 53rd regiment the year after, and on 16 February 1809 was appointed major in the Portuguese army under Marshal Beresford. He commanded the 1st Portuguese regiment with distinction at the battle of Busaco, the capture of Ciudad Rodrigo, the battles of Salamanca and Vitoria, and the capture of San Sebastian. He was appointed captain and lieutenant-colonel 1st foot guards (Grenadier Guards) on 25 July, and was knighted on 28 July 1814. He served as assistant adjutant-general in the Waterloo campaign. He retired from the guards on half pay on 27 May 1824. He was deputy adjutant-general in Canada in 1827&#150;30, and was afterwards appointed commandant of the cavalry depot, Maidstone.<br>
<br>Hill married, on 27 July 1821, Anna Maria Shore (<i>d</i>. 25 Feb 1886), second daughter of <script>writeSeealsoLink('../view/article/25452/', "John Shore, the first Lord Teignmouth")</script>; they had six surviving children.<br>
<br>Hill was a KCB (January 1815), and had the orders of the Tower and Sword of Portugal and of Maximilian Joseph of Bavaria. He died at Maidstone, Kent, on 8 January 1832, and was buried in Maidstone churchyard.<br>
<br>H. M. Chichester<span class="italic">, rev. </span> James Falkner </div>
