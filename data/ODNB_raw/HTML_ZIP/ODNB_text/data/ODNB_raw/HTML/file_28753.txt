<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Warner <span class="st"> [Garnier]</span></span>  (<em>fl. </em>1106), <span class="occ">Benedictine monk and homilist</span>, may have come to England from Bec. He became a monk of Westminster, where he was present when the relics of St Wihtburh were translated in 1106. He was known as <i>homeliarius</i> on the strength of the book of homilies that he dedicated to Gilbert Crispin (<i>d</i>. 1117), then his abbot. Both the creation and the loss of this collection are significant in an age when original homilies are not common, and even the most modest addition to the surviving corpus would have been welcome. Gilbert Crispin may have provided him with a model through his own preaching, or perhaps even encouraged him directly in the work.<br>
<br>G. R. Evans </div>
