<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Wilson,  Charles Robert</span>  (1863&#150;1904), <span class="occ">historian of India</span>, born at Old Charlton, Kent, on 27 March 1863, was the only son of Charles Wilson, army tutor, and his wife, Charlotte Woodthorpe, <i>n&eacute;e</i> Childs. Educated at the City of London School, where he gained the Carpenter scholarship on leaving, he was elected to a scholarship at Wadham College, Oxford, in 1881. He graduated BA in 1887, having been placed in the first class in mathematical moderations in 1883 and in the final classical school in 1886. On leaving Oxford he entered the Indian educational service in Bengal, being successively professor at Dacca and at the Presidency College, Calcutta; principal of the Bankipur College, Patna; and inspector of schools. In 1900 he was appointed officer in charge of the records of the government of India, an appointment which carried with it that of assistant secretary in the home department. Soon afterwards his health broke down, and he died in penury, unmarried, at his home, 10 Avenue Mansions, Clapham Common, London, on 24 July 1904 and was buried in Streatham cemetery.<br>
<br>Wilson was a devoted student of the early history of the English in Bengal, ransacking the documentary evidence in India, at the India Office, at the British Museum, and wherever else it might be found. He was admitted to the degree of DLitt at Oxford in 1902. Apart from several articles in the <span class="italic">Journal</span> of the Asiatic Society of Bengal, dealing chiefly with aspects of the Indian mutiny, and a catalogue of the society's paintings (1897) he wrote on the historical tombs of Bengal (1896), on the early history of the British in Bengal (1895&#150;1911) and on Old Fort William in Bengal (2 vols., 1906).<br>
<br>J. S. Cotton<span class="italic">, rev. </span> H. C. G. Matthew </div>
