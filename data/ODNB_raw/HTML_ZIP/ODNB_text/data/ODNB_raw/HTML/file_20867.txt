<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Pinnock,  Osbern</span> <span class="st"> [<em>known as </em>Osburn Pinnuc Claudianus]</span>  (<em>fl. </em><em>c.</em>1148), <span class="occ">Benedictine monk, theologian, and lexicographer</span>, was a monk at St Peter's Abbey, Gloucester, under Abbot Hamelin (1148&#150;79) and possibly Gilbert Foliot (1139&#150;48). The name Pinnuc  (BL, Royal MS 8 B.xxi) suggests birth near Stanway, Gloucestershire; the name Claudianus  (<span class="italic">DNB</span>) means &#145;of Gloucester&#146;; he may be the Osbern who maintained a cell in Wales, perhaps Cardigan  (<span class="italic">Letters and Charters</span>, letter 10). His theological works, preserved in BL, Royal MS 6 D.ix (owned by the Lincoln Carmelites, but probably written at Gloucester), are unpublished: a dialogue with a Nicholas on the <span class="roman">Pentateuch</span> (there is an extract in Wright, <span class="italic">Biographia</span>); a six-book treatise on <span class="roman">Judges</span>, addressed to Foliot when he was bishop of Hereford (1148&#150;63); treatises on the incarnation, nativity, sacraments of the passion, and resurrection. His most influential work, extant in at least twenty-five manuscripts, was the <span class="italic">Panormia</span> or <span class="italic">Liber derivationum</span>, an alphabetical list of lexical derivatives, dedicated to Hamelin; this is rich in its citations and is a modest advance on Papias's mid-eleventh-century <span class="italic">Elementarium doctrinae rudimentum</span>. Its prologue, in a very exotic style, recounts a dream in which Grammar laments her neglect and says that she intends to pour lists of words into Osbern's mouth. The <i>commedia</i> <span class="italic">Babio</span> uses the <span class="italic">Panormia</span>'s vocabulary for comic effect.<br>
<br>A. G. Rigg </div>
