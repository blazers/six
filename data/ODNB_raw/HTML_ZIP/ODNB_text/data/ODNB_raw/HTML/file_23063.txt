<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Ram,  James</span>  (1793&#150;1870), <span class="occ">lawyer and legal writer</span>, was born at Berechurch, Essex, where he was baptized on 24 March 1793, the son of James Ram and his wife, Jane. He was indentured to a London firm of solicitors, but later entered Pembroke College, Cambridge, where he graduated BA in 1817 and proceeded MA in 1823. After making what was then the grand tour between 1818 and 1822, he entered the Inner Temple, where he was called to the bar on 21 November 1823. A pupil of the conveyancer Richard Preston, he practised in London and Ipswich, where he lived in later life. He married Elizabeth Jane (1799/1800&#150;1881), the only daughter of Ralph Willett Adye, army officer and inspector of artillery, and had five sons and three daughters.<br>
<br>As a legal author Ram obtained a reputation for painstaking research, methodical arrangement, and lucidity of style. He published works on jurisprudence, property, debt, and a father's custodial rights over his children. He died at 22 Silent Street, Ipswich, on 11 August 1870. His son Edward Ram (1843&#150;1918), vicar of St John's, Timberhill, Norwich, was a prominent ritualist clergyman.<br>
<br>J. M. Rigg<span class="italic">, rev. </span> Eric Metcalfe </div>
