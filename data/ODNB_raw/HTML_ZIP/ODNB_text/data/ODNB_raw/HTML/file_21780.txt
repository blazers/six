<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Pegge,  Samuel</span>  (1733&#150;1800), <span class="occ">antiquary and writer</span>, was born in Godmersham, Kent, on 21 February 1733, the only surviving son of <script>writeSeealsoLink('../view/article/21779/', "Samuel Pegge (1704&#150;1796)")</script>, antiquary, and his wife, Anne (<i>d</i>. 1746), daughter of Benjamin Clarke, of Stanley, near Wakefield. He went to school in Chesterfield, Derbyshire, and in 1751 matriculated at St John's College, Cambridge. In 1754 he was called to the bar at the Middle Temple. On 12 February 1759 he married Martha (1732&#150;1767), daughter of Dr Henry Bourne, physician, of Chesterfield. They had a son, <script>writeSeealsoLink('../view/article/21778/', "Sir Christopher Pegge (1764/5&#150;1822)")</script>, and a daughter, Charlotte Anne (<i>d</i>. 1793). Through the patronage of the lord chamberlain, the duke of Devonshire, he was appointed a groom of the privy chamber to the king on 8 October 1762. He married second, on 1 May 1773 at St Martin-in-the-Fields, Goodeth Belt.<br>
<br>Pegge early acquired a considerable proficiency in music, writing many catches, glees, and songs. He was also the author of some theatrical prologues and epilogues, and of some pleasant tales and epigrammatic poems. His other publications included biographical memoirs, a memoir of his father, and several works of an antiquarian nature, including &#145;Illustrations of the churchwardens' accompts of St Michael Spurrier-Gate, York&#146; in <span class="italic">Illustrations of the Manners and Expences of Antient Times</span> (1797) by John Nichols, and two works on the royal household, <span class="italic">Curialia, or, An Historical Account of some Branches of the Royal Household</span> (1782&#150;1806) in five parts, the last two being edited by Nichols, and <span class="italic">Curialia miscellanea, or, Anecdotes of old times, regal, noble, gentilitian, and miscellaneous, including authentic anecdotes of the royal household</span> (1818), also edited by Nichols. On 2 June 1797 Pegge was elected a fellow of the Society of Antiquaries. He died on 22 May 1800 at Scotland Yard, Whitehall, London, and was buried on the west side of Kensington churchyard.<br>
<br>Thompson Cooper<span class="italic">, rev. </span> K. D. Reynolds </div>
