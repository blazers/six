<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Foli,  Allan James</span> <span class="st"> [<em>real name </em>Allan James Foley]</span>  (1835&#150;1899), <span class="occ">singer</span>, was born Allan James Foley on 7 August 1835 at Cahir, co. Tipperary. After working as a carpenter he went to America, where he sang in church choirs, then studied in Naples with Bisaccia. He made his d&eacute;but as Elmiro in Rossini's <span class="italic">Otello</span> in Catania in December 1862, going on to sing in Turin, Modena, Milan, and, in 1864, at the Th&eacute;&acirc;tre Italien in Paris. In 1865 he joined Mapleson's company at Her Majesty's Theatre, singing Saint-Bris in Meyerbeer's <span class="italic">Les Huguenots</span> (17 April), the Second Priest in <span class="italic">The Magic Flute</span> (6 July), and the Hermit in <span class="italic">Der Freisch&uuml;tz</span> (28 October). He appeared at Drury Lane and Covent Garden until 1887, performing at least sixty roles: these included Daland in the first English performance of Wagner's <span class="italic">Der fliegende Holl&auml;nder</span> in 1870. He was also much in demand in oratorio and at festivals. His oratorio d&eacute;but was on 25 April 1866 in Handel's <span class="italic">Israel in Egypt</span> at the National Choral Society, singing the duet &#145;The Lord is a man of war&#146; with Charles Santley, though his first real success came with a performance of Haydn's <span class="italic">The Creation</span> at the Sacred Harmonic Society. His festival appearances included roles at Norwich (1869), Birmingham (1870), and Gloucester (1871). He also sang Jacob in the first performance of George Macfarren's <span class="italic">Joseph</span> at the Leeds Festival on 21 September 1877 and Herod in the first English performances of Berlioz's <span class="italic">L'enfance du Christ</span> in Manchester under Hall&eacute; on 30 December 1880 and in London on 26 February 1881. In Russia he sang Caspar (<span class="italic">Der Freisch&uuml;tz</span>), Rossini's Mos&egrave; and Pietro in Auber's <span class="italic">La muette de Portici</span> with great success; he also visited Vienna and toured America. In 1892 he toured South Africa, and in 1893 Australia, and in 1896 he sang again in London. He was a popular ballad singer, and for this repertory he billed himself as Signor Foli; his operatic and concert appearances were as A. J. Foli. His voice was described as &#145;a rich powerful bass of more than two octaves from E below the line to F&#146;  (Grove, <span class="italic">Dict. mus.</span>, 1st edn). Bernard Shaw, however, complained of his poor articulation. He died at the Royal Hotel, Southport, on 20 October 1899.<br>
<br>John Warrack </div>
