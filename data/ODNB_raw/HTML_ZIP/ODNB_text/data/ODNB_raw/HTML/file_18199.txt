<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Martin,  Josiah</span>  (1685&#150;1748), <span class="occ">religious writer</span>, was born on 11 June 1685 in Sussex, the son of John Martin (<i>d</i>. 1695) and his wife, Mary, <i>n&eacute;e</i> Rickman (1659?&#150;1691). Martin was a member of the Peel monthly meeting of Friends. He became a good classical scholar and was spoken of by James Gough, in his 1772 translation of Madame Guyon's life, as a man whose memory was esteemed for &#145;learning, humility, and fervent piety&#146;. Martin is best-known in connection with <span class="italic">A Letter from One of the People called Quakers to Francis de Voltaire, Occasioned by his Remarks on that People in his Letters concerning the English Nation</span> (1741). It was twice reprinted, in London and in Dublin, and translated into French. It was a temperate and scholarly treatise and was much in favour at the time. He edited, and contributed a long preface to, <span class="italic">The Archbishop of Cambray's Dissertation on Pure Love</span> (1735) and was one of the first of several Quakers who helped to make major works of the continental quietists available in English, thus contributing to the development of quietism in eighteenth-century English Quakerism. His other publications included a substantial appendix to an edition of Anthony Pearson's <span class="italic">The Great Case of Tithes</span> (1730). Martin was one of a number of Quakers who, in 1736, met with Count Zinzendorf, founder of the Herrnhuter Br&uuml;dergemeinde, to discuss religious views. He also corresponded with the Dutch Quaker historian Willem Sewel about his publications.<br>
<br>Martin died, unmarried, on 18 February 1748, in the parish of St Andrew's, Holborn, London, and was buried in the Quaker burial-ground at Bunhill Fields. He left the proceeds of his library of 4000 volumes, sold by catalogue on 29 May 1749, to be divided among his nephews and nieces. The Quaker author Joseph Besse was his executor.<br>
<br>Charlotte Fell-Smith<span class="italic">, rev. </span> David J. Hall </div>
