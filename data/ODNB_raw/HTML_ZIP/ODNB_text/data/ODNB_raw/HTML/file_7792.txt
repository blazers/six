<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Don,  George</span>  (1798&#150;1856), <span class="occ">botanist and plant collector</span>, was born at Doo Hillock, Forfarshire, Scotland, on 17 May 1798, the eldest of the five sons of George Don (1764&#150;1814), clockmaker turned nurseryman, who discovered many highland plants new to Scotland, and principal gardener (1802&#150;6) at the Royal Botanic Garden, Edinburgh. <script>writeSeealsoLink('../view/article/7790/', "David Don (1799&#150;1841)")</script> was a younger brother. After horticultural experience in the nursery of Messrs Dickson at Broughton, near Edinburgh, Don worked from 1816 to 1821 as foreman gardener in the Chelsea Physic Garden. In December 1821 he began a voyage with Captain Edward Sabine to Madeira, Sierra Leone, S&atilde;o Tom&eacute;, the West Indies, and Brazil, his task everywhere to collect seeds, plants, bulbs, and herbarium specimens for the Horticultural Society's garden. He returned in February 1823 having successfully assembled many new plants but, as he violated an agreement not to publish on what he had collected, the society dismissed him in 1825. He published, in 1827, <span class="italic">A Monograph of the Genus Allium</span>, a preprint from <span class="italic">Memoirs of the Wernerian Society</span> (6, 1832, 1&#150;102). This and his &#145;Review of the genus <i>Combretum</i>&#146; in <span class="italic">Transactions of the Linnean Society</span> (15, 1826, 412&#150;41) made evident his botanical ability and he was commissioned to prepare <span class="italic">A General System of Botany and Gardening</span> (4 vols., 1831&#150;8), an excellent work, but one which was financially unsuccessful and never completed. He later provided the botanical section for the <span class="italic">Encyclopaedia metropolitana</span> (2, 1843). Don was elected an associate of the Linnean Society of London in 1822 and a fellow in 1831. He died, unmarried, in Kensington on 25 February 1856.<br>
<br>William T. Stearn </div>
