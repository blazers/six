<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Oliver,  William</span>  (<em>c.</em>1804&#150;1853), <span class="occ">landscape painter</span>, born in Sudbury, Suffolk, the son of William Oliver, is first recorded as an artist in 1829 when he exhibited the first of thirty-six paintings at the Society of British Artists in Suffolk Street, London. In 1834 he was elected a member of the New Society of Painters in Water Colours, and his drawings appeared annually at its exhibitions until 1854. He also exhibited twenty-nine landscapes in oils, mainly views in France and Italy, at the Royal Academy between 1835 and 1853, and fifty-four at the British Institution from 1836. In 1843 he published a folio volume, <span class="italic">Scenery of the Pyrenees</span>, lithographed by George Barnard, Thomas Shotter Boys, and Carl Hughe, among others.<br>
<br>On 21 September 1840 Oliver married <a href="#cosubject_20720">Emma Eburne [<i>see below</i>]</a>, whom he had first met when she was fifteen, when he encouraged her to take drawing lessons. They had one son and one daughter. After their marriage they travelled every autumn, painting the landscape scenery of England, Wales, and Germany. Oliver died on 2 November 1853 at Langley Mill House, Halstead, Essex. His oil painting <span class="italic">Foligno</span> and a watercolour of the city of Strasbourg are in the Victoria and Albert Museum, London.<br>
<br>
<a name="cosubject_20720"></a><span class="headword">Emma Sophia Oliver <span class="st"> [<em>n&eacute;e </em>Eburne]</span></span>  (1819&#150;1885), <span class="occ">landscape painter</span>, was born in London on 15 August 1819, the daughter of William Eburne (<i>d</i>. <i>c</i>.1831), a coach builder, and his wife, Mary, of Rathbone Place, London. Emma Oliver was elected a member of the New Society of Painters in Water Colours in 1849, and exhibited landscapes in watercolours and oils at the Royal Academy, British Institution, Society of British Artists, and at various provincial galleries.<br>
<br>In 1855, following the death of William Oliver in 1853, Emma Oliver married John Sedgwick (1812&#150;1882), a solicitor, of Watford, Hertfordshire, but continued to paint under her first husband's name. Many of her later landscapes were painted in Venice, and along the Rhine. She died on 15 March 1885 at the Brewery House, Great Berkhamsted, Hertfordshire. One of her landscapes, <span class="italic">Vale of Dedham, Essex</span> (1876), is in the Victoria and Albert Museum.<br>
<br>R. E. Graves<span class="italic">, rev. </span> Anne Pimlott Baker </div>
