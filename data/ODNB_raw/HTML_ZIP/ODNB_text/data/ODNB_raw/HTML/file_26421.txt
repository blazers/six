<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Stevens,  Francis</span>  (1781&#150;1823), <span class="occ">landscape painter</span>, was born, probably at Exeter, on 21 November 1781. He was a pupil of <script>writeSeealsoLink('../view/article/19544/', "Paul Sandby Munn")</script>, and became a skilful painter of landscape and cottage architecture, working chiefly in watercolours. He exhibited at the Royal Academy in 1804 and 1805, and in the latter year was elected an associate of the newly founded Society of Painters in Water Colours; he was promoted to full membership in 1809. Stevens was one of the originators of the Chalon Sketching Society in 1808. He was a member of the Norwich Society of Artists from 1810. In 1815 he published <span class="italic">Views of Cottages and Farmhouses in England and Wales</span>, which included his etchings after drawings by Munn, John Varley, Samuel Prout, and others. In the following year he became a drawing-master at Sandhurst. He painted in Yorkshire and Nottinghamshire as well as his native county of Devon. Later he settled at Exeter, whence he sent works to the Royal Academy in 1819 and 1822. He died of apoplexy at Exeter in 1823. His <span class="italic">Lustleigh Cleeve</span> is in the Devon and Exeter Institution, Exeter. Other examples of his work are in the Victoria and Albert Museum, London.<br>
<br>F. M. O'Donoghue<span class="italic">, rev. </span> Annette Peach </div>
