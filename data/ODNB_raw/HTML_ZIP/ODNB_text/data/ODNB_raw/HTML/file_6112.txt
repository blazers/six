<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Clifford <span class="st"> [<em>later </em>Constable]</span>, Sir  Thomas Hugh</span>, first baronet  (1762&#150;1823), <span class="occ">topographer and botanist</span>, was born on 4 December 1762 in London, the eldest son of Thomas Clifford (1732&#150;1787) (fourth son of Hugh, third Baron Clifford of Chudleigh) and Barbara Aston (<i>d</i>. 1786), youngest daughter and coheir of James, fifth Lord Aston of Forfar. He belonged to a well-established Roman Catholic family with strong French connections and was educated in the academy opened at Li&egrave;ge by the English ex-Jesuits after their expulsion from Bruges. He continued his studies at the college of Navarre, in Paris, after which he travelled on foot over Switzerland. After the death of his mother he settled at Tixall in Staffordshire, the estate of the Astons, which he inherited from her. On 17 June 1791 he married Mary Macdonald (<i>d</i>. 1825), second daughter of John Chichester of Arlington, Devonshire. They had one son, Sir Thomas Aston Clifford, second baronet.<br>
<br>During periods of residence at Bath, Clifford gave a cordial welcome to the loyalist exiles from France, and when Louis XVIII visited that city in 1813, a few months before the restoration of the French monarchy, he twice invited him to his table. By patent dated 22 May 1815 Clifford was created a baronet at the special request of Louis XVIII. In 1821 he succeeded to the estates of Francis Constable of Burton Constable, Yorkshire and Wycliffe Hall, Oxfordshire and took the name of Constable only. With his brother <script>writeSeealsoLink('../view/article/5642/', "Arthur Clifford")</script> he published <span class="italic">A Topographical and Historical Description of the Parish of Tixall</span> (1817), to which he appended his <span class="italic">Flora Tixalliana</span> (reissued separately in the following year). Some of his devotional reflections were published privately, at his own expense, in 1814. His works were published under the surname Clifford. He appears to have resided at Paris towards the end of his life, and his <span class="italic">Description of Tixall</span> was issued there. A zealous Roman Catholic, he is said to have been a follower of the Abb&eacute; Carron (1760&#150;1821), an influential French cleric active in London between 1799 and 1814. He died at Ghent on 25 February 1823.<br>
<br>Thompson Cooper<span class="italic">, rev. </span> Alexander Goldbloom </div>
