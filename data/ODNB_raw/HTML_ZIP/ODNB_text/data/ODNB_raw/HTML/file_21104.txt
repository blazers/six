<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Paget, Sir  Charles</span>  (1778&#150;1839), <span class="occ">naval officer</span>, born on 7 October 1778, was fifth son of Henry Bayly Paget, ninth Baron Paget, first earl of Uxbridge of the second creation (1744&#150;1812), and his wife, Jane (1746&#150;1817), daughter of the Revd Arthur Champagne, dean of Clonmacnoise. <script>writeSeealsoLink('../view/article/21112/', "Henry William Paget, first marquess of Anglesey")</script>, <script>writeSeealsoLink('../view/article/21101/', "Sir Arthur Paget")</script>, and <script>writeSeealsoLink('../view/article/21106/', "Sir Edward Paget")</script> were elder brothers. Paget entered the navy in 1790 under the patronage of Sir Andrew Snape Douglas, and, having served in the North Sea and the channel, was, on 12 December 1796, promoted lieutenant of the guardship <i>Centaur</i> in the Thames. On 27 June 1797 he was promoted to command the sloop <i>Martin</i> in the North Sea, and on 17 October 1797 was posted to the <i>Penelope</i> in the channel. From October 1798 to April 1801 he commanded the <i>Brilliant</i> in the channel, and afterwards the <i>Hydra</i> in the channel and the Mediterranean until November 1802. On 30 March 1803 he commissioned the large frigate <i>Endymion</i>, and commanded her for the next two years, cruising in the channel, the Bay of Biscay, and on the coast of Spain or Portugal. In 1804 he captured four Spanish treasure ships from South America, gaining &pound;26,000 prize money, much of which he spent on a country seat and a wife. He married, on 7 March 1805, Elizabeth Araminta (<i>d</i>. 1843), daughter of Henry Monck of Foure, co. Westmeath; they had four sons and six daughters.<br>
<br>Paget was superseded in April 1805. He afterwards commanded various frigates or ships of the line in the channel, and from 1812 to 1814 the <i>Superb</i> (74 guns), in the Bay of Biscay and on the coast of North America. From 1817 to 1819 he was in command of one of the royal yachts in attendance on the prince regent; on 19 October 1819 he was made a KCH; on 30 January 1822 he was appointed groom of the bedchamber; and on 9 April 1823 was promoted rear-admiral. From 1828 to 1831 he was commander-in-chief at Cork, and was made a GCH on 3 March 1832. On 10 January 1837 he was made vice-admiral, and commanded on the North America and West Indies station until his death. Paget sat as tory MP for the family boroughs of Milborne Port (1804&#150;6) and Caernarfon Boroughs (1806&#150;26, 1831&#150;3, and 1833&#150;4). He died of yellow fever at St Thomas, Jamaica, on 27 January 1839.<br>
<br>J. K. Laughton<span class="italic">, rev. </span> Andrew Lambert </div>
