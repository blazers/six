<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Lorkin,  Thomas</span>  (<em>c.</em>1528&#150;1591), <span class="occ">physician</span>, was born at Frindsbury in Kent, to Thomas Lorkin and Joan Huxley. He matriculated at Pembroke College, Cambridge, on 12 November 1549, graduated BA in 1552, proceeding MA in 1555, and was created MD in 1560. Lorkin married Catherine (<i>d</i>. 1582), daughter of John Hatcher, regius professor of physic, in November 1560. They had a son and six daughters, one of whom married Edward Lively, regius professor of Hebrew at Cambridge.<br>
<br>Lorkin was at first, from 1551 to 1553, a fellow of Queens' College, but from 1553 until 1560 was a fellow of Peterhouse. On 21 April 1564 he was created regius professor of physic; he was respondent in the <span class="roman">Physic Act</span> kept before the queen in the same year, and in 1590 he obtained a grant of arms for the five regius professors. From 1572 until 1585 he was rector of Little Waltham in Essex. He had become a Catholic in his youth, and in later years opposed puritan preaching in the university.<br>
<br>Lorkin published one work: <span class="italic">Recta regula et victus ratio pro studiosis et literatis</span> (1562). His &#145;Carmen Latinum decastichon&#146; is prefixed to the manuscript <span class="italic">Historia Anglicana</span> by John Herd  (Cotton. MS Julius, C., ii.136).<br>
<br>Lorkin died on 1 May 1591, and was buried in Great St Mary's Church, Cambridge. In his will he left certain estates to Pembroke College, Queens' College, and Peterhouse. His medical books he bequeathed to the university library. About 140 volumes reached the library in December 1594; many of them contain annotations by Lorkin, and have his monogram on the title page.<br>
<br>
<a name="cosubject_17020"></a><span class="headword">Thomas Lorkyn</span>  (<em>d.</em> 1625), <span class="occ">diplomat</span>, graduated BA from Emmanuel College, Cambridge, in 1601, proceeded MA in 1604, and was incorporated at Oxford on 30 August 1605. He accompanied Thomas Puckering on his travels between 1611 and 1613, and in 1619&#150;20 he journeyed with the second son of Robert Cary, earl of Monmouth. In 1623 he was secretary to the embassy at Paris which negotiated the marriage of Prince Charles and Henrietta Maria. He continued to correspond with Puckering after they had parted, and many of his letters appear in Birch's <span class="italic">Court and Times of James I</span> (1848). Two addressed to the earl of Carlisle are in the British Library  (MS Egerton 2596, fols. 57, 112). Lorkyn was drowned in a channel storm about November 1625.<br>
<br>W. A. J. Archbold<span class="italic">, rev. </span> Sarah Bakewell </div>
