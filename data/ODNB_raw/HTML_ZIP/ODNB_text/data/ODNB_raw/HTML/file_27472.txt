<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Tiptoft <span class="st"> [Tibetot]</span>,  Robert</span>, Lord Tiptoft  (1228?&#150;1298), <span class="occ">soldier and administrator</span>, succeeded to the lands of his father, Henry, in January 1250. He was a staunch supporter of Henry III and the Lord Edward during the troubles of the 1260s. In November 1265 he was made constable of Portchester Castle. He accompanied the Lord Edward to the Holy Land in 1270 [<i>see also </i><script>writeSeealsoLink('../view/theme/94804', "Lord Edward's crusade")</script>], and in January 1275 was made constable of Nottingham Castle. He took a leading part in the negotiations with, and campaigns in, Wales in 1277 and 1282&#150;3 and after the conquest become one of Edward I's key officers in Wales. He was appointed justice of south Wales on 8 June 1281 and held the post until his death; he was also constable of the royal castles of Carmarthen and Cardigan. He sat in parliament in 1290, but there is no record of the writs of summons.<br>
<br>Tiptoft took a leading part in the suppression of the revolt of Rhys ap Maredudd in 1287&#150;8. Rhys's pretext for revolt was the compulsory introduction of &#145;English customs&#146; by Tiptoft. Tiptoft took Rhys's chief castles, but he did not effect his capture until 1292. In 1294 Tiptoft was appointed a counsellor and lieutenant of John of Brittany, earl of Richmond, in the expedition sent to recover Gascony. John of Brittany sent him to negotiate an alliance with Sancho IV of Castile. He was also left in command of Rions on the retreat of the English army before Charles of Valois, but had to surrender on 7 April 1295. He was summoned to take part in Edward I's Scottish expedition of 1297, and died at his manor of Nettlestead (Suffolk) on 22 May 1298.<br>
<br>With his wife, Eve (perhaps a member of the Chaworth family of Kidwelly), Tiptoft had a surviving son, Pain (1279&#150;1314), who is commonly reckoned first Lord Tibetot or Tiptoft. His son, John (1313&#150;1367), second lord, was grandfather of John Tiptoft (<i>c</i>.1378&#150;1443).<br>
<br>W. E. Rhodes<span class="italic">, rev. </span> R. R. Davies </div>
