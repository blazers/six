<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Boaden,  John</span>  (1792/3&#150;1839), <span class="occ">portrait and figure painter</span>, was one of the nine children of <script>writeSeealsoLink('../view/article/2730/', "James Boaden (1762&#150;1839)")</script>, the dramatist and critic, and his wife, Sarah (1763/4&#150;1838). Boaden exhibited at the Royal Academy from 1810 to 1833, at the British Institution from 1810 to 1839, and at the Society of British Artists from 1827 to 1840. Boaden's sitters included many theatrical figures, such as the celebrated actress Fanny Kemble (1809&#150;1893) (ex Sothebys, 25 November 1998). He often portrayed actors and actresses in their stage roles, and his dramatic sketches of John Philip Kemble in eight of his principal characters (exh. British Institution, 1818) were seen by Kemble himself, who &#145;thought the whole of them exceedingly like him&#146;  (Lane, 1). They were later lithographed by Richard Lane. Boaden assisted with illustrations for his father's book entitled <span class="italic">An inquiry into the authenticity of various pictures and prints, which from the decease of the poet to our own times have been offered to the public as portraits of Shakespeare</span> (1824). According to Mark Haworth-Booth, Boaden's portrait of the poet the Revd C. H. Townshend (exh. RA, 1828; Victoria and Albert Museum, London, Townshend bequest) captures an early &#145;Byronic and Pushkinian splendour&#146; which predates the sitter's melancholy decline  (Haworth-Booth, 12). He also painted rustic peasant figures, set in landscape and often in regional or national costume, notably Constantine Soferes (Victoria and Albert Museum, London, Townshend bequest), a youth in Albanian costume.<br>
<br>Boaden's style was compared to Sir Thomas Lawrence and Harlow  (Seguier, 21), but his work, according to Redgrave, &#145;although pleasing, did not rise above mediocrity&#146;  (Redgrave, 45). Boaden died, unmarried, at his home, 58 Newman Street, Oxford Street, London, on 4 April 1839. He was buried at Kensal Green.<br>
<br>Rebecca Vir&aacute;g </div>
