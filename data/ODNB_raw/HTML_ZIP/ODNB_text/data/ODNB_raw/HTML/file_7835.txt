<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Dormer,  James</span>  (1679&#150;1741), <span class="occ">army officer</span>, was born on 16 March 1679, the son of Robert Dormer (1628?&#150;1689) of Dorton, Buckinghamshire, and his second wife, Anne [<i>see </i><script>writeSeealsoLink('../view/article/66726/', "Dormer, Anne")</script>], daughter of the diplomat <script>writeSeealsoLink('../view/article/6397/', "Sir Charles Cotterell")</script>. He was appointed lieutenant and captain 1st regiment of foot guards in May 1702, in which rank he was wounded at Blenheim, and later served at Ramillies. Appointed colonel of Lord Mohun's regiment of foot in May 1708, he went to Spain, and distinguished himself at Saragossa in 1709, and was taken prisoner with General James Stanhope at Brihuega in Castile in December 1710. He was awarded &pound;200 for his losses by pillage at Brihuega and at Bilbao on his way home on parole  (<span class="italic">Calendar of Treasury Papers</span>, 137.8). He was made brigadier-general in February 1711; his regiment was disbanded in 1713. In 1715 he was commissioned as colonel to raise a regiment of dragoons in the south of England, which became the 14th hussars. He commanded a brigade during the Jacobite rising in Lancashire, and was engaged with the rebels at Preston. Transferred to the colonelcy of the 6th foot in 1720, in June 1725 he was sent as envoy-extraordinary to Lisbon, where he had a dispute with Thomas Burnett, the British consul; he was appointed a major-general in 1727 and lieutenant-general in 1735, colonel 1st troop of Horse Grenadier Guards in 1737, and governor of Hull in 1740.<br>
<br>Dormer was a member of the <script>writeSeealsoLink('../view/theme/73609', "Kit-Cat Club")</script>, and, according to Nichols, the collector of a fine library. He died at Crendon, Buckinghamshire, on 24 December 1741 where he was buried on 2 January 1742. He was unmarried, and bequeathed the estates of Chearsley in Buckinghamshire and Rousham in Oxfordshire to his cousin <script>writeSeealsoLink('../view/article/6399/', "Sir Clement Cottrell (afterwards Cottrell-Dormer)")</script>, master of the ceremonies to George II.<br>
<br>H. M. Chichester<span class="italic">, rev. </span> Jonathan Spain </div>
