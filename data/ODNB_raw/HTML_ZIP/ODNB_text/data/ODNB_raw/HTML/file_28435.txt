<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Waldegrave,  George Granville</span>, second Baron Radstock  (1786&#150;1857), <span class="occ">naval officer</span>, eldest son of <script>writeSeealsoLink('../view/article/28444/', "William Waldegrave, first Baron Radstock (1753&#150;1825)")</script>, and his wife, Cornelia (1762/3&#150;1839), second daughter of David Van Lennep, of the Dutch factory at Smyrna, was born in London on 24 September 1786. In 1794 his name was placed on the books of the <i>Courageux</i>, commanded by his father, but he seems to have first gone to sea in 1798 in the <i>Agincourt</i>, his father's flagship at Newfoundland. After eight years' service, on 16 February 1807, he was made captain. From 1807 to 1811 he commanded the <i>Thames</i> (32 guns), in the Mediterranean, and from 1811 to 1815 the <i>Volontaire</i> in the Mediterranean, and afterwards on the north coast of Spain. During these eight years he was almost constantly engaged in attacking the enemy's coasting trade, in destroying coast batteries, or in cutting out and destroying armed vessels. After paying off the <i>Volontaire</i> (38 guns), he had no further service. On 4 June 1815 he was made a CB.<br>
<br>Waldegrave married, on 7 August 1823, Esther Caroline (1800&#150;1874), youngest daughter of John Puget of Totteridge, Hertfordshire, a director of the Bank of England; they had one son and two daughters. On 20 August 1825 he succeeded his father as Lord Radstock. From September 1831 to November 1841 he was naval aide-de-camp to the monarch, and on 23 November 1841 was made rear-admiral. He became vice-admiral on 1 July 1851.<br>
<br>During his last forty years Radstock was active in the administration of naval charities, promoted the study of the French wars, and formed a valuable collection of books and pamphlets on naval history. This was presented by his widow, Esther, Lady Radstock, to the library of the Royal United Service Institution. Radstock was a tory, with strongly held views on naval subjects, shown by his commissioning a new biography of Earl Howe, to refute whig aspersions in Edward Brenton's <span class="italic">Naval History of Great Britain</span>.<br>
<br>Radstock died on 11 May 1857 at 26 Portland Place, London, and was buried in Highgate cemetery. He was succeeded by his son, <script>writeSeealsoLink('../view/article/47065/', "Granville Augustus William Waldegrave, third Baron Radstock (1833&#150;1913)")</script>, lay evangelist and philanthropist, who visited India, &#145;distributing Bibles among the natives in memory of Queen Victoria&#146;  (<span class="italic">Annual Register</span>, 1913, 122).<br>
<br>J. K. Laughton<span class="italic">, rev. </span> Andrew Lambert </div>
