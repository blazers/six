<span class="headword">Hesletine <span class="st"> [Heseltine]</span>,  James</span>  (<em>c.</em>1692&#150;1763), <span class="occ">organist and composer</span>, was trained by Dr John Blow of the Chapel Royal. He left the choir there when his voice broke in 1707. In January 1711 he was appointed organist of Durham Cathedral, which post he held until his death. He is reported to have been additionally organist of St Katharine by the Tower, London, meeting his obligations there by use of a deputy. Much of his music has been destroyed: in a fit of pique over some misunderstanding at Durham, he apparently tore all his own compositions from the cathedral choirbooks, but not before six of them had been published in Durham in <span class="italic">A Collection of Anthems</span> (1749). An anthem, &#145;Unto thee will I cry&#146;, dated 17 September 1707, is in the British Library (Add. MS 30860), and other manuscript pieces are in Lambeth Palace Library. On 24 February 1730 he married Frances Wheler, daughter of <script>writeSeealsoLink('../view/article/29193/', "Sir George Wheler")</script>, canon of Durham. He died in Durham a childless widower on 20 June 1763, and was buried in the galilee of Durham Cathedral. His property was claimed by a nephew and niece in America.<br>
<br>L. M. Middleton<span class="italic">, rev. </span> K. D. Reynolds </div>
