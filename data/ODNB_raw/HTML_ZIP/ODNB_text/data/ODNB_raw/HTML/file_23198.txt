<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Rawson,  George</span>  (1807&#150;1889), <span class="occ">hymn writer</span>, was born in Park Square, Leeds, on 5 June 1807 into a Congregationalist family. Educated at Clunie's School, Manchester, he was articled to a firm of Leeds solicitors, and ultimately practised for himself. Retiring from business, he went to Clifton, Bristol, where he attended Highbury Chapel. He died at Clifton on 25 March 1889.<br>
<br>Rawson was an active Congregationalist layman and hymn writer. His earliest verses appeared anonymously, under the signature of a Leeds Layman, because he refused to allow his name to be appended to his hymns. In 1853 he assisted the Congregationalist ministers of Leeds in compiling the <span class="italic">Leeds Hymn-Book</span>, to which he contributed six hymns. He wrote twenty-seven compositions for the <span class="italic">Psalms and Hymns for the Use of the Baptist Denomination</span> (1858). He also published two notable collections of his own verses: <span class="italic">Hymns, Verses and Chants</span> (1876) and <span class="italic">Songs of Spiritual Thought</span> (1885). His best-known hymns are &#145;By Christ redeemed&#146;, &#145;Father in high heaven dwelling&#146;, and &#145;We limit not the truth of God&#146;.<br>
<br>J. C. Hadden<span class="italic">, rev. </span> Leon Litvack </div>
