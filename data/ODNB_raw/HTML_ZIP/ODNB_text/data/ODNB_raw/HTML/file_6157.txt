<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Cooke,  Edward</span>  (<em>fl. </em>1676&#150;1678), <span class="occ">playwright and translator</span>, wrote <span class="italic">The Divine Epicurus, or, The Empire of Pleasure over the Vertues</span> (1676), translated from the philosopher Antoine Le Grand. In 1678 he produced <span class="italic">A Just and Seasonable Reprehension of Naked Breasts and Shoulders</span>, translated from the work of Jacques Boileau. Cooke's major work was <span class="italic">Love's Triumph, or, The Royal Union</span> (1678), a drama dedicated to Mary, princess of Orange, which portrays Oroondates, prince of Scythia, in love with Statira, and Artaxerxes, prince of Persia, in love with Berenice, and ends with nuptials.<br>
<br>Another Edward Cooke wrote <span class="italic">Bartas junior, or, The world's epitome: man, set forth in his 1. Generation, 2. Degeneration, 3. Rejuvenation</span>, published in London in 1631 though probably drafted twelve years earlier. He may also have been responsible for <span class="italic">Duello foiled, or, The whole proceedings in the orderly disposing of a design for a single fight between two valiant gentleman</span> (n.d.).<br>
<br>F. D. A. Burns </div>
