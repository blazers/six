<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Hughes,  William</span>  (1587/8&#150;1663?), <span class="occ">translator and compiler of legal works</span>, was the eldest son of Reginald Hughes (<i>d</i>. 1616), a citizen of London and member of the Fishmongers' Company, and his wife, Margaret. Hughes matriculated at St Alban Hall, Oxford, on 18 January 1605, aged seventeen, and was admitted to Gray's Inn on 7 November 1606. There is no date for his call to the bar, but he was made an ancient on 7 June 1627, and on 14 March 1628 his son, also William, was admitted to the inn.<br>
<br>Hughes was more of a translator and compiler of legal works than a reporter of cases in the strictest sense. His first published work was <span class="italic">The Parson's Law</span> (1641). On 25 September 1645 the Stationers' Company records an entry for <span class="italic">The Mirror of Justice &hellip; Translated out of the Old French into English by William Hughes of Gray's Inn</span>. Other works followed in the 1650s, including <span class="italic">Reports of Certain Cases &hellip; Reviewed &hellip; by &hellip; Justice Godbolt</span>, published by Hughes in 1652. <span class="italic">The Commentaries upon Original Writs</span>, which produced the original writs from the books, duly edited, was published in 1655. <span class="italic">An Exact Abridgement of Public Acts</span>, covering 1640&#150;56, appeared in 1657, and in 1659 he produced <span class="italic">The Declarations and Other Pleadings</span> in Coke's reports.<br>
<br>Hughes continued to publish after the Restoration. <span class="italic">The Grand Abridgement of the Law Continued</span>, a three-volume collection of the principal case and points of the common law of England, appeared in 1660&#150;63. In 1663 <span class="italic">An Exact Abridgement of All the Statutes in Force</span>, a comparative work of the sixteenth to eighteenth years of Charles I, and the twelfth to fourteenth of Charles II, was published, as was <span class="italic">Gregories Moot-Book &hellip; much Inlarged by William Hughes</span>. <span class="italic">An Exact Abridgement in England of the Cases Reported by Sir F. Moore</span>, collected by Hughes, was published in 1665.<br>
<br>The date of death of William Hughes is unknown, but is conjectured to be 1663.<br>
<br>Stuart Handley </div>
