<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Bonar,  James</span>  (1757&#150;1821), <span class="occ">Greek scholar and writer</span>, was born on 29 September 1757, the eighth son of the ten children of <script>writeSeealsoLink('../view/article/2816/', "John Bonar (1721&#150;1761)")</script>, minister of Cockpen and Perth, and Christian Currier (1724&#150;1771). He was educated at Edinburgh high school and at the University of Edinburgh. Bonar entered the office of the solicitor of excise where his elder brother <script>writeSeealsoLink('../view/article/2817/', "John Bonar")</script> was employed; John later became the first solicitor of excise in Scotland. James, by contrast, is better known for his scholarship. On 9 December 1777 he was admitted as a member of the Speculative Society of Edinburgh University (of which John Bonar was a co-founder) and was elected an extraordinary member on 24 December 1781. For several years he was treasurer of the Royal Society of Edinburgh. He was one of the original promoters of the Astronomical Institution, and a founder of the Edinburgh Subscription Library in 1794. In March 1797 he married Marjory Maitland (<i>d</i>. 1854), daughter of James Pyott, bailie of Montrose.<br>
<br>Bonar contributed the article &#145;Posts&#146; in <span class="italic">Encyclopaedia Britannica</span> (1794) and the entries, among others, &#145;Alphabet characters&#146;, &#145;Etymology&#146;, &#145;Excise&#146;, and &#145;Hieroglyphics&#146;, for the <span class="italic">Edinburgh Encyclopaedia</span> (1808&#150;18). He wrote <span class="italic">Disquisition on the Origin and Radical Sense of the Greek Prepositions</span> (1804), edited a new edition of <span class="italic">Ewing's Greek Grammar</span>, and published an English edition of Hans Holbein's <span class="italic">The Dance of Death</span> in 1788. A regular contributor to the <span class="italic">Edinburgh Magazine</span>, <span class="italic">Missionary Magazine</span>, and <span class="italic">Scottish Register</span> in the period 1790&#150;95, Bonar also wrote the memoir of his brother <script>writeSeealsoLink('../view/article/2813/', "Archibald Bonar (1753&#150;1816)")</script>, which is prefixed to the second volume of the latter's <span class="italic">Sermons, Chiefly on Devotional Subjects</span> (1815&#150;17). James Bonar died on 25 March 1821. He was survived by his wife and eight children including <script>writeSeealsoLink('../view/article/2818/', "John James Bonar (1803&#150;1891)")</script> [<i>see under </i><script>writeSeealsoLink('../view/article/2812/', "Bonar, Andrew Alexander")</script>], <script>writeSeealsoLink('../view/article/2814/', "Horatius Bonar (1808&#150;1889)")</script>, and <script>writeSeealsoLink('../view/article/2812/', "Andrew Alexander Bonar (1810&#150;1892)")</script>.<br>
<br>Philip Carter </div>
