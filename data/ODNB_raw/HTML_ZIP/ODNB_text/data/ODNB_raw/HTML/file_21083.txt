<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Pagan,  James</span>  (1811&#150;1870), <span class="occ">newspaper editor and antiquary</span>, the son of James Pagan and Elizabeth Blackstock, was born on 18 October 1811 at Trailflat, in the parish of Tinwald, near Dumfries, where his father was a bleacher. The family removed to Dumfries shortly after James's birth, and he received a sound education at Dumfries Academy. On leaving school he was apprenticed as a compositor in the office of the <span class="italic">Dumfries Courier</span>, and afterwards became a reporter for the paper. He soon left to become partner in a printing firm in London; but in 1839 he settled in Glasgow on the staff of the <span class="italic">Glasgow Herald</span>, and also edited a little broadsheet, the <span class="italic">Prospective Observer</span>. In 1841 Pagan married Ann, <i>n&eacute;e</i> McNight-Kerr, a native of Dumfries and a friend of Robert Burns's widow, Jean. They had three sons (two of whom died in infancy) and two daughters.<br>
<br>In 1856 Pagan was appointed successor to George Outram as editor of the <span class="italic">Glasgow Herald</span>, which he converted in January 1859 from a tri-weekly into a daily paper. He was also, from 1857, the correspondent of <span class="italic">The Times</span> in Glasgow. Under his editorship the <span class="italic">Herald</span> became one of the first provincial daily papers. Pagan greatly expanded coverage of local news, verbatim reporting of political and religious speeches, and news by telegraph. He was a shrewd, genial man, and a keen snuff taker (he used his snuff-box also as a store for shorthand notes).<br>
<br>Pagan was a devoted student of Glasgow's history and antiquities, and published <span class="italic">Sketches of the History of Glasgow</span> (1847), <span class="italic">History of the Cathedral and See of Glasgow</span> (1851), <span class="italic">Glasgow Past and Present; Illustrated in Dean of Guild Reports</span> (2 vols., 1851), <span class="italic">Old Glasgow and its Environs</span> (1864), and <span class="italic">Relics of Ancient Architecture and other Picturesque Scenes in Glasgow</span> (1885). Pagan died in Glasgow on 11 February 1870.<br>
<br>George Stronach<span class="italic">, rev. </span> H. C. G. Matthew </div>
