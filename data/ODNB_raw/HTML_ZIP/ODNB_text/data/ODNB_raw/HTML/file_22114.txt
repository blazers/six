<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Philipps, Sir  Erasmus</span>, fifth baronet  (1699&#150;1743), <span class="occ">politician</span>, was born on 8 November 1699, the eldest son of <script>writeSeealsoLink('../view/article/37850/', "Sir John Philipps, fourth baronet (c.1666&#150;1737)")</script>, of Picton Castle, Pembrokeshire, and his wife, Mary (<i>d</i>. 1722), the daughter and heir of Anthony Smith, an East India merchant. His cousin Katherine Shorter was the first wife of Sir Robert Walpole. He is sometimes wrongly identified as the author of several economic tracts, including <span class="italic">An Appeal to Common Sense, or, Some Considerations Offer'd to Restore Publick Credit</span> (1720) and <span class="italic">The State of the Nation in Respect to her Commerce, Debts, and Money</span> (1725). These publications were in fact the work of <script>writeSeealsoLink('../view/article/73339/', "Erasmus Philips (fl. 1720&#150;1751)")</script>.<br>
<br>Philipps was educated privately before matriculating at Pembroke College, Oxford, on 4 August 1720. He left Oxford in the following year without graduating (extracts from the diary he kept at the university are reprinted in <span class="italic">N&amp;Q</span>, 2nd ser., 10, 1860, 365&#150;6, 443&#150;5), and entered as a student at Lincoln's Inn on 7 August 1721. On 5 January 1737 he succeeded as fifth baronet following his father's death. Since February 1726 he had been MP for Haverfordwest, a seat he held for the rest of his life. He was a loyal supporter of the Walpole administration, although he did vote against the ministry over the selection of the chairman for the election committee in December 1741. He died, unmarried, on 15 October 1743 from drowning when he was thrown from his horse into the River Avon. On his death the baronetcy passed to his brother <script>writeSeealsoLink('../view/article/39715/', "John Philipps")</script>, MP for Carmarthen and later Petersfield and then Pembrokeshire.<br>
<br>W. A. S. Hewins<span class="italic">, rev. </span> Philip Carter </div>
