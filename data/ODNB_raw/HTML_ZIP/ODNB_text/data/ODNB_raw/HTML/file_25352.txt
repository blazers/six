<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Shepreve <span class="st"> [Sheprey]</span>,  William</span>  (<em>c.</em>1540&#150;1598), <span class="occ">Roman Catholic priest and scholar</span>, nephew of John Shepreve, was born near Abingdon, Berkshire, about 1540. On 16 February 1555 he was elected to a scholarship at Corpus Christi College, Oxford, &#145;aged fourteen or thereabouts&#146;  (Wood, <span class="italic">Ath. Oxon.</span>, 2.668). He was made a probationer of his college in November 1558 and graduated BA on 15 February 1560, &#145;which was the highest degree he took in this university&#146;  (ibid.).<br>
<br>A devoted Catholic, Shepreve left Oxford for the Low Countries. In 1574 he arrived at the English College in Douai and was ordained to the priesthood later that year. Soon after his arrival at Douai, Shepreve continued his studies and began to read for a BTh at the Catholic university there. He was examined on 20 November 1575 and admitted BTh on 23 December. On 5 May 1576 he was appointed principal of the College of Anchin, also in the diocese of Arras. He is recorded as one of the doctors who were admitted DTh at Douai.<br>
<br>Shepreve was sent to Rome on 9 November, intending to live in Italy and to help establish an English college there; he accompanied doctors Stapleton, White, and Martin. In Rome he was &#145;exhibited to&#146; (maintained by) Gabriele Paleotti, cardinal archbishop of Bologna, whom he served as a chaplain. Two letters from Bologna to the rector of the English College, one by Paleotti and another by Shepreve, survive. A capable writer and biblical interpreter Shepreve devoted his skills to providing a thorough companion to the Marian office. His <span class="italic">Connexio literalis psalmorum in officio beatae Mariae virginis</span> (Rome, 1596) was the only work to be published. In manuscript, Shepreve left two minor glosses on the <span class="roman">Pauline epistles</span> and an exposition of the Marian office as well as an anthology of popular scriptural passages.<br>
<br>Shepreve died at St Severian's, Rome, in 1598, &#145;to the great reluctance of those who knew him&#146;  (Wood, <span class="italic">Ath. Oxon.</span>, 2.668).<br>
<br>J. Andreas L&ouml;we </div>
