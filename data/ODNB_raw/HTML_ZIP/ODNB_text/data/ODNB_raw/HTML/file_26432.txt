<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Stevenson, Sir  John Andrew</span>  (1761&#150;1833), <span class="occ">composer</span>, was born at Dublin in November 1761, the second of the two sons of John Stevenson, of Glasgow, a violinist in the state band in Dublin. He was a chorister of Christ Church, Dublin, under Dr Richard Woodward, from 1771 to 1775, and a member of St Patrick's Cathedral choir from 1775 to 1780. He became a vicar-choral in St Patrick's in 1783, and in Christ Church in 1800. He was created MusD of Trinity College, Dublin, in 1791, and was knighted in 1803. In 1814 he was appointed the first organist and music director at the castle chapel.<br>
<br>As a composer, Stevenson was influenced by Haydn. He is remembered mainly for <span class="italic">A Selection of Irish Melodies</span>, first published in 1808, arrangements of Irish airs with words by Thomas Moore, which was reprinted many times during the nineteenth century. He composed several operas, including <span class="italic">The Contract</span>, a comic opera written with Philip Cogan and first performed in 1782, <span class="italic">Love in a Blaze</span> (1799), and <span class="italic">The Bedouins</span> (1801). His only important church music was a selection of services and anthems published in two volumes in 1825. An oratorio, <span class="italic">The Thanksgiving</span>, was performed in 1831 at the Dublin festival, and twenty-six <span class="italic">Sacred Melodies</span> for the psalms were issued in 1822. He also wrote 173 glees and catches for the Hibernian Catch Club.<br>
<br>Affable and sociable, Stevenson dressed in the height of fashion and moved in the best circles. He married Anne, the widow of William Singleton and the daughter of a Mr Morton of Rahoboth, near Dublin, who worked in the custom house. She died in 1802. The couple had two sons and two daughters. Stevenson died on 14 September 1833 at Headfort House, Kells, co. Meath, the seat of Thomas Taylour, second marquess of Headfort, whose first wife was Olivia (<i>d</i>. 1834), Stevenson's elder daughter. He was buried on 17 September in the Lambart vault of St Mary's Church, Painstown, near Beau Park, co. Meath.<br>
<br>J. C. Hadden<span class="italic">, rev. </span> Anne Pimlott Baker </div>
