<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Knowles,  John</span>  (1781&#150;1841), <span class="occ">naval surveyor and biographer</span>, was born on 24 March 1781 at 14 Union Street, Deptford, London, the eldest of the three sons of John Knowles (<i>d</i>. 1814), shipwright, and his wife, Mary Posgate, and was baptized on 19 April 1781 at St Paul's, Deptford. He was privately educated at Bexleyheath. On 30 March 1798 he was appointed as one of the four clerks to the surveyors in the Navy Office. By 1806 he had risen to chief clerk (salary &pound;500, later &pound;650, p.a.). He was superannuated with a pension of &pound;498 in August 1832, thereafter becoming managing director of the Economic Life Assurance Society until 1835.<br>
<br>Knowles was the author of <span class="italic">An Inquiry into the Means Taken to Preserve the British Navy</span> (1821), in recognition of which he was elected, in July 1821, fellow of the Royal Society, and <span class="italic">The Principles and Practice of Constructing Ships</span> (1822). Knowles is best-known, however, for his long friendship with <script>writeSeealsoLink('../view/article/10254/', "Henry Fuseli")</script> and his biography of the painter. Knowles was introduced to Fuseli by their mutual friend John Bonnycastle in June 1805. Since the Navy Office was only a few steps from the Royal Academy across Somerset Place, Knowles and Fuseli saw each other almost daily for the next twenty years. The <span class="italic">Life and Writings of Henry Fuseli</span> (3 vols., 1831) was an expanded version of Knowles's anonymous memoir in the <span class="italic">Annual Biography and Obituary for the Year 1826</span> (232&#150;70). Knowles's biography is notable for its factual accuracy; the accompanying volumes made accessible all of Fuseli's writings on art, including the <span class="italic">Lectures on Painting</span> (comprising twelve lectures, separately published by Knowles in 1830), the &#145;Aphorisms on Art&#146;, and the unfinished &#145;History of Art in Italy&#146;. Knowles was executor to both the painter and his widow, Sophia (<i>d</i>. 1832). Knowles's collection of paintings included choice works by Giorgione, Titian, Rembrandt, Van Dyck, Gainsborough, and Reynolds, and fourteen paintings by Fuseli.<br>
<br>Knowles died, unmarried, at Ashburton, Devon, on 21 July 1841, having moved from London in 1840 to live with his brother Samuel Posgate Knowles. He was buried in the Whitheare family tomb in Ashburton churchyard. His portrait was painted by S. P. Knowles, Samuel Drummond, and Charles Landseer, and a marble bust was executed by William Behnes (exh. RA, 1836). Photographs of these remain in a private collection. Knowles was one of the original members of the Athenaeum and a lithograph by &#145;W. D.&#146; after Landseer's portrait was included in Thomas McLean's <span class="italic">Athenaeum Portraits</span> (no. 25).<br>
<br>D. H. Weinglass </div>
