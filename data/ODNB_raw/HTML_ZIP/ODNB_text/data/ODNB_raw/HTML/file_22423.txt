<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Pocock,  Isaac John Innes</span>  (1819&#150;1886), <span class="occ">barrister</span>, was born at Maidenhead, Berkshire, on 28 July 1819, the only son of <script>writeSeealsoLink('../view/article/22422/', "Isaac Pocock (1782&#150;1835)")</script>, playwright and painter, and his wife, Louisa Hime. After schooling at Eton College he studied at Merton College, Oxford, where he held a postmastership (scholarship), took second-class honours in classics in 1841, and graduated BA in 1842. Admitted to the Inner Temple in January 1842, he was called to the bar in November 1847 and was subsequently a JP for Berkshire. On 4 April 1850 he married Anna Louisa (<i>b</i>. 1824), daughter of Benjamin Currey of Eltham Park, Kent. In 1872 he printed privately <span class="italic">Franklin, and Other Poems</span>. He died at his home, Curtisfield, Maidenhead, Berkshire, on 28 May 1886; he was survived by his wife.<br>
<br>G. Le G. Norgate<span class="italic">, rev. </span> M. C. Curthoys </div>
