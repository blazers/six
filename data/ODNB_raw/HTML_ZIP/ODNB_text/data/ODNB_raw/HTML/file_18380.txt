<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Mamesfeld,  Henry</span>  (<em>c.</em>1270&#150;1328), <span class="occ">ecclesiastic</span>, belonged to a family that may have come from Mansfield in Nottinghamshire. He was ordained deacon and priest during 1296 in Lincoln diocese, the same year that he secured Flintham rectory, Nottinghamshire, in the diocese of York. He received licences of absence from successive archbishops to study for one year in 1297 and 1299 and for three years in 1306. Between 1288 and 1296 he was a fellow of Merton College. An MA of Oxford, he had incepted as a doctor of theology by 1309 when, on 27 October, his election as chancellor of the university was confirmed. In 1311, during his tenure, new statutes were drawn up for the &#145;scholars of Master William of Durham&#146;&#151;that is, University College. By December of the following year Mamesfeld had vacated the office.<br>
<br>Mamesfeld was one of those who attended the provincial council summoned to York in 1311 by Archbishop William Greenfield (<i>d</i>. 1315) for the examination of the templars in the northern province. A canon of Lincoln, he was collated on 22 May 1311 to the Sanctae Crucis prebend, and in 1316 to that of Asgarby. In 1315 he was elected dean of Lincoln and in January 1320 bishop, but he declined to accept the latter office. He died in the second half of 1328; probate of his will (the details are not extant) followed on 6 December.<br>
<br>The early fourteenth-century painted glass in twelve and possibly all fourteen side windows of the choir of Merton College was given by Mamesfeld. These windows depict saints in central panels beneath elaborate pinnacled canopies. All but two of those on the south-east have the saints flanked by kneeling figures of the donor&#151;also beneath canopies&#151;in gown, hood, and cap with the inscription: &#145;Magister Henricus de Mamesfeld me fecit&#146;. To Oriel College he gave a copy of Peter Lombard's <span class="italic">Sentences</span>, but his principal gift was of eight volumes to Merton College, one of which, the <span class="italic">Quodlibeta</span> of Godfrey de Montibus, is now Balliol College MS 211. The others comprised glossed versions of the gospels, the epistles of St Paul, the book of <span class="roman">Exodus</span>, and of the psalter, together with copies of St Thomas Aquinas's <span class="italic">De veritate</span> and <span class="italic">Contra Gentiles</span>, and Gregory's <span class="italic">Moralia</span>. William Wheatley, a master at the schools of Stamford and Lincoln, his contemporary at Oxford, included him in his dedication of a commentary on the <span class="italic">De consolatione philosophiae</span> of Boethius, which John Pits and Thomas Tanner, following him, attributed to Mamesfeld himself.<br>
<br>Roy Martin Haines </div>
