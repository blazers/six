<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Ready,  William James Durant</span>  (1823&#150;1873), <span class="occ">marine painter</span>, was born on 11 May 1823 in London, the son of a customs clerk. He taught himself painting, before moving to the United States for five years. He painted in oils and watercolours, mainly scenes of the south coast of England. Ready did not exhibit any paintings until 1861, when a watercolour, <span class="italic">View on the Thames</span>, was shown at the Suffolk Street Gallery in London. He exhibited three paintings at the British Institution&#151;<span class="italic">Gorlestone Pier, Suffolk</span> in 1861, <span class="italic">Dirty Weather Clearing off</span> in 1862, <span class="italic">Coast Scene Near Harwich</span> in 1865, and two at the Royal Academy in 1867. His pictures are usually signed W. F. R., but the Royal Academy paintings are signed W. F. Durant. The painter David Roberts is said to have admired his work. He died in Brighton, Sussex, on 29 November 1873.<br>
<br>Anne Pimlott Baker </div>
