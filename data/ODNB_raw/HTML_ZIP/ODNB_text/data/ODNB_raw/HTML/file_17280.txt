<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Lyon <span class="st"> [Lyoun]</span>,  John, of Auldbar</span>  (<em>fl. </em>1608&#150;1649), <span class="occ">poet</span>, was the eldest son of Sir Thomas Lyon of Auldbar and his first wife, Agnes (1540?&#150;1586?), daughter of Patrick, Lord Gray, and widow of Sir Robert Logan of Restalrig, and Alexander, fifth Lord Home. He was declared heir to his father on 6 August 1608. Subsequently he was frequently imprisoned for debt. On 16 February 1611, he married Eupham, a daughter of George Gladstanes, archbishop of St Andrews, but died childless. He is last mentioned in 1649, in the records of the commissary court of Brechin, as a citizen there; the date of his death is unknown.<br>
<br>Lyon has been identified with the author of &#145;Teares for the Death of Alexander, Earle of Dunfermeling&#146;, a poem of about 250 lines, printed by Andro Hart in 1622, and reprinted by the Bannatyne Club in 1823. The one copy of the original print known to exist is held in the Huntington Library. In the dedication to Lady Beatrix Ruthven, Lady Cowdenknowes, daughter of the first earl of Gowrie, the poet states that he is related to her by &#145;band of blood&#146;, and signs himself &#145;your Ladiships Cousen, most humblie devoted to serue you, John Lyoun.&#146; This may be explained by the relationship between the lady's husband, Home of Cowdenknowes, and Lyon of Auldbar's mother, by her marriage to Lord Home.<br>
<br>T. F. Henderson<span class="italic">, rev. </span> J. K. M<sup>c</sup>Ginley </div>
