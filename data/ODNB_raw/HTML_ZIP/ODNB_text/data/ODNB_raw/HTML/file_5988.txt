<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Colquhoun,  John</span>  (1748&#150;1827), <span class="occ">theological writer</span>, was born at Luss in Dunbartonshire in January 1748. His brother was the Revd James Colquhoun of Perth. He was originally a shepherd and weaver but, after attending a village school, he studied at Glasgow for the Scottish ministry. He was licensed in August 1780 and took up a charge in South Leith in March 1781. He was married; his wife's name was Euphemia. A supporter of the SPCK and the Edinburgh Continental Society, his publications include <span class="italic">A Treatise on Spiritual Comfort</span> (1831) and two treatises on the covenant of grace and of works. He died on 27 November 1827 at South Leith.<br>
<br>J. M. Rigg<span class="italic">, rev. </span> Emma Major </div>
