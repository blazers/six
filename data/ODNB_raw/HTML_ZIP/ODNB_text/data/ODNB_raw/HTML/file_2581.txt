<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Blake,  Malachi</span>  (<em>bap. </em>1687, <em>d.</em> 1760), <span class="occ">Presbyterian minister</span>, was born at Blagdon, near Taunton, Somerset, and baptized there on 16 August 1687, the second son of Malachi Blake (1651&#150;1705). He appears to have been educated and trained for the ministry by his father, an itinerant preacher in the south-west of England and afterwards Presbyterian minister at Wellington, Somerset, where he was credited with &#145;laying the foundation of the dissenting cause&#146;  (Murch, 244).<br>
<br>The younger Malachi Blake's first ministerial appointment was at Langport, Somerset, in 1712, and then from 1717 until his death at Blandford Forum, Dorset, where he proved a popular preacher. He was minister there when the town was badly damaged by fire on 4 June 1731. Afterwards he wrote <span class="italic">A Brief Account of the Dreadful Fire at Blandford Forum</span>, to which he added a sermon and an address to the inhabitants of the town. The work was published for private circulation in 1735. A second edition appeared in the same year and a third in 1736.<br>
<br>In later years Blake suffered increasingly from depression, and he died at Blandford Forum on 15 February 1760, aged seventy-two. His younger brother William (1688&#150;1772), a woolstapler, was father of Malachi (1724&#150;1795), Presbyterian minister of Whitney and Fullwood, and William (1730&#150;1799), Presbyterian minister of Crewkerne.<br>
<br>Alexander Gordon<span class="italic">, rev. </span> M. J. Mercer </div>
