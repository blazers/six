<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Puleston,  Hamlet</span>  (1632&#150;1662), <span class="occ">writer</span>, was born at Old Alresford, Hampshire, in 1632, the son of Richard Puleston (<i>b</i>. 1591), and Mary Marshall (<i>fl</i>. 1622&#150;1632), and nephew of <script>writeSeealsoLink('../view/article/22873/', "John Puleston")</script>. Hamlet's father was born at Burcott in Oxfordshire, but was descended from a Flintshire family. He graduated from Hart Hall, Oxford, BA in 1611, MA in 1613, BD in 1620, and DD in 1627. He obtained a fellowship at Wadham College, Oxford, which he resigned in 1619, and was a prebendary of Winchester in 1611&#150;16 and rector successively of Leckford, Hampshire (1616), Kingsworthy (1618), and Abbotsworthy. He was moderator of philosophy in 1614, and humanity lecturer in 1616 at Oxford.<br>
<br>Hamlet, admitted a scholar of Wadham on 20 August 1647, graduated BA on 23 May 1650 and proceeded MA on 25 April 1653. He at first declined to subscribe to the ordinances of the parliamentary visitors, but subsequently became a fellow of Jesus College, and was nominated <i>moderator dialecticae</i> on 19 May 1656. Wood says also that he became &#145;a preacher in those parts&#146;, presumably Oxfordshire  (Wood, <span class="italic">Ath. Oxon.</span>, 3.544). He ultimately settled in London. He published in 1661 <span class="italic">Monarchiae Britannicae singularis protectio, or, A brief historical essay tending to prove God's especial providence over the British monarchy</span>. A slight piece, its ambitions confined to a brief history of the monarchies of England, Scotland, Ireland, and Wales and the conjunction of the royal lines in the person of Charles II, it was reissued as the <span class="italic">Epitome monarchiae Britannicae</span> in 1663.<br>
<br>Puleston died in London at the beginning of 1662 &#145;in a poor condition and in an obscure house&#146;  (Wood, <span class="italic">Ath. Oxon.</span>, 3.544). The administration of his estate was granted on 27 April.<br>
<br>G. Le G. Norgate<span class="italic">, rev. </span> Sean Kelsey </div>
