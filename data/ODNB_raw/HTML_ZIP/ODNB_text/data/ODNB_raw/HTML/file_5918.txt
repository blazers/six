<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Veale,  George</span>  (<em>b. </em>1757, <em>d.</em> in or before 1833), <span class="occ">viola player</span>, was baptized on 2 October 1757 at St Andrew's Church, Holborn, London, the son of Timothy Veale and his wife, Elizabeth. He was first heard of in 1784, when he took part in the Handel memorial concerts. He played in the Covent Garden Theatre band and took part in the oratorio performances at the Drury Lane Theatre. He was admitted to the Royal Society of Musicians in 1788, and during the 1790s played in the annual concerts in St Paul's Cathedral sponsored by the Royal Society of Musicians. From 1812 to 1817 he played in the band at Drury Lane.<br>
<br>It used to be thought (incorrectly) that Veale was the pseudonymous author of a satire on Dr Burney's <span class="italic">Musical Tour</span> entitled <span class="italic">Musical Travels through England</span> (1774), which appeared under the name of Joel Collier. The second edition contains a fictitious account of the last illness and death of Collier. It was later suggested that it was written partly by John Laurence Bicknell and partly by Peter Beckford.<br>
<br>Veale had died by 6 January 1833, when the Royal Society of Musicians gave his sister &pound;12 to cover his funeral expenses.<br>
<br>Anne Pimlott Baker </div>
