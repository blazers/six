<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Sherman,  John</span>  (<em>d.</em> 1671), <span class="occ">historian and Church of England clergyman</span>, was the son of Samuel Sherman (<i>d</i>. 1644), clothier, of Dedham, Essex, and his wife, Esther (<i>d</i>. 1646), daughter of Robert Burgess. Sherman's early life is obscure, but in 1645 he was admitted as a pensioner at Queens' College, Cambridge, and he matriculated in 1647. He graduated BA in 1649&#150;50. He subscribed to the engagement, was elected unanimously a fellow of Jesus College, Cambridge, in 1650, and proceeded MA in 1653. He was incorporated at Oxford the same year.<br>
<br>Two years later, in 1655, Sherman was appointed rector of Graveley, Cambridgeshire. He was university proctor in 1660&#150;61, and the following year the university presented him to Wilmsloe in the diocese of Chester, but he was never instituted because the former incumbent retained the living through the <span class="roman">Act of Indemnity</span>. In 1662 Sherman became rector of Harlton, Cambridgeshire. He wrote <span class="italic">Historia Collegii Jesu Cantabrigiae</span>, recording the histories of the nunnery of St Rhadegund and the college which replaced it from their foundations to Edmund Boldero's mastership (1663&#150;79). Sherman's work is considered diligently researched and fairly reliable, although his royalism distorted his analysis of his own times. J. O. Halliwell printed an inaccurate version in 1840.<br>
<br>In 1663 Sherman was one of the syndics for restoring Lambeth's library, and in 1664 he was one of the twelve university preachers. He also contributed to the marble paving in Jesus College chapel. In 1665 he was admitted DD by royal mandate. A John Sherman was married on 2 November 1666 to Elizabeth Clare at Graveley. Sherman was appointed prebend and archdeacon of Sarum in 1670, died in London on 27 March 1671, and was buried in the chancel of Jesus College chapel. His will was proved on 29 March 1671 by his brother Edmund.<br>
<br>Sherman should not be confused with his namesake John Sherman DD, a graduate of Trinity College, Cambridge, and author of several religious tracts, who died, probably in Norwich, in 1663.<br>
<br>J. B. Mullinger<span class="italic">, rev. </span> S. L. Sadler </div>
