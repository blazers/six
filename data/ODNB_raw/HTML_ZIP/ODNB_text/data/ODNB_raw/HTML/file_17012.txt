<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Lord,  Henry</span>  (<em>b. </em>1563), <span class="occ">clergyman and ethnographer</span>, was born in Oxfordshire; his parents' names are unknown. He matriculated from Magdalen Hall, Oxford, on 15 April 1580, but seems to have left without a degree. In January 1624 the East India Company appointed him chaplain to the company factory at Surat in Gujarat for five years at a salary of &pound;60 per annum, and advanced him two months' pay and &pound;20 to buy books. Lord arrived with the rest of the English fleet in October 1624; by December 1625 he was threatening to return to England, but the president of the factory, Thomas Kerridge, forbade him to go.<br>
<br>At Kerridge's urging Lord studied the religion of the Hindu Banias, &#145;out of their Manuscripts, and by renewed accesse, with the helpe of Interpreters, made my collections out of a booke of theirs called the SHASTER&#146;  (H. Lord, <span class="italic">Display of Two Forraigne Sects</span>, pt 1, sig. B2<i>r</i>), as well as the <span class="roman">Zend-Avesta</span> of the Parsis. He gave accounts of these in his two-part work, <span class="italic">A display of two forraigne sects in the East Indies vizt: the sect of the Banians the ancient natives of India and the sect of the Persees the ancient inhabitants of Persia</span> (1630), which he dedicated to Archbishop Laud and to the East India Company. The book offered an invaluable account of Hindu and Parsi cosmography for European readers, albeit framed by a condemnation of their &#145;vaine Superstitions, and composed Forgery&#146;  (Lord, <span class="italic">Display</span>, pt 1, 93) deployed &#145;to beget in good Christians, the greater detestation of these Heresies&#146;  (ibid., pt 2, 53). Pierre Briot translated the work into French in 1667, and it was reprinted repeatedly, for example in Awnsham Churchill and John Churchill's <span class="italic">Collection of Voyages and Travels</span> (1732, 1744, 1752), in Bernard Picart's <span class="italic">C&eacute;r&eacute;monies et coutumes religieuses de tous les peuples du monde</span> (1723&#150;42), and in John Pinkerton's <span class="italic">General Collection of the Best and most Interesting Voyages</span> (1808&#150;14). The date and place of Lord's death are not known.<br>
<br>David Armitage </div>
