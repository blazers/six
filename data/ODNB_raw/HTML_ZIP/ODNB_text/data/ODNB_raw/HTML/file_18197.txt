<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Martin,  Jonathan</span>  (<em>c.</em>1705&#150;1737), <span class="occ">organist</span>, was a chorister in the Chapel Royal under Dr William Croft, and studied the organ with Thomas Roseingrave. He deputized for Roseingrave at St George's, Hanover Square, and for John Weldon at the Chapel Royal, before succeeding the latter as organist on 7 May 1736; Weldon's post of composer to the Chapel Royal went to William Boyce. The only known composition by Martin is the song &#145;To thee, o gentle sleep&#146; in Nicholas Rowe's <span class="italic">Tamerlane</span>. Shortly before his death he gave a concert at the Stationers' Hall, where &#145;though he had scarcely strength to sit upright, he played two voluntaries on the organ, showing fine invention and masterly hand&#146;  (Hawkins, 3.893). He died of consumption on 4 April 1737 and was buried in the west cloister of Westminster Abbey. An inscription for his tomb was written by Vincent Bourne and included in Bourne's <span class="italic">Miscellaneous Poems</span> (1772).<br>
<br>L. M. Middleton<span class="italic">, rev. </span> K. D. Reynolds </div>
