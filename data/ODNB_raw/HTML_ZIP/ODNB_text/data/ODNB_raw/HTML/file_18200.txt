<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Martin,  Leopold Charles</span>  (1817&#150;1889), <span class="occ">costume historian and numismatist</span>, was born on 6 December 1817, and was baptized on 7 January 1818 at St Mary Street, St Marylebone, Middlesex, the second son of <script>writeSeealsoLink('../view/article/18192/', "John Martin (1789&#150;1854)")</script>, painter, and his wife, Susan. He was the godson of Leopold, later first king of the Belgians. He became an excellent French and German scholar, a skilled artist, and an authority on costume and numismatics. His wife was the sister of the artist and cartoonist Sir John Tenniel, of <span class="italic">Punch</span>. In 1836 Lord Melbourne presented him to a clerkship in the Stationery Office, which he held for many years. He died in London on 8 January 1889.<br>
<br>With his elder brother, Charles (1810&#150;1906), Martin published in 1842 two volumes entitled respectively <span class="italic">Civil Costumes of England, from the Conquest to George III</span> (sixty-one plates, drawn from ancient manuscripts and tapestries, illuminated in gold and colours) and <span class="italic">Dresses Worn at her Majesty's Bal Costum&eacute;, May 1842</span>. He also wrote <span class="italic">Contributions to English Literature by the Civil Servants of the Crown and East India Company from 1794 to 1863</span> (1865). In conjunction with Charles Tr&uuml;bner he issued in 1862 an elaborate work, <span class="italic">The Current Gold and Silver Coins of All Countries</span> (2nd edn, 1863), the plates of which he drew himself. Martin was also author of the handbooks <span class="italic">Cardiff</span> and <span class="italic">Swansea and Gower</span> (1879). Shortly before he died he had begun to contribute to the <span class="italic">Newcastle Weekly Chronicle</span> a series of &#145;Reminiscences&#146;, about his father, the first of which appeared in the number for 5 January 1889.<br>
<br>Gordon Goodwin<span class="italic">, rev. </span> Annette Peach </div>
