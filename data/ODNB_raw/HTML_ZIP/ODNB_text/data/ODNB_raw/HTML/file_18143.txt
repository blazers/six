<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Marshall,  John</span>  (1782/3&#150;1841), <span class="occ">writer on statistics</span>, was for many years a supernumerary at the Home Office. He compiled <span class="italic">An account of the population in each of six thousand of the towns and parishes in England and Wales, as returned to parliament at each of the three periods 1801, 1811, and 1821</span> (1831) while he was employed on the commission to inspect the boundaries of the cities and boroughs, for purposes of the <span class="roman">Reform Bill</span>, and made some disingenuous efforts to secure the enfranchisement of a few very small places. Marshall was subsequently made an inspector of factories. He produced <span class="italic">A digest of all the accounts relating to the population, productions, revenues, financial operations, manufactures, shipping, colonies, commerce of the United Kingdom</span> (2 vols., 1833). Three thousand copies of this book, on the motion of Joseph Hume, were bought by the government at 2 guineas each and distributed among the members of both houses of parliament, who treated them with the disrespect incidental to parliamentary papers. He also compiled topographical and statistical details of Berkshire (1830), the palatine of Lancashire (1832), and London (1832). He compiled a report, <span class="italic">Mortality of the Metropolis, 1629&#150;1831</span> (1832), made a study of the returns made to parliament since the start of the nineteenth century (1835), and supervised a &#145;remodelled edition&#146; of Brookes's <span class="italic">London General Gazetteer</span> (1831). Marshall died on 11 March 1841 in Stamford Street, Blackfriars.<br>
<br>Gordon Goodwin<span class="italic">, rev. </span> Alan Yoshioka </div>
