<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Lowe,  James</span>  (<em>d.</em> 1865), <span class="occ">journalist and translator</span>, was the only son of James Lowe of Liverpool. In his early career he was editor of a newspaper at Preston, and from 1843 to 1863 edited <span class="italic">The Critic of Literature, Science, and the Drama</span>. He was also a contributor to <span class="italic">The Field</span> and <span class="italic">The Queen</span>, and was one of the secretaries of the Acclimatisation Society.<br>
<br>Lowe projected a <span class="italic">Selected Series of French Literature</span>, to consist of translations from memoirs and letters, of which the first volume, containing part of Madame de S&eacute;vign&eacute;'s correspondence, appeared at London in 1853; no more seems to have been published. In 1857 he published a translation of Victor Schoelcher's <span class="italic">Life of Handel</span>. He died of erysipelas on 29 October 1865 in London, at 10 Lancaster Place, the Strand.<br>
<br>J. M. Rigg<span class="italic">, rev. </span> Zo&euml; Lawson </div>
