<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Ryck,  William  de</span>  (1635&#150;1697<span class="mult">x</span>9), <span class="occ">history painter</span>, was born in Antwerp, where he was a pupil of Erasmus Quellinus. He trained as a goldsmith, and was still in Antwerp in 1684. According to Vertue a<blockquote>Print [a large sheet] the Story of St. Catherine. several figures. the Judges of the lawes pronouncing sentence against her.&#151;designd painted and <i>Engraved</i> &lt; a sort of scrach'd etching. Touch'd up &gt; by Will De Rijck [was] dedicated to Ferdinand Van Beughem Bishop of Antwerp. (Vertue, <span class="italic">Note Books</span>, 4.20)</blockquote>He took up painting when in England where he had arrived by 1688. Two paintings signed by de Ryck and dated 1688 are known to have been painted in England. Waterhouse reproduced examples of portraits by de Ryck and noted that he painted &#145;in a style like that of Riley&#146;  (Waterhouse, 237). His only known recorded work was <span class="italic">Tarquin and Lucretia</span>. De Ryck died in London between 1697 and 1699. He had a daughter, Catharina, who also became a painter.<br>
<br>Susan Cooper Morgan </div>
