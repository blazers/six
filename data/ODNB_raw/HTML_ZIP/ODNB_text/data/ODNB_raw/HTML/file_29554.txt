<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Williams,  Thomas Walter</span>  (1763&#150;1833), <span class="occ">barrister and legal writer</span>, was the son of Walter Williams, a London attorney who lived in Lamb's Conduit Street. He entered St Paul's School on 6 November 1772, and afterwards studied law and was called to the bar. He was not much known as a pleader, his repute chiefly resting on his writings. He died in 1833.<br>
<br>As a writer, Williams was prolific. He published numerous abstracts of acts of parliament, and several legal handbooks. He also edited the <span class="italic">Law Journal</span> between 1804 and 1806 with J. Morgan and in 1825 brought out a new edition of the work of William Sheppard, <span class="italic">The Precedent of Precedents</span> (1655).<br>
<br>E. I. Carlyle<span class="italic">, rev. </span> Jonathan Harris </div>
