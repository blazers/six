<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Watkins,  Charles</span>  (<em>d.</em> 1808), <span class="occ">lawyer and legal writer</span>, was the youngest son of Revd William Watkins of Gelli in Llanwytherine parish, Monmouthshire. He was called to the bar at the Middle Temple on 10 June 1803. However, he practised from 1799 as a certificated conveyancer until his death.<br>
<br>During his lifetime Watkins was author of some able treatises on aspects of the law. These included an account of the powers of the king, as guardian of the duchy of Cornwall, as well as a study of the law of descents (1793). His <span class="italic">Reflections on Government in General</span> appeared in 1796. Watkins also edited the introduction (on the feudal system) for the fourth edition of Sir Geoffrey Gilbert's <span class="italic">Law of Tenures</span> (1796). Two further works no doubt arose from his professional practice. These were <span class="italic">A Treatise on Copyholds</span> (2 vols., 1797&#150;9) and also <span class="italic">Principles of Conveyancing, Designed for the Use of Students</span> (1800). This work went into at least nine editions by 1845, and was regarded as &#145;a masterly and practical sketch&#146;  (Allibone, <span class="italic">Dict.</span>, 3.2602). Watkins died at Gloucester on 15 January 1808.<br>
<br>J. M. Rigg<span class="italic">, rev. </span> Robert Brown </div>
