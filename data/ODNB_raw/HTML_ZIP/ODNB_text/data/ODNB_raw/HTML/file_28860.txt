<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Watson,  Robert</span>  (<em>fl. </em>1581&#150;1611), <span class="occ">almanac maker</span>, matriculated as a sizar of Queens' College, Cambridge, on 22 November 1581 and graduated BA from Clare College in 1585. He had returned to Queens' College by 1589, in which year he was licensed by the university to practise physic. He lived at Braintree, Essex, where he combined the study of medicine with that of astrology. He published for several years an almanac containing a forecast for the year. The earliest surviving example is for the year 1594, the latest for 1611.<br>
<br>John Mott of Braintree, who had been with the earl of Essex on the expedition to Cadiz in 1596, has recently been identified as the author of a 30,000 line Jacobean poem by J.M. (now BL, Add. MSS 14824&#150;14826) of which book 8, composed about 1621&#150;5, consists of a series of Chaucerian tales told by the ship's crew on the return voyage. The ship's cook recounts the story of Mott's Braintree neighbour &#145;Nostaw&#146;, (the name disguised by inversion of its letters). The poem begins<blockquote>
<div>Nostaw sometimes skild in Astronomie'<br>
</div>
<div>mongst th' Country Medices held he soveraigntie<br>
</div>
<div>for both those Arts he certainly profest&hellip;<br>
</div>
<div>Quod gratis grate was his motto still<br>
</div>
<div>w<sup>ch</sup> w<sup>th</sup> his practice suited very ill<br>
</div>
<div>his Lying-bookes at highest rate he sold<br>
</div>
<div>he could not give his physicke though he would<br>
</div>
<div>Eger &amp; Indiges evermore did knock<br>
</div> (BL, Add. MS 14825, fol. 171<i>r</i>&#150;<i>v</i>)</blockquote>The poem runs on with the increasingly scurrilous tale of Watson's seduction of one of his patients, of his being afflicted with the pox, trying to keep this fact from his wife, and ultimately dying of this disease.<br>
<br>Nothing is known of Watson's later years nor of his date of death.<br>
<br>E. I. Carlyle<span class="italic">, rev. </span> Anita McConnell </div>
