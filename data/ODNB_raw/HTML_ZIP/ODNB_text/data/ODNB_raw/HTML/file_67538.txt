<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Baillie,  James</span>  (<em>bap. </em>1672, <em>d.</em> 1744), <span class="occ">advocate</span>, was baptized on 26 April 1672, the son of William Baillie of Glenlewin (<i>d</i>. 1714), advocate, and Eupham(ia) Watson (<i>d</i>. before 1682). His father's uncle was a Baillie of Lamington in Lanarkshire, which family had produced at least one judge, William Baillie, Lord Provand (<i>d</i>. 1593), and a number of soldiers during the political turbulence of the seventeenth century, including William Baillie (<i>d</i>. 1653) and Robert Baillie (<i>d</i>. 1684).<br>
<br>Little is known of James Baillie's early life and education; however, he passed advocate on 25 July 1702, having taken his public examination on a digest title <i>de servitudibus</i>. He was not active in the business and affairs of the Faculty of Advocates at the time, for his name does not feature regularly in the minute books of the faculty. It may thus be supposed that he was not in the front rank of pleaders before the court of session. Baillie married Jean Aytoun (1677&#150;1774), daughter of Aytoun of Kinnaldie, on 29 April 1709. Thereafter little is recorded of Baillie until his edition of the sixteenth-century writer Thomas Craig's great polemic legal and political treatise <span class="italic">Jus feudale</span> was published at the press of Thomas and Walter Ruddiman in 1732. Regarded by D. M. Walker in his <span class="italic">Scottish Jurists</span> (1985) as the best, Baillie's edition was the third to be published. He included a short life of Craig, and rationalized the order of the text, adding short commentaries on the main subdivisions. He also added useful supplementary notes placing Craig's scholarship in a wider context. This work took up Baillie's energies for some time and at some cost, for the records of the Faculty of Advocates show that a subvention of &pound;30 sterling was paid to him in January 1736, and members of faculty were strongly recommended to purchase their own copies. Baillie died on 17 November 1744.<br>
<br>K. J. Campbell </div>
