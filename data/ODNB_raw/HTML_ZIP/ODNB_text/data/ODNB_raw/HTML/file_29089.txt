<div class="para">
<span class="headword">West, Sir  John</span>  (1774&#150;1862), <span class="occ">naval officer</span>, was born at Twickenham, Middlesex on 28 July 1774, the eldest son of Lieutenant-Colonel Temple West (<i>d</i>. 1783), of the Grenadier Guards, and his wife, Jane, daughter of Pitt Drake. He was the grandson of <script>writeSeealsoLink('../view/article/29098/', "Vice-Admiral Temple West")</script> and, through his grandmother, great-grandson of Admiral Sir John Balchen; his father was the second cousin of William Pitt the younger; his grandfather's sister was the first wife of Alexander Hood, Viscount Bridport. West entered the navy in June 1788 on board the <i>Pomona</i>, with Captain William Domett, himself a follower of Alexander Hood. He was afterwards in the <i>Salisbury</i>, flagship of Vice-Admiral Mark Milbanke, and in the <i>London</i>, flagship of Alexander Hood. He was promoted lieutenant on 27 July 1793, and in 1794 was a lieutenant of the <i>Royal George</i> in the battles of 1 June 1794 and 23 June 1795. On 7 September 1795 he was made commander; in December was appointed to the sloop <i>Diligence</i> in the West Indies, and on 15 November 1796 was posted to the frigate <i>Tourterelle</i> (30 guns). From 1807 to 1809 he commanded the <i>Excellent</i> (74 guns), in the Mediterranean, and from 1809 to 1814 the <i>Sultan</i> (74 guns), in the Mediterranean and on the home and West Indian stations.<br>
<br>West married, in May 1817, Harriett (<i>d</i>. 1858), only daughter of John Adams of Northamptonshire; they had three sons and two daughters. West became a rear-admiral on 12 August 1819, vice-admiral on 22 July 1830, and admiral on 23 November 1841; he was made a KCB on 4 July 1840. From April 1845 to April 1848 he was commander-in-chief at Devonport, with his flag in the <i>Queen</i> (110 guns). He was made admiral of the fleet on 25 June 1858, and a GCB on 18 May 1860. He died, at his residence, 99 Eaton Square, London, on 18 April 1862. West's career was made by family connections, and distinguished largely by longevity.<br>
<br>J. K. Laughton<span class="italic">, rev. </span> Andrew Lambert </div>
