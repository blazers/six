<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Phillips,  Giles Firman</span>  (1780&#150;1867), <span class="occ">landscape painter</span>, was born in January 1780 in Houndsditch, City of London, and baptized on 28 January 1780 at White Row Chapel, Spitalfields, London, the son of John Phillips, a coal merchant, and his wife, Dorothy Pawsey. He was known for his landscapes in watercolours, his favourite subjects being views on the Thames. He was a founder member of the New Watercolour Society in 1831 but resigned a few years later. He was a frequent exhibitor at the Society of British Artists, at the British Institution, and occasionally at the Royal Academy and other exhibitions from 1830 to 1866. He is also known to have engraved a miniature portrait of the actress Eliza O'Neill in 1815 and another in stipple after a miniature of the actress Sarah Siddons by H. Hone in 1825. Phillips published in 1838 <span class="italic">Principles of Effect and Colour, as Applicable to Landscape Painting</span> (3rd edn, 1840) and <span class="italic">The Theory and Practice of Painting in Watercolours, as Connected to the Study of Landscape</span>; in 1839 <span class="italic">A Practical Treatise on Drawing and Painting in Water-Colours</span>, and in 1840 <span class="italic">The Art of Painting and Drawing Simplified</span>. He lived in London, and at one time at Greenwich. On 11 June 1800 he married at St Luke's Church, Finsbury, Lydia Arnold; they had four children. Phillips died at his home, 150 New North Road, Islington, Middlesex, on 31 March 1867, aged eighty-seven. His wife predeceased him. Examples of his work are in the Victoria and Albert Museum, London.<br>
<br>L. H. Cust<span class="italic">, rev. </span> Annette Peach </div>
