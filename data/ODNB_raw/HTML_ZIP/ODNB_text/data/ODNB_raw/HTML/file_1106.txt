<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Baker,  Anne Elizabeth</span>  (1786&#150;1861), <span class="occ">philologist</span>, was born at Northampton on 16 June 1786, and was baptized on 25 July at the Castle Hill Independent Church, the daughter of Richard Baker and his wife, Ann. She was the younger sister of <script>writeSeealsoLink('../view/article/1118/', "George Baker (1781&#150;1851)")</script>, the historian of Northamptonshire. Neither she nor her brother married, and for sixty years they shared a house and were constant companions. She was educated at home. Early on she showed an interest in antiquarian subjects, and it was her removal in 1812 of the whitewash from the carved capitals of St Peter's, Northampton, that led to the church's subsequent restoration. Between about 1815 and 1840 she travelled with her brother as he collected materials for his <span class="italic">History</span>, acting as his amanuensis, compiling the sections on geology and botany, and making drawings. Some of these she engraved for the published work. &#145;Provincialisms&#146; which she collected while on these travels formed the basis of her <span class="italic">Glossary of Northamptonshire Words and Phrases, to which are Added, the Customs of the County</span> (2 vols., 1854), which was highly regarded by her contemporaries. Anne Elizabeth Baker died at Hazelrigg House, Gold Street, Northampton, on 22 April 1861.<br>
<br>Thompson Cooper<span class="italic">, rev. </span> Paul Stamper </div>
