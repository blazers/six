<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Walpole,  Robert</span>  (1781&#150;1856), <span class="occ">classical scholar</span>, was born in Lisbon, Portugal, on 8 August 1781, the eldest son of Robert Walpole, clerk of the privy council and envoy to Portugal, and his first wife, Diana, daughter of Walter Grossett. <script>writeSeealsoLink('../view/article/28595/', "Horatio Walpole, first Baron Walpole")</script>, was his grandfather. He was educated at Charterhouse and matriculated at Merton College, Oxford, in 1797, but migrated to Cambridge, where he was admitted to Trinity College in 1800. He was made a scholar and won the Sir William Browne medal for a Greek ode in 1801. He graduated BA in 1803, MA in 1809, and BD in 1828. In 1805 he published <span class="italic">Comicorum Graecorum fragmenta</span>. In 1809 he became rector of Itteringham, Norfolk, in 1815 rector of Tivetshall, Norfolk, and in 1828 rector of Christ Church, Marylebone, Middlesex. He held Itteringham and Christ Church until his death.<br>
<br>Soon after going down from Trinity, Walpole travelled in Greece, and in 1817 he published his <span class="italic">Memoirs Relating to European and Asiatic Turkey</span> (2nd edn, 1818), and in 1820 <span class="italic">Travels in Various Countries of the East</span>, two interesting volumes consisting mainly of unpublished papers written by John Bacon Sawrey Morritt, John Sibthorp, Dr Hunt, and other travellers, with descriptions of antiquities and notes and excursuses by Walpole himself. He was also joint author with Sir William Drummond of <span class="italic">Herculanensia</span>, published in 1810, a work chiefly concerned with the explanation of a philosophical papyrus recently discovered at Herculaneum.<br>
<br>On 6 February 1811 Walpole married Caroline Frances, daughter of John Hyde; they had two sons and two daughters. Walpole died in Harewood Street, London, on 16 April 1856. He had estates at Carrow Abbey, near Norwich, and at Scole Lodge, Osmundeston, Norfolk.<br>
<br>W. W. Wroth<span class="italic">, rev. </span> Richard Smail </div>
