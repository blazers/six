<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Denne,  John</span>  (1693&#150;1767), <span class="occ">Church of England clergyman and antiquary</span>, was born on 25 May 1693 at Littlebourne, Kent, the eldest son of John Denne, woodreeve to the see of Canterbury. He was educated at the grammar school, Sandwich; the King's School, Canterbury; and at Corpus Christi College, Cambridge, whence he matriculated in 1709. He graduated BA (1713), MA (1716), and DD (1728). He was tutor and fellow of his college from 1716 to 1721. Following ordination in 1716, he was presented to the perpetual curacy of St Benedict's Church, Cambridge. In 1721 he became rector of Norton by Daventry, Northamptonshire, exchanging that living in 1723 for the vicarage of St Leonard, Shoreditch.<br>
<br>In 1724 Denne married Susannah (1703&#150;1780), youngest daughter of <script>writeSeealsoLink('../view/article/3178/', "Samuel Bradford (1652&#150;1731)")</script>, bishop of Rochester, to whom he was domestic chaplain for many years. They had three children: John (<i>d</i>. 1800), chaplain of Maidstone gaol; <script>writeSeealsoLink('../view/article/7499/', "Samuel Denne (1730&#150;1799)")</script>, the antiquary; and Susannah. While Denne was vicar St Leonard's was rebuilt by George Dance, between 1736 and 1740. From 1725 to 1728 Denne delivered (but did not publish) the Boyle lectures. In 1728 he was appointed archdeacon and prebendary of Rochester and was vicar of St Margaret's, Rochester, until 1731 when he resigned for the rectory of St Mary, Lambeth, which he held until his death. He was for some time prolocutor of the lower house of convocation.<br>
<br>As an antiquary Denne arranged and bound up the archives of Rochester Cathedral and the acts of the courts of the bishop and archdeacon. He collated Hearne's edition of the <span class="roman">Textus Roffensis</span> with the original at Rochester. He compiled &#145;A history of the chancellors of the diocese of Rochester&#146; and made collections for a history of the cathedral, including transcribing from the registers entries of burials from over 100 years. Manuscripts relating chiefly to Rochester made by him and continued by his son Samuel Denne were rescued from destruction when purchased by the British Museum in 1841  (BL, Add. MSS 11819&#150;11826). Denne was especially learned in ecclesiastical history and contributed materials to John Lewis's <span class="italic">The History of the Life and Sufferings of John Wicliffe</span> (1720). He published three other historical works and fifteen sermons, including <span class="italic">The Blessing of a Protestant King and Royal Family to the Nation</span> (1727). Having suffered from ill health since 1759, he died on 5 August 1767 at Rochester and was buried in the cathedral there.<br>
<br>W. W. Wroth<span class="italic">, rev. </span> John Whyman </div>
