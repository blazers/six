<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Roberts,  John</span> <span class="st"> [<em>pseud. </em>Si&ocirc;n Lleyn]</span>  (1749&#150;1817), <span class="occ">poet</span>, was born in Chwilog Bach, Llanystumdwy, in the Eifionydd region of Caernarvonshire. Little is known of his parentage and early years, but he spent most of his life as a schoolmaster at Pwllheli, Ll&#375;n.<br>
<br>Approximately 250 of Roberts's poems, mostly in the strict metres, survive in manuscripts, a small proportion of which appeared in print during the course of the nineteenth century in journals and other publications. These serve chiefly to illustrate the nature and scale of the poetical activity which characterized his corner of Wales during the latter part of the eighteenth century. Si&ocirc;n Lleyn competed at local eisteddfodau and at the events organized by the London Gwyneddigion Society. He also addressed in verse several Caernarvonshire poets&#151;Dafydd Ddu Eryri features prominently here, as does Si&ocirc;n's nephew, Si&ocirc;n Wyn o Eifion, and lesser lights such as Hywel Eryri and Robin yr Aber. That he considered himself a member of the poetic fraternity in a wider sense is evident in his elegies to figures as diverse as Lewis Morris, Ieuan Fardd, Robin Ddu, Si&ocirc;n Rhydderch, Twm o'r Nant, and Elis y Cowper.<br>
<br>Roberts was instrumental in founding the Calvinistic Methodists' cause at Penmount in Pwllheli, and he commemorated in verse two of the movement's founders, namely Daniel Rowland and William Williams Pantycelyn, as well as local preachers such as Robert Roberts Clynnog. A victim of facial cancer, Roberts died at Penrallt, Pwllheli, on 7 May 1817, and was buried two days later at Deneio, Pwllheli. He had been married twice, his second marriage having taken place by July 1812, but no information is available about either of his wives.<br>
<br>A. Cynfael Lake </div>
