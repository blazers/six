<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Binyon,  Edward</span>  (1828&#150;1876), <span class="occ">landscape painter</span>, was born in Manchester. He painted in oils and watercolours and exhibited nine paintings, mainly of Capri and Naples, at the Royal Academy between 1857 and 1876, including <span class="italic">The Arch of Titus</span> (exh. 1859), <span class="italic">Coral Boat at Dawn, Bay of Naples</span> (exh. 1875), and <span class="italic">Hidden Fires: Vesuvius from Capodimonte</span> (exh. 1876). He also exhibited at the Society of British Artists and the Dudley Gallery in London. His <span class="italic">The Bay of Mentone</span> was frequently reproduced. Binyon died on 18 July 1876 at via Piazza 5, Capri, where he had lived for many years. He was survived by his wife, Mary.<br>
<br>R. E. Graves<span class="italic">, rev. </span> Anne Pimlott Baker </div>
