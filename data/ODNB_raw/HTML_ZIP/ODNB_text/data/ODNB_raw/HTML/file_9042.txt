<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Eyston <span class="st"> [<em>alias </em>Elston]</span>,  John</span> <span class="st"> [<em>name in religion </em>Bernardine of St Francis]</span>  (1627&#150;1709), <span class="occ">Franciscan friar</span>, was a son of Thomas Eyston, esquire (1588&#150;1669), of Finchampstead, Berkshire, a councillor-at-law and representative of an old recusant family settled at East Hendred, Berkshire, and Mary (<i>d</i>. 1671), daughter of Thomas Yate of Lyford, Berkshire. He entered the English Franciscan recollects at St Bonaventure's, Douai, was professed in 1644, and was ordained priest in 1651. After studying in the University of Douai he taught there for eighteen years as professor of philosophy and theology, taking his doctorate of divinity in 1668. In 1693 he became prefect of studies at Douai. On a number of occasions he acted as spiritual director and confessor to the English nuns of the third order regular of St Francis at Aire (1668&#150;71, 1683&#150;6, 1691&#150;5, 1700&#150;06) and Princenhoff, near Bruges (1674&#150;7, 1687&#150;91, 1696&#150;7) in the Spanish Netherlands. While at Aire he published in 1684 <span class="italic">The Christian Duty Composed by B. Bernard Francis Student in Divinity</span>, a commentary on the creed, the virtues, the commandments, and sacraments, of which there were a number of Irish editions at the end of the eighteenth century. It is reputed to have been the first book published in that town. He went as a missionary to England in 1677 but, because of lameness and the persecution occasioned by the Popish Plot, he returned to the continent where he acted as definitor to the general chapter (1680&#150;83, 1686&#150;9), holding the title &#145;Jubilat doctor&#146;. He retired to Douai in 1708 and died suddenly on 28 May 1709 of an apoplectic fit during mass at St Bonaventure's Friary. He was buried in the south cloister at St Bonaventure's near the door.<br>
<br>Geoffrey Scott </div>
