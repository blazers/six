<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Shrubsole,  William</span>  (<em>bap. </em>1760, <em>d.</em> 1806), <span class="occ">organist</span>, the youngest son of Thomas Shrubsole, a farrier, was born at Canterbury and baptized on 13 January 1760. He was a chorister in the cathedral from 1770 to 1777, and organist at Bangor Cathedral from 1782 to 1784, when he was dismissed for frequenting conventicles. He then became organist of Spa Fields Chapel, Clerkenwell, London, a post he held until his death. He was a successful teacher in London, and among his pupils were William Russell (1777&#150;1813), the organist of the Foundling Chapel, and Benjamin Jacob, of Surrey Chapel. The 1794 musical directory describes him as an alto singer, and in that capacity he is said to have performed at Drury Lane and Westminster Abbey. Shrubsole composed the famous hymn tune known as &#145;Miles Lane&#146;, set to the hymn &#145;All hail! the power of Jesus' name&#146;, by Edward Perronet. &#145;Miles Lane&#146; was first published anonymously in the <span class="italic">Gospel Magazine</span> in November 1779, and then under Shrubsole's name in S. Addington's <span class="italic">Collection of Psalm Tunes</span> (1780). Shrubsole became intimate with Perronet at Canterbury, and Perronet, besides making him one of his executors, left him a substantial share of his property in 1792. Shrubsole died in London on 18 January 1806 and was buried at Bunhill Fields. The first strain of &#145;Miles Lane&#146; was engraved on his tombstone.<br>
<br>J. C. Hadden<span class="italic">, rev. </span> Nilanjana Banerji </div>
