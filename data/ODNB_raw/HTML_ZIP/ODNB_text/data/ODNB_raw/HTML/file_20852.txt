<span class="headword">Orridge,  Benjamin Brogden</span>  (1814&#150;1870), <span class="occ">antiquary</span>, set up in business in London as a medical agent and valuer. From 1863 until 1869 he was an active member of the court of common council for the ward of Cheap. As chairman of the committee of the Guildhall Library, he distinguished himself by his exertions for the preservation and investigation of the mass of records belonging to the corporation.<br>
<br>Orridge was a fellow of the Geological Society, and a member of the London and Middlesex Archaeological Society. To the <span class="italic">Transactions</span> of the latter he contributed some valuable papers, including the &#145;City friends of Shakespeare&#146; (3.578&#150;80) and an &#145;Account of some eminent members of the Mercers' Company&#146;, read at the general meeting held at Mercers' Hall on 21 April 1869. In 1867 Orridge published his most substantial work, <span class="italic">Some Account of the Citizens of London and their Rulers, from 1060 to 1867</span> (1867), a useful summary of the biography of the lord mayors, accompanied by pedigrees of the more distinguished of their descendants among the aristocracy. Among his other publications were a <span class="italic">Letter</span> (1866) on civic records, <span class="italic">Some Particulars of Alderman Philip Malpas and Alderman Sir Thomas Cooke, KB</span> (1868), and <span class="italic">Illustrations of Jack Cade's rebellion, from researches in the Guildhall records; together with some newly found letters of Lord Bacon</span> (1869). Orridge died, after a long illness, at his home, 33 St John's Wood Park, London, on 17 July 1870.<br>
<br>Gordon Goodwin<span class="italic">, rev. </span> H. C. G. Matthew </div>
