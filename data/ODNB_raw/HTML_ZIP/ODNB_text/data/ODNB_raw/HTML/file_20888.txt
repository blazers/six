<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Osgar <span class="st"> [Oscarus]</span></span>  (<em>d.</em> 984), <span class="occ">abbot of Abingdon</span>, was one of the clerks who left Dunstan's community at Glastonbury to go with &AElig;thelwold when he was appointed abbot of Abingdon, <i>c</i>.954. He was sent by &AElig;thelwold to Fleury on the Loire to learn the Benedictine rule, and returned with a written account of it. When &AElig;thelwold became bishop of Winchester in 963, he appointed Osgar his successor in the abbacy of Abingdon. Osgar was present at the expulsion of secular canons from Winchester, and made a speech on that occasion recorded by Wulfstan in his life of St &AElig;thelwold. In a letter from Fleury, written partly in cipher, an abbot is blamed for not returning a copy of Florus's commentary on St Paul's epistles; the name &#145;Oscarus&#146; fits the cipher, and the borrower is no doubt identical with the abbot of Abingdon. Osgar purchased and obtained large tracts of land for his monastery and his name is appended to a number of genuine charters of the years 967&#150;74. He died at Abingdon in 984, having finished the buildings begun there by his mentor &AElig;thelwold.<br>
<br>Mary Bateson<span class="italic">, rev. </span> Marios Costambeys </div>
