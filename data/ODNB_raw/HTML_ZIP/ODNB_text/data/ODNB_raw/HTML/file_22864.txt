<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Pugh,  Herbert</span>  (<em>fl. </em>1758&#150;1788), <span class="occ">landscape painter</span>, was born in Ireland, and moved to London about 1758. He contributed to the first exhibition of the Society of Artists in 1760, sending <span class="italic">Landscape with Cattle</span>. In 1765 he gained a premium at the Society of Arts, and in 1766 was a member of the newly incorporated Society of Artists. He continued exhibiting with them up to 1776. He tried his hand at some pictures in the manner of Hogarth, but without success, although some of these pictures were engraved. Pugh lived in The Piazza, Covent Garden, London. His death in London soon after 1788 was hastened by intemperate habits. Two views of London Bridge by Pugh were contributed to the &#145;Century of British art&#146; exhibition at the Grosvenor Gallery in 1888.<br>
<br>L. H. Cust<span class="italic">, rev. </span> J. Desmarais </div>
