<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Seaxburh <span class="st"> [Sexburga]</span></span>  (<em>d.</em> 674?), <span class="occ">queen of the Gewisse</span>, was the wife of <script>writeSeealsoLink('../view/article/4996/', "Cenwalh, king of the Gewisse")</script>, and has the distinction of being the only woman to appear in an Anglo-Saxon regnal list. Her reign is said to have begun in 672 after the death of her husband and to have lasted a year, though she may actually have ruled slightly longer than twelve months, as the next reign recorded in the <span class="roman">Anglo-Saxon Chronicle</span> is entered under 674. However, Bede characterizes the period after Cenwalh's death as a confused time when &#145;subkings took upon themselves the government of the kingdom&#146;  (Bede, <span class="italic">Hist. eccl.</span>, 4.12) and the chronicle compilers may have tidied up what was in reality a much more complex situation. In other cases where Anglo-Saxon widows are known to have exercised royal power they did so on behalf and in the name of their sons. Such regnal information that exists implies that Seaxburh ruled in her own right and it is not impossible that she was herself a descendant of the West Saxon royal house. On the other hand, the form of her name could suggest that she belonged to the East Saxon royal line, many of whose members bore names compounded with <i>Seax</i>, which does not seem to have been a name element favoured by descendants of Cerdic.<br>
<br>Barbara Yorke </div>
