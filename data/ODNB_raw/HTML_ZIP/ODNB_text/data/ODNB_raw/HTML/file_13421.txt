<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Hodges,  Edward</span>  (1796&#150;1867), <span class="occ">organist and composer</span>, was born at Bristol on 20 July 1796. He became organist at Clifton church, Bristol, and then of St James's Church (1819) and St Nicholas's Church (1821), both also at Bristol. Around this time he began composing services and anthems. In 1825 he took the degree of doctor of music from Sidney Sussex College, Cambridge. His academic interests were reflected in his literary essays and writings on music, such as <span class="italic">An Apology for Church Music and Music Festivals</span> (1834) and contributions to the <span class="italic">Quarterly Musical Magazine</span> and <span class="italic">Musical World</span>, some of which were reprinted in <span class="italic">Essays on the Objects of Musical Study</span> (1838).<br>
<br>In 1838 Hodges went with his family to America. In New York he was appointed organist to St John's Episcopal Chapel, and in 1846 to the newly opened Trinity Church, whose organ was built from his specifications. Besides composing psalms, hymn tunes, and several more services, some of which were published in New York between 1858 and 1865, notably as part of <span class="italic">The Trinity Collection of Church Music</span> (1864), Hodges continued with his literary endeavours, publishing <span class="italic">An Essay on the Cultivation of Church Music</span> (1841).<br>
<br>In 1863, prostrated by illness, Hodges resigned his appointments and returned to England. He died at Beaufort Buildings, Clifton, Bristol, on 1 September 1867. He was survived by his daughter Faustina Hasse Hodges (<i>b</i>. near Malmesbury, 7 August 1823, <i>d</i>. Philadelphia, 4 February 1895) and his son John Sebastian Bach Hodges (<i>b</i>. Bristol, 1830, <i>d</i>. Baltimore, 1 May 1915), both of whom became organists and composers. Faustina Hodges edited much of her father's music and published a memoir of him, <span class="italic">Edward Hodges, Doctor in Music of Sydney Sussex College</span> (1896).<br>
<br>L. M. Middleton<span class="italic">, rev. </span> Nilanjana Banerji </div>
