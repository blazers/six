<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Hoare,  Clement</span>  (1788/9&#150;1849), <span class="occ">vine grower</span>, cultivated a vineyard at Sidlesham, near Chichester, from where he moved in 1841 to Shirley vineyard, near Southampton. He was the author of several works on the practicalities of vine growing, including, in 1844, <span class="italic">A Descriptive Account of an Improved Method of Planting and Managing the Roots of Grape Vines</span>. This proposed growing vines in hollow pillars, a method which &#145;at once met with the condemnation it merited&#146;  (<span class="italic">Cottage Gardener</span>, 1849). Soon after the publication of this work, which he financed himself, Hoare became insolvent, his vineyard was broken up, and &#145;heart-subdued, he was sunk before his time into the grave&#146;  (ibid.). He died on 18 August 1849, at Vauxhall, Surrey, aged sixty.<br>
<br>Giles Hudson </div>
