<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Thomas,  Frederick Jennings</span>  (1786&#150;1855), <span class="occ">naval officer</span>, younger son of Sir John Thomas, fifth baronet (1749&#150;1828), of Wenvoe Castle, Glamorgan, and his wife, Mary, daughter of John Parker of Hasfield Court, Gloucestershire, was born in the New Forest, Hampshire, on 19 April 1786. He entered the navy in March 1799 on the <i>Boston</i> on the North American station, and afterwards in the West Indies. In autumn 1803 he joined the <i>Prince of Wales</i>, flagship of Sir Robert Calder, and was present in the action off Cape Finisterre on 22 July 1805. On 19 September he was appointed acting lieutenant of the <i>Spartiate</i> (commission confirmed 14 February 1806) and was at Trafalgar. He continued in the <i>Spartiate</i> off Rochefort, and afterwards in the Mediterranean until November 1809, when he was for a few months on the <i>Antelope</i>, flagship of Sir John Duckworth, and was then sent to Cadiz, where for the next three years he helped to defend the town against the French flotilla. He was promoted commander on 4 March 1811, and second in command of the British flotilla. Towards the close of 1813 he was acting captain of the <i>San Juan</i> (74 guns), flagship of Rear-Admiral Samuel Hood Linzee at Gibraltar. He was posted captain on 8 December 1813 and returned to England with Linzee in the frigate <i>Eurotas</i> in 1814. He had no further employment afloat. On 7 August 1816 he married Susannah, only daughter of Arthur Atherley of Southampton. They seem to have settled in that neighbourhood and had three sons and a daughter. In 1818 Thomas invented a lifeboat with three keels, intended to prevent it capsizing, and he proposed a pier at Brighton and a bridge across the River Arun in Sussex. He published <span class="italic">England's Defence</span>. He accepted the retired rank of rear-admiral on 1 October 1846 and died at Hill, near Southampton, on 19 December 1855. He was buried at Millbrook, near Southampton.<br>
<br>J. K. Laughton<span class="italic">, rev. </span> Andrew Lambert </div>
