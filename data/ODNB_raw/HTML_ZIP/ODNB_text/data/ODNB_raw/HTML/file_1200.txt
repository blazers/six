<span class="headword">Balguy,  Charles</span>  (1708&#150;1767), <span class="occ">physician</span>, son of Henry Balguy of Newbold, Derbyshire, was born at Derwent Hall, Derbyshire, and was educated at Chesterfield grammar school and St John's College, Cambridge. He took the degree of MB in 1731 and MD in 1750. He practised in Peterborough, and was secretary of the literary club there. He contributed to the <span class="italic">Philosophical Transactions</span>, and in 1741 he published, anonymously, a translation of Boccaccio's <span class="italic">Decameron</span>. This was reprinted several times. He wrote some medical essays and particularly a treatise on the &#145;sweating sickness&#146;, entitled <span class="italic">De morbo miliari</span> (1758). He died at Peterborough on 28 February 1767 and was buried in the chancel of St John's Church, where there is a marble monument to his memory bearing the inscription &#145;a man of various and great learning&#146;.<br>
<br>S. O. Addy<span class="italic">, rev. </span> Claire L. Nutt </div>
