<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Blackwood,  Henry</span>  (<em>d.</em> 1614), <span class="occ">physician</span>, was the son of William Blackwood (<i>d</i>. <i>c</i>.1547) and Helen Reid (<i>d</i>. <i>c</i>.1547) and elder brother of the writer <script>writeSeealsoLink('../view/article/2545/', "Adam Blackwood")</script>, who was also a judge of the parliament of Poitiers. He was born in Dunfermline, and after studying <i>belles-lettres</i> and philosophy, was sent by his uncle, Robert Reid, bishop of Orkney, to the University of Paris, where, about 1551, he taught philosophy. After studying medicine he graduated MD, was incorporated a member of the College of Physicians of Paris, and ultimately became dean of the faculty. At one time physician to the duc de Longueville, he was remembered for remaining in Paris to treat patients during an outbreak of plague. Blackwood died in 1614. He edited <span class="italic">In organum Aristotelis commentaria</span>, <span class="italic">Collatio philosophiae atque medicinae</span>, and <span class="italic">De claris medicis</span>, and left in manuscript &#145;Animadversio in omnes Galeni libros&#146;, &#145;Hippocratis quaedam cum MSS. collata&#146;, &#145;In Alexandrum Trallianum comment.&#146;, and &#145;Locorum quorumdam Plinii explicatio&#146;. <span class="italic">Hippocratis Coi prognosticorum libri tres, cum Latina interpretatione, ad veterum exemplarium fidem emendati et recogniti</span> (1625) was attributed to him, but was really edited by his son, Henry, professor of medicine and surgery at Paris, who died at Rouen on 17 October 1634. George Blackwood, an uncle, taught philosophy at Paris in 1571, took holy orders, and obtained preferment in France.<br>
<br>T. F. Henderson<span class="italic">, rev. </span> Rachel E. Davies </div>
