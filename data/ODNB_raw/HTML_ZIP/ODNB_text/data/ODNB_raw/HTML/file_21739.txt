<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Peck,  James</span>  (<em>b. </em>1773, <em>d.</em> in or after 1824), <span class="occ">composer and music publisher</span>, was born in London in 1773 and was perhaps (judging from several references in the <span class="italic">Gentleman's Magazine</span>) a member of a family of printers and booksellers residing at York and Hull. A musician named Peck, aged 100, died at Bath on 5 February 1784, but a connection with James cannot be established. Peck worked in London. His glee &#145;Kisses&#146; was published by Preston about 1798; others, notably <span class="italic">Love and Sparkling Wine</span> (1796) and <span class="italic">Hail, Britannia</span>, were printed by himself at Westmorland Buildings, Aldersgate Street. From about 1802 he was based at 47 Lombard Street, from where he issued most of his publications, but by December 1824 he was at 52 Paternoster Row. Among his other works, many of which were for use by the Methodist church, were <span class="italic">Two Hundred and Fifty Psalm Tunes</span> (1798?), <span class="italic">Peck's Collection of Hymn Tunes, Fugues &amp; Odes</span> (1799&#150;1800), <span class="italic">Soft be the Gently Breathing Notes</span> (1803), <span class="italic">Peck's Miscellaneous Collection of Sacred Music</span> (1805&#150;15), <span class="italic">The Vocal Preceptor, or, A Concise Introduction to Singing</span> (5th edn, <i>c</i>.1810), <span class="italic">The Flute Preceptor</span>, <span class="italic">Advice to a Young Composer, or, Short Essay on Vocal Harmony</span> (1811), <span class="italic">Sacred Gleanings, or, Hymn Tunes Adapted for Two Flutes</span>, and <span class="italic">Peck's Beauties of Sacred Harmony, or, Vocalist's Pocket Book</span> (1824?). <span class="italic">Peck's Pocket Arrangement, or, General Collection of Psalm and Hymn Tunes with Interludes</span> (3 vols., 1833?) was published by his son John Peck, the organist at St Faith's, in conjunction with another son, James Peck junior. John Peck's <span class="italic">The Union Tune Book, being a collection of psalm and hymn tunes, adapted for use in Sunday schools and congregations</span> appeared in 1837. In 1850 James Peck's business, which John Peck had taken over, was based at 44 Newgate Street. Peck's death has not been traced.<br>
<br>L. M. Middleton<span class="italic">, rev. </span> David J. Golby </div>
