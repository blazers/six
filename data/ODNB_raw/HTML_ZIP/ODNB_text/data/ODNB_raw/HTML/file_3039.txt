<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Bowen,  John</span>  (1755/6&#150;1832), <span class="occ">painter and genealogist</span>, was born in Shrewsbury, the eldest son of <script>writeSeealsoLink('../view/article/3037/', "James Bowen (bap. 1718, d. 1774)")</script>, painter and topographer, of that town. He studied the local antiquities under his father, who had compiled notes and sketches towards a history of Shropshire during his travels with John Mytton. Bowen traced out the pedigrees of Shropshire families and became especially skilful in deciphering and copying ancient manuscripts. He almost certainly used his own and his father's materials to compile much of <span class="italic">The History and Antiquities of Shrewsbury</span> that was published under the name of Thomas Phillips in 1779. He contributed to the <span class="italic">Gentleman's Magazine</span> drawings and accounts of the seals of the town of Droitwich and of Shrewsbury exchequer. Four of his drawings of views of Shrewsbury were engraved by Vandergucht and his engraving of some Roman inscriptions was published in <span class="italic">Philosophical Transactions</span> (49.196). He died in Shrewsbury on 19 June 1832, aged seventy-six.<br>
<br>Jennett Humphreys<span class="italic">, rev. </span> A. P. Woolrich </div>
