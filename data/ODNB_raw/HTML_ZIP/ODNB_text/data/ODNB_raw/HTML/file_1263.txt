<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Bampton,  John</span>  (<em>fl. </em>1317&#150;1341), <span class="occ">Carmelite friar and theologian</span>, according to John Bale (quoting an untraced Leland reference) came from the west country. He may possibly be identified with the Carmelite of the same name who was ordained deacon at Winchester on 17 December 1317, and who was listed as from the Gloucester convent when he was licensed to hear confessions in the Worcester diocese in 1328. Bampton is known to have studied at Cambridge, where on 9 September 1337 he was licensed to hear confessions in the diocese of Ely. He was recorded as <i>magister</i> and a member of the Cambridge community on 21 December 1341, when he received a licence to hear confessions for a further two years. He studied and lectured on the works of Aristotle, and he wrote one philosophical work, <span class="italic">De veritate propositionum</span>, which consisted of eight <i>quaestiones</i>, and a possible second work, <span class="italic">Lecturas scholasticas in theologia</span>. Both are now lost.<br>
<br>Richard Copsey </div>
