<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Pickering,  George</span>  (1794&#150;1857), <span class="occ">landscape painter</span>, was born in Yorkshire. He may be the George, son of Robert and Elizabeth Pickering, who was baptized on 3 May 1794 at Kirby Misperton, Yorkshire. A pupil of John Glover, he took over the teaching practice of the drawing-master George Cuitt the younger in Chester about 1820. Between 1815 and 1818 he exhibited eleven landscapes at the Society of Painters in Water Colours (the Old Watercolour Society), including several views painted near Matlock: <span class="italic">Near the Old Bath, Matlock, Derbyshire: Wood Scene with Cattle Drinking at a River</span> is in the Victoria and Albert Museum, London. He also exhibited four landscapes at the Society of British Artists galleries in Suffolk Street, London, in 1827&#150;8.<br>
<br>Pickering's drawings were engraved by Edward Francis Finden for both series of John Roby's <span class="italic">Traditions of Lancashire</span> (1829 and 1831). He also drew many of the landscapes which are engraved in George Ormerod's <span class="italic">History of the County Palatine and City of Chester</span> (1819) and Edward Baines's <span class="italic">History of the County Palatine of Lancaster</span> (1824). He exhibited watercolours at the Liverpool Academy, and in 1836 he had a studio at 53 Bold Street, Liverpool. He later moved to Grange Mount, Birkenhead, Cheshire, where he died on 8 March 1857.<br>
<br>Albert Nicholson<span class="italic">, rev. </span> Anne Pimlott Baker </div>
