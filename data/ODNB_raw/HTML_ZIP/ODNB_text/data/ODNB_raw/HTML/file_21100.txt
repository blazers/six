<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Paget, Lord  Alfred Henry</span>  (1816&#150;1888), <span class="occ">official in the royal household</span>, was the second son of <script>writeSeealsoLink('../view/article/21112/', "Henry William Paget, first marquess of Anglesey (1768&#150;1854)")</script> from his second marriage, to Lady Charlotte, <i>n&eacute;e</i> Cadogan (<i>d</i>. 1853), the divorced wife of Sir Henry Wellesley. He was born on 29 June 1816, and educated at Westminster School. He became a lieutenant in the Blues in 1834, purchased an unattached company in 1840, and exchanged into his father's regiment, the 7th hussars, in which he served for several years, rising to the rank of general on the retired list in 1881. On 8 April 1847 he married Cecilia (<i>d</i>. 1914), second daughter and coheir of George Thomas Wyndham of Cromer Hall, Norfolk, and had six sons (of whom the eldest was <script>writeSeealsoLink('../view/article/76746/', "Sir Arthur Henry Fitzroy Paget")</script>) and six daughters.<br>
<br>Paget was MP for Lichfield from 1837 to 1865, and held court office under all the whig ministries during that period. He was equerry to the queen in 1837&#150;41 and chief equerry and clerk-marshal in 1846&#150;52 and 1852&#150;8, and again held those positions in 1859&#150;74 (the clerk-marshal having ceased to change with the ministry in 1866). In 1874 he resigned as chief equerry, remaining clerk-marshal until his death (on 24 August 1888, on his yacht off Inverness), after which the post was abolished.<br>
<br>K. D. Reynolds </div>
