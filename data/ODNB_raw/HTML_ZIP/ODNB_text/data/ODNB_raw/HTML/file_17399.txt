<div class="para">
<span class="headword">McCormick,  Charles</span>  (<em>c.</em>1755&#150;1807), <span class="occ">historian and biographer</span>, was born in Ireland, the son of Charles McCormick of Rathkeal, near Limerick. He was educated at the Middle Temple, London, and on 18 July 1783 matriculated at St Mary Hall, Oxford, graduating BCL on 18 June 1794. Thereafter he abandoned law for literature. His principal published works are <span class="italic">The History of England, from the Death of George the Second to the Peace of 1783</span> (n.d.), <span class="italic">The Secret History of the Court and Reign of Charles the Second</span> (1792), and <span class="italic">Memoirs of &hellip; Edmund Burke</span> (1797), a supposedly &#145;disgraceful piece of party virulence&#146;  (Lowndes, 1434). In 1805 he published <span class="italic">Light Reading at Leisure Hours</span>, and followed this with a continuation of Rapin's <span class="italic">History of England</span>. His death from dropsy, in London on 29 July 1807, meant his proposed history of Ireland remained unfinished.<br>
<br>McCormick's writing brought him little financial reward. On his death his wife, of whom no further details are known, was left in such poverty that a public appeal was set up for her support.<br>
<br>Thompson Cooper<span class="italic">, rev. </span> Philip Carter </div>
