<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Ua Car&aacute;in,  Gilla in Choimded <span class="st"> [Gilbertus; </span></span> <span class="st"> Gilbert O'Caran]</span>  (<em>d.</em> 1180?), <span class="occ">archbishop of Armagh</span>, had previously been bishop of Raphoe; Gilla in Choimded means &#145;Servant of the Lord&#146;. The date of his accession as bishop of Raphoe is not known, but it had certainly occurred by 1156&#150;7 when he witnessed a charter of Muirchertach Mac Lochlainn, king of Cen&eacute;l nE&oacute;gain and claimant to the high-kingship of Ireland, in favour of the Cistercian abbey of Newry, in which he was styled bishop of T&iacute;r Conaill, a territory which was coterminous with the bishopric of Raphoe. Since no bishop of T&iacute;r Conaill (the land of the Cen&eacute;l Conaill) or Raphoe is listed as having been present at the Synod of Kells, 1152, Ua Car&aacute;in may have been consecrated by the papal legate, Cardinal Giovanni Paparo, immediately after that synod. He is listed as swearing fealty to Henry II in 1172, his forename latinized as Gilbertus. He was translated to the see of Armagh about 1175. In 1177 he was taken prisoner at the capture of Downpatrick by John de Courcy, when he was in the company of M&aacute;el Sechlainn Mac Lochlainn bearing the <span class="italic">Can&oacute;in Ph&aacute;traic</span> (believed to refer to the manuscript now known as the <span class="roman">Book of Armagh</span>), but was released shortly afterwards. The date of his death is not known, but it may be deduced to have been earlier than February 1180, when a charter witnessed by Lorc&aacute;n Ua Tuathail, archbishop of Dublin, as papal legate (who is known to have left for England in that month), was also witnessed by Ua Car&aacute;in's successor, &#145;T&#146; (Tommaltach, or Thomas), archbishop of Armagh. The text of a charter issued by Ua Car&aacute;in confirmed Ballyboghill, Dublin, formerly held by his predecessor Cellach (archbishop of Armagh from 1105 to 1129), to St Mary's Abbey, Dublin.<br>
<br>M. T. Flanagan </div>
