<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Donnegan,  James</span>  (<em>fl. </em><em>c.</em>1820&#150;1841), <span class="occ">lexicographer</span>, was a doctor of medicine of a foreign university, who practised in London from about 1820 to 1835. His <span class="italic">New Greek and English lexicon, principally on the plan of the Greek and German lexicon of Schneider</span> (1826) was commended by Bishop Edward Maltby as &#145;an important acquisition&#146;  (Maltby, 1). Subsequent editions (1831, 1837, 1842) contain many improvements and additions; an American edition, &#145;revised and enlarged by R. B. Patton&#146;, was published at Boston (1836), and another at Philadelphia (1843). In his preface to the fourth edition (1842), written in 1841, Donnegan revealed that since 1837 he had been in poor health, and expressed his thanks to Sir Robert Holt Leigh, a classical scholar, of Hindley Hall, near Wigan, Lancashire, with whom he was staying.<br>
<br>Gordon Goodwin<span class="italic">, rev. </span> John D. Haigh </div>
