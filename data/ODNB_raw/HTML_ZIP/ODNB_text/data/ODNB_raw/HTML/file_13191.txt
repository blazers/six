<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Heywood,  Thomas</span>  (1797&#150;1866), <span class="occ">antiquary</span>, third son of Nathaniel Heywood (<i>d</i>. 1815), banker, and his wife, Ann Percival (<i>d</i>. 1847), younger brother of <script>writeSeealsoLink('../view/article/13179/', "Sir Benjamin Heywood")</script>, and elder brother of <script>writeSeealsoLink('../view/article/56315/', "James Heywood")</script>, was born at Manchester on 3 September 1797. He was educated at Manchester grammar school, which he entered on 2 August 1811. In 1818 he became a partner in his father's bank, but retired in 1828 as a result of the changed circumstances following his elder brother's assumption of control. He purchased Hope End, Ledbury, Herefordshire, where he lived until his death. Heywood married, on 2 October 1823, Mary Elizabeth (<i>d</i>. 1870), daughter of John Barton of Swinton, Lancashire; they had one son and two daughters, the youngest of whom was <script>writeSeealsoLink('../view/article/38034/', "Mary Elizabeth Sumner")</script>, founder of the Mothers' Union.<br>
<br>Before leaving Manchester Heywood had collected a remarkable library of local books, which was dispersed in 1835: his collection of tracts and pamphlets is now in Chetham's Library, Manchester. He served the office of boroughreeve of Salford in 1826, and that of high sheriff of Herefordshire in 1840. He was an early member of the council of the Chetham Society, to which his financial knowledge was a valuable asset, and edited several of its publications. Three of these&#151;<span class="italic">The Norris Papers</span> (1846), <span class="italic">The Moore Rental</span> (1847), and <span class="italic">The Diary of the Rev. Henry Newcome</span> (1849)&#151;still have some value. He was also the author of a small number of antiquarian articles in journals.<br>
<br>Heywood died at Hope End on 20 November 1866 and was buried at Wellington Heath, Herefordshire. His general library was sold in Manchester in 1868.<br>
<br>C. W. Sutton<span class="italic">, rev. </span> Alan G. Crosby </div>
