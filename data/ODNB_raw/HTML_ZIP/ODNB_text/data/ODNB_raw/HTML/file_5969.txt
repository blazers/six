<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Collyer,  Joseph, the elder</span>  (1714/15&#150;1776), <span class="occ">translator and bookseller</span>, was probably the son of Joseph Collyer (<i>d</i>. 1724), a bookseller and treasurer of the Stationers' Company. He married Mary Mitchell [<i>see </i><script>writeSeealsoLink('../view/article/5971/', "Collyer, Mary (1716/&#150;1762)")</script>], who was also a translator and novelist; their son, <script>writeSeealsoLink('../view/article/5970/', "Joseph Collyer the younger")</script> [<i>see under </i><script>writeSeealsoLink('../view/article/65008/', "Boydell, John, engravers")</script>], became a well-known engraver. Collyer had a shop in Plough Court, London. Collyer the elder married a woman named Anne after the death of his first wife.<br>
<br>Collyer's translations include selections from Voltaire (1754), Plutarch (1762), and J. J. Bodmer (1767); and shortly before his death he completed a translation from the German of Sophie von La Roche's work, as <span class="italic">History of Lady Sophia Sternheim</span>. He also published <span class="italic">The Parent's and Guardian's Directory</span> (1761); <span class="italic">A History of England</span> (14 vols., 1774&#150;5); and, with D. Fenning, <span class="italic">A New System of Geography</span> (2 folio vols., 1765&#150;6).<br>
<br>According to a contemporary, Collyer was &#145;habituated to domestic life and studious retirement; he filled up the relations of husband and father with endearing affection; though an author by profession, he possessed no kind of petulance, self-sufficiency, or envy&#146;. He suffered a &#145;lingering illness and want of employment&#146;, but &#145;his long course of severe application had afforded him no opportunity to make provision for the decline of life&#146;  (<span class="italic">New System</span>, 1.4). Collyer died on 20 February 1776, probably at his home in Islington, at the age of sixty-one. He left a widow, Anne.<br>
<br>Thompson Cooper<span class="italic">, rev. </span> Joyce Fullard </div>
