<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Galensis,  John</span>  (<em>fl. </em>1210), <span class="occ">canon lawyer</span>, was of Welsh origin but took up residence at Bologna where he studied at the university. His principal accomplishment was the compiling of the <span class="italic">Compilatio secunda</span> (between 1210 and 1212 or 1215 at the latest). Chronologically the third of the <span class="italic">Quinque compilationes antiquae</span> to be made, this collection contains selections from papal decretals chiefly from the pontificates of Clement III (1187&#150;91) and Celestine III (1191&#150;98), that is, decretals which fall between those in the <span class="italic">Compilatio prima</span> and those in the <span class="italic">Compilatio tertia</span> of 1210, and was thus known as the second compilation or the <span class="italic">Decretales medie seu intermedie</span>. The selections are arranged systematically in five books. The collection drew heavily on the works of Gilbert the Englishman and Alan the Englishman. A measure of the work's significance is that of its 330 chapters nearly 75 per cent were included in the greatest of medieval canonical collections, the <span class="italic">Decretals</span> of Gregory IX (1234). Galensis also wrote a partial summary and a gloss for his own collection, as well as glosses and an <span class="italic">apparatus</span> for the <span class="italic">compilatio tertia</span>. He is to be distinguished from the Franciscan theologian of the same name, who died about 1285.<br>
<br>F. Donald Logan </div>
