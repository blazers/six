<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Exeter,  Walter  of</span>  (<em>supp. </em><em>fl. </em>1301), <span class="occ">supposed writer</span>, is said by the sixteenth-century bibliographer John Bale to have written a &#145;Life of Guy, earl of Warwick&#146;, in 1301 at the instance of one Baldwin, a citizen of Exeter. Bale saw this work, which does not survive, in the bookshop of Gerbrand Harkes in Oxford. It is likely to have been a version of the medieval romance of <script>writeSeealsoLink('../view/article/11797/', "Guy of Warwick")</script>. Bale conjectures that Walter was a mendicant friar, though he gives the place at which Walter wrote his work as St Caroc, Cornwall, which was in fact a cell of the Cluniac house of Montacute, Somerset. Another Walter of Exeter listed by Bale is a ghost, created by Henry Kirkestede by misreading a reference to Walter Map, archdeacon of Oxford, in a copy of Higden's <span class="italic">Polychronicon</span>.<br>
<br>Marios Costambeys </div>
