<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Boase,  George Clement</span>  (1829&#150;1897), <span class="occ">bibliographer</span>, born at Chapel Street, Penzance, on 20 October 1829, was the second son of John Josias Arthur Boase (1801&#150;1896), a banker, and his wife, Charlotte (1802&#150;1873), daughter of Robert Sholl of Truro. <script>writeSeealsoLink('../view/article/2735/', "Charles William Boase")</script> and <script>writeSeealsoLink('../view/article/37202/', "Frederic Boase")</script> were his brothers. He was educated at Regent House Academy and the grammar school at Penzance, and for a short time in 1844 at Bellevue House Academy, Penryn. From that year to 1846 he was in a local bank at Penzance; from 1847 to 1850 he was with Nehemiah Griffiths, ship and insurance broker, at 2 White Hart Court, Lombard Street, London; and from 1850 to 1854 he was a clerk with Ransom &amp; Co., bankers, at 1 Pall Mall East.<br>
<br>Boase sailed for Australia on 29 April 1854. He became corrector of the press on the <span class="italic">Melbourne Age</span>. He went on to become a gold-digger at Simpson's ranges, and then worked in a general store. During 1855&#150;64 he was tutor with the Darchy family on the Murrumbidgee River, New South Wales, and on Lachlan River, and was also correspondent of the <span class="italic">Sydney Morning Herald</span>. In 1864 he returned to Britain, and managed the business of Whitehead &amp; Co., provision merchants, from 1865 to 1874, when he retired and occupied himself in biographical and antiquarian literature. During his retirement years he lived in London, successively at 15 Queen Anne's Gate and at 36 James Street (later 28 Buckingham Gate), where he collected a unique library illustrative of the biography of the nineteenth century.<br>
<br>Boase was the joint author, with W. P. Courtney, of the <span class="italic">Bibliotheca Cornubiensis</span> (1874&#150;82), and the sole author of a kindred volume, <span class="italic">Collectanea Cornubiensia</span> (1890). With his brothers Charles William and Frederic, he compiled <span class="italic">An Account of the Families of Boase or Bowes</span> (1876), helped in the compilation of the works on Exeter College, Oxford, by his elder brother, Charles William, and the magnificent compilation <span class="italic">Modern English Biography</span> (1892&#150;1901) by his youngest brother, Frederic. He compiled with W. P. Courtney, for Walter William Skeat, the Cornish portion of <span class="italic">A Bibliographical List of the Works &hellip; Illustrative of the Various Dialects of English</span> (English Dialect Society, 1877), and he assisted John Ingle Dredge in his tracts on Devon bibliography. He was a frequent contributor to <span class="italic">Notes and Queries</span> and the <span class="italic">Western Antiquary</span>. He supplied 723 memoirs to the <span class="italic">Dictionary of National Biography</span>. Boase died at 13 Granville Park, Lewisham, London, on 1 October 1897, and was buried at Ladywell cemetery on 5 October 1897.<br>
<br>W. P. Courtney<span class="italic">, rev. </span> Nilanjana Banerji </div>
