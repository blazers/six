<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Clissold,  Stephen</span>  (<em>bap. </em>1789, <em>d.</em> 1863), <span class="occ">Church of England clergyman and writer</span>, was born at Reading and baptized there on 25 September 1789, the son of Stephen Clissold (1764&#150;1834), owner of a cloth mill in Stroud, Gloucestershire, and his wife, Diana Mortimer (1765&#150;1840). <script>writeSeealsoLink('../view/article/5689/', "Augustus Clissold")</script> was his younger brother. He matriculated from Clare College, Cambridge, in 1815, proceeding BA in 1819 and MA in 1822. He was admitted to Lincoln's Inn on 11 May 1819. However, he abandoned the law for the church, being ordained priest in 1828. He was rector of St Nicholas, Wrentham, Suffolk, from 1830 to 1853, and honorary canon of Norwich Cathedral. Clissold dabbled in political economy, publishing <span class="italic">Letters of Cincinnatus</span> (1815), <span class="italic">Considerations on the Trade, Manufacture, and Commerce of the British Empire</span> (1820), and <span class="italic">National Piety the Source of National Prosperity</span> (1828). He also published the <span class="italic">Official Account of the Parochial Charities &hellip; Belonging to the Blything Union, Halesworth</span> (1838). On 17 June 1824 Clissold married Charlotte Matilda (1798&#150;1852), daughter of Sir Thomas Sherlock Gooch, fifth baronet, of Benacre Hall, Suffolk; they had at least two sons, Edward and Stephen. He died at Wrentham on 12 May 1863 and was buried in the parish churchyard there.<br>
<br>Francis Watt<span class="italic">, rev. </span> H. C. G. Matthew </div>
