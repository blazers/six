<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Atkins,  Samuel</span>  (<em>fl. </em>1787&#150;1808), <span class="occ">marine painter</span>, contributed annually to the Royal Academy between 1787 and 1796 (except in 1790). He was in the East Indies from 1796 to 1804, when he returned to England, and continued to exhibit until 1808. He worked in oil and watercolour, specializing in small-scale shipping scenes. The watercolour collections of the Victoria and Albert Museum and the British Museum each have an example of his work. Further examples of his work are in the collections of the Ulster Museum, Belfast; National Maritime Museum, Greenwich; Leeds City Art Gallery; and the Ashmolean Museum, Oxford. A picture entitled <span class="italic">Shakespeare's Cliff, Dover</span>, was engraved after him by R. and D. Havell. Nagler attributes to this Samuel Atkins the originals of two engravings of sea subjects after &#145;&#151;Atkins&#146;  (<span class="italic">Allgemeines K&uuml;nstlerlexikon</span>): <span class="italic">Ships in Sight of Harbour</span>, engraved in aquatint by H. Merke; and <span class="italic">A Sea Piece</span>, by F. Janinet. He also attributes a watercolour drawing, <span class="italic">Seascape with Ships</span>, to Atkins.<br>
<br>Ernest Radford<span class="italic">, rev. </span> Michael Marker </div>
