<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Douglas,  William</span>, first earl of Queensberry  (<em>d.</em> 1640), <span class="occ">landowner</span>, was the eldest son of Sir James Douglas of Drumlanrig (<i>d</i>. 1615) and his wife, Mary, eldest daughter of John Fleming, fifth Lord Fleming, and his wife, Elizabeth; his parents married in 1581. By a contract dated 20 July 1603 he married Isobel (<i>d</i>. 1628), later Lady Isobel, daughter of <script>writeSeealsoLink('../view/article/15462/', "Mark Ker, Lord Newbattle (b. in or before 1559, d. 1609)")</script>, who was created earl of Lothian in 1606, and his wife, Margaret Maxwell. They had four sons and two daughters.<br>
<br>Having been knighted before 14 July 1612, Douglas entered into possession of the regality of Drumlanrig on the death of his father. He was active in his family's often violent disputes with their neighbours, being rebuked by the privy council in 1606 and fined in 1614. Nevertheless, in 1617 he entertained James VI at Drumlanrig, and he played a leading role in the government's attempts to tackle the lawlessness of the middle shires. In the early 1620s he was involved in the discussions on the wool trade and manufacturing, and, a decade later, on Charles I's plans for fishing. He was created viscount of Drumlanrig, Lord Douglas of Hawick and Tibberis in 1628, and earl of Queensberry in 1633. He added the barony of Torthorwald to his estates in 1622, the town of Hawick in 1623, and the baronies of Sanquhar and Cumnock in 1637. He died on 8 March 1640, and was succeeded by his eldest son, <script>writeSeealsoLink('../view/article/7895/', "James Douglas (d. 1671)")</script>.<br>
<br>Alsager Vian<span class="italic">, rev. </span> David Menarry </div>
