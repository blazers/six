<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Blacker,  George Dacre</span>  (1791&#150;1871), <span class="occ">Church of Ireland clergyman</span>, was the elder son of James Blacker, a Dublin magistrate, and was born in Dublin. He entered Trinity College, Dublin, in 1806, was elected a scholar in 1809, and proceeded BA in 1811 and MA in 1858. He was for several years curate of St Andrew's, Dublin, chaplain of the city corporation, and rector of Taghadoe. In 1840 he became vicar of Maynooth and a prebendary in St Patrick's Cathedral. Blacker wrote several local historical works including the <span class="italic">Castle of Maynooth</span> (1853) and <span class="italic">A Record of Maynooth Church</span> (1867), which were printed for private circulation. He was also the author of <span class="italic">Types and Prophecies Relating to Messiah</span> (1829). He died unmarried at his home, The Glebe, Maynooth, co. Kildare, on 23 May 1871, and was buried on 27 May in the Leinster mausoleum, by Maynooth parish church.<br>
<br>[Anon.]<span class="italic">, rev. </span> David Huddleston </div>
