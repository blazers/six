<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Proby,  Granville Leveson</span>, third earl of Carysfort  (1781&#150;1868), <span class="occ">naval officer</span>, was the third son of <script>writeSeealsoLink('../view/article/22832/', "John Joshua Proby, first earl of Carysfort (1751&#150;1828)")</script>, and his first wife, Elizabeth (<i>d</i>. 1783), only daughter of Sir William Osborne, bt, of Newtown, co. Tipperary. He was educated at Rugby School (1792&#150;8), and was aged seventeen, somewhat older than was usual, when he entered the navy in March 1798 on the <i>Vanguard</i>, with Captain Edward Berry, and Rear-Admiral Sir Horatio Nelson. He was at the battle of the Nile, and following Berry to the <i>Foudroyant</i>, took part in the blockade of Malta and in the capture of the <i>G&eacute;n&eacute;reux</i> on 18 February 1800, and of the <i>Guillaume Tell</i> on 31 March 1800. In 1801, still in the <i>Foudroyant</i>, then the flagship of Lord Keith, he was at the operations on the Egyptian coast. He afterwards served in the frigates <i>Santa Teresa</i> and <i>Resistance</i>, and in 1803&#150;4 in the <i>Victory</i>, Nelson's flagship in the Mediterranean. On 24 October 1804 he was promoted lieutenant of the frigate <i>Narcissus</i> and in May 1805 was appointed to the <i>Neptune</i> and took part in the battle of Trafalgar. On 15 August 1806, through his father's friends' influence, he was promoted to command the sloop <i>Berg&egrave;re</i>, and on 28 November 1806 was posted to the <i>Madras</i> (54 guns). In 1807 he commanded the frigate <i>Juno</i> (32 guns) in the Mediterranean; in 1808&#150;9 the <i>Iris</i> (32 guns) in the North Sea and the Baltic; in 1813&#150;14 the <i>Laurel</i> (38 guns), at the Cape of Good Hope; and in 1815&#150;16 the <i>Amelia</i> (38 guns) in the Mediterranean.<br>
<br>On 5 April 1818 Proby married Isabella (<i>d</i>. 1836), daughter of Hugh Howard, a younger son of the first countess of Wicklow. They had four sons and four daughters. From February 1816 to June 1829 he was whig MP for co. Wicklow, where his family had a significant interest. Supported by the dominant interest of William, fourth Earl Fitzwilliam, Proby was elected <i>in absentia</i> and unopposed, while at sea. He reportedly did not speak in the house and voted with the Grenvillite opposition and for Roman Catholic relief.<br>
<br>Proby had no further service afloat, but became in due course rear-admiral on 23 November 1841, vice-admiral on 16 June 1851, and admiral on 9 July 1857. He succeeded as third earl of Carysfort on the death, on 11 June 1855, of his brother John, second earl (<i>b</i>. 1780). Proby died on 3 November 1868 at his seat, Elton Hall, Huntingdonshire.<br>
<br>J. K. Laughton<span class="italic">, rev. </span> Andrew Lambert </div>
