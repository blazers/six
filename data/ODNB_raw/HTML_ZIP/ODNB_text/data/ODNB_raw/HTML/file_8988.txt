<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Evans,  William</span>  (<em>d.</em> 1776?), <span class="occ">Presbyterian minister and lexicographer</span>, was probably born in Carmarthenshire, perhaps in Cefngwili, Llanedi. He is believed to have been educated at Carmarthen Academy under Dr Jenkins between 1768 and 1772. His chief claim to notice is based on his work <span class="italic">A New English&#150;Welsh Dictionary</span>, compiled while he was a student and published in 1771. This was used by William Richards as a source for his own dictionary of 1798, but was not fully superseded as a second edition appeared in 1812. The dictionary was described by the bibliographer the Revd D. Silvan Evans as a not irresponsible work. William Evans was for some years pastor of the Presbyterian congregation at Sherborne, Dorset, but, in 1776, owing to poor health, he took charge of a congregation at Moretonhampstead, Devon. However, he was able to remain in this post only for a few weeks, and probably died shortly after taking it up.<br>
<br>R. M. J. Jones<span class="italic">, rev. </span> Dylan Foster Evans </div>
