<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Tomkins,  John</span>  (<em>c.</em>1663&#150;1706), <span class="occ">Quaker preacher and biographer</span>, was born to Quaker parents. His father died when he was very young but his mother, who married again, ensured that he received a religious education, and he helped her in her business. He assisted the poor and the sick, and he became a good preacher.<br>
<br>Tomkins's collection of Quaker biographies, <span class="italic">Piety promoted, in a collection of dying sayings of many of the people called Quakers. With a brief account of some of their labours in the gospel and sufferings for the same</span>, was the first and a very influential example of an important Quaker form that continued into the twentieth century in the <span class="italic">Annual Monitor</span>. Tomkins wrote the first three parts of <span class="italic">Piety Promoted</span> (1701, 1702, and 1706) and the series continued to be expanded throughout the eighteenth century. It was reprinted in various forms until 1856 and was translated into Latin and French. Later contributors included Joseph Gurney Bevan and John Kendal. Tomkins's own brief biography appears in part 5 (1721). His other works include pieces on prayer and the <span class="roman">Bible</span>.<br>
<br>Tomkins, who was married and had children, died at Maryland Point, Stratford-le-Bow, Middlesex, on 12 July 1706.<br>
<br>Charlotte Fell-Smith<span class="italic">, rev. </span> Emma Major </div>
