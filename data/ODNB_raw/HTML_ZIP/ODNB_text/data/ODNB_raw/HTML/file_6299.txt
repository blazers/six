<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Corby <span class="st"> [Corbie]</span>,  Ralph</span>  (1598&#150;1644), <span class="occ">Jesuit</span>, was the son of <script>writeSeealsoLink('../view/article/6298/', "Gerard Corby (1558&#150;1637)")</script> and his wife, Isabella Richardson (<i>d</i>. 1652), and was born on 25 March 1598, at Maynooth, co. Kildare, his parents having been compelled to retire to Ireland from co. Durham to escape persecution for recusancy. He was taken at five to England by his parents and he spent his childhood in the bishopric of Durham or in Lancashire. Afterwards he studied at the English College in St Omer (1613&#150;19), at St Gregory's College, Seville (1619&#150;21), and at St Alban's College, Valladolid (1621&#150;5), where he was ordained priest about 1624. He entered the Society of Jesus at Watten in 1625. Two of his brothers, <script>writeSeealsoLink('../view/article/6297/', "Ambrose Corby (1604&#150;1649)")</script> and Robert Corby, also joined the society. About 1631 he was sent to the English mission, and worked in Yorkshire and in co. Durham. He was arrested by parliamentary soldiers at Hamsterley Hall, near Ebchester, co. Durham on 8 July 1644, when vesting for mass; he was conveyed by sea to London and committed to Newgate on 22 July, together with John Duckett, a secular priest. At their trial at the Old Bailey on 4 September they both admitted that they were priests; they were condemned to death and were executed at Tyburn on 7 September 1644. Corby was beatified by Pius XI on 15 February 1929. Corby's remains were burned by order of the sheriff.<br>
<br>Thompson Cooper<span class="italic">, rev. </span> G. Bradley </div>
