<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Taylor,  Simon</span>  (1742&#150;1772/1796), <span class="occ">botanical artist</span>, was born in October 1742 and trained in the drawing-school of William Shipley in London. About 1760 he was engaged by Lord Bute to paint the rare plants in the Royal Botanic Gardens at Kew. John Ellis wrote to Carl Linnaeus on 28 December 1770: &#145;We have a young man, one Taylor, who draws all the rare plants of Kew Garden for Lord Bute; he does it tolerably well: I shall employ him very soon&#146;  (<span class="italic">Correspondence of Linn&aelig;us</span>, 1, 1821, 255). He was also employed by the physician and botanist John Fothergill. He died in 1772 (or possibly 1796). After Lord Bute's death in 1792, a large collection of paintings of plants on vellum by Taylor was sold by auction in 1794. The paintings he executed for Fothergill were sold on Fothergill's death in 1780 to Catherine the Great, empress of Russia, for &pound;2000, not a high price considering that Taylor usually charged 3 guineas for each of his paintings. Examples of his work are in the British Museum, London, and the Royal Botanic Gardens at Kew.<br>
<br>G. S. Boulger<span class="italic">, rev. </span> Annette Peach </div>
