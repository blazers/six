<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Oliver,  John</span> <span class="st"> [<em>pseud. </em>Cerddi Cystudd]</span>  (1838&#150;1866), <span class="occ">Welsh-language poet</span>, the son of John Oliver and his wife, Sarah Thomas, was born on 7 November 1838 at Whitehall, Llanfynydd, a small village in Carmarthenshire, where his parents kept a shop. He spent seven years (1843&#150;50) at the village school and nearly four at a Carmarthen school. Before he was sixteen he was admitted to the Presbyterian college in the same town. Here he made great progress with the regular studies, and read widely, on his own account, in English and German literature. He was soon able to preach with equal facility in Welsh and English. He left college in his twenty-first year, and abandoned an intention of continuing his studies at Glasgow, owing to failing health.<br>
<br>Subsequently Oliver preached occasionally and devoted himself to Welsh poetry. Most of his Welsh poems were written during his enforced retirement. His most ambitious poem is &#145;Dafydd, ty wysog yr ar glwydd&#146; (&#145;David, the Prince of the Lord&#146;). Other long poems are &#145;Prydferthwchanian&#146; (&#145;The Beauties of Nature&#146;), &#145;Mab y weddw o Nain&#146; (&#145;The Widow of Nain&#146;), and &#145;Drylliad y &#147;royal charter&#148;&#146; (&#145;The Wreck of the Royal Charter&#146;), all showing great promise. His shorter poems, however, are his best, particularly &#145;Myfyrdod&#146;, a meditation or soliloquy. Of his English poems, &#145;Life&#146; and &#145;When I die&#146; can be singled out for attention but, being his earliest productions, they are inferior to his Welsh poems.<br>
<br>Oliver died on 24 June 1866 at Mountpleasant, Llanfynydd, of consumption, and his remains were interred in the parish churchyard of Llanfynydd, of which he had sung so sweetly. In 1867 his collected works (Welsh and English) were published under the name Cerddi Cystudd by his brother, the Revd Henry Oliver.<br>
<br>R. M. J. Jones<span class="italic">, rev. </span> Clare L. Taylor </div>
