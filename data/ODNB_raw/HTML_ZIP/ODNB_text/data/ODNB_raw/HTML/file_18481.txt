<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Meager,  Leonard</span>  (<em>c.</em>1624&#150;<em>c.</em>1704), <span class="occ">gardener and writer on gardening</span>, worked for Philip Hollman of Warkworth, Northamptonshire, and later at the Brompton Park nursery. His most popular work, which ran to eleven editions between 1670 and 1710, was <span class="italic">The English Gardener, or, A Sure Guide to Young Planters and Gardeners, in Three Parts</span>. This deals with fruit trees and the kitchen garden, and arranges plants in categories such as &#145;Herbs for setting knots&#146; and &#145;Florist's flowers&#146;. <span class="italic">The New Art of Gardening; with the Gardener's Almanack</span> (1697) was followed by <span class="italic">The mystery of husbandry, or, Arable, pasture, and wood-land improved; to which is added, the countryman's almanack</span> (1697), with 61 chapters in 61 pages on how to improve the soil. Meager died about 1704.<br>
<br>W. A. S. Hewins<span class="italic">, rev. </span> Anne Pimlott Baker </div>
