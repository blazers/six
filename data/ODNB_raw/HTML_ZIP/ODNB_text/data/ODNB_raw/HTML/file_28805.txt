<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Washbourn,  John</span>  (1792&#150;1847), <span class="occ">bookseller and antiquary</span>, son of <a name="cosubject_28805"></a><span class="headword">John Washbourn</span>  (1761&#150;1829) and his wife, Mary Careless (<i>d</i>. 1833), was born in Gloucester on 2 May 1792. His father, a member of a family long connected with the Unitarian chapel in Gloucester, was a <span class="occ">bookseller</span> there in Westgate Street; born on 13 February 1761, he was mistakenly identified in the <span class="italic">Dictionary of National Biography</span> as the compiler of <span class="italic">Bibliotheca Glocestrensis: a collection of scarce and curious tracts relating to the county and city of Gloucester illustrative of and published during the Civil War</span>, published in Gloucester. The second part appeared first in 1823, but the first part, containing a historical introduction by John Webb, did not appear until 1825. This compilation was made, as the editorial preface stated, by &#145;John Washbourn, jun.&#146;.<br>
<br>Soon after his father's death on 25 April 1829, the younger John Washbourn, who had joined his father's business as a young man, seems to have left Gloucester. He died on 12 April 1847 at 5 Stonefield Street, Islington, in his fifty-fourth year, leaving a widow and eight children.<br>
<br>Brian Frith </div>
