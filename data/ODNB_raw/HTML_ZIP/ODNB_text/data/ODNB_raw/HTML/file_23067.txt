<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Ramage,  Craufurd Tait</span>  (1803&#150;1878), <span class="occ">writer</span>, born at Annefield, near Newhaven, on 10 September 1803, the son of John Ramage of Leith and Elizabeth, <i>n&eacute;e</i> Lumsdaine. He was educated successively at Wallace Hall Academy, Dumfriesshire, at the Edinburgh high school, and from 1820 at Edinburgh University, where he graduated MA in 1825. While at the university he took private pupils, including Archibald Campbell Tait, afterwards archbishop of Canterbury, with whom he maintained a lifelong friendship. After leaving college Ramage became tutor in the family of Sir Henry Lushington, and spent three years with his pupils in Naples (1825&#150;8), afterwards making a tour of the kingdom of the Two Sicilies between April and June 1828. For thirteen years after his return he was tutor in the family of Thomas Spring-Rice, afterwards Lord Monteagle. He contributed to the <span class="italic">Quarterly Journal of Education</span>, the <span class="italic">Penny Cyclopaedia</span>, and the seventh edition of the <span class="italic">Encyclopaedia Britannica</span>. In 1839 he married Mary (<i>b</i>. 1821/2, <i>d</i>. after 1878), daughter of Robert Paterson of Rock Ferry, Cheshire; they had two sons and three daughters.<br>
<br>In 1841 Ramage was appointed vice-master of Wallace Hall Academy; on the death of Dr Mundell he succeeded to the rectorship in 1842. He was a justice of the peace for Dumfriesshire from 1848, and in 1852 the University of Glasgow conferred on him the degree of LLD. He died at Wallace Hall on 4 December 1878.<br>
<br>Ramage published four anthologies, from Greek (1864), Latin (1864), French and Italian (1865), and German and Spanish (1868). He also wrote a <span class="italic">Defence of the Parochial Schools of Scotland</span> (1854), defending the established church, <span class="italic">The Nooks and By-ways of Italy</span> (1868), a history of Drumlanrig Castle (1876), and <span class="italic">Bible Echoes in Ancient Classics</span> (1878), and in addition contributed to <span class="italic">Notes and Queries</span>. An abridged edition of <span class="italic">Nooks and Byeways</span>, published as <span class="italic">Ramage in South Italy</span>, includes letters written to his mother from Italy between August 1825 and August 1828.<br>
<br>Thompson Cooper<span class="italic">, rev. </span> H. C. G. Matthew </div>
