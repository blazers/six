<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Lyttelton,  William Henry</span>  (1820&#150;1884), <span class="occ">Church of England clergyman</span>, the second of the three sons of <script>writeSeealsoLink('../view/article/17312/', "William Henry Lyttelton, third Baron Lyttelton (1782&#150;1837)")</script>, and his wife, <script>writeSeealsoLink('../view/article/38715/', "Lady Sarah Lyttelton (1787&#150;1870)")</script>, eldest daughter of <script>writeSeealsoLink('../view/article/26125/', "George John Spencer, second Earl Spencer")</script>, was born on 3 April 1820. After education at Winchester College he matriculated at Trinity College, Cambridge, in Michaelmas term 1838 and graduated MA in 1841. Ordained priest in 1844, he held the curacy of Kettering, Northamptonshire, from 1843 to 1845, but for reasons of poor health spent much of this period travelling in Germany, where he came under the influence of a liberal and evangelical theology somewhat at variance with his family's high-churchmanship. In 1847, however, he was appointed by his brother, <script>writeSeealsoLink('../view/article/17307/', "George William Lyttelton, fourth Baron Lyttelton (1817&#150;1876)")</script>, to the rectory of Hagley, Worcestershire, where he remained until his death. He was appointed an honorary canon of Worcester in 1850, and a canon of Gloucester in 1880.<br>
<br>Lyttelton married, on 28 September 1854, Emily, youngest daughter of <script>writeSeealsoLink('../view/article/21903/', "Henry Pepys (1783&#150;1860)")</script>, bishop of Worcester. After her death on 12 September 1877, he married, on 5 February 1880, Constance Ellen, youngest daughter of the Hon. Grantham Yorke, dean of Worcester, who survived him. There were no children of either marriage.<br>
<br>Besides contributing a chapter on the physical geography and geology of the Clent district to William Harris's <span class="italic">Clentine Rambles</span> (1868), Lyttelton published some minor works of apologetics. He died at Great Malvern, Worcestershire, on 24 July 1884 and was buried at Hagley.<br>
<br>J. M. Rigg<span class="italic">, rev. </span> G. Martin Murphy </div>
