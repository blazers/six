<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Osmund</span>  (<em>d.</em> 805<span class="mult">x</span>11), <span class="occ">bishop of London</span>, succeeded Bishop Heathoberht, who died in 801, and was probably consecrated by Archbishop &AElig;thelheard on his return from Rome in 802 or 803. He attended the council of &#145;Clofesho&#146; in October 803, where he subscribed acts concerning the rights of the sees of Worcester and Canterbury and an ordinance against the appointment of laymen as heads of monasteries, and was attended by an abbot, three priests, and one other. He was also present at a synod held at &#145;Acleah&#146; in August 805. His successor, &AElig;thelnoth, appears as bishop in 811.<br>
<br>William Hunt<span class="italic">, rev. </span> Marios Costambeys </div>
