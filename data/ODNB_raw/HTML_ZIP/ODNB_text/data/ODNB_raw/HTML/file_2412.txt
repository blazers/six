<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Bingham,  Peregrine</span>  (1754&#150;1826), <span class="occ">biographer and poet</span>, was the second son of <script>writeSeealsoLink('../view/article/2406/', "George Bingham (1715&#150;1800)")</script>, rector for fifty-two years of Pimperne, Dorset, and Sarah Beale (<i>d</i>. 1756). He was educated at Winchester College, before matriculating at New College, Oxford on 15 March 1771. He was admitted as a scholar there on 31 August 1772, and he proceeded BCL on 7 July 1780. He was a fellow of New College from 1774 to 1787. He became rector of Edmondsham, Dorset, on 8 January 1782, and of Berwick St John, Wiltshire, in 1817. At one time he was chaplain of HMS <i>Agincourt</i>.<br>
<br>Bingham wrote memoirs of his father, prefixed to <span class="italic">Dissertations, Essays, and Sermons, by the Late George Bingham, B.D.</span> (2 vols., 1804). These memoirs, which are abridged in John Hutchins's <span class="italic">The Histories and Antiquities of &hellip; Dorset</span>, gave rise to a controversy between the author and the rector of Critchill which is evident in his <span class="italic">Sequel or Continuation of the Memoirs</span> (1805) and <span class="italic">An Answer to the Reply or Defence of the Rector of Critchill</span> (1805). Bingham also wrote <span class="italic">The Pains of Memory, a Poem, in Two Books</span> (1811; 2nd edn, 1812). He married Amy, daughter of William Bowles; they had two sons, one of whom was <script>writeSeealsoLink('../view/article/2413/', "Peregrine Bingham the younger (bap. 1788, d. 1864)")</script>, a legal reporter and writer. Bingham died on 28 May 1826, aged seventy-two.<br>
<br>Thompson Cooper<span class="italic">, rev. </span> Rebecca Mills </div>
