<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Aston,  Joseph</span>  (1762&#150;1844), <span class="occ">journalist and playwright</span>, the son of William Aston (1733/4&#150;1826), gunsmith, was born in Deansgate, Manchester. In his youth he contributed to the <span class="italic">Manchester Herald</span>, a liberal newspaper, until governmental pressure and popular loyalism forced the paper's closure in 1793. In 1803 he opened a stationer's shop at 84 Deansgate, where, on 1 January 1805, he issued the prospectus of the <span class="italic">Manchester Mail</span>, published at 6<i>d</i>. and professing &#145;no political creed&#146;. From 1809 until 1825 he was publisher and editor of the <span class="italic">Manchester Exchange Herald</span>, a conservative journal. Afterwards he moved to Rochdale, where he started the <span class="italic">Rochdale Recorder</span> in January 1827. This ran to sixty-five issues, until March 1828.<br>
<br>Aston married Elizabeth Preston (<i>d</i>. 1852) of Manchester in October 1790, and they had at least two daughters. He was the friend and executor of Thomas Barritt, the antiquary, and Charles Mayne Young, the actor. For about thirty-four years he also enjoyed a friendship with James Montgomery, poet and editor of the <span class="italic">Sheffield Iris</span>, who submitted to him many of his manuscripts for revision and criticism.<br>
<br>Aston himself was a poet of little accomplishment, and often published his verses in his own paper. His <span class="italic">Metrical Records of Manchester</span> (1822) is a humorous portrayal of Manchester history in verse. Of his three dramatic pieces, <span class="italic">Conscience</span>, a tragedy, was performed at the Theatre Royal, Manchester, in 1815, with moderate success. He also wrote <span class="italic">Retributive Justice</span>, another tragedy, and <span class="italic">A Family Story</span>, a comedy. Of his five other published works all but one, <span class="italic">Lancashire Gazetteer</span> (1808), relate to Manchester. In 1826 he published a collection, <span class="italic">Plays, Poetry and Prose</span>. Aston died at Chadderton Hall, near Oldham, Lancashire, on 19 October 1844, and was buried at Tonge, near Middleton.<br>
<br>T. F. Henderson<span class="italic">, rev. </span> Zo&euml; Lawson </div>
