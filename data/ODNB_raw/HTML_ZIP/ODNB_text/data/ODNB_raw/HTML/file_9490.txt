<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Fisher,  Daniel</span>  (1731&#150;1807), <span class="occ">Independent minister and tutor</span>, was born at Cockermouth. He was educated at Plaisterers' Hall Academy in London, where he was a student from 1748 to 1751. From 1752 to 1771 he was minister to the Independent congregation at Common Close, Warminster, where he also kept a school.<br>
<br>In 1771 Fisher was appointed tutor in classics and mathematics at Homerton College, where he became in 1781 divinity tutor. He was described by one of his students, William Walford, as &#145;grave, regular and punctilious&#146;, but lacking &#145;ingenuity and imagination&#146;  (Walford, 104&#150;5).<br>
<br>Fisher was a rigid Calvinist and staunch dissenter. He married twice. Little is known of his first wife, Mary, other than that they had a daughter, Martha. In 1803 he married Elizabeth Toms (<i>d</i>. 1831), the second daughter of Isaac Toms of Hadleigh. Fisher died at Hackney on 13 August 1807 after a lingering illness, and was buried on 20 August at Bunhill Fields.<br>
<br>J. M. Rigg<span class="italic">, rev. </span> M. J. Mercer </div>
