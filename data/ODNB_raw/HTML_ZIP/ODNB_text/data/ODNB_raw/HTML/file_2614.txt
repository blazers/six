<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Bland,  Robert</span>  (1740&#150;1816), <span class="occ">man-midwife</span>, was born at King's Lynn, Norfolk, the son of David Bland, an attorney in the town, and was educated at the London hospitals. He married some time about 1774; the classical scholar <script>writeSeealsoLink('../view/article/2615/', "Robert Bland (1779?&#150;1825)")</script> and the politician <script>writeSeealsoLink('../view/article/2617/', "William Bland (1789&#150;1868)")</script> were among his four children. Bland received the degree of MD from the University of St Andrews in 1778, and was admitted licentiate of the Royal College of Physicians on 30 September 1786. He was connected to the Westminster General Dispensary from 1774 until 1781. He obtained an extensive practice as an accoucheur in London, and his reputation was such that he was engaged to write all the articles on midwifery for Rees's <span class="italic">Cyclopaedia</span>. To the <span class="italic">Philosophical Transactions</span> (no. 71/1, 22, 35&#150;72) he contributed in 1781 a paper entitled &#145;Some calculations of the number of accidents or deaths which happen from parturition; proportion of male and female children born; of twins, monstrosities &hellip; taken from the reports of the Westminster General Dispensary&#146;, and in the same year a &#145;Table of the chances of life from infancy to twenty-six years of age&#146;. He published in 1794 <span class="italic">Observations on Human and Comparative Parturition</span>, and he was also the author of <span class="italic">Proverbs Chiefly Taken from the Adagia of Erasmus, with Explanations; and Illustrated by Examples from the Spanish, Italian, French, and English Languages</span> (2 vols., 1814). From 1796 until 1816 Bland was chairman of the Basingstoke Canal Navigation Company. He was described as being &#145;skilled in his profession and of extensive experience but eccentric in manner&#146;  (Vine, 46). He died at Leicester Square, London, on 29 June 1816. His library was sold later by Sothebys.<br>
<br>[Anon.]<span class="italic">, rev. </span> Michael Bevan </div>
