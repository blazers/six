<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Denny,  Henry</span>  (1803&#150;1871), <span class="occ">entomologist</span>, was for forty-five years curator of the museum of the Literary and Philosophical Society in Leeds. Before his appointment he had published on the genus <i>Pselaphus</i> (<span class="italic">Monographia Pselaphorum et Scydmaenorum Britanniae, or, An essay on the British species of the genera Pselaphus of Herbst, and Scydmaenus of Latreille</span>, 1825). His studies were to follow this direction for the rest of his life, and, while performing his duties as curator, he made himself a leading authority on the subject of parasitic insects.<br>
<br>Denny was the first salaried curator of the Leeds museum, and was dedicated to the institution. The entomologist William Kirby (1759&#150;1850), to whom Denny dedicated his first monograph, tried unsuccessfully to secure for the latter employment as illustrator on a serial entomological publication. In 1842 the British Association for the Advancement of Science made a grant to Denny of 50 guineas for the purpose of assisting him in the study of British <i>Anoplura</i>. That year he published <span class="italic">Monographia Anoplurorum Britanniae, or, An essay on the British species of parasitic insects belonging to the order Anoplura of Leach</span>. He died at Leeds on 7 March 1871, and a fund amounting to &pound;883 was raised by subscription for the benefit of his widow, Ann, and their younger children.<br>
<br>Robert Harrison<span class="italic">, rev. </span> Yolanda Foote </div>
