<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Osred II</span>  (<em>d.</em> 792), <span class="occ">king of Northumbria</span>, was the son of <script>writeSeealsoLink('../view/article/39121/', "King Alhred")</script> [<i>see under </i><script>writeSeealsoLink('../view/article/20928/', "Oswulf")</script>] and his queen, Osgifu, and thus descended from Ida, founder in 547 of the Bernician dynasty of Northumbrian kings, by two collateral lines: that is via Ida's son Ocga on his mother's side, and by Ida's son Eadric on his father's side. In view of the fact that his father married in 768, Osred is unlikely to have been more than nineteen years old when he came to the throne, after the murder of &AElig;lfwald I, in 788. In 790 he was deposed, tonsured at York, and expelled, to be replaced by &AElig;thelred I (<i>d</i>. 796), who had himself been exiled in 779. According to the Northumbrian annals, Osred was on the Isle of Man in 792 when he relied on the proffered support of certain nobles to attempt to return to Northumbria. He was deserted by his soldiers, however, and captured by King &AElig;thelred who had him killed at a place called &#145;Aynburg&#146; on 14 September. He was buried in the church of the monastery of Tynemouth, Northumbria.<br>
<br>David Rollason </div>
