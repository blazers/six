<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Douglas,  James</span>, fourth Baron Douglas  (1787&#150;1857), <span class="occ">Church of England clergyman and aristocrat</span>, fifth son of Archibald Stewart Douglas, first Baron Douglas (1748&#150;1827), and his second wife, Lady Frances Scott (1750&#150;1817), youngest daughter of Francis Scott, earl of Dalkeith, was born on 9 July 1787. Having been educated for the church at Christ Church, Oxford, he was appointed in 1819 rector of Marsh Gibbon, Buckinghamshire, and in 1825 rector of Broughton in Northamptonshire. At the time of these appointments, which he held concurrently until 1848, there was little prospect of his succeeding to the title and estates, as he was then the third surviving son. But both his elder brothers died unmarried&#151;Archibald, second Baron Douglas, in 1844, and Charles, third Baron Douglas, in 1848, when the estates and title fell to him. On 18 May 1813 James Douglas married Wilhelmina (1791&#150;1857), daughter of <script>writeSeealsoLink('../view/article/19619/', "General James Murray")</script>, fifth son of the fourth Lord Elibank. They had no children and, on his death at Bothwell Castle on 6 April 1857, the title of Lord Douglas became extinct, and the estates passed to his sister, Lady Montagu. He was buried at Douglas.<br>
<br>Henry Paton<span class="italic">, rev. </span> K. D. Reynolds </div>
