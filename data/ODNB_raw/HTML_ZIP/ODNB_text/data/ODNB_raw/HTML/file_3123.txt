<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Boyes,  John Frederick</span>  (1811&#150;1879), <span class="occ">classical scholar</span>, was born in London on 10 February 1811, the son of a Yorkshireman, Benjamin Boyes. His father being then resident in Charterhouse Square, in October 1819 Boyes entered Merchant Taylors' School. After a very creditable school career extending over nearly ten years, he went in 1829 as Andrew's civil law exhibitioner to St John's College, Oxford, having relinquished a scholarship which he had gained in the previous year at Lincoln College. He graduated BA in 1833 (MA 1835), taking a second class in classics. Soon afterwards he was appointed second master of the Forest School, Walthamstow, and eventually succeeded to the headmastership, which he held from 1844 to 1848. Among a large circle of discriminating friends, he enjoyed a high reputation for culture and scholarship. The fruits of his extensive reading and literary taste were to be seen in his published works: <span class="italic">Illustrations of the tragedies of Aeschylus and Sophocles, from the Greek, Latin, and English poets</span> (1844); <span class="italic">English Repetitions, in Prose and Verse</span> (1849); <span class="italic">Life and Books, a Record of Thought and Reading</span> (1859); and <span class="italic">Lacon in Council</span> (1865). The closing years of his life were largely devoted to practical benevolence, in the exercise of which he was as humble as he was liberal. He died at 10 St James's Terrace, Harrow Road, London, on 26 May 1879, leaving a widow, Charlotte Augusta Cobourg Boyes.<br>
<br>C. J. Robinson<span class="italic">, rev. </span> M. C. Curthoys </div>
