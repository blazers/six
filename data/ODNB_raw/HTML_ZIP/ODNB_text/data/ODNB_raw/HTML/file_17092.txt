<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Lower,  Richard</span>  (1782&#150;1865), <span class="occ">poet</span>, was born at Alfriston, Sussex, on 19 September 1782, the son of John Lower, bargeman, and his wife, Sarah. His father owned the barge the <i>Good Intent</i>, and was the first person to navigate the little River Cuckmere from the sea to Longbridge. Richard Lower was physically too weak to adopt his father's calling; having received a fair education, he opened a school about 1803 in the parish of Chiddingly, where he resided until within a few months of his death. He also carried on the business of land surveyor.<br>
<br>From the time of his childhood Lower was addicted to rhyming, much to his mother's displeasure. His best-known production is <span class="italic">Tom Cladpole's jurney to Lunnon, told by himself, and written in pure Sussex doggerel by his Uncle Tim</span>, printed in 1830 as a sixpenny pamphlet and reprinted at least twice. Of this upwards of twenty thousand copies were sold, chiefly among the cottagers in east Sussex, who, however, resented Lower's sarcasms at their expense. It was followed in 1844 by <span class="italic">Jan Cladpole's trip to Merricur, written all in rhyme by his father, Tim Cladpole</span>, which expressed the author's anti-slavery sentiments.<br>
<br>Lower married Mary Oxley (<i>b</i>. 1780) on 9 April 1803, and he died at the residence of their third son, Joseph Richard Lower, surveyor, at Swan Lane, Tonbridge, Kent, on 30 September 1865. His second son, <script>writeSeealsoLink('../view/article/17090/', "Mark Antony Lower FSA (1813&#150;1876)")</script>, wrote several antiquarian works. His eldest daughter, Mrs Quaife, was well known as a nurse in America in the Federal army.<br>
<br>G. C. Boase<span class="italic">, rev. </span> Megan A. Stephan </div>
