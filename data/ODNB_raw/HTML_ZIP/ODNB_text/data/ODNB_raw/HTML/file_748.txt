<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Ashe,  Jonathan</span>  (<em>b. </em>1766), <span class="occ">Church of Ireland clergyman and freemason</span>, was born at Limerick, the son of Jonathan Ashe. He entered Trinity College, Dublin, on 26 March 1783, took his BA in 1787, and became BD and DD in 1808. He was ordained a deacon in the Church of Ireland in September 1787 and was licensed in May 1790, becoming curate of St Andrew's, Dublin, in the same year. At some point he also became a freemason: he was warden of the Royal Arch Lodge 190, Dublin, in 1795 and helped to found the Society for the Schooling of the Orphan Female Children of Distressed Masons. He was also a popular preacher of masonic charity sermons.<br>
<br>Almost nothing is known of the rest of Ashe's life; he left Dublin shortly after 1801 and was living in Bristol in 1813, where he published a work entitled <span class="italic">The masonic manual, or, Lectures on freemasonry, containing the instructions, documents, and discipline of the masonic economy</span>. This work, which seems to have been largely plagiarized from William Hutchinson's <span class="italic">Spirit of Masonry</span> of 1775, was dedicated to the duke of Sussex, then grand master of the order, and claimed to offer &#145;a fair review and estimate of masonry&#146;. The work was edited, with annotations and additions, by George Oliver in 1843 and by John Edward Cox in 1870 and went into several editions. After publishing this work&#151;his only claim to fame&#151;Ashe fell into obscurity; even the date of his death is not recorded in accounts of Irish freemasonry.<br>
<br>John Westby-Gibson<span class="italic">, rev. </span> David Huddleston </div>
