<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Donaldson,  John</span>  (1799&#150;1876), <span class="occ">writer on agriculture</span>, was born in Northumberland and was probably related to another agricultural writer, <script>writeSeealsoLink('../view/article/7799/', "James Donaldson (fl. 1794&#150;1796)")</script>. In all, John Donaldson wrote, contributed to, and edited more than a dozen works on agricultural topics, covering such subjects as clay lands and loamy soils, farm buildings, manures, and the management of crops, describing himself on their title pages as a professor of botany. In addition to these, he also published <span class="italic">Agricultural Biography</span> (1854) and wrote, for William Owen the bookseller, a periodical detailing country fairs to be held during the year. Donaldson was presented to the Charterhouse in Holborn, London, by the prince consort in August 1855, and died a poor brother there on 22 March 1876, leaving a will in favour of Elizabeth Paine, a widow.<br>
<br>Thomas Seccombe<span class="italic">, rev. </span> Giles Hudson </div>
