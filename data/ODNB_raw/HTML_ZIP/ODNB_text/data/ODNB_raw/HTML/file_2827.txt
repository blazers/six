<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Bond,  John James</span>  (1819&#150;1883), <span class="occ">archivist</span>, was born on 9 December 1819, the son of William Andrew Bond of Ashford, Kent. In 1841 he entered the public service as a clerk, assisting <script>writeSeealsoLink('../view/article/5852/', "Henry Cole")</script>, his brother-in-law, in the arrangement of the public records when they were transferred from Whitehall to the Royal Riding School of Carlton House. Bond became an assistant keeper in 1855 and in August 1862 he was appointed superintendent of the literary search room. In 1866 he published a <span class="italic">Handy Book of Rules and Tables for Verifying Dates of Historical Events</span>. This work was well regarded but Bond never rose above the rank of assistant keeper because of his &#145;oddness&#146;, which by 1883 had reached the point where it could be called mental instability. He was convinced that he was an illegitimate son of the duke of Sussex, a belief that his colleagues regarded as delusionary. He died on 9 December 1883 at his home, 96 Philbeach Gardens, Earls Court, London, where he had lived with his sister, Marian Fairman Cole.<br>
<br>Thompson Cooper<span class="italic">, rev. </span> Joanne Potier </div>
