<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Day,  James</span>  (<em>fl. </em>1637), <span class="occ">poet</span>, is entirely unknown, apart from a volume of devotional verse, entitled <span class="italic">A New Spring of Divine Poetrie</span>, published in 1637 with an acrostic dedication &#145;To Mistris Bridget Rudge&#146; and commendatory verses by H. G. and T. J. The two principal poems in the volume are &#145;The Worldes Metamorphosis&#146; and &#145;Christ's Birth and Passion&#146;; these are followed by some shorter poems. T. J. may well have been Thomas Jordan, printer and plagiarist. At any rate, Day's work seems not to have sold well, and in 1646 the unsold copies were reissued by Jordan, with a new title-page describing them as the <span class="italic">Divine Raptures</span> of Jordan himself.<br>
<br>A. H. Bullen<span class="italic">, rev. </span> Matthew Steggle </div>
