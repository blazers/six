<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Clifford,  Hugh Charles</span>, seventh Baron Clifford of Chudleigh  (1790&#150;1858), <span class="occ">aristocrat</span>, eldest son of Charles, sixth Baron Clifford (1759&#150;1831), and Eleanor Mary (<i>d</i>. 1835), daughter of Henry Arundel, eighth Baron Arundell of Wardour, was born at New Park, Somerset, on 22 May 1790, the eldest boy in a family of twelve. The family were Roman Catholics and he was educated at Stonyhurst, and in 1814 attended Cardinal Consalvi to the Congress of Vienna. He served as a volunteer through a large portion of the Peninsular campaigns. He succeeded his father in 1831 and took his seat in the House of Lords, the first of his family to do so since 1678. He supported the whigs, but seldom took part in the debates except on questions connected with Roman Catholicism. In his later years he lived chiefly in Italy, near Tivoli. On 1 September 1818 he married Mary Lucy (1799&#150;1831), only child of <script>writeSeealsoLink('../view/article/28987/', "Thomas (afterwards Cardinal) Weld")</script> of Lulworth Castle, Dorset; they had two daughters and four sons. The eldest son, Charles Hugh Clifford, became eighth baron; the third was <script>writeSeealsoLink('../view/article/5651/', "Sir Henry Hugh Clifford")</script>. The other sons took orders in the Roman Catholic church, and one daughter became a nun of the Sacr&eacute; C&#339;ur. Clifford was the author of a number of political pamphlets on Ireland, India, and the corn laws. He died at Rome on 28 February 1858 of the effects of a wound in the ankle, and was buried beside his father-in-law, Cardinal Weld, in Rome, on 2 March 1858.<br>
<br>T. F. Henderson<span class="italic">, rev. </span> K. D. Reynolds </div>
