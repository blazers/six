<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Sime,  James</span>  (1843&#150;1895), <span class="occ">journalist and writer</span>, born on 31 October 1843, was the eldest son of the Revd James Sime of Airdrie, minister of the Independent congregation in Fraserburgh, Aberdeenshire, and afterwards of Wick and Thurso, Caithness (<i>d</i>. 19 September 1865 at Thurso, aged sixty), and his wife, Jane Anderson of Glasgow (<i>d</i>. 28 January 1889 at Edinburgh). He was educated at Anderson's Gymnasium, Aberdeen, which he left in 1859 for Edinburgh University (MA 1867). He married, on 6 October 1865, Jessie Aitken Wilson (youngest sister of Sir Daniel Wilson, president of Toronto University, and of Professor George Wilson of Edinburgh University). In 1866, having given up the idea of entering the ministry, he went to Germany, and studied German literature and philosophy, first at Heidelberg University, and afterwards at Berlin. During his stay in Germany he collected materials for his <span class="italic">Life of Lessing</span>, and he visited most of the places connected with his hero and with Goethe and Schiller. On his return from Germany he settled at Norland Square, Notting Hill, London, in 1869, and began journalism. In 1871 he joined the staff of the Edinburgh Academy but, finding the work uncongenial, resigned and returned to London in 1873 to writing, which occupied him until his death.<br>
<br>Sime was successively connected with <span class="italic">The Globe</span>, the <span class="italic">Pall Mall Gazette</span>, and the <span class="italic">St James's Gazette</span> (under Frederick Greenwood), writing mainly on social and educational topics, and continental politics. He was a frequent contributor to <span class="italic">The Athenaeum</span>, the <span class="italic">Saturday Review</span>, and the <span class="italic">English Illustrated Magazine</span>, did weekly work for <span class="italic">The Graphic</span> and the <span class="italic">Daily Graphic</span> for many years, and for some time was on the staff of <span class="italic">Nature</span>. He had planned a history of Germany on a fairly large scale, but his everyday work and premature death prevented the achievement of this ambition. From 1880 he lived at 1 Queen Anne's Grove, a house which he had built in Bedford Park (the new &#145;arty&#146; garden suburb of London at Chiswick, designed by Norman Shaw).<br>
<br>Sime's publications included <span class="italic">History of Germany</span> (1874), <span class="italic">Life of Lessing</span> (2 vols., 1877), <span class="italic">Schiller</span> (1882), <span class="italic">Mendelssohn's Letters</span> (1887), <span class="italic">Life of Goethe</span> (1888), and numerous articles on German history, literature, and biography for the ninth edition of <span class="italic">Encyclopaedia Britannica</span>. Sime died at his home, 1 Queen Anne's Grove, Bedford Park, Middlesex, of influenza, on 20 March 1895, and was buried on 25 March at Hampstead cemetery, London. His wife and their daughter, Georgina Jessie, survived him.<br>
<br>F. Y. Powell<span class="italic">, rev. </span> Roger T. Stearn </div>
