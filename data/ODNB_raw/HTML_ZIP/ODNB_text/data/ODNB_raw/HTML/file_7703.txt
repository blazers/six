<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Dixon,  Joshua</span>  (<em>bap. </em>1743, <em>d.</em> 1825), <span class="occ">physician and biographer</span>, was baptized at St Nicholas's Church, Whitehaven, Cumberland, on 12 August 1743, the son of Joshua and Elizabeth Dixon. He graduated MD from the University of Edinburgh in 1768, and thereafter practised medicine in Whitehaven. In 1775 Dixon married Ann Fletcher; they had three sons and a daughter. Dixon was instrumental in the establishment of the dispensary at Whitehaven in 1783, and acted as its physician and chief administrator. His medical career was characterized as &#145;one continued scene of usefulness and benevolence&#146;  (<span class="italic">GM</span>, 185). Dixon died on 7 January 1825 in Lowther Street, Whitehaven, and was buried at St Nicholas's Church on the 14th.<br>
<br>Dixon was the author of <span class="italic">The Literary Life of William Brownrigg, M.D., F.R.S.</span> (1801). Brownrigg, of whom Dixon was a former pupil, was a Whitehaven medical practitioner and chemist who conducted important scientific research on salt making, mineral waters, and the effects of fire-damp. Brownrigg was noted for his diffidence in publishing, and Dixon wrote his biography to help publicize Brownrigg's findings, and also to idealize Brownrigg's character.<br>
<br>Thompson Cooper<span class="italic">, rev. </span> Marten Hutt </div>
