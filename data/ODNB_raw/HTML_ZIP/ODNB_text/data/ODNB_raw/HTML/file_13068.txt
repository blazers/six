<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Herdman,  John</span>  (1762?&#150;1842), <span class="occ">physician</span>, born in Scotland, became a member of the Medical Society of Edinburgh on 14 December 1793 and a member of the Royal College of Surgeons of the City of Edinburgh on 26 December 1797, of which he was subsequently elected a fellow. Herdman practised for twelve years at Leith and was a supporter of the Brunonian system (albeit in a modified form) of John Brown (1735&#150;1788). On 12 July 1800 he took the degree of MD at Aberdeen, and on 31 March 1806 he was admitted a licentiate of the Royal College of Physicians. After moving to London he was made physician to the City Dispensary and physician to the duke of Sussex.<br>
<br>Herdman withdrew from practice upon entering Magdalene College, Cambridge, in 1809; he migrated to Trinity College in 1811, where he graduated BA in 1814 and MA in 1817. He was ordained deacon in the Church of England in 1816, and he preached occasionally at Alnwick, Howick, and other towns in Northumberland. His marriage to the daughter of C. Hay of Lesbury brought him considerable wealth, a large portion of which he spent on charitable causes.<br>
<br>In 1795 Herdman published <span class="italic">An Essay on the Causes and Phenomena of Animal Life</span>, in which he examined the physical properties of animal life: the senses, the nervous system and the structure of the body, and the external means required to sustain life. A second edition was published in 1806. He also wrote <span class="italic">Dissertations on White swelling of the Joints and the Doctrine of Inflammation</span> (1802); <span class="italic">Discourse on the Epidemic Disease Termed Influenza</span> (1803); <span class="italic">Discourses on the Management of Infants and the Treatment of their Diseases</span> (1804); and <span class="italic">A Letter proposing a Plan for the Improvement of Dispensaries and the Medical Treatment of the Diseased Poor</span> (1809). Herdman died at Lesbury House, near Alnwick, Northumberland, on 26 February 1842, aged eighty.<br>
<br>Gordon Goodwin<span class="italic">, rev. </span> Kaye Bagshaw </div>
