<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Fellowes <span class="st"> [Fellows]</span>,  James</span>  (<em>fl. </em>1735&#150;1751), <span class="occ">painter</span>, is known only through a series of modest portraits of sitters from the north Wales and Cheshire areas. These are, principally, <span class="italic">Arabelle Pennant</span> of Flintshire (Manchester City Galleries) and <span class="italic">The Revd Richard Davies</span> of Llannerch Park (ex Christies, 2 November 1984), both of 1735; five portraits of members of the Griffith and Mostyn families (each 1736; Rhuallt, Flintshire, in 1957); <span class="italic">Mrs Elizabeth Pennant</span> of Nantwich (1740; ex Christies, 1 July 1938); fifteen portraits&#151;one of 1738, another of 1746&#151;of, or related to, the Puleston family of Emral, Flintshire (ex Christies, 18 December 1933); <span class="italic">Roger Wilbraham</span> (<i>d</i>. 1754) of Nantwich (1741; ex Christies, 11 April 1997); and <span class="italic">Mrs Catherine Vaughan</span> (1751; Nannau, Denbighshire, in 1957).<br>
<br>It has been assumed, possibly correctly, that he was also the James Fellowes who painted the earlier portraits of Humphrey Gower (1638&#150;1711), master of St John's College, Cambridge (who was a nonjuring sympathizer), and Thomas Wilson (1663&#150;1755), bishop of Sodor and Man, which were engraved, respectively, in 1719 and 1726; and an altarpiece, <span class="italic">The Last Supper</span> (1713&#150;14), painted for Richard Welton, the nonjuring rector of St Mary's, Whitechapel, London, showing White Kennet as Judas Iscariot; it was removed in 1714 and remains, in modified form, in St Albans Abbey (an engraving is with the Society of Antiquaries).<br>
<br>L. H. Cust<span class="italic">, rev. </span> John Ingamells </div>
