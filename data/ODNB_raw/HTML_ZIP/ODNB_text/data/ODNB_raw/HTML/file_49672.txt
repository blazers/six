<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Cachepol,  Walter</span>  (<em>d.</em> 1369), <span class="occ">canon lawyer</span>, came from the diocese of Hereford. When he was already an Oxford master of arts and a tonsured cleric, although not yet in minor orders, he was collated by Archbishop Islip on 6 January 1362 to the rectorship of Lyminge in Kent, a benefice he held until his death. Cachepol entered almost immediately into his course of studies in canon law, and received a <i>cum ex eo</i> licence on 27 August 1364 to hold for one year a benefice <i>in absentia</i> while studying at university. Proceeding through minor and major orders, he was ordained priest on 30 March 1366.<br>
<br>Cachepol's lectures provide a major source for our knowledge of the canon-law lecture hall in the medieval English universities. They form the core of the principal surviving collection of such university lectures  (BL, Royal MS 9 E.viii). This collection contains the lectures of twenty Oxford canonists, yet carries as a heading for the table of contents &#145;Lectura Walteri Cachepole super decretales &hellip;&#146;, an indication of their substantial prominence. Twenty-six titles from the standard canon-law texts (<span class="italic">Decretals</span>, <span class="italic">Liber sextus</span>, <span class="italic">Clementines</span>, <span class="italic">Extravagantes</span> of John XXII) were treated in Cachepol's lectures (fols. 27&#150;94<i>v</i>). Although not showing originality, his lectures can be taken as fairly representative of the type and quality of such scholastic acts and of canon-law studies generally in the second half of the fourteenth century.<br>
<br>Cachepol died, with apparent suddenness, at some time between 14 and 27 April 1369, while teaching at Oxford and in the midst of lecturing on the title &#145;De electione&#146; of the <span class="italic">Liber sextus</span>. At that point a student wrote in his notes, <i>heu, cum hic moritur doctor</i> <i>Walterus Cachepol</i> (&#145;when, alas, Dr Walter Cachepol died&#146;; BL, Royal MS 9 E.viii, fol. 94<i>v</i>). His will, dated 14 April 1368, requesting burial in the parish church of St Magnus the Martyr, London, was proved on 27 April 1369.<br>
<br>F. Donald Logan </div>
