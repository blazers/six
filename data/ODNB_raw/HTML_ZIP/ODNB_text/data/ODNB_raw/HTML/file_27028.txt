<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Taylor,  Helen</span>  (1818&#150;1885), <span class="occ">children's writer</span>, was the only child of Martin Taylor (1788&#150;1867), of Ongar, Essex, and his first wife, Elizabeth Venn. Martin Taylor was the second surviving son of the nonconformist preacher <script>writeSeealsoLink('../view/article/27033/', "Isaac Taylor (1759&#150;1829)")</script>, of Ongar, whose family were well known for their skill at engraving and their writing; <script>writeSeealsoLink('../view/article/27034/', "Isaac Taylor (1787&#150;1865)")</script> and <script>writeSeealsoLink('../view/article/27040/', "Jefferys Taylor")</script> were Martin's brothers. Martin was regarded as the least talented of the Taylor children; in 1809 he was sent to London to work in a publishing house in Paternoster Row. Thereafter he and his family are little noticed in any account of the Taylor family.<br>
<br>Helen was presumably born in London. Her immediate family, like that of her uncle Isaac Taylor of Stanford Rivers, apparently became members of the Church of England. Her publications indicate involvement in religious education for children. Two, <span class="italic">Sabbath Bells: a Series of Simple Lays for Christian Children</span> (1844) and <span class="italic">Missionary Hymns, for the Use of Children</span> (1846), emulate the children's verses of her aunts <script>writeSeealsoLink('../view/article/27039/', "Jane Taylor")</script> and <script>writeSeealsoLink('../view/article/10681/', "Ann Gilbert")</script> [<i>see under </i><script>writeSeealsoLink('../view/article/27039/', "Taylor, Jane")</script>]. Helen also wrote <span class="italic">The Child's Book of Homilies, by a Member of the Church of England</span> (1844), and she contributed to <span class="italic">The Teacher's Treasury</span>. Her works have been generally obscured by the great outpouring of Sunday school literature in the middle of the nineteenth century as well as the fame of her aunts. Helen Taylor never married. She died on 25 June 1885 at Mayfield, Parkstone, Dorset, and was buried at Parkstone.<br>
<br>Barbara Brandon Schnorrenberg </div>
