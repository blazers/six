<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Pearce,  Thomas</span>  (<em>fl. </em>1725&#150;1756), <span class="occ">legal writer and lawyer</span>, is a figure about whom nothing is known apart from his publications. The earliest of these was <span class="italic">The Laws and Customs of the Stannaries in the Counties of Cornwall and Devon</span> (1725). Two other works were devoted to elucidating the office and duties of JPs (1754 and 1756). Another publication, <span class="italic">The Poor Man's Lawyer, or, Laws Relating to the Inferior Courts Laid Open</span>, appeared in 1755. Pearce has been confused with Thomas Pearse (<i>d</i>. 1743), MP for Weymouth and Melcombe Regis, but it is clear the two men were different.<br>
<br>J. M. Rigg<span class="italic">, rev. </span> Robert Brown </div>
