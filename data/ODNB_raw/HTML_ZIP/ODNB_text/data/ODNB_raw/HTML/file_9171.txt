<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Farmery,  John</span>  (<em>d.</em> 1590), <span class="occ">physician</span>, a native of Lincolnshire, matriculated as a pensioner of King's College, Cambridge, in November 1561, taking his BA there in 1565 and his MA in 1568. He seems then to have practised medicine in London, as an empiric, with powerful patrons. The College of Physicians licensed him on 4 February 1587 and admitted him as a candidate a few days later on 22 February. He became a fellow on 28 February 1589, with an injunction to proceed MD within two years. In September 1589 he graduated MD at Leiden, after receiving testimonial letters from the London college.<br>
<br>In the same year Farmery was directed by the college to draw up, with Edward Atslowe, Lancelot Browne, and Robert Preest, the formulae of syrups, juleps, and decoctions for the <span class="italic">Pharmacopoeia</span>. He is thought also to be the author of <span class="italic">A Methode of Measuringe and Surveyinge of Land</span>, &#145;published by J. F., practitioner in physick&#146; (1589).<br>
<br>Farmery died in the spring of 1590. In his will dated 15 March, and proved 7 April 1590, he described himself as living in &#145;Alderbert strete&#146; in the parish of St Mary Aldermanbury, and asked to be buried in his parish church. Farmery was a Roman Catholic. His friend Richard Smith MD, was &#145;supraveisor&#146; of his will. With his wife, Anne (<i>d</i>. 1613), he had two daughters, Mary and Elizabeth. His widow afterwards married (licence dated 26 February 1593) the physician Edward Lister, and was buried in the church of St Mary Aldermanbury on 11 December 1613.<br>
<br>Charles Creighton<span class="italic">, rev. </span> Sarah Bakewell </div>
