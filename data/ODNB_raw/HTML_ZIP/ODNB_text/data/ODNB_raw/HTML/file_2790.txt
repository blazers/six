<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Bold,  John</span>  (1679&#150;1751), <span class="occ">Church of England clergyman</span>, was born at Laughton, near Welford, Leicestershire, the son of John Bold, also an Anglican clergyman. Educated first of all at a school in Kibworth Beauchamp, he entered St John's College, Cambridge, in 1695, and received his BA in 1699. He returned to Leicestershire and was master of a small school at Hinckley from 1698 to 1732 (which brought him an annual salary of &pound;10). He was ordained deacon in 1701, became a priest in 1703, and was curate of Stoney Stanton, near Hinckley (&pound;30 p.a.), from May 1702 until his death.<br>
<br>Bold wholly devoted himself to the religious welfare of his parishioners, and, although without private means, lived frugally and was able out of his small income to help parishioners in need, and to make several charitable bequests at his death.<br>
<br>Bold was the author of three pious works: <span class="italic">The Sin and Danger of Neglecting the Public Service of the Church</span>, which appeared in 1745, and was frequently reissued by SPCK; <span class="italic">Religion the most Delightful Employment</span>; and another which praised the pleasures of holy living, <span class="italic">The Duty of Worthy Communicating Recommended and Explained</span>. Bold died on 29 October 1751, and was buried in the churchyard at Stoney Stanton.<br>
<br>Sidney Lee<span class="italic">, rev. </span> Robert Brown </div>
