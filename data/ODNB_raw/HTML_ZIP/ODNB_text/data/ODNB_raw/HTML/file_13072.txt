<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Hereberht</span> <span class="st"> [St Hereberht, Herebert, Herbert]</span>  (<em>d.</em> 687), <span class="occ">hermit</span>, resided on the island in Derwent Water which still bears his name. He was a disciple and close friend of St Cuthbert, to whom he paid an annual visit for spiritual advice. The two friends both died on 20 March 687. In 1374 Thomas Appleby, bishop of Carlisle, directed the vicar of Crossthwaite to hold a yearly mass on St Herbert's Isle on his feast day (20 March), and granted forty days' indulgence to those who visited it then. The ruins of a circular stone building on the island may be associated with Hereberht.<br>
<br>C. L. Kingsford<span class="italic">, rev. </span> Marios Costambeys </div>
