<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Pitt,  William</span>  (1749&#150;1823), <span class="occ">writer on agriculture</span>, was born at Tettenhall, near Wolverhampton, Staffordshire. He lived in Pendeford, near Wolverhampton, moving later to Edgbaston, Birmingham. Pitt was one of those employed by the board of agriculture to prepare reports on the state of agriculture in each county, and he wrote reports on four counties, starting with <span class="italic">A General View of the Agriculture of the County of Stafford, with Observations on the Means of its Improvement</span> (1794). This was followed by similar reports on the agriculture of Northamptonshire (1809), Worcestershire (1813), and Leicestershire (1819); all but the last contain lists of plants. Pitt's other publications included <span class="italic">On Agricultural Political Arithmetic</span> (1803), <span class="italic">The Bullion Debate, a Satirical Poem</span> (1811), <span class="italic">A Comparative Statement of the Food Produced from Arable and Grass Land</span> (1812), and <span class="italic">A Topographical History of Staffordshire</span> (1817). Pitt died on 18 September 1823, and was buried at Tettenhall.<br>
<br>W. A. S. Hewins<span class="italic">, rev. </span> Anne Pimlott Baker </div>
