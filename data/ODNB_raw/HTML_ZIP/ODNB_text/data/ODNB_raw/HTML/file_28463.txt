<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Walford,  Thomas</span>  (1752&#150;1833), <span class="occ">antiquary</span>, born on 14 September 1752, was the only son of Thomas Walford (<i>d</i>. 1756) of Whitley, near Birdbrook in Essex, and his wife, Elizabeth Spurgeon (<i>d</i>. 1789) of Linton in Cambridgeshire. He was an officer in the Essex militia in 1777, and was appointed deputy lieutenant of the county in 1778. In March 1797 he became a captain in the provisional cavalry, and the following May he was promoted major. In February 1788 he was elected a fellow of the Society of Antiquaries, in October 1797 a fellow of the Linnean Society, in 1814 a member of the Geological Society, and in 1825 a fellow. In 1818 he published <span class="italic">The Scientific Tourist through England, Wales, and Scotland</span>. In this he described the principal objects of antiquity, art, science, and the picturesque in Great Britain, under the county headings. The work was too comprehensive to be exhaustive, and its value varied with Walford's personal knowledge of the places he describes.<br>
<br>Walford died, apparently unmarried, at Whitley on 6 August 1833, and was buried at Birdbrook parish church. He published several papers in <span class="italic">Archaeologia</span>, <span class="italic">Vetusta monumenta</span>, and <span class="italic">Transactions of the Linnean Society</span> and left several manuscripts, including a history of Birdbrook in Essex and another of Clare in Sussex.<br>
<br>E. I. Carlyle<span class="italic">, rev. </span> J. A. Marchand </div>
