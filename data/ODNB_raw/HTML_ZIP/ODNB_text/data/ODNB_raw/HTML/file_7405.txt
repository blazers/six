<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Bruyn,  Theodore  de</span>  (1730&#150;1804), <span class="occ">landscape painter</span>, was born in Amsterdam. He studied at Antwerp under Nicholaes van den Bergh, and moved to England to carry out decorative work for the duke of Norfolk in 1768. He entered the Royal Academy Schools in October 1773, aged forty-three, as a sculptor, but proceeded to exhibit landscape paintings at the Royal Academy, the Society of Artists, and the Free Society of Artists over the next twenty years. He contributed elegant, well-designed works to the genre of country house views. His <span class="italic">View of Painshill</span>, exhibited in 1790, was sold at Christies in 1950; others included <span class="italic">Luxborough House, Essex</span>, <span class="italic">Sudley House</span>, and <span class="italic">Lulworth Manor</span>. He also exhibited rustic landscapes with cattle and figures. His grisaille paintings of sculpture bas-relief were well regarded. The chapel at Greenwich Hospital and Carlton Towers, Yorkshire, contain grisaille decorations by de Bruyn. He died in London in 1804. His two sons, John, born on 9 September 1764, and Henry (<i>b</i>. 1772), both entered the Royal Academy Schools as painters in 1782 and 1792 respectively, and subsequently exhibited at the Royal Academy.<br>
<br>Ernest Radford<span class="italic">, rev. </span> R. J. Lambert </div>
