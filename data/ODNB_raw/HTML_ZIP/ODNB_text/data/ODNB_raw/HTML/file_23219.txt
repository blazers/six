<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Read,  Richard</span>  (1745?&#150;1790?), <span class="occ">engraver</span>, of unknown origins, was a pupil of the London engraver James Caldwell when in 1771 he gained a premium of the Society for the Encouragement of Arts, Manufactures, and Commerce for his drawing. He gave his address as Birmingham in 1777, when he exhibited the mezzotinto print <span class="italic">A Storm</span> with the Society of Artists. This, his most ambitious plate, reproduced <span class="italic">Perdita Found by an Old Shepherd and a Clown</span>, a scene from <span class="italic">The Winter's Tale</span> after Paul Sandby, and was published by Valentine Green, another artist from the west midlands. Read did not engrave many prints of consequence, and his principal employment may have been in some other aspect of design. Dodd's verdict that &#145;he became a slovenly practitioner both in the chalk style and in mezzotint and eventually abandoned his practice to become a dealer and printer of some of Bartolozzi's worn out plates&#146; is hardly unfair  (Dodd, fol. 35). Read is thought to have died in 1790.<br>
<br>Timothy Clayton and Anita McConnell </div>
