<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Watkins,  John</span>  (<em>fl. </em><em>c.</em>1786&#150;1831), <span class="occ">writer</span>, born in Devon, was educated at Bristol for the nonconformist ministry. Becoming dissatisfied, he joined the Church of England about 1786 with his friend Samuel Badcock (1747&#150;1788) and for some years ran an academy in Devon. His first independent publication appeared in 1792, entitled <span class="italic">An Essay towards the History of Bideford</span>. The work was of great local interest, containing among other things an account of the depositions in a trial for witchcraft held at Exeter on 14 August 1682. In 1796 he published <span class="italic">The Peeper: a Collection of Essays, Moral, Biographical, and Literary</span>, which was dedicated to Hannah More. Watkins showed great interest in biography, even compiling a <span class="italic">Universal Biographical and Historical Dictionary</span>, which appeared in 1800. It went through several editions by 1827, and was translated into French, with additions, in 1803 by Jean Baptiste L'Ecuy of Paris. Among similar compilations are <span class="italic">Characteristic Anecdotes of Men of Learning and Genius</span> (1808) and <span class="italic">Boydell's heads of illustrious and celebrated persons, from the reign of Queen Elizabeth to William III, with biographical memoirs</span> (1812). Watkins's <span class="italic">Memoirs of Sheridan</span> (1816) was the first life of Richard Brinsley Sheridan to be published. Other memoirs published by him were <span class="italic">Memoirs of Queen Sophia Charlotte</span> (1819); <span class="italic">Memoirs of the Life and Writings of Lord Byron</span> (1822), translated into German in 1825; <span class="italic">A Biographical Memoir of &hellip; Frederick, Duke of York and Albany</span> (1827); and <span class="italic">The Life and Times of &#145;England's Patriot King&#146;, William IV</span> (1831). He also translated from the Latin George Buchanan's <span class="italic">History of Scotland</span>, adding a continuation (1827), and wrote a memoir of Hugh Latimer, prefixed to his <span class="italic">Sermons</span> (1824). On the title pages of his later publications, Watkins is described as LLD but from where he obtained the degree is unknown. So is the date of his death, the last that is known of him being a preface dated 30 May 1831. He moved to London soon after beginning his literary work, about 1794, and it is possible that he died there.<br>
<br>E. I. Carlyle<span class="italic">, rev. </span> Nilanjana Banerji </div>
