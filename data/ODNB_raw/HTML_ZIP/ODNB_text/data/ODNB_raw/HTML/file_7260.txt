<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Davies,  Robert</span>  (1793&#150;1875), <span class="occ">antiquary</span>, eldest son of Peter Davies and his wife, Ann, daughter of Robert Rhodes, was born at York on 19 August 1793, and educated there at St Peter's School. Having qualified as a solicitor in 1814, he practised for many years in York. He married in 1826 Elizabeth, youngest daughter of George Cattle of York.<br>
<br>As town clerk from 1828 until 1848, Davies was active in local implementations of the <span class="roman">Municipal Reform Act</span>, and left his legal partnership in 1834 to serve the new corporation. He was a member of the Yorkshire Philosophical Society from 1824 and his legal and antiquarian interests resulted in an edition of <span class="italic">The Freeman's Roll of the City of York</span> (1835) and publication of <span class="italic">Extracts from the Municipal Records of the City of York</span> (1843). After his retirement he was elected a magistrate and continued his scholarly work, concentrating on more recent history. His <span class="italic">Memoir of the York Press</span> (1868) remains an important source for the history of printing, and his posthumously published <span class="italic">Walks through the City of York</span> (1880), edited by his widow from papers read to the Yorkshire Philosophical Society, adopted an approach to topographical and historical writing that has often been copied. He contributed to publications of the Surtees Society and other local societies, and was elected a fellow of the Society of Antiquaries on 22 December 1842. Davies died at his residence in The Mount, in the city of York, on 23 August 1875, and was buried in York cemetery on 29 August.<br>
<br>William Joseph Sheils </div>
