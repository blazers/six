<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Thomas,  Matthew Evan <span class="st"> [Matthew Edward]</span></span>  (1788&#150;1830), <span class="occ">architect</span>, became a student of the Royal Academy in 1812, and in 1815 he gained the academy's gold medal for a design for a palace. He went to Italy in the following year, and remained there until 1819. During his stay he was elected a member of the academy at Florence, and of the Accademia di San Luca at Rome. After his return he exhibited architectural drawings at the Royal Academy between 1820 and 1822. In 1826 he exhibited at the Society (later Royal Society) of British Artists <span class="italic">All Souls College, Oxford</span> and <span class="italic">L'arco dei Bosari, Verona</span>. In 1827 and 1828 he exhibited three portraits. Thomas appears in the left foreground of Sir George Hayter's <span class="italic">The Trial of Queen Caroline</span> (1820&#150;23), holding a pencil, and with a small notebook balanced on the back of the chair behind which he is standing. His name is included in <span class="roman">Hayter's Key</span> for the picture and he is described in the National Portrait Gallery's publication of the Key as &#145;a reporter&#146;  (Walker, 1.614). He died at Hackney on 12 July 1830, and was buried in St John's Wood Chapel.<br>
<br>Walter Armstrong<span class="italic">, rev. </span> Helene Furja&#324; </div>
