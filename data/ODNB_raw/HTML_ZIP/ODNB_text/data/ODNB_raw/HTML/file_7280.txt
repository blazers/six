<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Davis,  Edward</span>  (1833&#150;1867), <span class="occ">genre painter</span>, was born at Worcester and learned drawing there before attending the Birmingham School of Design, then under the management of J. Kyd. When Kyd transferred to the Worcester School of Design Davis accompanied him, studying there for three years and winning several prizes. He first exhibited at the Royal Academy in 1854, giving his address as 22 Foregate Street, Worcester. He showed <span class="italic">Meditation</span>, depicting an old villager sitting by a fireside, and <span class="italic">Parting Words</span>, a deathbed scene. In the following year he sent <span class="italic">A Cottage Scene</span> to the academy and by 1856 he was living in London, at 16 Russell Place, Fitzroy Square. Among his most highly regarded works were <span class="italic">On the Way to School</span> (engraved by William Ridgway), <span class="italic">Granny's Spectacles</span>, <span class="italic">Doing Crochet Work</span>, <span class="italic">Words of Peace</span>, and <span class="italic">The Little Peg-Top</span>, which was prestigiously placed on the line at the Royal Academy. He died young, while studying in Rome, on 12 June 1867. Examples of his paintings, which show careful draughtsmanship and a fondness for chubby figures, are in the Ashmolean Museum in Oxford. An album of his drawings was sold at Christies in 1951.<br>
<br>L. A. Fagan<span class="italic">, rev. </span> Suzanne Fagence Cooper </div>
