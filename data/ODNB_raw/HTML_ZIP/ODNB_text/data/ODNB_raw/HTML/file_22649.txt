<div class="para">
<span class="headword">Powell,  Griffith</span>  (1560/61&#150;1620), <span class="occ">college head</span>, was the third of four sons of John ap Hywel of Prysg Melyn, Llansawel, Carmarthenshire, and his wife, Annes, daughter of Gruffydd ap Henry. He matriculated at Jesus College, Oxford, on 24 November 1581, graduated BA on 28 February 1584, and proceeded MA on 21 June 1589, BCL on 12 July 1593, and DCL on 23 July 1599. From the time he entered Jesus College, of which he became a fellow in 1589 or 1590, his life was spent at Oxford. Under Francis Bevans (1586&#150;1602) and John Williams (1602&#150;13) he played a large part in the running of the college. He was &#145;an energetic and capable tutor&#146; and a &#145;noted philosopher&#146;, writing two important works on Aristotle, <span class="italic">Analysis analyticorum posteriorum</span> and <span class="italic">De sophisticis elenchis</span>, published respectively in 1594 and 1598. He also showed himself &#145;very diligent, industrious and careful&#146; for the good estate of the college. Powell succeeded Williams as principal in 1613. His careful management of legacies and endowments made it possible to establish more resident fellowships and scholarships, and Jesus College became popular particularly with students from south Wales in Powell's time. His appeal to the Welsh clergy and gentry raised &pound;764 5<i>s</i>. 6<i>d</i>., which was used to build the hall, buttery, and kitchens, and the outer quadrangle of the college, with the chapel, was completed within a year of Powell's death. He died, unmarried, at Oxford on 28 June 1620, and was buried in St Michael's Church, Oxford. By a nuncupative will made on the day of his death, and proved on 15 June 1621, he left his estate of &pound;648 17<i>s</i>. 2<i>d</i>. to the college; it was used to buy land in Flintshire to maintain a fellowship at the college.<br>
<br>Barrie Williams </div>
